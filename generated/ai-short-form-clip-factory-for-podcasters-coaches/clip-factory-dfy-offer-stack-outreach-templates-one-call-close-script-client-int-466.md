3) ONE-CALL CLOSE SCRIPT (15 MIN)
====================
Objective: Close a DFY package on the call; capture SaaS signals second.

Open (1 min)
- “Thanks for taking this—my goal is to see if we can reliably turn your episodes into short-form clips that drive {{CTA}}.”

Diagnose (4 min)
1) “Where do you post today (TikTok/Reels/Shorts) and how often?”
2) “What’s stopping consistency—time, editing, or not knowing what to clip?”
3) “What’s the #1 outcome you want from shorts? (leads, subscribers, course sales, brand)?”

Pitch (3 min)
- “Here’s what I recommend: we take your last episode and deliver 8 ready-to-post clips with captions + hooks in 72 hours. If it works, we move to monthly.”

Offer + Price (1 min)
- “Starter is $499 for 8 clips. If you want ongoing, $999/mo gets you 32 clips (4 episodes).”

Close (2 min)
- “Do you want to start with the $499 test, or jump straight to the monthly?”

If hesitation: objection handling (examples)
A) “We already have an editor.”
- “Totally—most editors can cut, but consistency usually breaks on hooks/captioning + knowing what moments convert. We can run a one-episode test and you can compare performance.”

B) “Not sure shorts work for my audience.”
- “Fair. Let’s treat it as a 2-week experiment: 8 clips, measured by saves/shares and profile clicks. If it doesn’t move metrics, we stop.”

C) “Budget.”
- “If you want, we can start smaller: $499 test. If 2–3 clips hit, the monthly is funded by the lead flow.”

Capture SaaS signal (2 min, after yes)
- “If this were automated software later: would you pay $99–$299/mo to upload a link and get suggested highlights + captioned exports? What would it need to include for you to keep it?”
- “Which matters most: speaker labels, brand templates, auto-hooks, b-roll cues, or scheduling?”

Next steps (2 min)
- “If you’re in, send the link + brand notes to agent_bob_replit+clip-factory@agentmail.to. We’ll confirm clip style and deliver by {{date}}.”
