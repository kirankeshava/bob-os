1) What you’ll receive (Delivery Pack)
We deliver a folder containing:
1. Final Clips (MP4) — vertical 9:16 exports, ready to post.
2. Captions File (SRT) — matches final edit.
3. B-Roll Cue Sheet (Google Doc or PDF) — timestamps and suggested overlays/cutaways.
4. Posting Suggestions (Doc) — hook line options, first-line caption options, and hashtag set.

2) Quality Standards (our QA checklist)
A) Safe framing / no cropped faces
• Subject’s eyes/face remain inside the “safe zone” (platform UI won’t cover key content).
• No accidental forehead/top-of-head crop during punch-ins.
• If using dynamic zooms, the face stays centered and stable.

B) Captions accuracy + readability
• Captions reflect what’s actually said (no hallucinations).
• Speaker changes are handled clearly.
• Lines are not too long; readability prioritized.
• Captions avoid covering the mouth/face when possible.

C) Audio leveling + clarity
• Dialogue is clearly audible on phone speakers.
• No harsh clipping/distortion.
• Background music (if used) is under voice and never competes.
• Major breaths/clicks reduced when distracting.

D) Hook clarity + pacing
• The first 1–2 seconds contain a clear hook (question, bold statement, or payoff preview).
• Dead air removed; pacing feels intentional.
• Jump cuts are clean (no jarring mid-syllable cuts unless stylistic and approved).

E) Visual polish
• B-roll/overlays support what’s being said (no random stock).
• No copyrighted or unsafe visual/audio elements added unless provided/approved.
• On-screen text is spelled correctly and consistent.

F) Export / formatting
• 9:16 vertical, 1080x1920.
• H.264 MP4 (unless you request different).
• Filename conventions: Client_Project_Clip##_(Platform)_v1.mp4

3) Review process (timestamp comments)
We provide a review link (Frame.io / Vimeo / unlisted YouTube) so you can leave timestamped comments.
How to comment:
• Watch the clip once through.
• Add comments at the exact timestamp.
• If requesting a caption fix, paste the corrected words.

4) Revision window
• You have 24 hours from delivery of the review link to request revisions.
• If we don’t hear back within 24 hours, we assume approval and close the delivery.
• We will confirm receipt of your revision notes and provide an estimated turnaround.

5) What counts as a free revision vs. change request
Free revisions (included):
• Caption typos / minor word fixes
• Small timing tweaks to captions
• Audio leveling adjustments
• Small trim changes (remove a sentence, tighten pause)
• Minor reframing changes (safe-zone fixes)

Change requests (scope change; handled separately):
• New clip selection from different part of the episode
• Changing the hook concept after delivery
• Adding new brand assets or changing style guide after editing
• Major restructuring (new story arc) beyond tightening
• New platform versions not originally specified

If you’re not sure, send the request. We’ll classify it clearly before doing extra work.

6) How to get the best results (client responsibilities)
To avoid preventable revisions, please provide:
• Target platforms (TikTok/Reels/Shorts)
• Tone/style references (2–3 examples)
• Brand rules (fonts/colors if any)
• Any banned words/topics
• Your CTA + link/handle
