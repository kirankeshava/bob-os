A) CLIENT-FACING POSITIONING ONE-PAGER (paste into email/DM or a doc)

Clip Factory — Turn 1 long-form episode into a week of vertical clips

Who it’s for
- Podcasters, coaches, consultants, course creators, and small brands posting on TikTok/Reels/Shorts.

Outcome (what you get)
- Ready-to-post 9:16 clips with:
  1) a strong hook (first 1–2 seconds)
  2) clean captions (burned-in or .srt)
  3) basic b-roll / cut suggestions (where to add screenshots, quick jump cuts, emphasis)
  4) platform-safe formatting (safe margins, aspect, length)

How it works (DFY)
1) You send a YouTube link or upload the episode.
2) We identify high-signal moments (story, contrarian take, “how-to”, proof/results, emotion).
3) We cut and caption in a consistent style.
4) You approve, we deliver a folder of finished vertical clips.

Why this beats hiring an editor (for most creators)
- Faster turnaround, consistent formatting, and you don’t need to manage an editor day-to-day.
- Designed for volume: consistent weekly clip output.

Typical packages (example—adjust on the call)
- Starter: 8 clips/week
- Growth: 15 clips/week
- Pro: 25 clips/week

C) DISCOVERY → PAID PILOT CLOSE SCRIPT (12–20 minutes)

Goal hierarchy for the call
1) Close DFY paid pilot (revenue now)
2) Capture SaaS requirements as secondary (“if we automated this later, what matters?”)
3) Ask for referrals if not a fit

Call structure

1) Opener (60 seconds)
“Thanks for the time. Context: we run Clip Factory—turn one long episode into a week of ready-to-post vertical clips (hooks + captions + formatting). You can see the process here: https://6ff73b98-dcff-4f53-a10d-9ad392f63342-00-1n4h4nuiq6w8j-dbyyfbxp.picard.replit.dev/sites/3. 

I’ll ask a few quick questions about your workflow, then I’ll recommend a pilot size that makes sense. Sound good?”

2) Diagnose current state (5–7 minutes)
- “Where do your episodes live? YouTube, Spotify video, Riverside, Zoom?”
- “How often do you publish long-form?”
- “How many shorts are you posting weekly today?”
- “What’s stopping you from posting more—time, editing, ideation, approvals?”
- “Do you have a preferred caption style (word-by-word, sentence, karaoke)?”
- “Any brand constraints? Fonts/colors, safe words, claims you avoid?”

3) Define success (2 minutes)
- “If this worked perfectly over the next 30 days, what would you want? More inbound leads? Course sales? Newsletter signups? Booked calls?”
- “Which platform matters most right now—Reels, TikTok, Shorts?”

4) Present DFY pilot (2–3 minutes)
“Based on what you said, I recommend we start with a pilot from 1 episode. We’ll deliver {{8/10/15}} vertical clips, ready-to-post, with hooks + captions + formatting. You’ll get a delivery folder and can request small revisions for style alignment.”

5) Close (direct question)
“Want to start with that pilot this week? If yes, send me the episode link today and we’ll confirm your caption/style preferences by email: agent_bob_replit+clip-factory@agentmail.to.”

6) If price pushback
- “No problem—what budget did you have in mind?”
- Offer smaller pilot: “We can do 6 clips to start, then scale if performance looks good.”
- Or cadence: “We can do every other week instead of weekly.”

7) Capture SaaS signal (2–4 minutes; do not derail the close)
“Separate from the pilot—if we later offered a self-serve tool where you paste a link/upload and it auto-finds highlights + exports 9:16 with captions, what would you pay $99–$299/mo for?”
Prompts:
- “Auto highlight detection accuracy vs control—what matters?”
- “Must-have: speaker labels/diarization?”
- “Must-have: brand template (fonts/colors), hook generator, niche templates?”
- “Dealbreaker: upload limits, turnaround, caption styling?”

8) Wrap + next step
“I’ll email a simple checklist: episode link, preferred platform, caption style, and branding notes. Reply there and we’ll kick off.”
