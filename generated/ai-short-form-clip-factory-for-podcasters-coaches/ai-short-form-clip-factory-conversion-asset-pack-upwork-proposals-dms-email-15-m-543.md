3) 15-MIN SALES CALL MINI-SCRIPT
==============================
Goal: confirm fit, define output, remove risk, close on next step (sample → batch).

Minute 0–1: Set frame
“Thanks for hopping on. Plan: I’ll ask a few questions about your content + goals, then I’ll walk you through how we turn long-form into clips that perform. If it’s a fit, next step is simple: send your latest link and we’ll return 1 sample clip in 24h.”

Minute 1–6: Discovery questions (pick 6–8)
1) “What’s the main goal for short-form right now: reach, lead-gen, authority, or sales?”
2) “Where do you want to post: TikTok, Reels, Shorts — which matters most?”
3) “How often do you publish long-form (weekly episodes, trainings, lives)?”
4) “What’s your current short-form output? What’s stopping consistency?”
5) “What topics/offers do you want clips to support?”
6) “Do you have examples of clips you love (any creators/brands)?”
7) “Any must-avoid: words, topics, claims, style constraints?”
8) “What does ‘success’ look like in 30 days? (views, saves, leads, booked calls)”

Minute 6–10: Pitch (tight)
“Here’s how we help: we take your long-form and package the best moments into native vertical clips — hook-first, tighter pacing, readable captions, and optional b-roll/overlay cues so the viewer never gets lost.

The big difference vs basic editing is packaging. We’re optimizing for the first 2 seconds + retention, not just trimming.

Delivery is batch-based so you stay consistent. Once we lock a style you like, it becomes a repeatable weekly system.”

“Fair. We use AI to speed up selection + captioning, but quality control is manual: we verify wording, timing, readability, and brand tone.
The sample clip is the proof. Send your latest link and we’ll return 1 sample clip in 24h, and you can judge quality before committing.”

“Understood. The way to think about it is cost per posted clip and cost per week of consistency.
If we deliver [X] clips/week, you’re buying a predictable content engine without hiring/training. Also, we can start smaller: a 5-clip test batch, then scale once we see what themes perform.
