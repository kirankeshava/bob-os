Step 1 — Intake (required before production)
Before editing starts, please fill out the Client Intake & Clip Preferences form:

Why this matters: it prevents brand-term mistakes, caption style mismatches, and avoidable revisions.

Step 2 — Clip creation (what we optimize for)
Every clip is QC’d for:
• Hook clarity in the first 1–2 seconds
• Safe framing (no cropped faces; subject inside safe zones)
• Accurate captions (no missing/changed meaning; corrected punctuation)
• Audio leveling (clear voice, controlled peaks; no harsh clipping)
• Clean jump cuts and pacing (remove dead air, keep meaning intact)
• Platform-ready vertical formatting (Reels/TikTok/Shorts)

Step 3 — Review (timestamped comments)
You’ll receive a review link (Frame.io / Vimeo / unlisted YouTube) that supports timestamp notes.
How to request changes:
1) Open the link
2) Leave comments at exact timestamps (e.g., 00:07–00:10 “tighten pause”)
3) Group similar requests (captions, framing, hook, b-roll)

Revision window:
• 24 hours from the time the review link is sent.
• If we don’t hear back within 24 hours, we’ll assume approval and move to final delivery (or close the revision window, depending on your package).

Step 4 — Final delivery (Delivery Pack)
Your delivery is a folder containing:
• Final MP4 clips (ready to post)
• Caption files (SRT) for each clip
• B-roll cue sheet (timecodes + suggested overlays)
• Posting suggestions: first-line caption options + hashtag set + recommended posting notes

File naming convention (what you’ll see)
• CLIENTNAME_Clip01_HOOKSUMMARY_1080x1920.mp4
• CLIENTNAME_Clip01.srt
• CLIENTNAME_BRoll-Cues_YYYY-MM-DD.pdf (or .docx)
• CLIENTNAME_Posting-Suggestions_YYYY-MM-DD.docx
