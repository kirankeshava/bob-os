CLIENT ONE-PAGER: HOW TO REVIEW CLIPS + REQUEST REVISIONS (Copy/Paste)

Thanks for trying Clip Factory. Here’s the fastest way to review and request changes so we can keep the 48-hour turnaround.

Where your clips are delivered
- You’ll receive a download link (or folder link) with:
  1) Final exports (TikTok / Reels / Shorts)
  2) A “NOTES” file listing each clip’s hook + suggested caption + posting notes
  3) (Optional) A review link for time-stamped comments

How to review (recommended order)
1) Watch each clip once without stopping (gut-check).
2) Re-watch and mark only MUST-FIX items.
3) Confirm brand/compliance items (names, claims, sensitive topics).

What counts as a revision (included)
Included revisions are limited to: captions/typos, minor timing, replacing a b-roll cue with another cue, small hook text tweaks, volume leveling, removing a sentence, or swapping the CTA end card.

What does NOT count as a revision (new request)
These require a new order because they change the creative direction or require new selects:
- “Pick different moments from the episode” (new highlight selection)
- New style/theme or new caption system after delivery
- Adding new footage you didn’t provide during intake
- Rebuilding multiple clips from scratch

Revision limits + SLA
- 1 revision round included per batch of 10 clips.
- You must request revisions within 48 hours of delivery.
- We return revisions within 24 hours after receiving a complete revision request.

INTERNAL MICRO-SOP: REVISION TRIAGE (fast handling)

Goal: protect SLA while keeping client happy.
1) Require the revision format above. If missing, reply requesting it.
2) Triage each request:
   - Included (minor edits) → accept and schedule
   - New request (new selects / new direction) → offer: “We can do that as an add-on/new batch after the free week.”
3) Cap scope: max 10 clips revised in the included round (1 pass). If they request multiple passes, confirm it’s outside included revisions.
4) Execute revisions in this order for speed: captions/typos → cut trims → audio leveling → CTA end card swaps → b-roll cue swaps.
5) Re-export only the changed clips; keep naming convention and version bump: _v2.
