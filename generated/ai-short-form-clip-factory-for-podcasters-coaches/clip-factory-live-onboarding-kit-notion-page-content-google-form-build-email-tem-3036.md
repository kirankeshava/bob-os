2) Our QA standards (what we check on every clip)
We QC every deliverable against the checklist below to reduce revisions and prevent platform issues.

A. Framing & safe zones (vertical-first)
- 9:16 vertical export.
- No cropped faces: eyes and mouth must remain fully visible across the clip.
- Keep key on-screen elements inside “safe zones” (avoid bottom UI / right-side UI overlays):
  - Bottom safe area: keep text above the bottom ~15%.
  - Right safe area (TikTok UI): keep text away from the right edge.
- Headroom: avoid cutting off hair/top of head; avoid extreme zooms unless intentional.

B. Hook clarity (first 1–2 seconds)
- Hook is clear immediately (strong first line + visual emphasis).
- Clip context is understandable without the full episode.
- No long preambles; remove dead air and soft starts.

C. Captions quality
- Captions are accurate to the spoken words (names/brands verified).
- Readability: high contrast, consistent style, and line breaks that match natural phrasing.
- Timing: captions appear/disappear aligned to speech; no lagging lines.

D. Audio leveling
- Balanced loudness and no sudden jumps.
- No clipping/distortion; reduce harsh sibilance if needed.
- Background noise minimized where possible without making voice sound “underwater.”

E. Editing rhythm
- Clean jump cuts; remove filler and long pauses.
- No distracting micro-cuts; pacing feels intentional.
- B-roll cues (if used) support the point and don’t obscure faces or key text.

F. Platform readiness
- File plays correctly on mobile.
- No banned words or prohibited claims (based on your intake notes).
- Export settings match standard social posting requirements (see Section 4).

3) Review flow (how to request revisions)
We provide a review link (Frame.io / Vimeo / YouTube unlisted) that allows timestamped comments.

How to comment effectively
- Leave comments at exact timestamps (e.g., “00:12 – change caption to …”).
- If requesting caption changes, paste the exact replacement text.
- If requesting a different hook, provide a suggested hook line or the angle you want.

Revision window
- You have 24 hours after delivery to request free revisions within scope (see Revision Policy below).
- If we do not hear back within 24 hours, we consider the delivery accepted and close the job.

4) Delivery pack (what you’ll receive)
Each batch is delivered as a “Delivery Pack” folder that includes:

1) Final MP4 clips
- Filename convention: CLIENT_EpisodeOrTopic_Clip##_##s_Platform.mp4

2) Captions file (SRT)
- One SRT per clip, matched by filename.

3) B-roll cue sheet
- A simple document listing timestamps and suggested b-roll overlays.

4) Posting suggestions (per clip)
- First-line caption (the line users see before “more”)
- Suggested hashtags (platform-appropriate)
- Optional CTA suggestion (follow, comment, link-in-bio, etc.)

Export baseline (unless your intake requests otherwise)
- Aspect ratio: 9:16
- Container: .mp4
- Codec: H.264
- Resolution: 1080x1920
- Frame rate: match source when reasonable (commonly 30 fps)
- Audio: AAC, 48 kHz

5) Revision policy (to avoid surprises)
Free revisions (within 24 hours) include
- Caption text corrections (typos/mis-hears)
- Minor timing fixes for captions
- Small safe-zone adjustments (text moved up/down, slight reframes)
- Minor audio leveling tweaks
- Swapping a b-roll overlay with another option from the cue sheet

Not free (scope change / new work)
- Re-cutting to a different section of the episode
- Changing the core hook angle or rewriting the clip concept after delivery
- Adding new brand elements not provided during intake
- Significant style change (new fonts/colors/template) after approval
- Extra versions for additional platforms beyond the originally requested formats

If you request a scope change, we’ll reply with options and a clear estimate before doing additional work.
