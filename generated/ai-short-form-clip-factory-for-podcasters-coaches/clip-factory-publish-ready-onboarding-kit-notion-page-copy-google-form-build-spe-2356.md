## 2) Our QA standards (what we check before you ever review)
We run QA on every clip to reduce revisions and protect your brand.

### A) Safe zones + framing
- Faces are not cropped awkwardly (forehead/chin/eyes not cut off).
- Subtitles sit **inside safe zones** (no UI overlap on TikTok/Reels).
- No critical text/graphics placed near screen edges.
- Any punch-ins/zooms preserve image quality and keep the subject centered.

### B) Captions accuracy + readability
- Captions match spoken words closely (no meaning changes).
- Proper nouns corrected when clear (names, brand terms, product names).
- Readability:
  - Line breaks make sense (no orphan words).
  - Timing feels natural (not late/early by > ~0.2–0.4s unless stylistic).
  - Highlighting/emphasis used consistently (if enabled).
- Profanity handling follows your preference (bleep, mask, or verbatim).

### C) Audio leveling + clarity
- Voice is clear and consistent; background noise reduced.
- No clipping/distortion.
- Loudness is normalized to a short-form-friendly level.
- If music is used: it never overpowers speech; licensed/royalty-free only.

### D) Jump cuts + pacing
- Cuts are intentional (removes dead air without sounding robotic).
- No jarring mid-word cuts.
- Visual rhythm matches the hook and key points.

### E) Hook clarity (first 1–2 seconds)
- The first frame + first line creates curiosity or states a clear outcome.
- On-screen text (if used) reinforces the hook (not redundant clutter).

### F) Export + platform formatting
- Vertical aspect ratio (9:16).
- Legible subtitle size on mobile.
- No black bars unless specifically requested.
- MP4/H.264 export settings suitable for TikTok/Reels/Shorts.

## 5) What counts as a free revision vs. a change request
Our goal is to be fair, fast, and consistent.

### Free revisions (included)
These are fixes that bring the clips in line with the agreed requirements:
- Caption typos or mis-hearings.
- Subtitle timing adjustments.
- Minor audio leveling tweaks.
- Safe-zone repositioning (captions/text too low/high).
- Small visual polish (missed zoom timing, minor crop/framing fix).
- Export issue (wrong format, missing file, corrupted file).

### Change requests (scope change)
These are **new creative directions** or re-edits that go beyond QA fixes:
- Changing the chosen hook/topic after delivery.
- Replacing large sections of the clip with different parts of the long video.
- Significant style changes (new subtitle style, new template, new pacing style) not previously requested.
- New branding package added after work began (logo, fonts, colors, end cards).
- New deliverable requests (additional clips, extra aspect ratios, alternate versions).

**Policy:** During the free launch period (Week 1), we’ll be generous, but we still use this classification to keep turnaround predictable and avoid missed deadlines.

# Pre-Flight Checklist Mapping (Internal)
Use this before editing starts to prevent avoidable revisions.

1) Platforms selected → confirm aspect ratio 9:16 and safe zone rules.
2) Tone selected → confirm pacing/jump cut aggressiveness and hook style.
3) Caption style selected → confirm subtitle template choice.
4) Profanity handling → confirm caption masking/bleep policy.
5) Music preference → confirm include/exclude and licensing.
6) Framing preference → confirm crop strategy and face safety.
7) Branding assets link/upload → verify access and apply consistently.
8) Banned words/topics → flag for editor; avoid in captions/on-screen text.
9) CTA link/goal → ensure posting suggestions match objective.
10) Examples provided → match style within reason; note any constraints.

If anything is missing/unclear, send an “Intake Confirmation” email before editing begins.
