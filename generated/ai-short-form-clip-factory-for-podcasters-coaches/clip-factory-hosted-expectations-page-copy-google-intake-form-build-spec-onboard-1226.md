Section 1 — What we produce (per clip)
We turn your long-form video or podcast into ready-to-post vertical clips optimized for TikTok / Reels / Shorts.
Each clip includes:
• Final vertical MP4 (1080×1920)
• Burned-in captions (readable, on-brand)
• Captions file (.SRT)
• Hook-forward structure (strong first 1–2 seconds)
• Clean jump cuts (remove dead air, minimize distractions)
• Optional b-roll / on-screen text cues (as a cue sheet)
• Posting suggestions (caption first line + hashtag set)

Section 2 — Your required intake (to avoid rework)
Before we start, please fill out our intake form (brand terms, caption style, platforms, banned words, etc.). This prevents errors like wrong names, misspellings, incorrect claims, or the wrong tone.

If you choose to skip the intake form, you can—but you must confirm in writing via email that we should proceed using “best judgment.”

Section 3 — Review flow (timestamp comments)
1) We send a review link (unlisted video link) where you can leave timestamped comments.
2) You reply with a single consolidated list of edits.
3) We apply the edits and deliver finals.

How to leave feedback (best practice):
• Comment with timestamps (e.g., “00:07 change caption to ‘…’”).
• Group notes into: Captions, Hook, Visual framing, Audio.
• Avoid sending changes across multiple emails—one consolidated message is fastest.

Section 4 — Revisions policy (24-hour window)
We include a 24-hour revision window starting when the review link is delivered.
• “Free revisions” are for mistakes or alignment to the agreed brief.
• “Paid changes” are new requests outside the brief (see below).

If we don’t receive revision notes within 24 hours, the delivery is considered accepted and we close the order.

Section 5 — What counts as FREE revisions
Free revisions include:
• Caption corrections (spelling/grammar, missed words, minor timing fixes)
• Fixing incorrect names/terms you provided in the intake form
• Audio fixes (peaking, inconsistent levels) to meet our delivery standard
• Cropping/framing fixes (e.g., face cut off, unsafe text area issues)
• Removing obvious filler moments we missed (quick trims)
• Formatting issues (wrong aspect ratio, export glitches)

Section 6 — What counts as PAID changes (scope change)
Paid changes typically include:
• Changing the clip’s core concept/hook after delivery (“new angle”, “different story”)
• Rewriting or substantially changing captions for style/voice after you approved the approach
• Adding new b-roll sourcing beyond the agreed cues (especially if it requires asset hunting)
• New clip selections from the long-form (new timestamps / new highlights)
• Brand repositioning requests after work has started (new tone, new audience, new offer)

If you request a paid change, we’ll quote the cost and timeline before proceeding.

Section 7 — Delivery pack (what you receive)
Your final delivery comes as a folder containing:
• /FINAL_MP4/ (ready-to-post vertical MP4s)
• /CAPTIONS_SRT/ (one .srt per clip)
• /BROLL_CUE_SHEETS/ (one PDF or doc per clip)
• /POSTING_SUGGESTIONS/ (caption first line + hashtags + platform notes)

Section 8 — Quality standards we check before delivery
We run a QC pass to confirm:
• No cropped faces; safe margins for UI overlays
• Captions accurate and readable (contrast, size, timing)
• Audio level consistent; no clipping/peaking
• Hook clarity in first seconds
• Smooth jump cuts; no distracting glitches
• Export settings correct for vertical platforms
