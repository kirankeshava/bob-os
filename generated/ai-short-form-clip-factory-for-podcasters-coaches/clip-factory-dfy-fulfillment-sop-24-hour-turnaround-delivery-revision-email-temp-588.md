GOAL
Deliver consistently strong vertical clips (9:16) from long-form video/podcasts with a predictable 24-hour turnaround for “sample” and 48–72 hours for larger batches—so we close DFY packages quickly and reach the $5k revenue gate before any SaaS build.

DFY PACKAGES THIS SOP SUPPORTS
1) Sample Clip (fast close offer): 1 vertical clip (20–45s) + burned-in captions + hook text suggestions. Turnaround: 24 hours.
2) Starter Batch: 5 clips/episode. Turnaround: 48 hours.
3) Growth Batch: 12 clips/episode. Turnaround: 72 hours.
Add-ons: extra caption styles, b-roll cue list, CTA variants, platform-specific versions.

TOOLS (NO SPEND DEFAULT)
- Editing: CapCut Desktop or DaVinci Resolve (free)
- Captions: CapCut auto-captions OR Whisper transcription (when needed)
- Audio cleanup: CapCut/Resolve basics (normalize + limiter)
- File delivery: Google Drive folder (client-owned or ours)
- Project tracking: Google Sheet/Notion board

QUALITY BAR (DEFINITION OF DONE)
A clip is “Done” when:
- Format: 1080×1920 (9:16), H.264 MP4, 30fps (or source), <150MB when possible
- Safe margins: key text not in top/bottom UI zones
- Captions: accurate, readable, timed, no profanity mistakes; 1–2 lines max on screen
- Hook: first 1.5 seconds has a clear pattern break (jump cut, zoom, on-screen hook, or cold open)
- Pacing: dead air removed, long pauses cut; tight jump cuts
- Audio: consistent loudness, no clipping, minimal background noise
- Brand consistency: consistent caption style for the batch
- Naming: standardized filenames + delivery notes

STANDARD WORKFLOW (SAMPLE CLIP IN 24 HOURS)
T0 — Payment + Intake received
1) Create client folder: /ClientName/ProjectName/YYYY-MM-DD/
2) Log job in tracker: Client, source link, package, due date, platform targets.

T0–T+2 hours — Content selection
3) Pull source into editor.
4) Identify 3 candidate moments (each 20–45s) using these heuristics:
   - Strong opinion or contrarian statement
   - Clear step-by-step tip
   - Emotional/confessional moment
   - “Mistake most people make…”
5) Choose BEST moment (or send 3 timecodes to client if required by contract).

T+2–T+6 hours — First cut
6) Build rough cut:
   - Remove intro fluff
   - Cut to punchline quickly
   - Add jump cuts or subtle zooms every 3–6 seconds if appropriate
7) Create hook overlay options (2–3 lines). Examples:
   - “Stop doing this in your marketing…”
   - “The 30-second fix for [problem]”
   - “Most coaches price wrong—here’s why”

T+6–T+12 hours — Captions + styling
8) Generate captions (auto + manual corrections).
9) Caption styling rules:
   - High contrast (white text w/ shadow or dark stroke)
   - Highlight keywords (1 color max)
   - Avoid covering faces/mouth when possible

T+12–T+18 hours — QA
10) Watch full clip with sound ON then OFF:
   - Sound ON: check pacing, audio consistency, caption timing
   - Sound OFF: check readability, hook clarity, visual interest
11) Final technical export settings (standard):
   - MP4, H.264, 1080×1920, AAC audio

T+18–T+24 hours — Delivery
12) Upload to Drive folder.
13) Send delivery email (template below) including:
   - Drive link
   - 1–2 posting suggestions
   - 2 alternate hooks
   - Ask for 1 revision note (time-stamped) within 48 hours

BATCH WORKFLOW (5–12 CLIPS)
- Step A: Timecode pass (find 12–20 candidate moments)
- Step B: Select final moments by score (0–3 each): hook strength, clarity, emotional pull, specificity
- Step C: Assembly line production
  1) Cut all clips first
  2) Caption all clips second
  3) QA all clips third
  4) Export + upload in one batch
- Naming convention:
  Client_Ep###_Clip01_"Topic"_v1.mp4

REVISION POLICY (KEEP IT TIGHT)
Included revisions:
- Sample Clip: 1 revision round (caption fixes + minor trims) within 48 hours.
- Batches: 1 revision round per 5 clips.
Not included (scope creep): rewriting the core message, replacing clip with a totally different segment after approval, heavy motion graphics.
Revision turnaround: 24–48 hours depending on queue.
