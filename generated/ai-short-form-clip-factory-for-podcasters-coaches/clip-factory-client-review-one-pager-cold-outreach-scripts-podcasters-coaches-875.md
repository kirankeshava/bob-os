Goal: Make approvals fast so we can keep the 48-hour delivery promise for your 10 clips.

1) Where you’ll receive clips
You’ll receive either:
- A folder link (Drive/Dropbox) with MP4 files + optional captions file, OR
- A review link (Frame.io/Vimeo/Drive preview) plus a download folder.
Each clip is named so you can approve quickly.

2) The 5-minute review method (fastest)
Please review in this order:
A) First 2 seconds: Is the hook strong and clear? (Yes/No)
B) Captions: Any wrong words/names/claims? (List timestamp + correction)
C) Visuals: Any awkward jump cut or missing b-roll cue? (Timestamp)
D) CTA end-card: Does it match your offer/handle? (Yes/No)
E) Platform fit: Safe areas (top/bottom) not covered by UI? (Yes/No)

3) How to request revisions (copy/paste format)
Reply to the delivery email with:
- Clip filename:
- Timestamp(s): (e.g., 00:07–00:10)
- Change needed: (exact text or instruction)
- Priority: Critical (must-fix) / Nice-to-have
Example:
Clip: EP12_C03_IG_9x16_v1.mp4
Timestamp: 00:09–00:12
Change: Replace caption “$10k” with “$1k”.
Priority: Critical

4) What counts as a revision (included)
Included revisions focus on accuracy + polish:
- Caption corrections (names, numbers, mis-hears)
- Minor timing tweaks (caption timing, cut timing)
- Swap 1–2 b-roll cues
- Replace end-card CTA text/handle

Not included (would be a new clip request)
- Choosing a completely different segment/topic
- Rewriting the clip concept from scratch
- Major style change after we locked the preset

5) Approval deadline (keeps momentum)
To keep the schedule tight, please send revision notes within 72 hours of delivery. If we don’t hear back, we’ll mark the batch as approved and close the order.

6) Best practice: “Batch feedback”
Instead of one-by-one notes, send one consolidated message for all 10 clips. It reduces back-and-forth and speeds the final turnaround.
