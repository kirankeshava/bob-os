Title: How to Give Feedback Fast (So We Still Hit 48 Hours)

Thanks for trusting Clip Factory. Our promise is speed + consistency: 10 ready-to-post vertical clips delivered within 48 hours. The #1 reason short-form projects slip is slow or scattered feedback. This page shows exactly how to review clips so we can hit the SLA.

Where you’ll review
- Primary: Frame.io review link (best for timestamped comments).
- Backup: Google Drive folder (use the included “Feedback Sheet” format).

The only feedback format we can act on quickly
1) Use timestamped notes.
   - Good: “00:03–00:06: Change caption to ‘3 mistakes buyers make…’”
   - Not workable: “Make it punchier” (no timestamp, unclear action)

2) Tag each note with a priority (required):
   - MUST-FIX (Required for accuracy/compliance/brand safety)
   - NICE-TO-HAVE (Only if it’s quick and doesn’t alter structure)
   - NEXT BATCH (Save it; we apply it to your next episode to keep this batch on-time)

3) Consolidate feedback into ONE pass.
   - Please avoid sending feedback across multiple emails/DMs.
   - One consolidated round = fastest turnaround.

Your review deadline (to protect the 48h delivery)
- Delivery time (T0): When we send the first draft clips.
- Client feedback due: Within 12 hours of T0.
- If feedback arrives after 12 hours: we still complete revisions ASAP, but the 48-hour SLA may shift.

What we can revise (included)
Included in the standard revision round (fast edits):
- Caption wording fixes (as long as meaning stays the same)
- Minor timing adjustments (±1–2 seconds)
- Hook text tweaks (short replacement lines)
- On-screen text spelling/grammar
- Removing a sentence for compliance/brand safety
- Adjusting CTA end card text (within the same template)

What counts as a new request (not included in the standard revision)
To keep speed and pricing fair, these are treated as out-of-scope and moved to “NEXT BATCH” (or quoted separately if urgent):
- Changing the clip selection after drafts are delivered (new highlights)
- Full restructuring (new narrative / new hook concept)
- Re-cutting to a different angle/argument
- Heavy b-roll sourcing beyond what’s already in the clip
- Adding new brand packages mid-batch (fonts/colors/logo animations) without prior approval

How to approve quickly
At the end of your review, leave ONE final comment:
- “APPROVED” (ready to post)
or
- “APPROVED with MUST-FIX items listed above”
