========================
A) DFY FULFILLMENT SOP (Operator-Ready)
========================

Goal: Deliver a “sample clip” in 24 hours (to win trust), then deliver the purchased batch within 72 hours (or the agreed SLA). Maintain consistent formatting for TikTok/Reels/Shorts and minimize revision loops.

Offer context (typical):
- Sample Clip (free or included): 1 vertical 9:16 clip, captions, hook/title options, and posting notes.
- Paid Batch: 8–30 clips/month (or per episode) depending on package.

A1) Inputs required from client (minimum viable intake)
1) Source content link(s): YouTube, Drive, Dropbox, Riverside, Descript, etc.
2) Clip goals: leads, awareness, podcast growth, course sales, newsletter signups.
3) Target platforms: TikTok / IG Reels / YT Shorts (which ones matter most).
4) Brand kit (if any): colors, fonts, logo, preferred caption style (word-by-word vs line-by-line).
5) Examples they like (2–3 links) + examples they hate (optional).
6) CTA + offer: e.g., “Comment ‘SCRIPT’”, “Link in bio”, “DM me ‘COACH’”.
7) Any compliance constraints: medical/finance disclaimers, banned words, etc.

A2) Production workflow (for each episode/video)
Step 1 — Ingest & log (10–15 min)
- Save source link(s) in the project tracker.
- Create a folder: ClientName/YYYY-MM-DD_EpisodeName/
- Download source (if needed) or confirm access.

Step 2 — Find “clip-worthy” moments (30–60 min depending on length)
Pick moments that meet at least 2 of the following:
- Clear, punchy claim (contrarian take, myth busting)
- Story moment (conflict → insight)
- Tactical steps (1–3 steps works best)
- Emotion (surprise, humor, frustration)
- Credibility (numbers, results, proof)

Target clip length guidelines:
- TikTok/Reels: 15–45s preferred; 45–75s for strong stories.
- Shorts: 20–60s; up to 90s if retention holds.

Step 3 — Edit structure (per clip)
- Cold open: remove greetings and fluff.
- First 1–2 seconds must contain the hook or pattern interrupt.
- Tight pacing: remove pauses, tangents, filler words when it improves clarity.
- Add subtle punch-ins (zoom 5–10%) for emphasis (avoid overdoing).

Step 4 — Captions standard
- Burned-in captions, high contrast.
- 2 lines max, large font, safe margins for platform UI.
- Highlight keywords (1–3 per sentence) using brand color or bold.
- Correct names/terms; do not auto-caption jargon without review.

Step 5 — 9:16 framing standard
- Center face; keep eyes roughly upper third.
- If screen share is critical, use split layout (speaker + content) or crop to key region.
- Maintain consistent template per client (font, caption style, logo placement).

Step 6 — B-roll cues (lightweight)
- If adding B-roll: only when it clarifies or boosts retention.
- Otherwise, add on-screen text callouts (1–3 words) to reinforce.
- If we’re not inserting B-roll yet, include “B-ROLL CUE” notes in delivery doc for client/editor.

Step 7 — Export settings
- Format: MP4 (H.264), 1080x1920, 30fps (match source if needed), AAC audio.
- Bitrate: 8–12 Mbps (balance quality + upload speed).
- File naming: Client_Episode_Clip01_HookKeyword_1080x1920.mp4

A3) Quality Assurance (QA) checklist (must pass before delivery)
- Hook lands in first 2 seconds.
- Captions: no obvious errors; names/brands correct.
- No captions covering face or key visuals; safe margins respected.
- Audio: loudness consistent; no clipping.
- No dead air at start/end.
- Clip length matches platform goal.
- CTA present if requested.

A4) Delivery process
- Deliver via Google Drive/Dropbox folder link.
- Include a “Posting Notes” doc (copy/paste bullets):
  - Suggested caption (1–2 options)
  - 3–8 hashtags (niche-specific)
  - CTA line
  - Best platform fit
- Send delivery email using Template C below.

A5) Revision policy (to prevent scope creep)
- Sample clip: 1 round of revisions included (captions + trimming + hook variants).
- Batch delivery: 1 round of revisions per clip, limited to caption fixes and small trims (no full re-edit).
