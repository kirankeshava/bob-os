CALL STRUCTURE (15–20 MIN)

0:00–1:00 — Set frame
“Thanks for taking this. I’m Bob. Quick agenda: I’ll ask a few questions about your content and goals, then I’ll share a simple clip system we use to turn long-form into consistent Reels/TikToks. If it’s a fit, we’ll pick a starter package and you’ll have your first clips in 48 hours. Sound good?”

1:00–6:00 — Diagnose (qualify for DFY)
1) “Where do clips fit in your growth right now—lead gen, brand, selling offers, or community?”
2) “What long-form do you have already—podcast, YouTube, Zoom trainings? How often do you publish?”
3) “What’s your current workflow for clips (if any)? What’s breaking—time, consistency, quality, or ideas?”
4) “What would ‘successful’ look like in 30 days? (followers, leads, booked calls, course sales)”
5) “Do you have brand constraints—fonts/colors, captions style, b-roll preferences, words to avoid?”

6:00–10:00 — Position the DFY offer (outcome + process)
“Here’s the system: you give us a link (YouTube/podcast/video file). We pull 10–30 high-signal moments, rewrite the first 1–2 seconds into a strong hook, add clean on-brand captions, plus b-roll cues so it doesn’t feel like generic ‘AI captions.’ We format platform-native 9:16 and deliver ready-to-post files with titles + suggested posting notes. You don’t manage editors or timelines—we just ship clips.”

10:00–12:00 — Offer options (pick one)
Option A (Starter): “8 clips/week, delivered 2x per week, 48-hour turnaround.”
Option B (Growth): “15 clips/week, includes 2 hook variants per clip so you can A/B test.”
Option C (Authority): “25 clips/week, includes a monthly ‘content angle map’ so you always have themes.”
(If they ask price, you can say: “I’ll recommend the right tier based on volume and goals; we can start small and scale if it works.”)

12:00–15:00 — Close with a low-friction next step
“If you want, we can start with a 7-day free trial batch: you send one long-form episode and we’ll deliver a starter set. If you like it, you roll into weekly delivery. What episode should we start with?”

LIGHT SaaS VALIDATION (30 seconds, only after they say yes or are warm)
“Separate question: if this were self-serve software where you drop a link and it outputs highlight clips with captions and templates, what would you need to pay $99–$299/month for it? Examples: speaker detection, brand templates, hook suggestions, niche templates, approvals, team accounts.”
(Log their exact words.)

OBJECTION HANDLING (copy/paste responses)

1) “I already have an editor.”
“Totally—most of our best clients do. We’re not replacing them; we’re replacing the bottleneck of finding moments + writing hooks + producing volume consistently. If your editor is great at long-form, we can feed them a clean clip list + hooks + caption style, or we can just take vertical off their plate so they focus on higher-value edits. Want to test us on one episode and compare output?”

2) “I don’t have time to manage this.”
“That’s the point—we run it off a link. The only input we need is (a) your best episode link and (b) 3 examples of clips you like. After that it’s weekly delivery. If you have 2 minutes now, tell me your CTA and your audience so we align clips to your offer.”

3) “This is too expensive.”
“Fair—let’s anchor to ROI. If 10 clips/week brings you even 1 qualified call or 1 sale, it pays for itself. Also we can start with the smallest weekly volume, prove it works, then scale. Would you rather start with 8/week and test for 7 days free?”

4) “My content isn’t viral / I’m not a TikTok person.”
“We don’t chase random virality. We cut for clarity + authority: strong hooks, crisp captions, and moments that build trust. The goal is consistent distribution so your best ideas actually get seen. Reels/TikTok are just channels—this is really about repurposing attention into leads.”

5) “AI captions look bad / I don’t want cheesy stuff.”
“Agreed. We use clean, brandable caption styling—no bouncing karaoke unless you want it. We also add b-roll cues and tighten phrasing so it looks intentional, not ‘auto-generated.’ I can match 2–3 reference creators you like.”
