**“Too expensive.”**
Makes sense. If we can reliably produce 8–15 clips/week, the cost per post is usually lower than ad hoc editing. Want to start with a small pilot (8 clips) and decide after?

**“No time / too busy.”**
That’s exactly why this works: you send a link, we do the rest. 10 minutes to set style once, then it’s plug-and-play.

**“Quality concerns.”**
Fair. I’ll send a sample clip plan first (timestamps + hook + caption style). If you like it, we produce the first batch—if not, you don’t continue.

**“We talk about sensitive client info.”**
We can clip only from public episodes you approve. You can also mark ‘do-not-clip’ segments and we’ll avoid them.

# Discovery call → close talk track (15 minutes)
1) **Context (2 min):** What’s the show/coaching offer? Who’s the audience?
2) **Current workflow (3 min):** Are you clipping now? Who does it? How many/week? What’s breaking?
3) **Definition of done (3 min):** Caption style, pacing, hook tone, b-roll vs clean podcast look, examples they like.
4) **Pilot proposal (4 min):**
   - “For your next episode, we’ll deliver {{8/15}} clips in 72 hours.”
   - “Includes 9:16 exports + burned-in captions + hook text.”
   - “$___/week. Start with $300 deposit today applied to week one.”
5) **Close (3 min):**
   - “If I send a short intake checklist, can you reply with the link + brand examples today?”
   - “Best email for delivery is this one?”

**Data capture (for SaaS later, ask quickly):** Would you pay $99–$299/mo for a self-serve tool that: link → highlights → captions export? Which add-ons matter (diarization, brand voice hooks, templates)?
