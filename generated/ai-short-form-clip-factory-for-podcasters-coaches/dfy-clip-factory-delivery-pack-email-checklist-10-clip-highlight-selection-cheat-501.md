## 3) 10-Clip Highlight Selection Cheatsheet (Speed + Consistency)

Goal: pick **10 clips** fast that will perform on TikTok/Reels/Shorts and require minimal editing. Use this when scanning the transcript or source.

### A) Non-Negotiable Selection Rules
1) **Hook in first 1.5 seconds**
   - If the clip needs “context” longer than 1.5s, it’s not a short.
2) **One idea per clip**
   - Don’t combine 2 teaching points. Split them.
3) **Pattern interrupt every 2–3 seconds**
   - Use hard cuts, punch-ins, b-roll cue, on-screen keywords, or beat-synced text changes.
4) **End with a clean landing** (last 0.5–1.0s)
   - Avoid trailing off. Add a micro-wrap or CTA.
5) **Length targets**
   - Default: **18–35 seconds**
   - If story/arc is strong: **35–55 seconds**
   - Avoid >60s unless client explicitly requests

### B) What to Look For (10 clip types)
Use a balanced mix so the batch feels “complete.”

1) **Contrarian take**
   - Example hooks:
     - “Unpopular opinion: [common advice] is wrong.”
     - “Stop doing [thing] if you want [result].”

2) **Simple framework (numbered)**
   - “3 steps to…”, “2 rules for…”, “The 4-part checklist…”

3) **Big mistake / myth**
   - “The biggest mistake I see is…”
   - “Most people think X, but it’s actually Y.”

4) **Before/after transformation**
   - “Here’s what changed when I did…”
   - “I went from [bad] to [good] by…”

5) **Story with punchline lesson**
   - “I learned this the hard way…”
   - Needs a clear ending sentence.

6) **Tactical walkthrough**
   - “Do this first, then this…”
   - Great for coaches/consultants.

7) **Hot seat / Q&A**
   - Take a question and answer in one clean clip.

8) **Mini-rant (emotion)**
   - “It drives me crazy when…”
   - Ensure brand-safe; avoid controversial claims.

9) **Specific example/case study**
   - “Here’s what we did for a client…” (remove sensitive details)

10) **Strong quote (tight, repeatable)**
   - Short, memorable, can be caption-forward.

### C) Hook Formulas (Copy Blocks)
Swap into the first line of the clip (and on-screen text).
- “If you’re [target], do this.”
- “Here’s the fastest way to [result].”
- “Nobody tells you this about [topic].”
- “You’re not getting [result] because of this.”
- “The real reason [common outcome] happens…”

### D) Pattern Interrupt Ideas (fast to execute)
- **0:00–0:02**: Big hook text (3–6 words)
- **Every 2–3 seconds**: alternate between:
  - Punch-in 105% / 112% / 100%
  - Hard cut removing micro-pauses
  - B-roll overlay for 0.6–1.2s
  - On-screen keyword swap (1–3 words)
  - Emoji-free icon callout (arrow, underline, highlight)

### E) CTA Placement (keep it light)
- Default: **last 2 seconds** as end card
- If educational: add mid-clip “Follow for part 2” only if truly segmented
- CTA examples:
  - “Follow for more [topic].”
  - “Comment ‘[keyword]’ and I’ll send the template.”
  - “DM ‘[keyword]’ for the checklist.”

### F) Fast Reject List (do NOT clip)
- Needs heavy context to understand
- Lots of hedging (“maybe,” “sort of,” “it depends”) without a clear takeaway
- Overly long anecdotes with no punchline
- Sensitive claims (medical/financial/legal) without disclaimers/permission
- Audio too poor to salvage quickly

### G) Timecoding Workflow (10 clips in ~25 minutes)
1) Scan transcript for bold statements + numbers + “the mistake is…”
2) Mark 20 candidates with timestamps
3) Choose best 10 using the mix above
4) Write 10 hook lines (one per clip) before editing begins
