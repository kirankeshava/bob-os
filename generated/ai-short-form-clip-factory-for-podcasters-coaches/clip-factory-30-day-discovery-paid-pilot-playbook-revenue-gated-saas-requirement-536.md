Call structure (20 minutes) — Discovery + Close Script:

“Thanks for taking 20. We help podcasters/coaches turn one long video into a week of ready-to-post vertical clips—hooks, captions, formatting—without hiring a full-time editor. If it’s useful, we can also run a low-risk pilot so you can judge output quality.”

9) “If we could reliably deliver X clips/week from your long-form with consistent quality, what would that be worth per month?”

- “If it’s $299/mo, what would you require?”
- “At what price would it be a no-brainer? At what price would it feel too expensive?”

16:00–19:00 Close to DFY paid pilot (don’t mention ‘SaaS build’)
Offer (pick one; keep simple):
A) Starter Pilot: “We take 1 episode and deliver 8 clips in 72 hours. If you like them, we roll into weekly.”
B) Weekly Cadence: “12 clips/week delivered in 2 batches (Mon/Thu), ready to post.”
Close language:
“Want to test this with one episode this week? If you send a link today, we can deliver by [date]. We’ll match the caption style you like and include 2–3 hook options per clip.”

If they hesitate:
- Risk reversal: “If the clips aren’t usable, we’ll revise once free. If still not a fit, we won’t ask you to continue.”
- Value framing: “One episode becomes a week of content. The goal is consistency without you managing an editor.”

19:00–20:00 Next steps + asset request
“Great—send your best recent episode link and 2 examples of shorts you love. Also confirm where you post (TikTok/Reels/Shorts) and any brand fonts/colors.”

“That’s exactly the point of the pilot—remove the production bottleneck. If you had 8–12 clips ready, could your team post them?”
- “Budget.”
“Understood. What range would make this an easy yes? We can start with a smaller weekly clip count and scale once it performs.”

Feature Scoring Rubric (to prioritize $99–$299/mo features):

5) Niche templates (coach/podcast/real estate)

Compute:
