## 1) What you’ll receive (Delivery Pack)
For each clip, you receive a complete “ready-to-post” package:
1. **Final MP4** (vertical, platform-ready)
2. **Captions file (SRT)** matching the final MP4
3. **B-roll / visual cue sheet** (timestamps + what to overlay)
4. **Posting suggestions**
   - Suggested **first line** (caption hook)
   - Suggested **hashtags** (platform-appropriate)
   - Suggested **CTA** (comment/DM/follow/link)

**Standard delivery format:** Google Drive folder with clearly named subfolders (see naming below).

## 2) Our quality standards (what we QA before you ever see it)
We run a final QA pass on every clip using the checklist below.

### A) Framing / “safe zones” / no cropped faces
- Subject’s face is **not cropped** (forehead/chin not unintentionally cut off).
- Important visuals stay within **safe zones** (not blocked by TikTok/Reels UI).
- If there are two speakers, framing remains readable; no one is pushed off-screen.

### B) Captions accuracy + readability
- Captions match spoken words (no meaning-changing errors).
- Punctuation and line breaks improve readability.
- Profanity/flagged terms handled per your preferences (from intake form).
- Captions are sized for mobile; not too low/high; not clipped.

### C) Audio leveling
- Audio is clear, consistent, and loud enough for phone speakers.
- No obvious peaking/distortion; background noise reduced where possible.
- Music (if used) never competes with dialogue.

### D) Editing rhythm: jump cuts + pacing
- Dead air removed.
- Cuts feel intentional (no distracting micro-jitters).
- Clip starts strong; no slow lead-in.

### E) Hook clarity
- Opening 1–2 seconds contains a clear hook (text + spoken moment aligned).
- Viewer understands “why keep watching?” immediately.

### F) Export / platform formatting
- Format: **MP4 (H.264)**
- Resolution: **1080×1920** (vertical 9:16)
- Frame rate: matches source where feasible (typically 30fps)
- Bitrate: high enough to avoid caption artifacting/compression blur

## 3) Review process (fast, simple, timestamped)
We provide a **review link** (Frame.io / Vimeo / unlisted YouTube) so you can leave **timestamped comments**.

### How to review
1. Watch each clip once fully.
2. If something needs changing, leave a comment **at the exact timestamp**.
3. Use this format to avoid back-and-forth:
   - **Timestamp:** 00:13
   - **Issue:** Caption typo (“their” → “there”)
   - **Change request:** Replace word + keep timing

### Revision window
- You have **24 hours** from delivery to request revisions included in the order.
- After the 24-hour window, we can still help, but requests may be treated as a new change request depending on scope.

---

## 4) What counts as a free revision vs. a paid change
We want you happy, but we also need clear boundaries to avoid scope creep.

### Free revisions (included)
We fix anything that is objectively incorrect or missed vs. the intake requirements:
- Caption typos / mis-transcriptions
- Wrong aspect ratio, cropping issues, clipped captions
- Audio leveling problems (too quiet, peaking) within reason
- Missed on-screen handle/CTA that you provided upfront
- Formatting/export errors
- Small timing adjustments to captions for readability

### Paid changes (out of scope)
These are new creative directions or new requirements after delivery:
- Changing the hook angle or “story” of the clip
- Recutting based on a different topic selection
- Swapping in new branding elements not provided before editing
- Major style changes (new caption style/theme/fonts) after approval
- Adding/removing large sections, building multiple alternate versions

If something is ambiguous, we’ll classify it quickly and transparently and propose the lightest fix.
