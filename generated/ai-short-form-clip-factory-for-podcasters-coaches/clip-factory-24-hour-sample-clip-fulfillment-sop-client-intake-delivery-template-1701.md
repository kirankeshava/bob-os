## Purpose
Fulfill a **free/low-friction “Sample Clip”** (or first paid micro-order) in **<24 hours** with consistent quality, minimal back-and-forth, and a built-in conversion path to paid DFY packages. This supports the near-term revenue goal and keeps SaaS work gated until $5k DFY is collected.

## Scope (What we deliver in the 24-hour sample)
**Deliverables (default):**
1) **1 vertical clip (9:16)**, 20–45 seconds, exported as MP4.
2) **Burned-in captions** (readable, high-contrast), with keyword emphasis (basic styling).
3) **Hook options (3 text hooks)** the client can use as on-screen opener + caption text.
4) **Posting notes**: suggested title/caption + CTA + hashtags (lightweight).

**Not included in sample (unless promised):** multi-clip batch, heavy motion graphics, custom brand pack, deep b-roll sourcing, complex sound design.

## Tools (no spend)
- Editing: CapCut Desktop / DaVinci Resolve (free) or existing editor workflow.
- Captions: CapCut auto-captions or Whisper-based transcription (if available free locally).
- Delivery: Google Drive link / Dropbox Basic / direct attachment if small.
- Tracking: Google Sheet CRM (client name, assets received, due time, delivered link, upsell status).

## SLA
- Clock starts **once assets are received**.
- First delivery within **24 hours**.
- **1 revision** included (text/caption timing + small trims). Additional revisions convert to paid.

## Step 1 — Intake request (send immediately)
Goal: get the minimum to cut a great clip without delays.

### Intake checklist (minimum)
1) Source content: YouTube link / Drive link / raw file
2) Target platform(s): TikTok / Reels / Shorts
3) Topic + audience: who it’s for
4) Tone: direct / hype / calm / educational
5) CTA: follow, book call, download lead magnet, comment keyword
6) Brand notes (optional): colors, font preference, logo
7) Examples they like (optional): 1–2 links

### File naming convention
- ClientName_Date_EpisodeTopic_Source
Example: “AcmeCo_2026-04-09_ColdEmailFixes_YTlink”

## Step 2 — Source review (10–15 minutes)
- Skim content at 1.5–2x.
- Mark 3–6 “candidate moments”:
  - Strong contrarian take
  - Specific numbers/steps
  - Emotional spike / story beat
  - Clear before→after claim
- Choose the best candidate that fits 20–45s.

## Step 3 — Clip assembly (30–60 minutes)
- Format: 9:16.
- Keep speaker large; crop to eyes/face.
- Remove dead air >200ms where possible.
- Add light punch-in cuts every 3–6 seconds.
- Add on-screen hook within first 1 second.

## Step 4 — Captions (20–40 minutes)
Caption rules:
- 2 lines max, large font.
- High contrast box/shadow.
- Emphasize 1–3 keywords per sentence.
- Fix obvious proper nouns + numbers.

## Step 5 — Simple “b-roll cue” (optional, fast)
If there’s an obvious visual aid:
- Add 1–2 quick overlays (icons, screenshots, big text) OR
- Provide cue notes in posting notes: “Overlay: ‘Step 1/2/3’”

## Step 6 — Export settings
- 1080x1920, H.264, 15–30 Mbps.
- Audio normalized to comfortable level.
- Filename: Client_Clip1_Topic_9x16.mp4

## Step 7 — QA checklist (5 minutes)
- Hook appears immediately
- Captions readable on mobile
- No cutoff words at start/end
- No long silent gaps
- CTA present (spoken or text)

## Step 8 — Delivery + upsell
Send deliverable link + next-step offer (paid package).
