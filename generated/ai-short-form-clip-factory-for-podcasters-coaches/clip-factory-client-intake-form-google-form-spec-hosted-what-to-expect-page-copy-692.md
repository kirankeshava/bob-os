1) Before we start (Intake)
To avoid avoidable revisions, we require the intake form before editing begins. It covers:
- platforms + clip length targets
- caption style and hook preferences
- brand terms + banned words
- framing notes (so faces/logos don’t get covered)

If you need us to start immediately without intake, reply in writing that you’re waiving intake preferences; we’ll use our default style and any later preference changes may become a paid change request.

2) What you’ll receive (Delivery Pack)
Your delivery includes a folder with:
- FINAL CLIPS (MP4): platform-ready vertical exports
- CAPTIONS (SRT): one file per clip
- B-ROLL CUE SHEET (PDF or Google Doc): timestamps + suggested overlays/visuals
- POSTING SUGGESTIONS (Doc):
  - “first line” caption for each clip
  - CTA suggestion
  - recommended hashtags (platform-specific)

3) Review flow (timestamp comments)
We’ll send a review link (Frame.io / Vimeo review / unlisted YouTube) where you can leave timestamped notes.
How to review fast:
- Watch each clip once without pausing.
- Rewatch and leave notes with timestamps for any needed changes.
- Group feedback by clip number (Clip_01, Clip_02, etc.).

4) Revision window (24 hours)
You have 24 hours from the moment the review link is delivered to submit revision notes.
- Notes received within the window are handled in the agreed scope.
- If we don’t receive notes within 24 hours, we consider the set approved and proceed to final delivery (or close the order if finals were already delivered).

5) Our QA standards (what we check before you ever see it)
Every clip is checked for:
- safe-zone framing (no cropped faces; captions don’t cover mouth/chin when possible)
- caption accuracy (names/terms spelled correctly; minimal drift)
- audio consistency (no clipping/peaking; voice clarity prioritized)
- hook clarity in first 1–2 seconds
- jump cuts that feel natural (no awkward mid-word cuts)
- platform export settings (vertical format, readable text size, clean bitrate)

6) Free revisions vs paid changes (scope protection)
Free revisions (included):
- typos, caption timing fixes, misheard words
- minor timing trims to improve pacing
- safe-zone/cropping adjustments
- small text style tweaks consistent with your intake (font size/color within reason)
- audio leveling tweaks (within the same source)

Paid changes (out of scope):
- new creative direction after delivery (different hook style, different clip selection strategy)
- swapping to different source footage or adding new footage
- heavy rewrites of captions to a new voice/tone not specified in intake
- additional clips beyond the original quantity
- reformatting for a new platform set not specified in intake

If you request a paid change, we’ll reply with a clear quote and turnaround before proceeding.

Internal rule (non-negotiable):
- No editing begins until (a) intake form is submitted, or (b) client explicitly waives intake in writing via email.
- If client waives intake: default caption style = clean & minimal; default hooks = strongest claim first; default profanity policy = keep as-is (unless obviously risky).

“To protect turnaround time and reduce revisions, we start production after the intake form is submitted (or you explicitly waive it in writing).”

This system reduces refund risk by locking requirements upfront, giving clients a clear timestamped review method, and enforcing a tight revision window tied to documented scope boundaries.
