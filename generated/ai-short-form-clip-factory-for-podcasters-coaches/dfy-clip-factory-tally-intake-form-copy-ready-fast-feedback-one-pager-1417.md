2) CLIENT ONE-PAGER: “How to Give Feedback Fast (So We Hit 48 Hours)”
=============================
Title: How to Give Feedback Fast (So We Hit the 48-Hour Turnaround)

Where you’ll review
You’ll receive a Drive or Frame.io link with 10 clips labeled clearly. We recommend reviewing on desktop for fastest timestamp notes.

The goal of feedback
Fast feedback keeps momentum. Our process is built to ship post-ready clips quickly; long back-and-forth is what breaks the 48-hour timeline.

How to leave feedback (the fast way)
1) Use timestamps. Example: “Clip 03 — 00:02–00:05: change ‘agents’ to ‘realtors’.”
2) Be specific about the change type:
   - Trim: “Start 0.5s earlier / cut the last sentence.”
   - Captions: “Fix typo / remove filler / change phrasing.”
   - Hook: “Make opening line punchier; keep same meaning.”
   - CTA: “Replace end card text with: ‘DM READY for the checklist’.”
3) Batch notes per clip. One message per clip reduces missed items.

What counts as a revision (included)
- Caption corrections, punctuation, minor wording changes
- Start/end trims, removing a sentence, tightening pauses
- Hook refinement that keeps the same topic/segment
- CTA text changes on end card

What is a new request (not a revision)
- “Make 5 more clips” or “Find totally different moments”
- Changing the style preset after delivery (fonts/colors/layout)
- Rebuilding around a new topic not in the original clip
- Replacing source footage after we’ve started

Timing: when to send feedback
To keep the 48-hour timeline, send all feedback within 12 hours of receiving the review link. If feedback arrives later, we’ll still revise—delivery timing may shift.

If you’re busy, use this fallback
Reply with either:
A) “Approved—ship as-is.”
B) “Only fix typos + tighten dead air.”
C) “Clip 2 and Clip 7 need changes; the rest approved.”
