What “good” looks like (quick checklist)
Review each clip for:
1) Hook (first 1.5 seconds): Does it start immediately with a compelling claim/question/contrast?
2) Captions: Are they readable on mobile, correctly timed, and not covering key visuals?
3) Pacing: Is there a pattern interrupt every ~2–3 seconds (cut/zoom/b-roll cue/graphic beat)?
4) Brand: Correct colors/fonts (if you provided them) + consistent caption style.
5) Compliance: No disallowed claims, no private info, no copyrighted music (unless you supplied/licensed it).
6) CTA: Clear “follow/subscribe/book/click” moment (usually end card or last 1–2 seconds).

How to request revisions (fastest method)
Reply to the delivery email (agent_bob_replit+clip-factory@agentmail.to) using this format:

SUBJECT: Revisions — [Client] — [Episode/Date]

For each clip needing changes:
- Clip filename: (example) CLIENT_Ep12_C03_ColdOpen_v1.mp4
- Timestamp(s): 00:02–00:05, 00:14
- Change request (be specific):
  - “Replace caption phrase ‘double your leads’ → ‘increase leads’”
  - “Remove b-roll cue at 00:14; keep original camera angle”
  - “Move captions up ~10% to avoid covering product demo”
- Priority: Must-fix / Nice-to-have

What counts as a revision (so we stay within 48-hour speed)
Included (standard):
- Text/caption wording corrections (minor)
- Small timing tweaks to captions
- Simple b-roll cue swap/removal
- Crop/position adjustment for safe areas
- End card/CTA text adjustment

Not included (requires a new request or re-scope):
- Recutting the entire clip with a different narrative
- Choosing a completely different highlight/topic for most clips
- Full re-style of all clips after style approval
- Adding new assets not provided at intake (logos, new b-roll libraries, new music licensing)

Revision policy (48-hour DFY)
- Delivery includes 1 revision round for up to 3 clips (or up to 10 total change notes) if requested within 48 hours of delivery.
- If you send consolidated feedback in one message, revisions are faster. Multiple piecemeal emails may delay turnaround.

Tip: Approve the style once, then scale
- If you like Clip #1–#2 style (captions, pacing, end card), confirm: “Style approved—apply to rest.” This reduces rework.
