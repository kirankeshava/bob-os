## 2) Quality standards (QA checklist we run on every clip)
We QA every clip before delivery to protect your brand and minimize back-and-forth.

### A) Hook clarity (first 1–2 seconds)
- Clip starts on the strongest, most curiosity-driving phrase.
- No dead air or “warm-up” words before the hook.
- Visuals support the hook (speaker face is visible; no distracting frames).

### B) Captions accuracy + readability
- **Verbatim accuracy:** no meaning-changing errors, names/brands corrected.
- **Timing:** captions match speech (not early/late by more than ~150ms).
- **Legibility:** high contrast, safe placement, no covering mouth/important visuals.
- **Line breaks:** short lines, no awkward mid-phrase breaks.
- **Profanity/brand terms:** handled per intake preferences (censoring/banned words).

### C) Framing / composition / safe zones
- 9:16 crop keeps **eyes and mouth visible**.
- No cropped faces (forehead/chin not cut off during key moments).
- Captions, usernames, and CTAs placed inside safe zones (avoid UI overlays).
- If we use auto-reframe, we verify the subject stays centered during movement.

### D) Audio quality + leveling
- No clipping/distortion.
- Speech consistently audible; background noise reduced.
- Loudness consistent across clips in the batch.
- If music is used: it never competes with speech; it ducks under voice.

### E) Cuts, pacing, and flow
- Jump cuts are intentional and clean (no jarring mid-word cuts).
- Awkward pauses removed.
- Clip length matches platform goal (typically 15–45s unless specified).
- On-screen text and b-roll changes support pacing instead of distracting.

### F) Platform compliance + formatting
- Export is optimized for TikTok/Reels/Shorts.
- No copyrighted music usage unless client provides rights/asset.
- No disallowed claims/medical/financial promises added by us.

### G) Export settings (baseline)
- Resolution: 1080×1920 (9:16)
- Codec: H.264 (MP4)
- Frame rate: match source where possible (commonly 30fps)
- Audio: AAC, 48kHz (or 44.1kHz if source requires)
- Bitrate: high enough to avoid artifacts (target ~10–20 Mbps)

## 3) Review flow (fast, timestamped)
We send a **review link** (Frame.io / Vimeo / unlisted YouTube) plus the Delivery Pack folder.

### How to request revisions
- Leave **timestamped comments** (e.g., “00:07 change caption wording” or “00:12 remove filler”).
- If you prefer email, list timestamps in your reply.

### Revision window
- You have **24 hours** from delivery to request included revisions.
- If you respond after 24 hours, we’ll still help, but it may be scheduled as a new request.

### Turnaround expectation
- We acknowledge revision requests quickly and deliver revision updates within the agreed window (typically 24–48h depending on batch size).

## 4) What counts as a free revision vs. a paid change (triage rules)
We use these rules to keep scope clear and protect deadlines.

### Included (free) revisions — our responsibility
These are fixes to meet the original brief/intake:
- Caption typos, timing drift, missing words (when audio is clear).
- Incorrect crop that cuts faces or violates safe zones.
- Audio leveling issues (too quiet, clipping) relative to our standard.
- Wrong export format or file issues (corrupt MP4, wrong resolution).
- Missed requested CTA/branding elements that were provided at intake.

### Usually included (free) — small preference tweaks (within reason)
- Minor caption style adjustments (punctuation, line breaks) if it doesn’t require re-editing the entire clip.
- Small timing tweaks to cut a pause or tighten pacing.

### Paid change (out of scope) — requires re-brief or new request
- Changing topic/angle: “Use a different moment from the episode instead.”
- Major rewrite: new hook concept, new structure, or heavy re-cut beyond minor tightening.
- Adding new graphics packages or brand redesign not supplied at intake.
- Multiple rounds beyond the included revision window or repeated subjective changes.
- Client requests that contradict initial constraints (e.g., banned words list added after editing is complete).

### If something is ambiguous
We default to: **fix objective errors for free**, and **quote/timebox subjective/strategy changes**.
