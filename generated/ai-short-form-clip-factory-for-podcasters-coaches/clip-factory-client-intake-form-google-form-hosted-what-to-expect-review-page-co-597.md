1) Before we start (Intake Required)
To protect quality and avoid rework, we begin editing after you submit the Clip Factory Intake Form (brand terms, caption style, targets, and guardrails). If you want us to proceed without intake, you must explicitly waive this in writing via email.

2) QA standards (what we check before you ever see a clip)
- Captions: spelling of brand terms and proper nouns, timing sync, readability, and safe line breaks.
- Framing: no cropped faces, safe margins for platform UI, and face-safe reframe on speaker.
- Audio: consistent loudness, no clipping/peaking, and reduced harshness where possible.
- Hook clarity: the first 1–2 seconds should clearly signal the topic and why to watch.
- Jump cuts: clean cuts without distracting artifacts; remove dead air.
- Export: vertical 9:16, platform-friendly bitrate, clear text readability.

3) Review flow (timestamp comments)
You’ll receive a review link (unlisted) where you can leave timestamped comments.
How to comment:
- Use timestamps (e.g., 00:12) and say exactly what you want changed.
- If it’s a caption error, paste the correct spelling.
- If it’s a content preference (tone/hook), describe the alternative you want.

Revision window:
You have 24 hours after we send the review link to request revisions included in scope.

4) What you receive in the Delivery Pack
You’ll get a single folder with:
- Final MP4 clips (ready to post)
- SRT caption files for each clip
- B-roll cue sheet (timecoded suggestions)
- Posting suggestions (first-line caption options + hashtag ideas + best platform notes)

5) Revisions: what’s free vs paid
Included (free) revisions during the 24-hour window:
- Caption spelling/accuracy fixes
- Minor timing tweaks (caption timing, quick trim adjustments)
- Framing fixes (face crop, safe margins)
- Audio leveling fixes (within reason)

Typically paid / out of scope:
- New direction after approval (e.g., “change the hook style for all clips”)
- New clip selection from different parts of the episode
- Re-editing due to new brand guidelines not provided in intake
- Adding net-new subtitles language or complex motion design not agreed upfront

6) Best results checklist (what to send us)
- Highest quality source video (or YouTube link)
- Speaker names + spelling
- Your CTA and target platform(s)
- 1–2 examples of clips you like

C) INTERNAL KICKOFF GATE RULE (add to SOP)
Rule: No work starts until the client submits the intake form and provides accessible source media links/files.
Exception: Client may waive intake ONLY by replying in writing from their email to agent_bob_replit+clip-factory@agentmail.to stating they approve proceeding without intake and accept that revisions may be limited.

Reason: Most refund-triggering problems come from assumptions about spelling, hook style, guardrails, and platform targets. This gate prevents avoidable QA misses and protects delivery speed and reputation.
