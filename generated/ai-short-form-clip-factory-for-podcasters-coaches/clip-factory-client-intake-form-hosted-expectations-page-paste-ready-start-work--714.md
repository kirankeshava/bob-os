2) START-WORK GATE RULE (internal + client-facing)
Policy Name: “Intake-First Production Gate”
Rule:
- No work begins until the client submits the Intake Form.
- If the client insists on starting immediately, they must explicitly waive the intake in writing (email reply is sufficient). In that case, any revisions caused by missing brand terms, banned words, caption style preferences, or platform targets are treated as paid changes, not free revisions.
Why this exists:
- It prevents the two biggest refund triggers: (1) brand/compliance misses and (2) expectation mismatches on caption style, hooks, and pacing.

Step 1 — Required Intake (before we start)
To protect your brand and avoid time-wasting revisions, we require a quick project intake form (platform targets, hook style, caption preferences, brand terms, and banned words). We start production after intake is submitted (unless you explicitly waive it in writing).

Step 2 — Editing + Quality Assurance
Every clip goes through a QA pass to check:
- Caption accuracy and readability
- No cropped faces / correct framing
- Audio leveling (clear voice, no peaking)
- Hook clarity in the first seconds
- Clean jump cuts and pacing
- Platform-safe export settings (vertical 9:16)

Step 3 — Review Link (timestamp comments)
We’ll send a review link where you can leave timestamped comments.
How to leave feedback:
1) Watch the clip(s).
2) Leave timestamped notes for anything you want adjusted.
3) Group feedback into: captions, framing, hook text, pacing, b-roll, or branding.

Revision Window
You have 24 hours after we send the review link to request revisions for that delivery round. This keeps production moving and helps us hit deadlines.

Step 4 — Final Delivery Pack (what you receive)
You’ll receive a Delivery Pack that includes:
- Final MP4 clip(s) ready to post
- Caption file(s) in SRT format
- B-roll cue sheet (time-coded suggestions)
- Posting suggestions (first-line caption + hashtag set ideas)

Revision Boundaries (to protect timelines)
Free revisions cover:
- Editing mistakes (caption errors, missed safe zones, audio issues)
- Minor on-screen text tweaks
- Small trim/pacing adjustments within the same clip
Paid changes include:
- New creative direction after approval (new hook concept, different style)
- Re-cutting from a different segment of the source
- Adding new assets or substantial new b-roll requirements
- Requests outside the revision window

We aim for fast turnaround, clean edits, and predictable delivery. If anything is unclear, email us anytime: agent_bob_replit+clip-factory@agentmail.to
