A) 10-Call Customer Interview Tracker (CSV-ready table)
Copy into Google Sheets/Airtable. Each row = one call.

Columns:
1) Date  2) Prospect Name  3) Role (podcaster/coach/agency/brand)  4) Niche  5) Audience size  6) Content cadence (episodes/week)  7) Current clip workflow (who edits / tools)  8) Current monthly spend ($)  9) Biggest pain (verbatim)  10) “Job to be done” (verbatim)
11) Must-have outputs (checklist): [9:16 export] [captions] [hooks] [titles] [b-roll cues] [auto highlights] [brand templates] [scheduling]
12) Must-have integrations: [YouTube link] [upload] [Drive/Dropbox] [Riverside] [Zoom] [Descript]
13) Quality bar (1–5)  14) Turnaround requirement (hours/days)
15) Dealbreakers (verbatim)
16) WTP test: $99/mo (Y/N/Maybe)  17) $199/mo (Y/N/Maybe)  18) $299/mo (Y/N/Maybe)
19) Preferred pricing model: [per episode] [per clip] [monthly] [credits]
20) MVP MoSCoW (Must/Should/Could/Won’t) — free text summary
21) Feature votes (0–3 each): diarization, brand voice hooks, niche templates, automatic highlight detection, caption styles, team seats, review/approve, analytics
22) “Paying signal” (none / interested / asks for link / agrees to deposit)
23) Next step date/time  24) Notes + objections  25) Consent to use clips as portfolio (Y/N)

Scoring rule (quick):
- Score = (WTP at $199 or $299 = +3) + (pain severity 4–5 = +2) + (needs ≥4 outputs = +1) + (wants turnaround <48h = +1). Prospects with Score ≥6 are prime DFY pilots and strongest SaaS validators.

C) Pilot → SaaS Conversion Playbook (when and how to pitch $99–$299/mo)
Goal: Use DFY pilots to (1) generate revenue now and (2) collect the proof and requirements to sell subscriptions later.

When to pitch SaaS (timing triggers):
1) Output confidence: Client approved ≥10 clips with minimal revisions OR says “this saves me hours.”
2) Cadence lock: Client commits to ongoing weekly content cadence (at least 4 clips/week).
3) Operational pain: Client asks for faster turnaround, more clips, or wants to involve a VA/team.

How to frame the transition (talk track):
- “We can keep doing this DFY, but once you’re posting consistently, the bottleneck becomes coordination. The subscription version is for teams that want the same outputs but self-serve: drop a link/upload, pick highlights, and export 9:16 with captions in your brand template.”
- “If you’re at {4–20} clips/month, it usually pencils out at $99–$299/mo depending on how much automation you want.”

Subscription packaging (positioning based on what they valued in the pilot):
- $99/mo (Starter): YouTube link/upload → transcript → choose highlights → export 9:16 with captions (1 brand template). Limit: {X} exports.
- $199/mo (Pro): Auto highlight suggestions + multiple caption styles + template library + faster renders. Limit: higher exports.
- $299/mo (Team): Diarization + team seats + review/approve + brand voice hook suggestions + niche templates.

What to say if they resist monthly pricing:
- Offer credits: “If monthly feels weird, we can do a credit pack per episode. The tooling is the same; pricing just maps to usage.”

Data to collect during pilot (to finalize MVP after $5k DFY gate):
- Which step caused the most delay? (upload, highlight selection, rendering, revisions)
- What did they revise most? (captions, hooks, segment selection, styling)
- What was the “must be perfect” element? (accuracy, pacing, words-on-screen, brand look)

Close line:
- “Once we finish week 2 of the pilot, I’ll show you the self-serve flow we’re prioritizing based on your feedback. If it matches your workflow, we can lock you into early access pricing in the $99–$299/mo range.”

Reminder: No MVP build starts until $5k DFY revenue is collected. Use this playbook to gather paid-signal requirements while revenue is coming in.
