H2: Our QA standards (what we check before you see anything)
Toggle: Captions accuracy
- Captions match spoken words (names/terms verified)
- Punctuation for readability; minimal paraphrasing unless requested
- Safe margins: captions not clipped by platform UI

Toggle: Framing / safe zones
- No cropped faces (eyes/mouth/hands not cut off)
- Subject remains centered; reframes are smooth
- Captions and key text sit inside safe zones (TikTok/Reels UI)

Toggle: Audio leveling
- Dialogue is clear and consistent
- No clipping/distortion; noise reduced
- Loudness is normalized (consistent perceived volume across clips)

Toggle: Hook clarity + pacing
- Hook lands in first 1–2 seconds
- Removes dead air; jump cuts feel intentional
- Clip ends cleanly (no abrupt cut mid-word)

Toggle: Export settings
- Vertical 9:16 (1080×1920)
- H.264 MP4 (unless otherwise requested)
- Crisp captions, high readability

H2: Review flow (fast, timestamped)
Paragraph:
We send a single review link (Frame.io / Vimeo / unlisted YouTube) where you can leave timestamped comments.

Bullets:
1) Open the review link.
2) Leave timestamp comments (e.g., “00:12 change caption to …”).
3) One consolidated revision round per clip (within the revision window).

H2: Revision window (24 hours)
Callout:
You have 24 hours from delivery to request revisions. This keeps production moving and ensures we can maintain consistent turnaround.

H2: What counts as a FREE revision vs a paid change
Two-column bullets (or toggles):
FREE revisions (included):
- Caption typos / mishears / punctuation fixes
- Minor timing adjustments to captions
- Small reframes to avoid cropped faces
- Audio tweaks (slightly louder/softer, minor noise)
- Replace a b-roll suggestion if it’s clearly off-topic

PAID changes (scope changes):
- New clip selection from different timestamps not in the original brief
- Major re-edit (new narrative structure, new hook direction)
- Brand refresh after work started (new fonts/colors/style)
- Additional aspect ratios or extra exports beyond what was agreed
- Adding/removing substantial content or new on-screen graphics packages
