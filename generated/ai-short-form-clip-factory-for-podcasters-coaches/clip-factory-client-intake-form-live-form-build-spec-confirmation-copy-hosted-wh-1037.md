SECTION 1 — What you’ll receive (Delivery Pack):
“For each clip, you’ll receive a Delivery Pack containing:
- Final vertical MP4 (platform-safe framing)
- Captions file (.SRT)
- B-roll cue sheet (what/when/why)
- Posting suggestions (first-line caption + CTA + hashtag direction)

We also maintain consistent file naming so you can batch-upload easily.”

SECTION 2 — Our QA standards (what we check before you see it):
“Before sending any review link, every clip is QC’d for:
- Safe zones: no important text/face cropped by app UI
- Captions accuracy: correct words, punctuation, and timing
- Audio: consistent loudness, no peaking/clipping, clear speech
- Hook clarity: first 1–2 seconds communicate the reason to keep watching
- Jump cuts: clean pacing without distracting micro-cuts
- Framing: no cut-off faces; speaker stays readable on mobile
- Export settings: crisp 9:16 output with platform-friendly encoding”

SECTION 3 — Review flow (timestamp comments):
“1) We send a review link (timestamp comments enabled).
2) Leave feedback as timestamped comments (one thread per change).
3) We apply revisions and send finals + the Delivery Pack.

This keeps changes unambiguous and prevents missed notes.”

SECTION 4 — Revision window:
“Our standard revision window is 24 hours from when we send the review link. If you need more time, reply to agent_bob_replit+clip-factory@agentmail.to before the window expires and we’ll confirm an extension (when possible).”

SECTION 5 — What counts as a free revision vs. paid change:
“Free revisions (included):
- Caption fixes (typos, timing drift, punctuation)
- Minor framing/safe-zone adjustments
- Small audio level tweaks
- Swapping a b-roll moment with another obvious option
- Tightening cut points without changing the story

Paid changes (out of scope):
- New clip selection from different parts of the episode not previously clipped
- Changing the hook angle/strategy after initial direction was approved
- Rewriting for a new audience/offer (positioning shift)
- Major structural rewrite (different story arc)
- Rush turnaround requests after timeline was agreed

If something is out of scope, we’ll quote the change before doing any extra work.”

SECTION 6 — To avoid delays (client responsibilities):
“To keep turnaround fast:
- Provide working source links (and access permissions)
- Submit the intake form with banned words/brand terms
- Consolidate feedback into timestamp comments

If we’re missing key info, we’ll pause until we receive it (to avoid wrong edits and refunds).”
