<h3>What we collect in the intake</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Platforms needed (TikTok / Reels / Shorts) + target audience + goal (growth, leads, webinar, course, etc.)</li>
  <li>Hook style preferences (bold, curiosity, contrarian, educational, story-based)</li>
  <li>Caption style (clean, karaoke, highlighted keywords), emoji/ALL CAPS preferences</li>
  <li>Brand terms + prohibited words + competitor names to avoid</li>
  <li>Preferred CTA + link destination + handle(s) to display</li>
  <li>Pronunciation notes for names/brands + any sensitive topics</li>
  <li>Your source video link(s) and any brand assets (logo, fonts, brand colors)</li>
</ul>

<h2>2) Our delivery format (What you receive)</h2>
<p>For each clip batch, you receive a “Delivery Pack” with everything needed to post fast:</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Final vertical MP4(s)</strong> — platform-ready</li>
  <li><strong>SRT caption files</strong> — one per clip</li>
  <li><strong>B-roll cue sheet</strong> — timecodes + suggested overlays/cutaways (where applicable)</li>
  <li><strong>Posting suggestions</strong> — first-line caption options + hashtags + best posting notes (per platform)</li>
</ul>

<h3>Default export settings</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Aspect ratio: <strong>9:16</strong> (1080×1920)</li>
  <li>Codec: H.264 (MP4), AAC audio</li>
  <li>Frame rate: match source when possible (typically 30fps)</li>
  <li>Audio target: clear voice, no clipping; consistent perceived loudness across clips</li>
  <li>Safe zones: key text and faces kept away from platform UI overlays</li>
</ul>

<h2>3) Review flow (timestamped feedback)</h2>
<p>We send a <strong>review link</strong> (unlisted YouTube / Vimeo / Frame.io—depending on what you prefer). Please leave feedback using timestamp comments, e.g. “00:12 change caption to…”</p>
<ol>
  <li>You receive a review link + a short note on what to look for.</li>
  <li>You add timestamp comments (or reply with a timestamp list).</li>
  <li>We implement revisions and send finals.</li>
</ol>

<h3>Revision window</h3>
<p><strong>24 hours</strong> after the review link is delivered. If we don’t hear back within the window, we treat the cut as approved and proceed to final delivery to protect timelines.</p>

<h2>4) QA promise (what we check before delivery)</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Hook clarity:</strong> opening 1–2 seconds are strong and context is clear</li>
  <li><strong>Captions accuracy:</strong> correct words, punctuation, speaker changes, and timing (no “caption drift”)</li>
  <li><strong>Framing:</strong> no cropped faces; subject stays centered; readable text; safe zones respected</li>
  <li><strong>Audio:</strong> voice is intelligible; levels consistent; no peaking/clipping; background reduced</li>
  <li><strong>Jump cuts:</strong> clean, intentional pacing; no distracting stutters or awkward pauses</li>
  <li><strong>Compliance:</strong> avoids obvious copyright flags and disallowed/banned terms you provide</li>
</ul>

<h2>5) What counts as a free revision vs. paid change</h2>
<p><strong>Free revisions (our miss):</strong> typos, caption inaccuracies, timing errors, accidental cropping, audio issues, missing safe zones, incorrect export settings, or anything that deviates from the intake instructions.</p>
<p><strong>Paid changes (scope change):</strong> new hook direction after approval, swapping topics, changing clip selection criteria, adding new CTAs/brand guidelines not provided in intake, requesting additional versions/lengths, or major re-edits after the review window.</p>
