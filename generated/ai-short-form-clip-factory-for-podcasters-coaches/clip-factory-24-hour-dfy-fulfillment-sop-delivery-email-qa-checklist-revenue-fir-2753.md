Purpose
Deliver a consistent, high-quality set of ready-to-post vertical clips from a long-form video/podcast within 24 hours of receiving assets. This SOP is designed to (1) close and retain DFY clients now (revenue), and (2) collect validated inputs for the future SaaS MVP once the $5k DFY gate is reached.

Definitions / Standard DFY Deliverable
- “Clip”: 9:16 vertical video, burned-in captions, hook-first intro, platform-safe margins, and a recommended post caption + hashtags.
- Standard pack (example): 8–12 clips per episode, 20–45s each (unless client requests otherwise).
- Optional add-ons: extra clips, 1:1 square crop, headline variants, b-roll inserts, CTA end-card.

Tooling (free/low-cost preference)
- Editing: CapCut Desktop (free) or DaVinci Resolve (free).
- Captions: CapCut auto-captions or Whisper transcription (if available internally).
- Storage/Delivery: Google Drive link or Dropbox link.
- Internal tracking: Google Sheet CRM columns (Client, Episode link, Due date/time, Status, Notes, Revisions).

Intake (T+0 to T+1 hour)
1) Confirm receipt (send “Intake Received” note).
2) Collect required inputs (if missing):
   - Long-form source link (YouTube/Drive) or file upload
   - Brand kit: logo (PNG), colors, font preference (or “use clean default”)
   - Preferred hook style (controversial, contrarian, question, stat, story)
   - Target audience + offer/CTA (newsletter, call booking, course)
   - Platforms (TikTok/Reels/Shorts) and any compliance rules
3) Create job row in tracking sheet with 24-hour due timestamp.

Highlight Selection (T+1 to T+4 hours)
1) Skim/watch at 1.5–2x speed to identify 10–20 candidate moments.
2) Select moments that match at least 2 of the criteria:
   - Clear payoff in <45 seconds
   - Strong emotional/curiosity hook within first 1–2 seconds
   - Standalone context (minimal backstory needed)
   - Teaches a tactic, reveals a mistake, or tells a quick story
3) Write “Hook Options” (2 per clip) to test positioning. Example structures:
   - “Most people get X wrong. Here’s the fix…”
   - “If you’re doing X, stop—do this instead…”
   - “The fastest way to achieve X (without Y)…”

Edit & Format (T+4 to T+18 hours)
For each chosen moment:
1) Create vertical sequence:
   - Primary: center on speaker face; punch-in cuts every 2–4 seconds.
   - Add top/bottom safe margins for UI overlays.
2) Hook-first trim:
   - Remove dead air; start at the most attention-grabbing phrase.
3) Captions:
   - Burn in captions; 1–2 lines max; high contrast; highlight keywords.
   - Fix proper nouns and numbers manually.
4) Light enhancements:
   - Optional b-roll cue text (e.g., “[B-ROLL: chart rising]”) if client likes.
   - Optional subtle sound bed if appropriate (avoid copyrighted music by default).
5) Export settings:
   - 1080x1920 (9:16), H.264 MP4, 30fps, ~10–20 Mbps.
6) Naming convention:
   - Client_EpisodeDate_Clip01_HookKeyword.mp4

Quality Assurance Checklist (T+18 to T+22 hours)
Per clip, confirm:
- Hook lands in first 1–2 seconds (no intro fluff)
- Captions are accurate (names, numbers, key claims)
- Captions stay within safe margins (not under platform UI)
- Audio is clear and normalized (no clipping)
- No awkward jump cuts or missing context
- Visual framing keeps speaker centered; minimal empty space
- Duration matches spec (20–45s typical unless requested)
- Includes suggested post caption + CTA idea

Delivery (T+22 to T+24 hours)
1) Upload:
   - Folder structure: /Client/EpisodeName/Final Clips + “Post Captions.txt”
2) Send delivery email (template below) with Drive/Dropbox link.
3) Ask 2 validation questions (for SaaS later):
   - “Would you prefer choosing highlights yourself from a list, or should we keep selecting for you?”
   - “If software did this automatically (highlights + captions + export), what would you pay monthly: $99, $199, or $299?”
4) Log feedback in the sheet (Feature requests, pricing sensitivity, objections).

Revision Policy (simple + revenue-protective)
- Included: 1 revision round per clip pack (caption corrections, trimming, reframing).
- Not included (counts as new work/add-on): changing clip selection entirely, major re-edit across all clips, new graphics package.
- Revision SLA: 24–48 hours depending on volume.
