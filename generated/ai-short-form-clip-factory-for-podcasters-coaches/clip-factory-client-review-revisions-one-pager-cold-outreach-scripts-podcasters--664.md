CLIENT ONE-PAGER: HOW TO REVIEW & REQUEST REVISIONS (Copy/Paste)

Thanks for using Clip Factory. To keep turnaround fast and hit the 48-hour promise, please review your clips using the steps below.

Where you’ll receive your clips
- You’ll get a download link (Drive/Dropbox) with a folder that includes:
  1) Final exports (ready to post)
  2) A “Notes” file with suggested post captions + hashtags (optional)
  3) A “Timestamps” file showing where each clip came from

How to review (fastest method)
1) Watch each clip once without pausing (does it hold attention?).
2) Watch again and check only 4 things:
   - Hook: first 1–1.5 seconds makes sense and grabs attention
   - Captions: readable on mobile, no major mis-transcriptions
   - Framing: subject is centered, not cropped awkwardly
   - End: clean ending + CTA (follow/comment/DM) fits your brand
3) If a clip is “good enough,” post it. Speed > perfection for short-form.

How to request revisions (required format)
Email: agent_bob_replit+clip-factory@agentmail.to
Subject line: “Revisions — [Your Brand] — [Episode/Asset Name]”

In the email, list revisions like this (copy/paste):
- Clip Filename: [exact filename]
  - Time: [00:00–00:00]
  - Change Requested: [what to change]
  - Reason/Goal: [optional]

Examples of good revision requests
- “Clip 03: 00:02–00:05 remove the ‘um’ pause and tighten jump cut to make the hook punchier.”
- “Clip 07: captions—replace ‘their’ with ‘there’ at 00:12.”
- “Clip 01: move captions slightly higher; they’re covering the bottom subtitle safe area.”

Revision policy (to protect the SLA)
- Included: 1 revision round for up to 3 clips (minor edits: trims, caption corrections, reframes, CTA swap).
- Not included (new work): changing the clip topic/angle entirely, new selects, adding new footage, new branding package, or additional clip volume.
- Turnaround for included revisions: delivered within 24 hours after we receive a correctly formatted revision request.

Posting note
Short-form performance is iterative. We optimize for volume + clarity, then improve using retention data (watch time, rewatches, saves, comments). If you want, reply with your top-performing clip each week and we’ll adapt the next batch.
