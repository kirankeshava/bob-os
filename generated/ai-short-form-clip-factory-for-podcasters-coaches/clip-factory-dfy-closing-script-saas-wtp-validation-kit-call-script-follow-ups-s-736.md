## 1) 20-minute call script (DFY close + SaaS validation)
**Goal:** close a paid pilot (deposit today) + capture WTP + feature priorities.

### A. Opener (1 min)
“Thanks for hopping on. Quick agenda: I’ll understand your content + editing workflow, show how our Clip Factory process works, and if it’s a fit we’ll lock a small paid pilot for the next 7 days. I’ll also ask a couple questions about a self-serve version we’re considering—only so we build what people will actually pay for.”

### B. Diagnose the pain (5 min)
1) “What’s your main channel right now—TikTok, Reels, Shorts, LinkedIn? What matters most: views, leads, or sales?”
2) “How many long-form episodes do you publish per week? Typical length?”
3) “What’s your current clip workflow (who edits, tools, turnaround time)?”
4) “What’s the bottleneck: finding highlights, writing hooks, captions, formatting, posting consistency?”
5) “If you could fix one thing in the next 2 weeks, what would it be?”

### C. Offer: DFY pilot (5 min)
Position: “We turn one long episode into ready-to-post vertical clips with strong hooks + captions + b-roll prompts.”

**Pilot outline (say verbatim):**
“For a pilot, you send one episode link (YouTube/Drive), and within 48–72 hours we deliver **X clips** (9:16), each with: (a) a scroll-stopping hook, (b) burned-in captions, (c) b-roll cues, and (d) platform-specific safe zones. We’ll also include a simple posting plan and suggested titles.”

**Close question:**
“If we can get you consistent clips without you managing an editor, would you want to start with a paid pilot this week?”

**Pricing anchor (choose based on their volume):**
- Option A: “Starter pilot: 8 clips from 1 episode — $499.”
- Option B: “Growth pilot: 15 clips from 1–2 episodes — $899.”

**Deposit close:**
“To start, we just need the episode link + a $250 deposit. If you like the pilot, we roll into weekly delivery.”

(If they push back)
“What price were you expecting? And is the main concern budget, quality risk, or turnaround?”

### D. SaaS validation (7 min) — quantify willingness-to-pay
Transition: “We’re considering a self-serve version after we prove the service. I want to make sure it solves the real problem.”

Ask and record:
1) **WTP range**: “If this was self-serve—upload/link → highlights → pick template → export vertical with captions—what would you pay monthly? $99, $149, $299, or not at all?”
2) **Must-have features (pick top 3):**
   - Auto highlight detection (best moments)
   - Captions with word-level timing
   - Speaker labeling (diarization)
   - Brand template (fonts/colors/logo)
   - Hook rewriting in your voice
   - Niche templates (coach/podcast/real estate/etc.)
   - Auto b-roll suggestions
   - Team seats/client approvals
   - Direct posting/scheduler
3) “Dealbreaker: what would make you cancel in month 1?”
4) “What file/link sources matter: YouTube, Riverside, Zoom, Drive, Dropbox?”
5) “How many clips per week would you realistically export?”
6) “Would you rather: (A) fewer clips but higher quality hooks, or (B) higher volume and you pick?”

### E. Wrap + next step (2 min)
If closing DFY:
“Great—send the link today and we’ll deliver by [date]. I’ll email a quick recap and payment instructions from agent_bob_replit+clip-factory@agentmail.to. You can also share this legitimacy link with your team: https://6ff73b98-dcff-4f53-a10d-9ad392f63342-00-1n4h4nuiq6w8j-dbyyfbxp.picard.replit.dev/sites/3.”

If not closing:
“No worries. Would it be okay if I send 2 sample clips from your latest episode? If you like them, we can start the pilot.”

## 3) Interview scoring rubric (turns calls into a ranked SaaS roadmap)
Use this immediately after each call.

### A. Quick fields to capture
- Prospect type: Podcaster / Coach / Agency / Brand
- Current output: [# episodes/wk], [# clips/wk]
- Tools: [Descript/CapCut/Premiere/Editor/etc.]
- Biggest pain (verbatim quote): “_____”
- DFY interest: Yes/No, Pilot size preference: 8 / 15 clips

### B. Willingness-to-pay (WTP) score (0–5)
- 0 = would not pay
- 1 = $49–$99 only
- 3 = $99–$149 comfortable
- 4 = $149–$299 comfortable
- 5 = $299+ or “this would replace my editor”

### C. Feature priority scoring per feature (0–3 each)
For each feature below, score:
- 0 = irrelevant
- 1 = nice-to-have
- 2 = important
- 3 = must-have

Feature list:
1) Link upload (YouTube/Drive) + ingestion
2) Auto transcription quality (English)
3) Highlight detection (segments)
4) Template-based 9:16 rendering
5) Burned-in captions (word-level)
6) Speaker labeling (diarization)
7) Hook rewriting (brand voice)
8) Niche templates (coach/podcast)
9) B-roll suggestions/cues
10) Team review/approve + comments
11) Export presets (TikTok/Reels/Shorts safe zones)

### D. Roll-up method (after 10 calls)
Create a table with columns: Feature | Avg Priority (0–3) | % “Must-have” | Avg WTP of those who want it | Notes (verbatim).

**Decision rule:** Prioritize features that have:
- Avg Priority ≥ 2.2 AND
- % Must-have ≥ 40% AND
- Avg WTP ≥ 3 (meaning $99–$149+)

This produces a defensible “people will pay for this” list before any build starts.
