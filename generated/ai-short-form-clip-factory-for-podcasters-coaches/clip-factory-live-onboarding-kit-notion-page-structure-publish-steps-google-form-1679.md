Section 1 — What you’ll receive (Delivery Pack)
For each batch/order, you’ll receive a Delivery Pack containing:
1) Final vertical MP4 clips (ready to post)
2) Caption files (SRT) for each clip
3) B-roll cue sheet (timecoded suggestions; optional overlays/cutaways)
4) Posting suggestions per clip (first-line caption + suggested hashtags + CTA notes)

Section 2 — Quality standards (our QA checklist)
We run a final QC pass on every clip. Our baseline QA standards:
• Safe framing: faces not cropped; key text remains inside safe zones; no critical elements near edges.
• Captions: accurate to spoken words; punctuation for readability; no repeated/garbled phrases.
• Readability: captions sized for mobile; high contrast; consistent style.
• Audio: speech clear and consistent; no clipping; background music (if used) does not overpower voice.
• Cuts/pacing: jump cuts feel intentional; no awkward dead air; hook lands fast.
• Hook clarity: opening line is compelling and matches the content.
• Export settings: correct aspect ratio (9:16), codec/bitrate suitable for social, and no black bars.

Section 3 — Review process (timestamp feedback)
How to review quickly and avoid back-and-forth:
1) We will send a review link (Frame.io/Vimeo/Unlisted YouTube) OR a Drive folder with preview MP4s.
2) Leave comments with timestamps (example: “00:07 change caption wording” or “00:12 remove ‘um’”).
3) Keep feedback consolidated in a single message/thread when possible.

Revision window: 24 hours from delivery.
If we do not receive revision notes within 24 hours, we consider the delivery accepted so we can keep production moving for all clients.

Section 4 — What counts as a free revision vs. a change request
Free revisions (included within the 24-hour window):
• Caption typo fixes or small wording corrections
• Minor timing adjustments (caption timing, small trim)
• Small audio-level tweaks
• Safe-zone/framing fixes (if something was cropped incorrectly)
• Fixing an issue that does not change the original scope

Change requests (not included; treated as a new request after the window or if scope changes):
• Changing the chosen hook/topic after delivery
• Requesting additional clips beyond the agreed quantity
• Major restructuring (new storyline, new angle, new CTA across all clips)
• New branding requirements not provided at intake
• Re-editing from a different source file/version than what was provided

If you’re unsure, email us and we’ll confirm whether it’s a free revision or a scope change before doing extra work.
