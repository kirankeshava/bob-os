“To match your brand and avoid revisions, we ask you to submit a short intake form (platform targets, caption style, banned words, must-spell terms, hook vibe). Production begins after required intake items are submitted unless you explicitly waive this in writing.”

Section: 2) What You’ll Receive (Delivery Pack)
“Your Delivery Pack includes:
- Final MP4 clips (vertical, ready to post)
- Caption file for each clip (SRT)
- B-roll cue sheet (suggested visual overlays and cutaway ideas)
- Posting suggestions per clip (first-line caption + hashtags + best platform fit)

We also use consistent naming so you can upload fast and stay organized.”

Section: 3) Review Process (Timestamp Notes)
“We send an unlisted review link (Frame.io / Vimeo / unlisted YouTube) so you can leave timestamped comments.
How to review:
1) Watch each clip once without pausing.
2) Rewatch and add timestamp comments for anything you want changed.
3) Group feedback into: captions, framing, pacing, audio, hook.

Tip: Timestamped notes get fixed faster than long paragraphs.”

Section: 4) Revision Window (24 Hours)
“You have a 24-hour revision window from the moment the review link is delivered. This keeps projects moving and protects your posting cadence.

If we don’t hear back within 24 hours, we treat the clips as approved and finalize delivery.”

Section: 5) Quality Standards We Check
“Every clip is checked for:
- No cropped faces (eyes/mouth stay safe)
- Captions accuracy + punctuation + spelling of brand terms
- Audio leveling (no peaking/distortion; consistent loudness)
- Hook clarity in the first 1–2 seconds
- Clean jump cuts (no awkward dead air)
- Platform-safe export settings (9:16, correct bitrate, readable captions)

If you spot anything we missed, we fix it under the free revision rules below.”

Section: 6) Free Revisions vs Paid Changes
Free revisions (included):
- Caption typos, misheard words, timing drift
- Audio leveling issues, noise reduction adjustments
- Cropping/framing mistakes (e.g., face cut off)
- Missing requested terms/style from your intake
- Minor pacing tweaks (small trims to remove dead air)

Paid changes (scope change):
- Changing clip direction after approval (new hook angle)
- Choosing entirely different moments from the long video
- Adding new brand rules after production started
- Major re-edit requests that create a new deliverable

If something is paid-change territory, we’ll quote before doing any additional work.”

Section: 7) Contact
