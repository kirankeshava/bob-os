WHAT COUNTS AS A “CLIP” (definition)
A “clip” is one vertical (9:16) video segment, typically 20–60 seconds, cut from your source content, including:
- A strong opening hook (on-screen text + edit pacing)
- Burned-in captions (readable, platform-optimized)
- Light jump cuts and pacing edits
- Optional b-roll cues (as text notes or suggested overlays)
Not included by default unless purchased as an add-on: heavy motion graphics, advanced VFX, custom character animation, or sourcing paid stock.

DELIVERY FORMAT (what you receive)
- MP4 files, 1080x1920 (9:16), ready to upload
- Captions burned in (and/or an accompanying caption text file if requested)
- A simple posting calendar suggestion (Growth/Pro or retainer)
- Hook bank (Growth/Pro or add-on)

TURNAROUND SLA (default)
- Starter (10 clips): delivered within 48 hours of receiving (a) payment + (b) usable source file + (c) completed intake.
- Growth (30 clips/week): first batch within 72 hours; remainder delivered in rolling batches across the week.
- Pro (60 clips/2 weeks): delivered in rolling batches, with first batch within 72 hours.
If the source file is low quality, missing, or access is blocked, turnaround pauses until resolved.

REVISIONS (simple rule)
- 1 revision round included per clip for subtitles timing/typos, hook text tweaks, and minor trims.
- Revisions do not include: changing the chosen clip topic entirely, rewriting the full narrative, or requesting new clips beyond the package quantity.
