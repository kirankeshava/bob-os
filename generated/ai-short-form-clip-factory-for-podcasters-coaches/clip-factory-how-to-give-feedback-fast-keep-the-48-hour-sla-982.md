The rules that keep the SLA
1) One round, consolidated.
- Please avoid sending feedback across multiple emails/texts.
- Combine all notes into one message (or one comment pass in Frame.io).

2) Timestamp everything.
- Use exact timecodes like 00:03–00:07.
- If there are multiple clips, include the clip name + timestamp.

3) Group feedback into “Must Fix” vs “Nice to Have.”
- Must Fix = incorrect words, compliance risk, wrong names/numbers, brand safety issues, major meaning errors.
- Nice to Have = preference tweaks (hook alternative, pacing preference, b-roll swap). Nice-to-haves are applied if they don’t risk the 48-hour schedule.

4) Don’t rewrite the whole clip.
- We can adjust hooks and captions, but full script rewrites, new story arcs, or changing the core point usually becomes a new clip request.

5) Keep changes objective.
Best notes:
- “Caption typo at 00:06: ‘their’ → ‘there’.”
- “Mute profanity at 00:11.”
- “Swap hook text to: ‘3 mistakes most sellers make…’ ”
- “Add b-roll cue: ‘show chart/graph’ at 00:09.”

Slow notes (avoid):
- “Make it more viral.”
- “Feels off.”
- “Can you try 5 different versions?”

What we can revise (fast)
- Caption corrections (typos, punctuation, timing)
- Hook text adjustment (1 alternative per clip unless otherwise agreed)
- Minor pacing trims (tighten dead air, remove filler)
- B-roll swaps from cues (if stock/b-roll is readily available)
- CTA end card text adjustments

What counts as a new request (not included in the standard revision)
- Changing the clip’s main topic/angle after delivery
- Requesting multiple alternate edits per clip (A/B/C versions)
- New b-roll sourcing that requires heavy research/licensing
- Rebuilding the edit from a different section of the episode
