C) 20-minute call script (discovery + close)
0:00–1:30 — Set frame
- “Thanks for taking the time. The goal is to understand your clip needs and see if we can take this off your plate. If it’s a fit, I’ll recommend a small paid pilot so you can test results fast.”
- “Sound good?”

1:30–6:00 — Workflow + pain
1) “What are you publishing right now (podcast, YouTube, webinars)? How long are your episodes?”
2) “What’s the current process to get clips out? Who does what?”
3) “What’s the biggest bottleneck: finding moments, editing time, captions, or posting consistency?”
4) “How many clips/week do you *want* to post vs what’s actually happening?”

6:00–10:00 — Define ‘winning clips’ (quality bar)
5) “When a clip performs well for you, what usually made it work—topic, hook, controversy, story, teaching moment?”
6) “Any hard requirements? (brand fonts/colors, camera framing, subtitles style, b-roll, emojis/no emojis, etc.)”
7) “Do you need platform-specific formatting (TikTok vs Reels vs Shorts) or is one master ok?”

10:00–13:30 — Buying intent + economics
8) “If this were solved—consistent clips weekly—what would that be worth to you per month?”
9) “Would you prefer done-for-you delivery or a software subscription that exports clips for you?”
10) WTP ladder (ask plainly):
   - “At $99/mo for software that takes a link/upload and exports vertical clips with captions—would you consider that a no-brainer, maybe, or no?”
   - “What about $199/mo?”
   - “What about $299/mo?”
11) “What would it need to do for you to happily pay $199–$299/mo?” (Capture exact phrasing.)

13:30–16:30 — Feature validation (for later SaaS; don’t promise build)
12) “Which of these is most valuable?” (Pick top 2)
   A) Auto highlight detection (best moments pulled automatically)
   B) Better captions (accuracy + readability + pacing)
   C) Brand templates (colors, fonts, layout)
   D) Speaker detection/diarization (labeling, cutting by speaker)
   E) Hook suggestions written in your ‘brand voice’
   F) B-roll cues (on-screen prompts: ‘insert screenshot / cutaway’)
   G) Topic/keyword optimization per platform
13) “Any dealbreakers? (privacy, upload limits, turnaround time, manual review, etc.)”

16:30–19:30 — Close to DFY paid pilot (primary revenue action)
- “Based on what you said, the fastest path is a short paid pilot so you can judge quality before committing.”
Offer (choose one based on lead size):
Option 1 (starter): “We take 1 episode/video and deliver 5 vertical clips with captions + 2 hook variations each within 72 hours. $___.”
Option 2 (weekly cadence): “Weekly: you send 1 long video, we deliver 10 clips/week, ready-to-post. $___/week.”

Deposits / next step language:
- “If you want, we can start this week. I’ll send a simple checkout/invoice and you can reply with your brand notes and 1 link.”
- “If you’re not ready to start today, what would you need to see to move forward?”

19:30–20:00 — Confirm next step
- If yes: “Great—what’s the best email to send details to?” (Use agent_bob_replit+clip-factory@agentmail.to as the sender.)
- If maybe: schedule a follow-up and request 1 sample episode.
