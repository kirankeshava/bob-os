## 1) What we produce
For each approved clip, you receive a delivery pack designed to publish immediately:
- **Final vertical MP4** (platform-ready export)
- **Caption file (SRT)** (matches on-screen captions)
- **B-roll cue sheet** (timestamps + suggested overlays/cutaways)
- **Posting suggestions** (first-line caption + CTA suggestion + hashtags)

---

## 2) Quality standards (our QC checklist)
Before any draft is sent, we verify:

### A) Safe zones + framing
- No cropped faces (forehead/chin not cut; no awkward crop on eyes/mouth)
- Subject stays inside vertical safe zones (UI areas avoided)
- On-screen text stays readable and away from platform UI

### B) Captions accuracy + readability
- Captions match the spoken words (no meaning drift)
- Proper names/brands corrected if provided in intake
- Punctuation and line breaks optimized for speed + comprehension
- Caption placement avoids covering key visuals

### C) Audio leveling
- Dialogue is consistent and audible throughout
- No harsh peaks/clipping; no muddy low-end
- Background music (if used) never competes with speech

### D) Jump cuts + pacing
- Cuts feel intentional (no accidental “blink cuts”)
- Silences and filler removed unless it adds comedic/intentional effect
- Visual pattern breaks (zoom/cutaway/subtitles emphasis) used to maintain retention

### E) Hook clarity
- First 1–2 seconds communicate a clear reason to keep watching
- Hook matches the clip’s payoff (no clickbait mismatch)

### F) Export settings
- Vertical format suitable for TikTok/Reels/Shorts
- Crisp text rendering (no blurry subtitles)
- File plays correctly on mobile

## 4) Review flow (timestamp comments)
We deliver **drafts via a review link** (Frame.io / Vimeo / unlisted YouTube) so you can leave timestamped notes.

**How to review efficiently**
1) Watch once without pausing
2) Watch again and leave timestamped comments (example: “00:07 – caption should say ‘client acquisition’, not ‘customer acquisition’”)
3) Consolidate feedback into one pass if possible

**Review link:** Provided in your delivery email.

---

## 5) Revision window (24 hours)
To keep production fast and predictable, we include a **24-hour revision window** starting when we send the draft/review link.

- Revisions requested within 24 hours are prioritized.
- After 24 hours, we may need to schedule revisions into the next available slot.

---

## 6) What counts as a free revision vs. a change request
We’re very flexible on true fixes and alignment—this policy just prevents scope creep.

### Free revisions (included)
- Caption typos / mishears / punctuation fixes
- Minor timing adjustments (caption sync, a cut that feels too abrupt)
- Safe-zone/framing fixes (cropped face, text too close to UI)
- Audio leveling fixes (too loud/quiet, clipping)
- Hook tightening using the **same** moment/topic
- Small on-screen text corrections (CTA/handle spelling)

### Change requests (out of scope for the current clip)
- Switching to a different segment/topic entirely
- Rebuilding the clip with a new structure after approval
- Adding new brand packages/assets not provided at intake
- Multiple rounds of iterative style exploration beyond the first correction pass

If a request is a change request, we’ll clearly label it as such and propose the next step.
