15-minute discovery-to-close call script (DFY-first)
Timebox: 15 minutes.

1) Set frame (1 min)
“Goal: understand your content + distribution goals, then I’ll tell you if we can reliably turn each episode into 10–30 clips/week. If it’s a fit, we’ll pick a package and next steps.”

2) Diagnose (6 min)
- “What’s the main product you’re selling behind the content?”
- “How often do you publish long-form?”
- “What’s your current short-form output per week?”
- “What’s stopping you from posting more—time, editing, ideas, or workflow?”
- “Which platform matters most next 30 days (TikTok/Reels/Shorts)?”

3) Pitch (4 min)
“Here’s what we do: you send a YouTube link/upload, we identify highlight segments, write 3–5 hook options, burn in captions, add b-roll cues if relevant, and deliver ready-to-post 9:16 files. You approve before we finalize.”

Offer the sample:
“I can start with 2 sample clips from your latest episode within {time}. If you like them, we move into a weekly package.”

4) Close (3 min)
- “If we could deliver {10/20/30} clips/week consistently, what would that be worth compared to hiring an editor?”
- Present best-fit package (one recommendation, not three):
“Based on {their goal}, I’d recommend {Package}. We start this week with {deliverables}. Want to get that going?”

Objection handles (quick)
- “We have an editor.” → “Great—then we can be the clip strategy engine: highlight selection + hooks + caption styling, and your editor can execute. Or we can take overflow.”
- “Need to think.” → “Totally. What’s the main uncertainty—quality, volume, or budget? If it’s quality, the 2 samples answer it.”
- “Too expensive.” → “If we cut volume in half, does it fit? Or we keep volume and reduce extras (b-roll cues) to hit your number.”

Light SaaS validation (30 seconds, only after DFY yes/maybe)
“If there were a self-serve tool that turns a link into highlight clips with captions/templates, what would you pay monthly: $99, $199, or $299? And what feature would make it a ‘must-have’?”
