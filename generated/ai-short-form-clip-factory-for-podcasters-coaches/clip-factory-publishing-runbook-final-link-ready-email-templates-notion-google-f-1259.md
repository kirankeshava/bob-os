Section 1 — What you will receive (Delivery Pack)
For each batch, you’ll receive a “Delivery Pack” folder containing:
1) Final clips (MP4)
   - Vertical format (9:16)
   - Platform-safe framing (no cropped faces)
2) Captions file (SRT) for each clip
3) B-roll cue sheet (Google Doc/PDF or .txt)
   - Timestamped cues like: “00:03–00:05: show product close-up”
4) Posting suggestions
   - First-line caption options (hook variants)
   - Hashtag set suggestions by platform (TikTok/Reels/Shorts)
   - Notes on CTA placement and pinned comment ideas

Section 2 — Quality standards we follow (QA)
We run QA on every clip to ensure:
- Safe zones: critical text stays inside platform-safe areas (top UI, bottom UI, side margins)
- Captions:
  - Accurate wording (no meaning changes)
  - Punctuation and line breaks for readability
  - Speaker labels if needed
- Audio:
  - Clear voice, controlled peaks
  - No harsh clipping, minimal background noise
  - Music (if used) never competes with speech
- Visual framing:
  - No cropped faces (eyes/forehead/chin) unless intentionally stylized
  - No awkward jump-cuts mid-word
  - B-roll overlays do not block captions
- Hook clarity:
  - First 1–2 seconds must communicate why to keep watching
  - Visible/legible on mobile
- Exports:
  - MP4 H.264
  - 1080x1920 (preferred)
  - 24–30 fps
  - Audio AAC

Section 3 — Review process (timestamped comments)
We deliver a review link or a review folder, depending on your preference:
Option A: Frame.io / Vimeo review link (preferred)
- Please leave timestamped comments (example: “00:07 change ‘your’ to ‘you’re’”).

Option B: Unlisted YouTube link
- Leave timestamps in a single message or comment list.

Option C: Google Drive folder
- Leave feedback in a single Google Doc titled “REVISION NOTES — [ProjectName]”.

Section 4 — Revision window (24 hours)
- You have 24 hours from delivery to submit revision notes.
- If we do not receive revision notes within 24 hours, we consider the delivery approved and close the order.
- We do this to protect turnaround times and keep your content cadence consistent.

Section 5 — What counts as a free revision vs paid change
Free revisions (included):
- Caption typos or mishears
- Minor timing adjustments (caption sync, cut timing)
- Fixing a missed safe-zone issue (text too high/low)
- Audio leveling tweaks if clearly off
- Swapping an incorrect b-roll clip when we selected it

Paid changes (scope change):
- Changing the hook concept after delivery (new angle/structure)
- Re-cutting the clip into a different story after approval
- Replacing large sections with new source footage after editing starts
- Adding new branded motion packages not agreed in advance
- Multiple rounds beyond the agreed revision window

If you’re unsure, email agent_bob_replit+clip-factory@agentmail.to and we’ll label it clearly as “free revision” or “paid change” before work begins.
