Section: Before We Start (Required)
To protect quality and deadlines, we start production after:
1) You submit the Clip Factory Intake Form (brand terms, style, platform targets)
2) We receive working source video link(s) (Drive/Dropbox/unlisted YouTube)
3) Any special compliance/disclaimer text is provided (if needed)

If you want to waive the intake step, you can—but it must be explicitly confirmed in writing via email, and it may reduce caption/name accuracy.

---
Section: Our QA Standard (What We Check)
Every clip is checked for:
- Safe zones: captions and key visuals avoid TikTok/Reels UI overlays
- Framing: no cropped faces; speaker stays centered; readable composition
- Captions: accurate spelling for provided brand terms; punctuation; minimal drift
- Audio: consistent loudness, no clipping/peaking, clean dialogue focus
- Hook clarity: first 1–2 seconds explain why to watch (without misrepresenting)
- Cuts: tight pacing; no awkward mid-word jump cuts
- Export settings: platform-ready vertical MP4 (H.264), crisp text, no artifacts

---
Section: Review Flow (How Approval Works)
1) We send a review link (Frame.io / Vimeo / unlisted YouTube) with numbered clips.
2) You leave timestamped comments on the review link (preferred). Example: “00:07 change ‘Acme’ to ‘ACME’.”
3) We apply revisions and deliver finals.

Revision window: You have 24 hours after the review link is delivered to request revisions (unless otherwise agreed in writing). This keeps projects moving and protects deadlines.

---
Section: What You Receive (Delivery Pack)
Your delivery includes a folder containing:
1) Final MP4 clips (ready-to-post)
2) Caption files (SRT) for each clip
3) B-roll cue sheet (timestamps + suggested overlays/b-roll moments)
4) Posting suggestions per clip:
   - First-line caption (hook line)
   - Suggested hashtags (non-spammy, niche-relevant)
   - Platform notes (TikTok/Reels/Shorts differences)

---
Section: Revisions vs Paid Changes (So There’s No Confusion)
Free revisions include:
- Caption spelling/grammar fixes
- Misheard words (reasonable corrections)
- Safe-zone/framing issues (cropped faces, text too low/high)
- Audio leveling fixes or obvious noise issues introduced in editing
- Export mistakes (wrong resolution, wrong file naming, missing SRT)
- Any mismatch vs what you provided in the intake form

Paid changes (scope change) include:
- New creative direction after approval (“make it funnier/more aggressive/less clickbait”)
- Re-cutting to entirely different moments not in the original request
- Adding new brand rules after delivery (new banned words, new style guide)
- Large batch additions (extra clips beyond order)
- New aspect ratios or platform packages not originally requested

If something is unclear, email us: agent_bob_replit+clip-factory@agentmail.to

---
Section: How to Get Fast Results
- Provide correct spelling for names/brands/products in the intake form
- Share 2–3 example clips you like (tone + caption style)
- Tell us what topics NOT to include
- Use timestamped comments on the review link for fastest turnaround

3) INTERNAL RULE — START-OF-WORK GATE (SHORT POLICY)
========================================
Policy: “No Intake, No Edit”
We do not begin editing until the client has (a) submitted the intake form and (b) provided working source links—unless the client explicitly waives the intake in writing via email. This single gate prevents wrong spellings, wrong hooks, wrong platforms, and refund-triggering expectation gaps.

Enforcement checklist (internal):
- Intake submitted (Y/N)
- Source links received + playable (Y/N)
- Brand terms + banned words captured (Y/N)
- Target platforms confirmed (Y/N)
- Deadline + revision window acknowledged (Y/N)

If any item is “N”, pause and request info before editing begins.
