A) CLIENT ONE-PAGER: HOW TO REVIEW & REQUEST REVISIONS (COPY/PASTE)
========================================
Title: How to Review Your Clips (and Request Revisions Fast)

What you’ll receive (typical delivery):
1) A folder with 10 vertical clips (TikTok/IG Reels/YT Shorts-ready)
2) A “Clip Notes” doc listing each clip’s title + hook + source timestamp
3) Optional: subtitle/b-roll cue notes (if included in your package)

How to review in under 10 minutes:
Step 1 — Watch each clip once without pausing.
Step 2 — Mark each clip as one of:
• APPROVE (post-ready)
• MINOR TWEAK (small change; keep the structure)
• REPLACE (not usable; request a different moment)
Step 3 — For any clip needing changes, use the exact format below.

Revision request format (required):
Paste this into your reply for each clip needing changes:
1) Clip filename:
2) Issue type (choose one): captions | hook | pacing | framing | b-roll cue | audio | other
3) Exact change request (1 sentence):
4) Timecode (if applicable): 00:00–00:00
5) If replacing: send 1–2 candidate timestamps from the long video (or describe the moment)

Examples of good requests:
• “CLIP_03… captions: change ‘$50k’ to ‘50K’ and move captions slightly higher. 00:02–00:07.”
• “CLIP_07… hook: replace first line with ‘Here’s why most people fail at X…’ Keep rest.”
• “CLIP_10… replace: this topic isn’t relevant. Use the segment where I explain Y at 18:40–19:25.”

What counts as a “minor tweak” (included in revision policy):
• Caption wording/spelling fixes
• Caption timing adjustments
• Slight crop/reframe adjustments
• Remove/add a short pause, tighten pacing
• Swap hook line text while keeping the same clip moment

What counts as a “replace” (may use replacement allowance):
• Different moment/topic requested (new source timestamp)
• Major restructuring (different angle/argument)

Review deadline (to protect speed):
Please respond within 72 hours of delivery. If we don’t hear back, we’ll consider the delivery approved so we can keep the 48-hour production schedule consistent.
