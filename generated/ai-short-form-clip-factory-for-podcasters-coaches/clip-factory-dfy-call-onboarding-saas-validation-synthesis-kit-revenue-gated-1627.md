A) 15-minute DFY call agenda that doubles as product validation
1) Context (2 min): “What are you posting today? Where do clips bottleneck?”
2) DFY offer fit (5 min): Confirm volume, turnaround, approval workflow.
3) Validation block (6 min): Ask the 6 questions below; capture exact phrases.
4) Close (2 min): Offer DFY package; if not ready, ask permission to send 1 sample clip.

The 6 validation questions (ask verbatim)
1) “If you could wave a wand, what would your clipping workflow look like end-to-end?”
2) “What makes a clip ‘good enough to post’ for you—hook, captions, pacing, b-roll, branding, or something else?”
3) “How important is accuracy vs speed for captions? What errors make you reject a clip?”
4) “Do you need speaker labels (diarization) or is single-speaker fine?”
5) “If this was software you used weekly, what would you expect it to do automatically vs what would you still want to control?”
6) “At what price would this feel like a no-brainer per month ($99 / $199 / $299)? What would it need to include to justify that?”

C) Feature scoring rubric (per feature, per interview)
Score each requested feature 1–5 on each dimension:
1) Frequency: how often it appeared across calls (1 rare → 5 constant)
2) Revenue impact: directly increases willingness-to-pay/retention (1 low → 5 high)
3) Differentiation: separates us from editors/other tools (1 commodity → 5 unique)
4) Implementation risk: (reverse score) 1 high risk/unknown → 5 low risk/known
5) Time-to-value: user gets benefit fast (1 slow → 5 immediate)
Weighted score formula (simple): (Frequency + Revenue + Differentiation + Time-to-value) * ImplementationRisk
Interpretation:
- 80+ = strong candidate for early SaaS tiering
- 50–79 = keep in backlog / upsell
- <50 = avoid until later

- Output lift: clips posted in first 7 days after delivery.
