## 2) Quality standards we follow (QA checklist summary)
We run QA on every clip before we send it.

### A) Framing + safe zones
- Subject’s **face is not cropped** (top of head, chin, sides) during key moments.
- Headroom and composition look natural in **9:16**.
- Captions and key text stay inside safe zones (not hidden by platform UI).
- No accidental overlays covering eyes/mouth.

### B) Captions accuracy + readability
- Captions match spoken words with high accuracy.
- Speaker changes are clear (if multiple speakers).
- Punctuation and line breaks improve readability.
- No confusing profanity replacements unless requested.

### C) Audio quality
- Speech is easy to understand on phone speakers.
- Loudness is leveled consistently across clips.
- No harsh clipping, distortion, or distracting background noise.

### D) Editing rhythm
- Hook is clear within the first 1–2 seconds.
- Jump cuts feel intentional (no awkward pauses, dead air, or confusing cuts).
- Visual emphasis supports the point (zoom/cut used to enhance clarity).

### E) Platform formatting
- Delivered in vertical (9:16) format.
- Clean exports with no accidental watermarks.
- Clip length and pacing optimized for short-form retention.

## 4) Revision policy (scope protection)
We want you thrilled with the clips while keeping turnaround reliable.

### Free revisions (included)
Free revisions cover **fixes** to match the agreed intent and our QA standards:
- Caption typos / missed words / timing fixes
- Minor cropping/safe-zone fixes (e.g., face too tight)
- Audio leveling adjustments
- Fixing an on-screen text error
- Removing a small awkward pause or tightening dead air

### Not free (scope change)
These are **change requests** beyond the original direction:
- Switching to a different segment/topic after delivery
- New clip selections not in the original scope
- Rewriting creative direction (new hook concept, new angle, new story)
- New branding package requests after edit started (fonts/colors/layout)
- Additional clips beyond the agreed quantity

If a request is a scope change, we’ll reply with:
1) what’s included for free, 2) what changes the scope, and 3) the simplest path forward.
