### 1) Before we start (Required Intake)
To avoid caption errors, brand mismatches, and unnecessary revisions, we require a short intake form before editing starts. It covers:
- Platforms (TikTok/Reels/Shorts)
- Hook style and caption style
- Brand terms and banned words
- Framing preferences (we avoid cropped faces by default)

If you choose to skip intake, we can proceed only with written confirmation that you accept “Clip Factory defaults.”

### 2) What you’ll receive (Delivery Pack)
Your delivery includes a structured folder containing:
- **Final MP4 clips** (vertical 9:16)
- **Caption files (.SRT)** for each clip
- **B-roll cue sheet** (simple notes for optional overlays/cutaways)
- **Posting suggestions** per clip: first-line caption + recommended hashtags + platform notes

### 3) Our QA standards (to protect your brand)
Before anything is sent, we run a final QC pass to confirm:
- Captions are accurate and readable (timed, minimal drift)
- Audio is leveled (no peaking/clipping; voice is clear)
- Hook is clear within the first seconds
- Faces are not unintentionally cropped; safe zones respected
- No distracting jump cuts; pacing is intentional
- Export settings match short-form platform expectations

### 4) Review process (timestamp comments)
You’ll receive a review link (or an unlisted video link) where you can leave timestamped notes.

**How to review efficiently:**
1. Watch each clip once straight through.
2. On the second pass, leave timestamp comments for:
   - wording/caption fixes
   - hook alternatives
   - any “do not say/show” items
3. Consolidate feedback into one round when possible.

### 5) Revisions (24-hour window)
To keep turnaround fast for creators, we offer a **24-hour revision window** starting from when the review link (or finals) are delivered.

Within that window, we’ll address eligible fixes (see below). After the window, additional changes may be scheduled as a new request.

### 6) What counts as a free revision vs. a paid change
**Free revisions (our responsibility):**
- Caption typos or mis-transcriptions
- Minor timing adjustments to captions
- Incorrect cropping that cuts off faces or key visuals
- Audio issues introduced during editing (peaking/clipping)
- Export/spec mistakes (wrong aspect ratio, file corrupted)

**Paid changes (scope changes):**
- Changing clip selection after approval (“use a different moment”) 
- New hook concepts beyond the agreed style/round
- Rebuilding edits due to brand guidelines not provided during intake
- Adding new assets not included initially (new b-roll libraries, new brand kits)

If something is ambiguous, we’ll tell you upfront whether it’s a fix (free) or a change (paid) before we proceed.

## C) Internal Rule — Start-Work Gating (Refund-Prevention)

**Rule:** No editing starts until the client submits the Intake Form OR explicitly waives it in writing.

**Default handling if intake is missing:**
- Use “Clip Factory defaults”: verbatim-ish captions cleaned for readability, face-safe framing, neutral hook style, platform-safe text placement.
- Send a short email: “We can start now using defaults, or you can complete the intake to reduce revisions.”

**Waiver language (copy/paste):**
“Client confirms they are waiving the Clip Factory Intake Form and accepts Clip Factory defaults for captions, hook style, and framing. Client understands revisions may be limited if brand requirements were not provided before editing began.”

**Why this exists:** Most refunds and churn come from mismatched expectations (style, wording, banned terms). This gate prevents silent assumptions and protects marketplace reputation.
