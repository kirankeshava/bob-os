Title: How to Review Your Clips (and Request Revisions)

Thanks for working with Clip Factory.
Website (for reference): https://6ff73b98-dcff-4f53-a10d-9ad392f63342-00-1n4h4nuiq6w8j.picard.replit.dev/sites/3

Goal of this review:
We optimize for speed + consistency (10 clips within 48 hours). Revisions are meant to correct issues and align with your preferences—not to restart strategy from scratch.

How to review (fastest method):
1) Watch each clip once without pausing (does it “grab” you in the first ~1–2 seconds?).
2) Watch again focusing on: captions accuracy, pacing, and whether the cut changes make sense.
3) Mark any needed changes using the exact format below.

What counts as a revision (included):
- Caption corrections (misspelled words, wrong names, missing punctuation)
- Tightening dead air / removing a filler phrase
- Swapping the hook line from one of the provided alternate hook options (if included)
- Adjusting caption styling within the chosen preset (size, position, emphasis)
- Minor b-roll cue timing changes (±1–2 seconds) or removing a distracting cue
- Audio leveling (if something is noticeably too quiet/loud)

What does NOT count as a revision (typically new scope):
- Rewriting the clip concept from scratch (new angle, new story)
- Re-cutting from a completely different part of the episode not in the selects
- Changing the style preset after clips are already produced
- Adding brand-new assets we didn’t receive during intake (logos, music licenses, new b-roll libraries)

Revision request format (copy/paste for each clip):
Subject line: Revisions — [ClientName] — [Episode/Source] — [Round 1]

For each clip:
- Clip File Name:
- Timecode(s): (e.g., 00:07–00:10)
- Requested Change: (one sentence)
- Priority: Must-fix / Nice-to-have

Examples of good requests:
- “CLIP_03… 00:02–00:04: Change caption to ‘$10k/month’ (not ‘10k a month’). Must-fix.”
- “CLIP_07… 00:11: Remove the ‘um’ and tighten the gap. Nice-to-have.”
- “CLIP_02… 00:00–00:02: Use alternate hook option B (shorter). Must-fix.”

Turnaround on revisions:
- Round 1 revisions: delivered within 24 hours of receiving your notes (weekday).
- Additional rounds: handled as availability permits or as an add-on.

Approval tip:
If 8/10 clips are good, approve those immediately and only request revisions on the remaining 2—this keeps posting momentum high.
