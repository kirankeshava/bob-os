Cold-call script (front desk → office manager → owner)
Purpose: get the right person and book a 15-minute demo.

1) Gatekeeper / front desk opener (20 seconds)
“Hi, this is Bob Smith. Quick question—who handles appointment confirmations and reducing no-shows for the practice? Office manager or practice manager?”

If they ask “What is this about?”
“Totally—we run two-way SMS confirmations and automated reschedules for dental practices so fewer hygiene/doctor slots go unused. It’s not a billing call. Who’s best to speak with?”

2) Office manager opener (30–45 seconds)
“Hi [Name], Bob Smith here. I’ll be brief. We help dental practices cut no-shows and last-minute cancels with two-way SMS confirmations, an easy reschedule link, and a waitlist that fills openings. We also show recovered production so it’s measurable. Can I ask—are no-shows or same-day cancels something you’re actively trying to reduce right now?”

Qualifying questions (pick 3)
- “About how many appointments do you have per day across hygiene + doctor?”
- “Do you send confirmations by text today, and can patients reply to confirm/reschedule?”
- “What happens when someone cancels within 24 hours—do you have a waitlist process?”
- “Which software are you on for scheduling (Dentrix, Open Dental, Eaglesoft, etc.)?”

Close for demo (clear CTA)
“If I could show you a simple workflow that typically saves 3–8 appointments a month and doesn’t require your team to chase confirmations manually, would you be open to a 15-minute walkthrough? If yes, what’s better—tomorrow morning or Thursday afternoon?”

Objection handling (short)
- “We already text patients.”
“Most practices do. The difference is two-way confirmations + automated reschedule + waitlist fill, plus we quantify recovered production. If you’re already doing it perfectly, the demo will make that obvious in 10 minutes.”
- “We don’t have time.”
“Understood—that’s why this exists. The goal is less calling, less back-and-forth, fewer dead chair hours. The demo is 15 minutes; if it’s not relevant I’ll disappear.”
- “We can’t change software.”
“We’re not asking you to replace your PMS. We start lightweight and work with your current scheduling workflow.”
- “Send info.”
“Absolutely—what’s the best email? I’ll send a 1-page overview and a live proof page. Also, should I address it to you or the owner?”
