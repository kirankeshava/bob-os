2) ICP + Qualification (for calling + quick disqualification)
Ideal Customer Profile (Dental):
- Single-location or 2–10 location groups
- 15+ appointments/day OR 300+ appointments/month
- Has recurring hygiene schedule + short-notice cancellations/no-shows
- Uses a PMS (Dentrix, Open Dental, Eaglesoft, etc.) and can export appointments or provide a daily list
- Has a waitlist OR “we wish we could fill cancellations faster” pain
- Decision maker reachable: owner dentist, practice manager, office manager

Fast Qualifying Questions (ask in <2 minutes):
1) “Roughly how many appointments do you see per day?”
2) “Do last-minute cancellations/no-shows hurt you weekly?”
3) “Do you have a waitlist or patients who want earlier slots?”
4) “How do you confirm—phone calls only, texts, or both?”
5) “If we cut no-shows even a little and filled same-week gaps, who would decide to turn it on?”

Disqualify if:
- <10 appointments/day AND very low cancellation sensitivity
- No ability/willingness to text patients
- Cannot provide appointment list (even via simple export)
- Decision maker unreachable and gatekeeper blocks all follow-up

4) Objection Handling (tight, dental-specific)
Objection: “We already text reminders.”
Reply: “Makes sense. The difference is we do two‑way confirmations plus automated reschedules and a waitlist fill so cancellations turn into filled slots. If you’re already doing it perfectly, you’ll know in 10 minutes and we’ll part friends. Worth a quick look?”

Objection: “Patients don’t respond / they ignore texts.”
Reply: “That’s common. Two things help: (1) we ask for a simple yes/no confirmation and (2) if they can’t make it, we give a reschedule link immediately and push the opening to your waitlist. Even a small lift—like filling one extra hygiene slot—often covers the month.”

Objection: “We don’t want to annoy patients.”
Reply: “Totally agree. We keep it minimal—typically 2–3 touches max, timed to when offices see the most no‑shows. And patients can opt out. The goal is fewer manual calls for your team and fewer empty chairs.”

Objection: “We don’t have time to set this up.”
Reply: “That’s why we do concierge setup. We only need a basic appointment export and your preferred confirmation rules. Most offices are live in under a week.”

Objection: “Is this HIPAA compliant?”
Reply: “We keep messages generic—no diagnosis or sensitive content. We only send appointment logistics/confirmation. On the demo we can walk through the exact message templates so you can approve them.”

Objection: “Email me.”
Reply: “Absolutely—what’s the best email? I’ll send a short summary from agent_bob_replit+no-show-bot@agentmail.to and include the proof page. Should I follow up tomorrow or would you prefer we book a 12‑minute time now?”
