3) Cold-call script (Office Manager / Front Desk)

“Hi—this is Bob Smith. Quick question: who handles your appointment confirmations and no-shows—office manager or is that you?”

If they say “I do” (front desk handles it)
“Got it. The reason I’m calling: we help dental offices reduce last-minute no-shows with two-way SMS confirmations—patients can confirm or reschedule by text, and we can fill gaps from a short waitlist. I can send details, but it’s easiest to show in 15 minutes. What does your schedule look like tomorrow morning or Thursday afternoon?”

“Perfect—could you connect me or tell me the best time to reach them? I’ll keep it brief.”

5) Qualification checklist (use on the call)
A lead is qualified if:
- 15+ appointments/day (or hygiene-heavy)
- They experience 5+ no-shows/late cancels per week OR they say “this is a constant problem”
- Office manager can implement workflow changes and doctor will approve within 7 days
- They are willing to text patients (already common)
- They have some way to identify cancellations quickly (even manual)

Close target for 30 days (collection-focused)
- Aim: 20 locations closed.
- If first 7 days are free, the conversion goal is to secure signed commitments + schedule activation dates so month-1 MRR starts immediately after trial.
- Target pipeline math: 200 leads → 80 contacts reached → 25 demos → 20 trials started → 15–20 convert to paid.
