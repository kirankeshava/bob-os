1) ICP + targeting (who to contact)
Primary vertical: general dentistry + cosmetic dentistry (single location and small groups, 1–5 locations).
Best-fit indicators:
- Appointment-heavy schedule (hygiene + restorative)
- Uses a PMS that can export appointments (any system is fine if they can export/report)
- Has an office manager/front desk lead (someone owns scheduling)
- Mentions “missed appointments”, “late cancellations”, “new patient availability”, “waitlist”, or has online booking but no two-way confirmation flow
Titles to target:
- Office Manager, Practice Manager, Operations Manager, Front Office Supervisor
- Owner Dentist / Managing Partner (secondary)

2) Qualification checklist (use in email replies + first 5 minutes of demo)
Ask these 6 questions:
1) Roughly how many appointments/day per location? (or per week)
2) What % no-shows / late cancels do you estimate? (even a guess)
3) Do you currently text patients? If yes, is it one-way or can patients confirm by replying?
4) When someone cancels last-minute, do you have a waitlist process? How is it managed today?
5) Can you export a daily/weekly appointment list (CSV/report) from your PMS or have your staff share a schedule file?
6) Who approves workflow changes like reminders/confirmation language (you, owner, or both)?
Qualify-in thresholds (soft):
- 10+ appointments/day and/or hygiene schedule that regularly has gaps
- Any meaningful no-show/late cancel pain (2%+ no-shows or “it happens daily/weekly”)
- Willing to run a 14-day trial/pilot

3) ROI story (talk track)
Dental no-shows are high-cost because the chair time is perishable. If a practice recovers even 3–6 appointments/month that would have been lost, the system often pays for itself. We position it as: “reduce no-shows + convert cancellations into reschedules + fill gaps from a waitlist,” and we show recovered revenue per location in simple reporting.
