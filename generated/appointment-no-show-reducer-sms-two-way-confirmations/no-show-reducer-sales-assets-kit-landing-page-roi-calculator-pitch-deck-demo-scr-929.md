Response:

Follow-up question:
“Do you currently track confirmation rate, or just reminders sent?”

Objection: “Our patients/customers don’t text.”

“You don’t need 100% participation. Even modest reply rates reduce no-shows because you catch the highest-risk appointments. Also, SMS is lightweight—people can confirm with one tap, no app install.”

“What percent of your intake forms already include a mobile number?”

“We keep messages minimal: no diagnoses, no sensitive details—just time/location and confirm/reschedule prompts. We can configure templates to match your privacy policy, and we can discuss BAA requirements based on your workflow.”

“Let’s anchor it to recovered revenue. If we prevent 10 no-shows a month and your average appointment is $150, that’s $1,500 recovered—before counting filled cancellations. If we can’t show a positive ROI in the first month, you shouldn’t keep it.”
