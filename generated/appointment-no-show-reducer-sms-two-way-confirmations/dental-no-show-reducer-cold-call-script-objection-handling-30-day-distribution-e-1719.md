2) OBJECTION HANDLING (tight answers)

A) “We already send reminders.”
“Totally—most offices do. The difference is two‑way confirmation and automated reschedule + waitlist fill. Instead of ‘reminder sent’, you get ‘confirmed’ or ‘needs to reschedule’—and openings get offered to your waitlist. Would it be crazy to take 12 minutes to see if it would reduce staff time and patch holes?”

B) “We don’t have a no-show problem.”
“Great—then this will be a quick ‘no’. Last question: in a typical week, do you get even 1–2 late cancels that you can’t refill? If yes, this is mainly about filling those gaps automatically. If not, we’ll stop here.”

C) “We’re too busy / not changing systems.”
“Understood. This is designed to be low-lift: we can start with a small slice—just hygiene confirmations and reschedules—and prove impact in days. If it doesn’t measurably help, you cancel. Worth a quick look?”

D) “Is this an integration?”
“We can start without a deep integration—simple daily schedule export or a lightweight workflow—then expand. The goal is speed to value: confirmations + reschedules + waitlist in week one.”

E) “How much does it cost?”
“$399 per month per location plus SMS pass‑through, and a one‑time setup during the pilot window. The point is ROI: a couple recovered appointments usually covers it. If you want, I’ll email the full one‑pager and we can see if it fits.”

F) “Send me info.”
“Absolutely—what’s the best email? And so I don’t spam you, if it looks relevant, should I follow up tomorrow or would you prefer a 12‑minute slot now?”
