Below is a ready-to-use Day-1 outbound package: (1) an import-ready lead list template for HubSpot (CSV columns), (2) a free lead-sourcing workflow to fill 200 leads fast using only Google Maps + business websites, (3) copy for email/call/text that references the legitimacy URL and business contact email.

1) HUBSPOT / CSV IMPORT COLUMNS (copy these as your header row)
- Company Name
- Website
- Phone
- City
- State
- Vertical (Dentist/Chiro/Med Spa/PT/Optometry)
- Location Count (1 / 2-5 / 6+ / Unknown)
- Contact First Name
- Contact Last Name
- Contact Title (Owner/Office Manager/Practice Manager/GM)
- Contact Email
- Source (Google Maps / Website / Directory)
- Notes (e.g., "Online booking", "Open Saturdays", "Mentions missed appts")
- Lead Status (New)
- Last Touch Date
- Next Step (Call / Email / Follow-up)

Formatting rules:
- Keep phone in plain format: 555-555-5555.
- Put the location’s public website (not Yelp) whenever possible.
- If you can’t find a named contact, use Contact First Name = "Front Desk" and Title = "Scheduling" temporarily; replace later.

2) FREE LEAD SOURCING WORKFLOW (repeatable, 0$ tools)
Goal: 200 leads = 2 city clusters × 5 verticals × ~20 leads each.

Step A — Pick city clusters (example):
- Cluster 1: Phoenix, AZ + Scottsdale, AZ
- Cluster 2: Tampa, FL + St. Petersburg, FL
(Any two metro areas work; pick where these businesses are dense.)

Step B — Pull businesses from Google Maps
Search queries (copy/paste):
- "dentist" + "City, ST"
- "chiropractor" + "City, ST"
- "physical therapy" + "City, ST"
- "optometrist" + "City, ST"
- "med spa" + "City, ST"

For each promising listing, capture:
- Company name
- Website
- Phone
- City/State
- Notes (e.g., “online booking”, “same-day appts”, “cosmetic dentistry”, etc.)

Step C — Find the best email (from the business website)
Open the website and check, in order:
1) /contact page (often has frontdesk@, info@, appointments@)
2) footer (email sometimes listed)
3) "About" or "Team" pages (may reveal owner name)
4) if no email visible: look for a contact form; still capture phone and keep email blank for call-first.

Email patterns to try (only if you have a person’s name on the site):
- first@domain.com
- firstname.lastname@domain.com
- office@domain.com / frontdesk@domain.com / scheduling@domain.com

Step D — Deduping
Before adding a row, search within your sheet for the same website or phone. If it exists, skip.

Step E — Prioritization notes (helps close faster)
Add a quick note if you see any of:
- Online booking link
- Multiple providers
- Extended hours
- “New patients welcome”
- Mentions late cancels/no-shows
These indicate higher appointment volume and bigger pain.
