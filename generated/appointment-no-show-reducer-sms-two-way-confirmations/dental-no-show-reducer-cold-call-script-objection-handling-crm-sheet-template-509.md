2) COLD CALL SCRIPT (Owner / Dentist)

Opener:
“Dr. [Name], Bob here — I’ll be brief. Do you want more control over no-shows and last-minute cancellations without adding front-desk work?”

Value prop (tight):
“We run two-way SMS confirmations so patients confirm YES/NO. If they reply NO, they get an immediate reschedule link; and we automatically text your waitlist to fill openings. We also give a simple report that estimates recovered revenue per month.”

Qualifying question:
“Roughly how many appointments per day do you run — and do no-shows show up more in hygiene, production, or both?”

Close:
“If I can show you the workflow in 15 minutes and it looks low-lift for your team, is it worth a quick look? Tuesday 10:30 or Wednesday 2:00?”

3) OBJECTION HANDLING (short, non-defensive)

A) “We already have reminders in our PMS.”
“Totally. Most offices do. The difference is two-way confirmation (YES/NO) plus automated reschedule + waitlist fill. Reminders alone still leave holes. If you’re willing, I’ll show the exact flow and you can decide if it’s redundant.”

B) “Patients hate texts / we don’t want to annoy them.”
“Agreed — we keep it minimal: confirmation + one follow-up, and only for scheduled patients. They can opt out. The goal is fewer calls for your team, not more noise.”

C) “We don’t have a waitlist.”
“Even a small one works. We can also build a lightweight ‘text me if anything opens’ opt-in list from existing patients who want earlier times.”

D) “How much is it?”
“$399/month per location plus pass-through SMS cost, and a one-time concierge setup so your team doesn’t do busywork. If it doesn’t measurably help, you can cancel — we can also do a risk-free first 14 days.”

E) “Send info.”
“Will do. What’s the best email? Mine is agent_bob_replit+no-show-bot@agentmail.to. And to make sure it’s relevant: is your bigger issue last-minute cancellations or true no-shows?”

F) “We’re too busy.”
“That’s exactly why we keep it low-lift. The demo is 15 minutes. If it’s not obviously helpful, we stop there.”

4) QUALIFICATION CHECK (ask 2–3 of these on the call)
- Approx appointments/day (or week)
- Where the problem is worse: hygiene vs production
- Current reminder method (PMS reminder vs manual calls)
- Do they have a waitlist or could they start one?
- Who owns the decision: owner, office manager, DSO ops?
- Is there a metric they care about: chair utilization, hygiene reactivation, production per day
