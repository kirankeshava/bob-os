## Cold Call Script (Office Manager / Front Desk)

### 0) Gatekeeper opener (10 seconds)
“Hi—this is Bob. Quick question for whoever manages appointment confirmations and no-shows—would that be you?”

If yes:
“Perfect. I’ll be brief. I run Appointment No-Show Reducer—we help dental offices cut no-shows using two-way SMS confirmations and an easy reschedule link, plus we can fill last-minute gaps from a waitlist. Could I ask you two quick questions to see if it’s relevant?”

If no:
“Who’s the best person for that—office manager or the doctor/owner? And what’s the best time to reach them?”

### 1) Qualify (30–45 seconds)
1) “Roughly how many appointments do you have on a normal day?”
2) “Do you have an estimate of no-shows or late cancellations per week?”

Optional follow-up:
“Do you currently text patients to confirm—one-way reminders, or can patients reply to confirm/reschedule?”

### 2) Micro pitch (20 seconds)
“Got it. The reason I’m calling: we run a simple two-way text workflow that asks patients to confirm, and if they can’t make it, we automatically push them to a reschedule link and notify a waitlist to fill the gap. We also show recovered revenue by location so you can quantify impact.”

### 3) Offer (reduce risk; 15 seconds)
“We’re doing a 7-day free launch for a handful of dental offices. No long contract—if it doesn’t reduce no-shows measurably, you just stop. If you like it after the free week, it’s $399/month per location plus SMS pass-through, and we can do concierge setup.”

### 4) Close for the meeting (10 seconds)
“Is it crazy to grab 15 minutes this week to see if this fits your workflow? If you want, I can email the details and proof page now: agent_bob_replit+no-show-bot@agentmail.to, and our info is here: https://6ff73b98-dcff-4f53-a10d-9ad392f63342-00-1n4h4nuiq6w8j.picard.replit.dev/sites/2.”

### 5) Booking options (choose one)
A) “Do you have your calendar open—what’s better, tomorrow morning or afternoon?”
B) “Should I send two time options by email and you can pick?”

### 6) If they ask “What do you need from us?”
“Just your confirmation workflow today, your appointment list source (export is fine), and the wording you want patients to see. We can start with only hygiene recalls or only new patients—whatever is easiest.”

## Objection Handling (short, non-pushy)

### “We already send reminders.”
“Totally—most offices do. The difference is two-way confirmation plus an automatic ‘can’t make it → reschedule’ path, and then we try to fill gaps from a waitlist. If yours is already two-way with reschedules and gap filling, you probably don’t need us. Quick question: can patients reply to confirm/reschedule and do you actively fill openings when someone cancels?”

### “We don’t want more tech / too busy.”
“Fair. That’s why we start with a 7-day free launch and keep the workflow minimal—no overhaul. We can run it on a subset (e.g., hygiene only) so it doesn’t add work. If I could show you a version that reduces calls and last-minute scrambling, is it worth 15 minutes to review?”

### “How much is it?”
“After the free week, it’s $399/month per location plus SMS pass-through. If you want concierge setup, that’s a one-time fee, but for week one we can launch free to prove impact.”

### “Send me info.”
“Absolutely. What’s the best email? I’ll send a short summary with the proof page link and a couple of time options. You can also reply to agent_bob_replit+no-show-bot@agentmail.to if that’s easier.”

### “We can’t text patients / compliance concerns.”
“Understood. We only message patients who have consented to SMS (most PMS systems capture this), include opt-out language, and keep messages strictly operational (confirm/reschedule). On a quick call I can show the exact templates and you can approve wording.”

### “Our no-shows aren’t that bad.”
“Makes sense. Even if it’s only 2–3 a week, one saved chair-time slot can cover the month. If you tell me your average production per missed appointment, I’ll estimate the ROI quickly.”

### “Not the decision maker.”
“No problem—who is best, and what do they care about most: revenue recovered, less front-desk workload, or patient experience? I can tailor the summary email.”
