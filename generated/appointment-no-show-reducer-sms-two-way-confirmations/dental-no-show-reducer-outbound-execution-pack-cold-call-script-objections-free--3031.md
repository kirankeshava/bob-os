3) OBJECTION HANDLING (SHORT, PRACTICAL)
==============================
Objection: “We already use reminders.”
Reply:
“Totally — most do. The difference is two-way confirmation plus an automated ‘No → reschedule’ flow and waitlist backfill. One-way reminders still leave you with late cancellations and empty chair time. If you’re already strong here, the demo will show it quickly.”

Objection: “Patients hate texts / we don’t want to spam.”
Reply:
“We keep it minimal: confirmation + one reminder, and only appointment-related. Patients can confirm with one letter. The goal is fewer calls for your team and fewer missed appointments.”

Objection: “We don’t have time to set this up.”
Reply:
“That’s why we do a concierge setup. We keep it lightweight: your appointment export, your message wording, and who should get alerts. Typical setup is under a week with minimal staff time.”

Objection: “What does it cost?”
Reply:
“It’s $399/month per location plus SMS pass-through, and a one-time concierge setup (typically $499) to launch quickly. If it doesn’t measurably reduce no-shows, you can cancel — and we can structure it as risk-free for the first couple weeks.”

Objection: “We need the dentist/owner to approve.”
Reply:
“Completely fair. Who is the right person? If you can join for 10 minutes, I’ll keep it tight and focus on impact on schedule utilization and front desk workload.”

Objection: “Is this HIPAA compliant?”
Reply:
“We keep messages appointment-focused and can avoid sensitive details. On the demo we’ll walk through exact wording and workflow so it matches your compliance comfort level.”

4) FREE LEAD LIST BUILD SPEC (FIRST 200 DENTAL LOCATIONS)
==============================
Target (ICP):
- Single-location dental practices or small groups (2–10 locations)
- High appointment density: general dentistry, family dentistry, hygiene-heavy offices
- Likely to feel pain: busy schedules, multiple hygienists, frequent cancellations

Geo strategy (fastest): pick 2–3 metro areas where you can call during business hours.
Example: Phoenix AZ, Dallas TX, Tampa FL (choose any).

How to find leads (free sources):
A) Google Maps
- Search terms: “dentist”, “family dentistry”, “general dentist”, “dental clinic”, “cosmetic dentist”
- Open each listing → capture: practice name, phone, website, address, rating (optional), hours
B) Practice websites
- Find “Team” or “Contact” page → office manager name, emails (sometimes)
- If no email listed: use contact form URL + generic email patterns (info@, hello@, office@)
C) LinkedIn
- Search practice name → employees → find Office Manager / Practice Manager / Operations Manager

What to exclude:
- Mobile-only practices with no stable front desk
- Specialty-only that’s low volume (some oral surgery may be lower appointment count)
- Locations without a website (harder trust + contact)

Data fields to capture (minimum viable):
- Practice Name
- Website URL
- Main Phone
- City/State
- Contact Name (Office Manager preferred)
- Contact Role
- Email (if available)
- LinkedIn URL (contact)
- Notes (current reminder tool if mentioned; hygiene-heavy; multiple locations)
