Primary vertical (first 30 days)
Dental practices (single-location and small groups/DSOs up to ~10 locations).
Why dental: high appointment density, costly no-shows, and typically a fast decision cycle (owner + office manager).

Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
- 1–10 locations; 1+ hygienists; consistent hygiene + restorative schedule
- Missed appointments are a known pain (or they’re “fine” but cancellations create gaps)
- Office manager/front desk can adopt a simple confirmation workflow
- They can text patients today (or are willing to start)

Core outcomes
- Fewer no-shows / late cancellations
- More confirmed appointments (less calling)
- More filled gaps from a simple waitlist workflow
- Clear analytics that quantify recovered production from “saved” appointments

In-scope features (the wedge)
1) Two-way SMS confirmations
   - Patients reply Y/N to confirm
   - Non-responders get smart follow-ups
2) Smart reminders
   - Timed reminder sequence (e.g., 72h/24h/2h) tuned for dental workflows
3) Reschedule link + assisted reschedules
   - If patient replies “No” (or requests change), we trigger reschedule instructions
4) Waitlist fill (gap recovery)
   - When a slot opens, we message the waitlist and book the first responder (simple rules)
5) Basic recovered-revenue analytics
   - Weekly summary: confirmations, cancellations caught early, slots refilled, estimated production recovered

Out of scope (for first 30 days)
- Deep custom PMS integrations (we keep onboarding lightweight)
- Complex multi-provider optimization / advanced BI dashboards
(These can be added once the wedge proves value.)

Implementation (concierge setup)
Week 1 launch plan (typical):
- Confirm reminder timings + message tone
- Import upcoming appointments (simple export or lightweight data feed)
- Configure office rules (which appt types, confirmation window, how to handle “No” replies)
- Go live and monitor replies; tune copy after first 50–100 patient messages

Why it pays for itself (ROI story)
If you save or refill just 1–2 appointments/month, most practices break even. Many practices see more: one filled hygiene opening or a saved restorative appointment can cover the monthly fee quickly. We quantify this weekly with a recovered-revenue summary per location.

Guarantee options (pick one; designed to reduce risk)
Option A — Cancel anytime: Month-to-month, cancel whenever.
Option B — 14-day risk-free: If you don’t like it in the first 14 days, cancel and you won’t be billed for the next month.
Option C — Performance protection: If after the first full month there’s no measurable improvement (confirmations up and/or no-shows down vs. baseline), we waive the next month’s fee while we fix it.

Qualification checklist (to decide fast)
A practice is a fit if:
- They have at least ~15 appointments/day (or consistent schedule volume)
- They can export a schedule (even CSV) or provide upcoming appointment list weekly
- They want fewer no-shows/cancellations AND are willing to use two-way texting
- They have a person responsible for handling “No / reschedule” replies (often front desk)
Not a fit if:
- No texting allowed by policy, or they refuse patient messaging
- Extremely low appointment volume (harder to measure impact)
