4) DEMO-TO-CLOSE SCRIPT (15 MINUTES)
Objective: confirm pain, show workflow, propose pilot, set next step to close.

Minute 0–2 (Context):
“Thanks for the time. In 15 minutes I’ll show (1) two-way confirmations, (2) reschedule link flow, (3) waitlist fill, and (4) a simple recovered-revenue report. If it’s not a fit, we’ll stop.”

Minute 2–6 (Discovery — ask, write down):
- “How many appointments per day across hygiene + doctor schedules?”
- “What’s your rough no-show rate for hygiene and doctor?”
- “When someone cancels, how often do you fill the opening?”
- “What system do you use today for reminders/booking?”

Minute 6–10 (Show the wedge):
- “Patient gets SMS: ‘Reply 1 to confirm, 2 to reschedule.’”
- “If reschedule: link with 2–3 slot options (or request new time), logged for staff.”
- “If cancel: offer to waitlist patients, first-come, confirm slot.”
- “Dashboard: confirmed %, rescheduled %, filled slots %, estimated revenue recovered.”

Minute 10–13 (ROI story in their numbers):
“Let’s do quick math. If you have ~[X] appts/day and even [Y] no-shows/week, saving 2 appointments/month often covers the $399. The point is measurable reduction within the first couple weeks.”

Minute 13–15 (Close the next step):
Option A (Pilot start):
“Next step is a 7-day pilot. We’ll run confirmations + reschedules on a subset (e.g., hygiene) and report back. If you see a measurable drop in no-shows/cancellations, we switch you to $399/mo and you can cancel anytime.”

Option B (Close now with guarantee):
“If you prefer to start fully, we can do concierge setup and launch within a week. If there’s no measurable improvement, we waive next month.”

Confirm decision process:
“Who besides you needs to sign off—doctor/owner? Can we book a 10-minute decision call for [date]?”

5) QUALIFICATION CHECKLIST (USE LIVE ON CALL)
Green lights:
- 40+ appointments/day OR frequent same-day gaps.
- No-show/cancel problem acknowledged by office manager.
- Hygiene schedule is high volume (easy to prove ROI).
- They can use SMS reminders (patients have mobile numbers).
- They’re willing to run a 7-day pilot and review results.

Red flags:
- Extremely low appointment volume (ROI hard).
- Decision maker unavailable and office manager cannot influence.
- Practice refuses texting or has poor patient mobile capture.
