Title: Free 7-Day No-Show Reduction Pilot (Concierge)

What it is
Appointment No-Show Reducer sends SMS reminders and collects two-way confirmations so you know who’s coming, who needs to reschedule, and when you can backfill gaps from a waitlist.

What’s included (7 days, free)
1) Reminder messages: configurable timing (e.g., 24h + 2h before)
2) Two-way confirmations: customers can reply YES / NO / RESCHEDULE
3) Reschedule workflow: when a customer can’t make it, we route them to reschedule options (your preference)
4) Waitlist gap-fill (optional): notify waitlist when a slot opens
5) Owner-safe fail-safes: opt-out handling (STOP), error alerts, and manual escalation
6) Weekly value report: confirmations, reschedules, filled gaps, and estimated recovered revenue

What we need from you (minimum)
- Your timezone + business hours
- A single point of contact for day-to-day questions
- Your reminder timing preference (when to message)
- Baseline metrics (last 4 weeks if available):
  • average weekly appointments
  • estimated no-show rate
  • average appointment value (or rough range)
- Consent/expectation: your customers already provided their phone number for appointment communication (typical for reminders). We always include opt-out language.

Success criteria (examples)
- Increase confirmation rate (e.g., +15–30%)
- Reduce no-shows (even 1–3 saved appointments/week is material)
- Fewer inbound calls/texts needed to manage scheduling

FAQ

Q: What if someone replies something weird?
A: We use high-confidence keyword rules (YES/NO/STOP/RESCHEDULE) and escalate edge cases to your team when unclear.

Q: Can we stop anytime?
A: Yes—pilot is free, and you can stop immediately.

5) 15-MINUTE ONBOARDING CALL SCRIPT (Pilot qualification + setup)
============================
Goal: confirm fit, capture baseline, set messaging rules, agree on go-live date.

0:00–1:00 Intro
“Thanks {{Name}} — I’m Bob. Quick agenda: understand your appointment flow/no-show pain, confirm we can run a safe 7-day pilot, collect baseline numbers, and pick a go-live date.”

1:00–5:00 Qualify + baseline
1) “Roughly how many appointments per week?”
2) “What’s your best estimate of no-show rate?”
3) “Average appointment value (or range)?”
4) “What’s the main no-show driver: forgetfulness, transportation, insurance, price, something else?”

5:00–9:00 Workflow + rules
1) “What reminder timing do you want? Common: 24h before and 2h before.”
2) “If a client replies NO or RESCHEDULE, do you prefer: (a) send a reschedule link, (b) have staff call them, or (c) offer 2–3 alternative times by text?”
3) “Do you have a waitlist we can use for last-minute openings? If yes, how should we prioritize (first-come, VIP, etc.)?”
4) “Any restricted hours we should not text (e.g., after 8pm)?”

9:00–12:00 Compliance + escalation
1) “We include opt-out language. If a client texts STOP, we stop.”
2) “If we can’t confidently interpret a message, we escalate to your staff contact instead of guessing.”
3) “If an integration fails, you’ll get an alert and we revert to a safe mode.”

12:00–14:00 Success metrics + reporting
“In the weekly report, we’ll show: messages sent, confirmations, reschedules, waitlist fills, and an estimated recovered revenue number. We’ll compare to your baseline.”

14:00–15:00 Close + next steps
“Let’s set go-live for {{Date}}. I’ll email a short summary of what we decided plus the baseline numbers we captured. If anything looks off, reply and we’ll correct before go-live.”
