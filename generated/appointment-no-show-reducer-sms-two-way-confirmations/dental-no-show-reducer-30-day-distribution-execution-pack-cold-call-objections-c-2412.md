5) OBJECTION HANDLING (SHORT, REPEATABLE)
A) “We already use [Solution] / our PMS does reminders.”
“Totally — most systems send reminders. The difference is two-way confirmations and automating the reschedule + filling the gap from a waitlist. If you’re still seeing holes in the schedule, that’s where this helps. Open to a 15-min compare?”

B) “We don’t have no-show issues.”
“Good problem to have. Quick check: do you ever get same-day cancels that you can’t fill? Even a small number can be worth solving if the setup is concierge and low lift. If it’s truly zero, I’ll back off.”

C) “Send info.”
“Absolutely—what’s the best email? I’ll send the proof link and a 30-second summary. If it resonates, what day this week should I follow up?”

D) “No budget.”
“We’re launching with a free 7-day setup and you can cancel anytime. If we can’t show measurable improvement (filled slots/recovered revenue) you shouldn’t pay. Worth a quick look?”

E) “Too busy.”
“Understood. That’s why we keep it to 15 minutes and do concierge setup. If I can’t make it simple, you can ignore it. Is morning or afternoon better?”

6) QUALIFICATION CHECKLIST (USE DURING CALL/DEMO)
Only push to close if 3+ are true:
- 30+ appointments/day OR dedicated hygiene schedule
- Reports at least 2 no-shows/cancels per week (or admits ‘holes’ happen)
- Has a waitlist (even informal) or wants one
- Office manager is willing to run a 7-day trial
- Can provide appointment export or daily schedule list (even CSV) during week 1
- Has ability to text patients (most do) and consent messaging process
Red flags:
- No-shows are genuinely near zero and no cancellations
- No access to any schedule/export and unwilling to do any manual process
