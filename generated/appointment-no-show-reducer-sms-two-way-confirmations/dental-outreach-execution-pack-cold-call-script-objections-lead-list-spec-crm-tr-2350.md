D) OBJECTION HANDLING (SHORT, PRACTICAL)
1) “We already send reminders.”
“Most offices do. The difference is two-way confirmations and automated reschedule + waitlist fill when someone cancels. Quick question: when someone texts back ‘can’t make it’, what happens today?”

2) “Patients ignore texts.”
“Some do—but two-way replies typically lift confirmations because it’s a yes/no action. Even if it only saves 2–3 appointments a month, it usually pays for itself. Want to sanity-check your numbers on a 15-minute call?”

3) “We don’t have time to set this up.”
“That’s exactly why we do concierge setup. We’ll ask for a simple appointment export and your confirmation rules, then we launch. The first week can be free so there’s no risk while we do the work.”

4) “Is this HIPAA compliant?”
“We avoid putting sensitive clinical info in texts—messages are appointment logistics only. On the call we’ll confirm your preferred wording and compliance requirements.”

5) “Who else uses this?”
“We’re launching with a small set of dental locations first; that’s why we’re offering a fast, low-risk rollout. If it doesn’t measurably reduce no-shows, you can cancel—no lock-in.”

6) “Send this to the doctor/owner.”
“Absolutely—what’s the best email for Dr. [Name]? And since you run scheduling day-to-day, can we do a quick 10 minutes with you first so the doctor only sees a clear recommendation?”
