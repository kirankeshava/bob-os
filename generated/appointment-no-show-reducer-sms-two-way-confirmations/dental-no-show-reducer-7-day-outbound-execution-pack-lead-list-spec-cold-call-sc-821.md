## C) Cold-call script (office manager first)
**Call opener (10 seconds):**
“Hi, is this the office manager? … I’m Bob. Quick reason I’m calling: we help dental practices cut no-shows using two-way SMS confirmations and easy rescheduling. Can I ask—are no-shows or late cancellations a problem for you guys lately?”

**If they say ‘a little’ / ‘yes’:**
“Got it. Roughly how many appointments do you see in a day—20, 40, 60? And do no-shows hit hygiene more, or procedures more?”

**Qualify (keep it fast):**
1) “Do you currently text patients reminders?”
2) “Can patients confirm by texting back, or is it one-way?”
3) “If someone cancels last minute, do you have a waitlist you can quickly fill?”

**Value hook (tie to recovered revenue):**
“The reason I ask—when practices switch to two-way confirmations + an automated ‘fill-from-waitlist’ flow, they usually recover a few appointments a week without adding staff work. We also show recovered revenue per location so you can quantify it.”

**Legitimacy (mention proof URL + email):**

**Close for meeting (15 minutes):**
“Would it be crazy to take 15 minutes this week to see if we can reduce no-shows for your hygiene schedule? What’s better—tomorrow morning or Thursday afternoon?”

## D) Objection handling (short + specific)
**1) ‘We already use reminders (Solutionreach/Weave/Podium/etc.)’**
“Totally—most practices do. The difference is the two-way confirmation workflow + reschedule link + waitlist fill, and we track recovered revenue. Quick question: can patients confirm by simply texting back, and does your system automatically offer that opening to your waitlist when someone cancels?”

**2) ‘Send me info’**
“Sure. What’s the best email? I’ll send a 6-line overview with the proof URL and pricing. Also—so I send the right thing—are hygiene no-shows your main pain, or last-minute cancels?”

**3) ‘No budget’**
“Understood. This usually pays for itself with one recovered appointment. If we could show measurable reduction, would $399/month per location be reasonable? If not, no worries—I’ll send info and you can revisit later.”

**4) ‘We’re too busy’**
“That’s exactly why this is helpful—front desk time is the bottleneck. Setup is concierge-style; we can launch in under a week once we know your reminder timing and confirmation rules. Could we do a 15-minute fit check?”

**5) ‘Owner needs to decide’**
“Makes sense. Are you the right person to evaluate workflow impact? If yes, let’s do a quick call together and I’ll join with specifics; or I can send a note you can forward with the proof URL and my email.”
