QUALIFICATION CHECKLIST (USE ON FIRST CALL)
Hard qualifiers (must have):
1) They currently confirm patients via manual calls/texts OR have reminders but still see meaningful no-shows/gaps.
2) They can define a baseline: typical no-show/cancel rate OR “holes per day/week.”
3) They can take action on reschedules/waitlist fills (someone owns the schedule).
4) They can give us one of: appointment export, daily schedule screenshot, or limited staff time to set rules.

Strong signals:
- Hygiene chair time is regularly underutilized due to late cancels/no-shows.
- They have a short-notice waitlist but no systematic way to fill gaps.
- They’ve tried reminder systems but don’t have two-way confirmations + reschedules.

OBJECTION HANDLING (SHORT)
1) “We already have reminders.”
“Totally — most offices do. The gap is usually two-way confirmations + reschedule handling + a waitlist workflow. One-way reminders still leave you with holes. Mind if I ask: do patients reply to confirm/cancel and does it automatically trigger a reschedule option?”

2) “We don’t want more work for the front desk.”
“Agreed — the goal is less work. Confirmations and reschedules are automated; staff only steps in when the patient requests a change or when you want to accept a waitlist fill. That’s why we start with a quick setup and then it runs.”

3) “Texting patients sounds risky / compliance.”
“We only message existing patients about their appointments and confirmations. We keep messages simple and opt-out friendly. On a demo I can show the exact templates. If you want to keep it conservative, we start with confirmation-only messaging.”

4) “What does it cost?”
“It’s $399 per location per month, plus a one-time $399 concierge setup to launch quickly. SMS is pass-through. We can cover pricing in 2 minutes, but first I want to confirm it would actually recover more than it costs — can I ask how often you see same-day holes?”
