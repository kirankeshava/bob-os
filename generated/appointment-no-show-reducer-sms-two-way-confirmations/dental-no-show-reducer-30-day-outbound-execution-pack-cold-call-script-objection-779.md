D) Objection handling (short, non-pushy)
1) “We already have reminders through our PMS.”
“Totally — most do. The difference is two-way confirmations and automated rescheduling + waitlist fill when someone cancels late. Your PMS reminder is usually one-way. If you already have two-way + waitlist fill, we may not be a fit. Worth 10 minutes to compare?”

2) “Patients hate texts / we don’t want to annoy them.”
“Agreed. We keep it minimal: one confirmation, one reminder, and only follow-up if they don’t respond. Patients can opt out. The goal is fewer calls for your team and fewer empty chairs.”

3) “No budget.”
“Understood. If you recover even one hygiene slot and one production slot a month, it often covers the fee. If it doesn’t, we can stop—cancel anytime. Would it help to see the math on a 15-minute call?”

4) “We don’t have time / too busy.”
“That’s exactly why this exists. Setup is concierge: we configure the messages, confirmation logic, reschedule link, and waitlist workflow. Your team mainly just approves the wording and tells us your scheduling rules.”
