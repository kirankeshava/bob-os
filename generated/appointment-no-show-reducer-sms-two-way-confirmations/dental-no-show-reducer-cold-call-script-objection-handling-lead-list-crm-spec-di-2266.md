1) COLD CALL SCRIPT (Office Manager-first)
Goal: book a 15-minute demo; do NOT try to “sell” on the call.

A. Opener (10 seconds)
“Hi, is this the office manager? … Great—my name is Bob Smith. Quick question: are you the person who handles appointment confirmations and no-shows?”

If yes:
“Perfect. I’ll be brief—this is about reducing last-minute no-shows and filling cancellations automatically.
I run a simple two-way SMS confirmation + reschedule workflow for dental practices. It texts patients, collects a YES/NO, and if they can’t make it, it pushes them to reschedule and pings a waitlist to fill the gap.
Can I ask you one question to see if this is even relevant?”

B. Qualifying question (choose one)
1) “Roughly how many hygiene appointments do you have in a day?”
2) “Do you have more trouble with day-before cancellations or same-day no-shows?”
3) “Do you currently text patients and get two-way replies, or is it mostly one-way reminders?”

C. Value statement (15 seconds)
“Got it. Most offices we talk to lose a few slots a week just from late cancels/no-shows. If we can recover even 2–3 appointments per month, it typically pays for itself.
We track confirmed vs. unconfirmed and show ‘recovered revenue’ so you can see if it’s working.”

2) OBJECTION HANDLING (short, non-pushy)

Objection: “We already use reminders from [PMS/vendor].”
Reply: “Totally. The key difference is two-way confirmation + automatic reschedule + waitlist fill. Many reminder tools send texts, but don’t convert a ‘no’ into a reschedule slot or fill the cancellation. The demo is just to compare your current workflow to what we’d automate.”

Objection: “Patients hate texts.”
Reply: “We keep it minimal and opt-out friendly. The goal is fewer calls for your team and fewer missed appointments. Messaging is short, branded to your practice, and only around upcoming visits.”

Objection: “We don’t have time to set this up.”
Reply: “That’s exactly why we include concierge setup. We ask for a simple export (or a daily list), your appointment types, and your preferred script. Most offices are live in under a week.”

Objection: “Owner has to approve.”
Reply: “Makes sense. Who’s best—doctor/owner or practice admin? If I send you the overview link plus a 2-minute summary, can you forward it? Then we can do a quick 15-minute call with both of you.”

Objection: “We don’t have a waitlist.”
Reply: “Then we start with confirmations + reschedules. If you want, we can build a simple ‘text me if something opens up’ waitlist over time. It’s optional.”

Objection: “How do you prove ROI?”
Reply: “We track confirmations, cancellations, reschedules, and filled slots. Then we estimate recovered revenue using your average production per visit. You’ll see the before/after in basic analytics.”

3) LEAD LIST BUILD SPEC (fastest list-building without paid tools)
Target vertical: Dental (single-location and small groups).

A. Geography
Pick 2–3 metros where you can call during business hours. Example: Dallas–Fort Worth, Phoenix, Atlanta.

B. Practice types (prioritize)
1) General dentistry with hygiene schedule
2) Family dentistry
3) Cosmetic dentistry (often higher $/visit)
Avoid (initially): hospital dental departments, very large DSOs with centralized procurement.

C. Roles to contact
Primary: Office Manager, Practice Manager, Front Office Lead.
Secondary: Owner/Doctor, Operations Manager (small group).

D. Sources (free)
Google Maps search (“dentist near [city]”), Yelp, Zocdoc listings, practice websites (contact forms), Facebook business pages.

E. What to capture per lead
- Practice name
- Location address
- Phone
- Website
- Office manager/practice manager name (if available)
- Email (if listed)
- Notes (PMS mentioned, online booking, hours)

F. Quick qualification heuristics from website
- Has online booking (good sign: volume)
- Mentions “text reminders” (means they value SMS)
- Multiple hygienists / multiple providers
