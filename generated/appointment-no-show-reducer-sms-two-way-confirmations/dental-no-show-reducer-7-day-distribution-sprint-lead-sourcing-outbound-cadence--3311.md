GOAL (Week 1, $0 spend): Book 8–12 demos with dental practices (single-location + small groups) and convert 3–5 into free 7-day pilots that are set up within 48 hours.

1) ICP + Targeting (who we contact)
- Primary: General dentistry practices with 3–15 operatories or 1–5 providers; appointment volume high enough that no-shows are a real issue.
- Decision makers to reach: Office Manager / Practice Manager (fastest), Owner Dentist (backup), Operations Manager (small DSO).
- “High pain” indicators (use in targeting): offers “same-day crowns,” “implants,” “hygiene recall,” “new patient specials,” busy schedule, multiple hygienists.
- Exclusions (for now): hospitals, Medicaid-only clinics, very small 1-chair offices, practices that only do emergency walk-ins.

4) Cold Call Script (Office Manager / Front Desk)
Goal: book a 15-minute call. Do not “pitch features” first—confirm pain + offer quick audit.

Opening (10 seconds)
“Hi, is this the front desk? My name is Bob. Quick question—who handles appointment confirmations and no-shows there, is that you or the office manager?”

If they say “me” or transfer to office manager:
“Got it. I’ll be brief. We help dental practices cut no-shows by using two-way SMS confirmations—patients tap confirm or reschedule—and we can also fill last-minute gaps from a small waitlist. I can send you a link that shows what it is (it’s here: https://6ff73b98-dcff-4f53-a10d-9ad392f63342-00-1n4h4nuiq6w8j.picard.replit.dev/sites/2). Can I ask: are no-shows and late cancels a noticeable problem for you in hygiene or doctor columns?”

If YES:
“Totally understood. Roughly how many no-shows/late cancels do you see in a typical week—more like 2–3, 5–10, or 10+?”

Then:
“Thanks—based on what you said, it usually takes 48 hours to set up a pilot. Would you be open to a 15-minute call so I can show how confirmations/reschedules work and what we’d need from you? What’s better—today after lunch or tomorrow morning?”

If NO / “we already text”:
“That makes sense. Most offices do reminders. The difference here is two-way confirmation + auto-reschedule link + tracking recovered revenue so you can see if it actually reduced no-shows. If I sent the overview and we did a quick 10-minute check to see if it’s redundant, would that be a bad use of time?”

If they object “we’re too busy”:
“I hear you. That’s why we keep it light—no new software for your staff to learn in week one. If you give me one export or even a daily schedule screenshot for the pilot, we can run the confirmation flow and you just see the results. Can we pencil a 15-minute call—worst case you’ll know it’s not worth it?”

If asked for email:
“Sure—what’s the best email for you? I’ll send it from agent_bob_replit+no-show-bot@agentmail.to with the link and 2-minute overview.”

Close:
“Perfect. I’ll send that now. What day/time is best for a quick 15-minute walkthrough?”

5) Objection Handling (quick responses)
- “We already have reminders.”
  “Totally. The win is two-way confirmations + reschedules, not one-way reminders. Offices usually see fewer ‘silent no-shows’ because the patient has to actively confirm or reschedule.”
- “Patients hate texts.”
  “We keep it minimal and compliant—only appointment-related texts, with opt-out. The message is short and looks like it’s from your office.”
- “We don’t have time to implement.”
  “Pilot is designed for low lift: we set up templates + routing, then you review and approve. We can start with one provider or hygiene only.”
