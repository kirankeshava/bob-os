E) Cold Call Script (Office Manager / Front Desk)
Objective: book a 15-min demo; do NOT pitch features endlessly.

Opener (10 seconds):
“Hi — is this the front desk? Quick question: who handles appointment confirmations and last-minute reschedules for the schedule — is that you or the office manager?”

If they say ‘me’ / transfer to office manager:
“Got it. I’m Bob. We help dental practices cut no-shows and late cancels using two-way text confirmations and an automated waitlist fill—so the hygiene schedule stays full. I can send details from our site here: https://6ff73b98-dcff-4f53-a10d-9ad392f63342-00-1n4h4nuiq6w8j.picard.replit.dev/sites/2 and email agent_bob_replit+no-show-bot@agentmail.to. 
Can I ask: on an average week, do you have more of an issue with no-shows, or with late cancellations?”

Qualifying questions (pick 2–3):
1) “Roughly how many appointments do you have per day across hygiene and doctor columns?”
2) “Do you currently text patients—do they ever reply to confirm, or is it one-way reminders only?”
3) “When someone cancels same-day, do you have a waitlist process—or does someone manually call down a list?”
4) “If you could recover even 2–3 appointments per week, would that be meaningful?”

Micro-pitch (20 seconds):
“The reason I’m calling is we set up two-way texting so patients explicitly confirm or reschedule. If they can’t make it, we automatically offer the slot to your waitlist. You get a simple report on recovered appointments and estimated revenue—per location. It’s designed to reduce the ‘empty chair’ problem without adding work for your team.”

CTA (close for time):
“Worth a quick look? I can do a 15-minute screenshare. What’s better—tomorrow morning or Thursday afternoon?”

If they ask price immediately:
“It’s $399 per month per location plus SMS pass-through, and there’s a one-time concierge setup of $399 to get it live quickly. Most offices justify it by recovering just one to two hygiene or doctor appointments per month. If it’s helpful, I can walk you through the exact workflow in 15 minutes.”

Objection handling (tight):
1) ‘We already have reminders.’
“Totally—most do. The difference is two-way confirmation + reschedule + waitlist fill. One-way reminders still leave you guessing until it’s too late. Are patients able to reply and actively confirm today?”

2) ‘Send info.’
“Absolutely. What’s the best email? I’ll send the link and a 3-bullet summary. Also—so I send the right thing—are you more concerned about hygiene openings or doctor chair time?”
(Then: “If it looks relevant, should we pencil a 15-min time now so it doesn’t get lost?”)

3) ‘Too busy.’
“Understood. That’s exactly why this helps—less manual calling, fewer surprises. I can do 15 minutes and keep it strictly to workflow + ROI. Is early morning before patients, or lunch, better?”

4) ‘We don’t want to change our process.’
“We’re not asking you to rebuild anything. The goal is to reduce work: confirmations happen by text, reschedules happen through a link, and waitlist offers go out automatically. Your team mainly just sees the outcomes.”

5) ‘Need owner approval.’
“Makes sense. Usually office managers evaluate the workflow first, then owners approve. If we do a 15-minute demo, would you want the owner on it—or should we do a quick first pass and then bring them in if it’s a fit?”
