D) COLD CALL SCRIPT (OFFICE MANAGER FIRST)
Goal: book a 15-minute demo, not to sell on the call.

Opener:
“Hi — is this the front desk? Quick one: who handles your appointment confirmations and last-minute cancellations — you or the office manager?”

If they are the office manager:
“Got it. I’ll be brief. I’m Bob — we help dental practices cut no-shows and same-day gaps using two-way SMS confirmations and an easy reschedule link, plus a waitlist to fill holes. Here’s the quick proof page: https://6ff73b98-dcff-4f53-a10d-9ad392f63342-00-1n4h4nuiq6w8j.picard.replit.dev/sites/2.
Do you mind if I ask: are no-shows/cancels enough of an issue that you’re spending time calling or trying to fill openings?”

Qualifying questions (pick 2–3):
1) “Do you confirm by phone, text, or both today?”
2) “When someone cancels within 24 hours, do you have a waitlist you can text quickly?”
3) “Is hygiene the hardest schedule to keep full, or doctor time?”

Micro-pitch (10 seconds):
“What we do is automate the reminders, collect YES/NO confirmations, let patients reschedule themselves if they can’t make it, and then we broadcast the open slot to a waitlist. You get a simple report showing appointments saved and estimated revenue recovered.”

Close for time:
“Worth a quick 15 minutes to see if it fits? I can do Tue 11:30 or Wed 2:00. If it’s easier, email works too — agent_bob_replit+no-show-bot@agentmail.to.”

If they say ‘We already use reminders’:
“Totally—most offices do. The difference is two-way confirmation + automated reschedule + waitlist fill. Reminders alone still leave you with ‘ghosts’ and holes. Are you currently capturing YES/NO replies and automatically offering the slot to a waitlist?”

If they say ‘Not interested / too busy’:
“No worries. Last question so I don’t bother you again: when you get a same-day cancellation, do you usually fill it—or does it typically stay open?”
If “stays open sometimes”:
“Understood. That’s exactly what this solves. I’ll send a 3-sentence summary with the proof page; if it’s not relevant, just reply ‘no’ and I’ll close the loop. What’s the best email?”

If they ask price:
“For a single location it’s typically $399/month/location plus SMS pass-through, and we can do concierge setup. But I only want to quote after I see your workflow—15 minutes is enough to confirm fit.”

If they ask for details by email:
“Absolutely. I’ll send it from agent_bob_replit+no-show-bot@agentmail.to with the proof link. What email should I use?”

E) OBJECTION HANDLING (QUICK HITS)
1) ‘Patients hate texts’ → “We keep it simple and compliant; patients can opt out. In practice, two-way confirmations reduce back-and-forth calls and patients like the reschedule link.”
