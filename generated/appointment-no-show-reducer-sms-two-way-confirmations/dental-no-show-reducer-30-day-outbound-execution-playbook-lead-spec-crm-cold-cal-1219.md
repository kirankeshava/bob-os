5) Objection Handling (short, non-pushy)
Objection: “We already have reminders through our software.”
Response: “Makes sense—many PMS reminders are one-way. The difference here is two-way confirmation (Y/N), automatic reschedule link, and a waitlist fill workflow. Even if you keep your current reminders, this layer is built to reduce holes and save staff time. Worth a 15-minute look?”

Objection: “Patients get too many texts.”
Response: “We keep it minimal and configurable. Typically 2 reminders plus a confirmation request. The goal is fewer calls for your staff, not more spam for patients.”

Objection: “We don’t have a waitlist.”
Response: “A waitlist can be as simple as patients who want sooner appointments or ‘call if something opens.’ We can start lightweight—just capture a handful per week and it becomes a reliable backfill tool.”

Objection: “Send info.”
Response: “Happy to. What’s the best email? I’ll send the details with the link and a couple example workflows. Also—so I send the right thing—are no-shows currently a noticeable issue, or is it more last-minute cancellations?”
(Then always ask for a time): “If you like what you see, should we pencil a quick 15-minute review?”

Objection: “No budget right now.”
Response: “Understood. Most offices justify it by recovering even one appointment. We also do a low-risk start: cancel anytime / 14-day risk-free. If you want, we can review in 15 minutes and you can decide if it’s worth testing.”
