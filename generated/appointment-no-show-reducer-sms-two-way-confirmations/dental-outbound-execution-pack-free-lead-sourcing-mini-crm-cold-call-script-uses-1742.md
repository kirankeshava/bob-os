## 1) Free Lead List Build Spec (Dental)

**Goal (Week 1):** build a list of **200 dental locations** (single location + small groups) with an owner/office manager contact path, then run **5 days of outreach** to book 15-min demos.

**Where to find leads (free):**
1) **Google Maps**: search terms: “dentist”, “family dentistry”, “cosmetic dentist”, “dental clinic” in chosen metros.
2) **Practice websites**: contact page often lists office manager/front desk email or a general inbox.
3) **Facebook pages**: often list phone + sometimes email.
4) **State dental association directories** (when public): use for verification.

**Pick 2–3 metros** where you can call during business hours. Start with cities with dense practices (e.g., Phoenix, Dallas, Tampa). 

**Inclusion filters (ICP fit):**
- 1–5 locations (or a small DSO where a regional ops person exists)
- Appointment-based, high hygiene volume
- Mentions “new patients welcome”, “same-day crowns”, “hygiene openings” (signals capacity matters)

**Disqualifiers (don’t prioritize):**
- Hospital/academic clinics
- Practices with only specialty procedures and very low appointment volume
- No phone number listed / unreachable

**Target roles/titles (for messaging):**
- Office Manager, Practice Manager
- Owner Dentist / Managing Partner
- Operations Manager (small DSO)
- Front Desk Lead / Scheduling Coordinator

**Data to capture for each lead (minimum):**
- Practice name, website, location address
- Main phone
- Contact email (office manager if possible; otherwise info@)
- Contact name + role (if found)
- Notes: number of Google reviews (proxy for size), hours, any “online booking” system

**5-Day cadence (per lead):**
- Day 1: Cold email #1 + LinkedIn DM (if found)
- Day 2: Phone call #1 (front desk) + voicemail

- Day 4: Phone call #2 + LinkedIn follow-up

## 3) Cold Call Script (Dental Office Manager / Front Desk)

**Purpose:** book a **15-minute demo**. Do not pitch everything—qualify and schedule.

### Opener (10 seconds)
“Hi — is this the front desk? This is Bob. Quick question: who handles your appointment confirmations and last-minute schedule gaps?”

If they say “that’s me” or transfer to office manager:

### Permission + one-line value (15 seconds)
“Got it. I’ll be brief— I help dental practices cut no-shows using **two-way SMS confirmations** and **auto-reschedules**, and if there’s a cancellation it can **text a waitlist** to fill the chair. Here’s our info if you want to verify: 
**https://6ff73b98-dcff-4f53-a10d-9ad392f63342-00-1n4h4nuiq6w8j.picard.replit.dev/sites/2** and **agent_bob_replit+no-show-bot@agentmail.to**.”

### Qualifying questions (60 seconds)
1) “Roughly how many patients do you have on the schedule per day?”
2) “Do you currently do confirmations by phone, text, or both?”
3) “About how many no-shows or same-day cancels do you see in a typical week?”
4) “When you get a cancellation, do you have a repeatable way to pull someone forward fast?”

### Bridge to meeting (15 seconds)
“Based on what you said, it sounds like even a small reduction would pay for itself quickly. The next step is a quick 15-minute walkthrough—if it’s not a fit, I’ll tell you in the first five minutes. Would **tomorrow at 11** or **2:30** work?”

## Objection Handling (keep it short)

**Objection: “We already text patients.”**
“Totally—most do. The difference is **two-way confirmation** (patients reply C/R), auto-reschedule link when they cancel, and a **waitlist blast** to fill openings. If you’re open to it, I can show you the flow in 15 minutes and you can compare it to what you have.”

**Objection: “Send info.”**
“Sure—what email should I send it to? I’ll include the proof page and a 2-minute overview. Also, should we pencil a quick 15-minute slot so it doesn’t get lost—tomorrow 11 or 2:30?”

**Objection: “We’re too busy / call back later.”**
“I get it. What’s a better time window—early morning or after lunch? I only need 15 minutes, and it’s specifically to reduce the calls and chaos you’re already dealing with.”

**Objection: “How much is it?”**
“It’s **$399/month per location** plus SMS pass-through, and a one-time setup depending on complexity. But it only makes sense if we can clearly recover more than that in saved chair time—happy to run the numbers with you on a 15-minute call.”

**Objection: “We use [PMS/vendor].”**
“Good—this usually works alongside your current system. The key is the confirmation/reschedule/waitlist workflow. On the demo I’ll show exactly how we’d run it for your practice.”

**Objection: “Owner decides.”**
“Perfect—what’s the best way to loop them in? If you want, we can do a 15-minute joint call. What times does the owner typically have available?”
