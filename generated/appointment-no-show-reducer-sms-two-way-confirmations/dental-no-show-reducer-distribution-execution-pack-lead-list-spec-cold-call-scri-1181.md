3) Objection Handling (Dental-specific)
Use: acknowledge → clarify → quantify → low-risk next step.

1) “We already have reminders in our PMS.”
“Totally. Most PMS reminders are one-way. The difference is two-way confirmations and guided rescheduling so you know who’s actually coming and you can recover slots. Are your reminders currently collecting a YES/NO and automatically prompting a reschedule?”

2) “Patients ignore texts.”
“Some do, but even partial confirmation lifts show rate. The real win is surfacing ‘no’ early enough to refill. If you got even 1 extra kept appointment per week, would this pay for itself?”

3) “We don’t want more work for the front desk.”
“Agreed — it should reduce work. Confirmations/reschedules are automated; you only handle exceptions. We also keep it simple: one reschedule link and a small waitlist list you can turn on/off.”

4) “We’re too busy to implement.”
“We designed it for week-1 launch. If you can export next week’s appointments (or share the basic schedule data), we can start texting within days. The demo is 15 minutes and you’ll know immediately if it’s worth it.”

5) “We can’t risk annoying patients.”
“Fair. Messaging is opt-out compliant and we keep it minimal (e.g., 2–3 touches max). You control wording and timing. The goal is fewer calls and fewer empty chairs.”

6) “What does it cost?”
“It’s $399/month per location plus SMS pass-through, and a one-time concierge setup (limited-time) to get you live quickly. If you want, I can send the one-page overview: https://6ff73b98-dcff-4f53-a10d-9ad392f63342-00-1n4h4nuiq6w8j-dbyyfbxp.picard.replit.dev/sites/2 — or we can do a quick 15-minute walkthrough and see if the ROI is obvious.”

7) “We don’t have a waitlist.”
“Many offices don’t have a formal one. We can start with a simple ‘text me if you want an earlier spot’ list. Even 10–30 patients is enough to fill some last-minute hygiene gaps.”

8) “We charge a no-show fee already.”
“That helps behavior, but it doesn’t fill the chair. We focus on recovered production: preventing the no-show and refilling openings when someone cancels.”

9) “Send me info.”
“Absolutely. What’s the best email? I’ll send the one-pager and a 2-minute overview. Also—if it looks relevant, should we pencil a 15-minute slot so it doesn’t get lost?”

“Small offices feel no-shows even more because each gap is a bigger % of the day. If you’re running hygiene + a doctor column, saving even 1 appointment/week usually covers it.”

11) “HIPAA concerns.”
