A) ICP + Targeting (who to list)
Primary ICP: single-location dental practices and small groups (2–10 locations) doing hygiene + restorative with a staffed front desk.
Geography: pick 2–3 metro areas where you can call during business hours (e.g., Phoenix, Dallas, Tampa).
Titles to reach:
- Owner/Dentist (often “DDS/Owner”)
- Office Manager / Practice Manager
- Front Office Lead (backup)
Qualification indicators (prefer): online booking or visible appointment request form, multiple hygienists, open 4–5 days/week, reviews mention “hard to get appointment,” “wait,” or “busy.”
Disqualify (initially): hospitals, university clinics, very low-volume boutique specialty-only (unless they run a large recall base).

B) Zero-Cost Lead List Build Spec (how to get 200 leads)
Free sources (in order):
1) Google Maps search (manual): “dentist”, “family dentistry”, “dental clinic” + city.
2) Yelp (manual): dentists category + city.
3) Practice websites: contact pages for office manager email forms, phone, and address.
4) LinkedIn search (free): practice name → employees → Office Manager/Practice Manager.

Data fields to capture (Google Sheet columns):
1. Practice Name
2. Location (City, State)
3. Address
4. Website URL
5. Main Phone
6. Decision-maker name (Owner or Office Manager)
7. Role (Owner / Office Mgr / Front Desk)
8. Email (if found)
9. LinkedIn URL (person)
10. Notes (hours, #providers, hygiene focus, etc.)
11. Outreach status (Not Contacted / Emailed 1 / Emailed 2 / Called / DM Sent / Replied / Demo Booked / Closed / Nurture)
12. Last touch date
13. Next follow-up date

“Totally—most practices do. The difference is two-way confirmations + automated reschedule + waitlist fill. Reminders alone don’t reliably turn ‘maybe’ into a confirmed appointment or refill the gap when someone cancels.”

2) “Patients hate texts.”
“We keep it minimal and opt-out friendly. The goal is fewer messages, higher certainty. Two-way confirmations typically reduce back-and-forth calls for your front desk.”

“We do concierge setup. Week 1 is lightweight: confirm your messaging rules, your reschedule link/workflow, and how you want the waitlist handled. Then we run it.”
