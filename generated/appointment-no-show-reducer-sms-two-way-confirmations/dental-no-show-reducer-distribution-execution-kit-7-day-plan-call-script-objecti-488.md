C) Objection Handling (tight, dental-specific)
1) “We already have reminders.”
Reply: “Totally—most do. The difference is two-way confirmations + automated reschedule + waitlist fill. One-way reminders still leave you chasing ‘no’ and empty slots. Do patients currently reply YES/NO by text and get an instant reschedule option?”

2) “We don’t want to annoy patients with texts.”
Reply: “Agreed. We keep it minimal and configurable—typically 2–3 touches max, and only message the waitlist when there’s a real opening. The goal is fewer phone calls for your team and fewer missed visits for patients.”

3) “No budget right now.”
Reply: “Understood. The way practices justify it is by recovered appointments. If we recover even 2 hygiene visits a month, it’s usually paid for. If it doesn’t measurably help, we can structure it risk-free (14 days or cancel anytime). Would it be crazy to take 12 minutes just to see the workflow?”

4) “We’re too busy to implement.”
Reply: “That’s exactly why we do concierge setup. We handle the reminder/confirmation templates and launch plan; you just tell us your confirmation rules and provide a basic schedule export. Usually it’s live in under a week.”

5) “Send me info.”
Reply: “Absolutely—what’s the best email? I’ll send the overview and a 2-minute read. Also, it’s easier if I include a couple of questions so it’s relevant—are you mainly trying to reduce no-shows, or fill last-minute cancellations?”
