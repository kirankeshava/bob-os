Who it’s for
Built for single-location dental practices and small DSOs that:
- Have 10+ appointments/day
- Lose chair time to no‑shows and same‑day cancellations
- Want a simple system the front desk can run without extra workload

What it does (the wedge offer)
1) Two‑way SMS confirmations
- Patients receive a reminder and can confirm by replying (ex: “C”) or request changes
- Staff can see confirmation status and exceptions

2) Smart reminder sequence
- Multi-touch reminders leading up to the appointment (timing tuned per practice)
- Escalation for unconfirmed appointments

3) Reschedule link + automation
- If a patient can’t make it, they get a reschedule option (instead of ghosting)
- Captures the intent to reschedule and reduces dead chair time

4) Waitlist fill for gaps
- When cancellations happen, send a broadcast to your waitlist for that time window
- First to confirm gets the slot; everyone else gets a polite closeout message

5) Recovered-revenue analytics (simple, per location)
- Track confirmations, saves (reschedules), and filled gaps
- Estimate recovered revenue based on your average production per hour/visit (you provide baseline)

ONBOARDING + LAUNCH CHECKLIST (Week 1)

Goal: launch confirmations + reschedules + waitlist fill within 3–5 business days.

1) Access & data (choose the easiest path)
A) Daily appointment export (preferred):
- Daily schedule CSV export from PMS (columns: patient first name, mobile, appt date/time, provider, appt type/notes if non-sensitive)
- Send via secure method or designated upload

B) Manual upload fallback:
- Front desk uploads a CSV once/day (2–5 minutes)

2) Messaging rules (15 minutes)
- Reminder timing (ex: 3 days + 1 day + 2 hours)
- Confirmation keyword(s): “C” confirm, “R” reschedule
- Office hours + exception handling rules (what happens after-hours)
- Brand voice + practice name in messages

3) Reschedule flow
- Define reschedule method: phone callback vs online link
- If online link exists (booking tool), provide URL and rules
- If phone callback: capture preferred callback windows

4) Waitlist setup
- Provide waitlist list (name + mobile + preference if available)
- Define “blast rules” (how many patients to message per opening; time windows)
- Define priority rules (first-come-first-serve vs staff approval)

5) Success metrics baseline (needed for recovered revenue)
- Approx no-show rate (even rough)
- Average production per appointment OR per hour
- Typical same-day cancellation rate

6) Staff handoff (30 minutes)
- Who monitors exceptions (name + email)
- What counts as “confirmed” and “needs follow-up”
- Escalation path for urgent issues

7) Go-live
- Send test messages to 2–3 staff phones
- Turn on for one provider or one day segment first (optional)
- Full rollout within 48 hours after validation
