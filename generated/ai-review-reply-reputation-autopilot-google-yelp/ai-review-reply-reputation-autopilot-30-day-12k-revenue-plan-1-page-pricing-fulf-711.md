(4) FULFILLMENT SCOPE (WEEK 1 DELIVERABLE) + SOP OUTLINE
Review sources supported week 1:
• Google Business Profile reviews
• Yelp reviews

Posting method (no API dependency; deliverable immediately):
• Default: client adds us as manager/collaborator for posting access.
  - Google: request “Manager” access to GBP.
  - Yelp: request Business Owner/Account Manager access where possible.
• Fallback (always available): “Client-post” workflow — we draft responses; client posts using copy/paste (templates provided). This removes API/access blockers and preserves deliverability.

Response SLA:
• Draft + QA within 24 business hours on Growth; within 12 business hours on Pro.

Escalation rules (brand-safety + risk control):
Auto-approve (we can post without asking) IF:
• 4–5★ review AND no red-flag keywords AND no refund/medical/legal claims.
Approval required BEFORE posting IF:
• 1–3★ review OR mentions: refund, lawsuit, injury, HIPAA/medical details, discrimination, harassment, employee names, threats, chargeback, insurance, “scam/fraud”, or any request for private info.
Escalation ticket created IF:
• 1–2★ review; OR reviewer alleges a safety issue; OR review is from a known customer incident.
Escalation SLA:
• Escalation alert sent to owner within 4 business hours (email) with a recommended response draft + next-step checklist.

Daily workflow (operator checklist):
1) Intake & setup (Day 0–2):
   - Collect brand voice, do/don’t language, offer constraints, escalation contacts, business hours, key services, competitor set.
   - Confirm posting method (manager access vs client-post).
   - Turn on review notifications (Google/Yelp alerts) to shared inbox.
2) Review capture (daily):
   - Check notifications (or manual check) 1–2×/day.
   - Log new reviews in tracker (date, platform, rating, theme, status).
3) Draft responses (daily):
   - Generate 1–2 response options; ensure policy compliance and tone.
   - Apply “no incentives/no gating/no private data” constraints.
4) QA & approval:
   - Auto-post if eligible; else send approval email to client with 1-click “approve/edit” instructions (or client-post copy).
5) Post & confirm:
   - Post response (or confirm client posted). Mark status “posted”.
6) Escalations:
   - For negative reviews, open ticket with recommended remediation steps; track resolution outcome.
7) Weekly KPI report (every Monday):
   - New reviews (#), average rating (current + 4-week trend), response rate %, median response time, negative review count, escalations opened/closed, top 3 themes, suggested ops improvements.

Explicit constraints (to keep deliverable and compliant):
• We do NOT do review gating, incentives, or “pay for reviews”.
• We do NOT guarantee removals; we can provide a dispute draft, but owner submits.
• We avoid collecting sensitive personal data; for medical/legal, responses direct to offline contact.
