4) 15-MINUTE SALES CALL SCRIPT (PILOT → ONBOARD)
========================

Goal: book pilot, confirm scope, request access.

1) Set agenda (30 sec)
“Thanks, [NAME]. In 15 minutes I’ll (1) confirm your review volume + locations, (2) show how we respond safely and escalate negatives, (3) outline pilot options and what access we need. Sound good?”

2) Diagnose (3–4 min)
• “How many locations?”
• “Google only, Yelp too?”
• “Avg reviews/week?”
• “Who replies today? How fast?”
• “Any brand voice rules? Things you never want said publicly?”

3) Problem cost (1 min)
“When reviews sit unanswered—especially 1–3 star—prospects assume no one’s listening. Fast, consistent responses usually improves trust and conversion.”

4) Solution (3–4 min)
“We do daily drafting (or posting if you prefer). Negative reviews trigger escalation per your SLA. You get a weekly KPI email (response time, sentiment, rating trend, top issues).”

5) Offer (2 min)
“Best next step is a short pilot. We set your tone, start replying daily, and establish the escalation rules. If it’s not obviously saving you time and improving responsiveness, we stop.”

6) Close + access (2–3 min)
“To start, we’ll need Google Business Profile manager access (or draft-only approvals), and Yelp access if included. I’ll send an onboarding checklist + brand voice questions. Who should be the escalation contact for 1–2 star reviews?”
