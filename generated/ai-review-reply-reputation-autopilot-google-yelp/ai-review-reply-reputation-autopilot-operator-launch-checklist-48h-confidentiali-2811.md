## Day 0 (Immediately after verbal yes / payment)
1) **Send Kickoff Email (Day 0)**
   - Attach/Include: Intake Form + Access Request instructions.
   - Confirm: start date (today), first draft approval window (24 hours), and escalation contact.
2) **Customer completes Intake Form (same day if possible)**
   - Required fields: brand voice, escalation rules, locations, review platforms, contact list.
3) **Access Request (GBP + Yelp)**
   - Customer forwards the pre-written access request email to whoever controls Google Business Profile and Yelp.
   - If an agency manages listings, ask them to add our email with the minimum required permissions.
4) **Set “Approval Mode” for first week**
   - Default: we draft responses; customer approves for first 7 days (or first 20 replies) before posting.

## Day 1 (Within 24 hours)
5) **Pull last 60–90 days of reviews (GBP + Yelp) once access is granted**
   - Build a baseline snapshot: average rating, # reviews, negative-review topics.
6) **Create 5 sample drafts for approval**
   - 2 positive, 2 neutral, 1 negative (de-escalation + solution).
   - Send to customer for quick “Yes/No + edits” to calibrate voice.
7) **Confirm escalation workflow**
   - Define what triggers escalation: 1–2 stars, legal threats, refunds, safety incidents, discrimination claims.
   - Confirm who gets notified (name + phone + email) and expected response SLA.

## Day 2 (Within 48 hours)
8) **Go live**
   - Posting rules:
     - Positive/neutral: auto-post after first-week calibration (or keep approval mode if requested).
     - Negative: draft + escalate; post after customer approval unless pre-authorized.
9) **Set weekly KPI reporting cadence**
   - Send “Week 1 success criteria” email and confirm the weekly report day/time.

## Week 1 Success Criteria (send in kickoff + reiterate Day 7)
- 90%+ of new reviews get a response within 24 hours (or agreed SLA).
- Zero off-brand or risky replies (brand-safe tone; no admissions of fault; no private info).
- All 1–2 star reviews escalated within 1 business hour of receipt.
- Weekly report delivered (rating trend, response time, volume, negative themes, resolved escalations).
