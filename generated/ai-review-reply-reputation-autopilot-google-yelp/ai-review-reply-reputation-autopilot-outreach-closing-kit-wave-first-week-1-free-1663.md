## 4) 20-Min Discovery Call Script (revenue-impact + objections)

### Agenda (set frame)
“Goal is simple: understand how reviews affect revenue for you, see how you handle replies today, and if it makes sense I’ll recommend a plan. Sound good?”

### Questions (8–10 minutes)
1) “Where do new customers usually come from—calls, online booking, walk-ins?”
2) “How often do you check Google reviews? Yelp?”
3) “Who replies today? How long does it usually take?”
4) “What happens when a negative review comes in?”
5) “Any topics we should never mention? (pricing, health claims, refunds, legal issues, staff names)?”
6) “Do you have multiple locations?”

### ROI prompts (2 minutes)
- “If your rating moved from {{x}} to {{x+0.2}}, would that impact calls/bookings?”
- “If we replied to 100% of reviews within 24 hours, would that help conversions/trust?”

### Show safety (2 minutes)
“We use a brand-voice profile + prohibited topics list. Anything negative or sensitive triggers escalation. You can choose approval mode: (1) we draft-only, (2) we post with your pre-approval, or (3) autopost with audit log.”

### Objection handling
**‘We can do it ourselves.’**
“Totally—most can. The issue is consistency and speed. This is a system that ensures every review gets a quality reply and negatives escalate immediately, without taking staff time.”

**‘AI risk / wrong tone.’**
“Valid concern. That’s why we start with draft-only or pre-approval for week 1, calibrate voice, and use escalation triggers. Nothing gets posted outside your policy.”

**‘Access concerns.’**
“We can run draft-only without access. If you want posting handled, you add us as a manager on Google Business Profile and as an agent/authorized user where possible on Yelp.”

### Close (last 3 minutes)
“Based on what you shared, I recommend we start the free week today in draft-only mode, calibrate the tone, then switch to posting once you’re comfortable. If you’re good with that, I’ll send a 1-page order form and an invoice for setup + month 1 upfront (month 1 starts after the free week). What email should I send it to?”
