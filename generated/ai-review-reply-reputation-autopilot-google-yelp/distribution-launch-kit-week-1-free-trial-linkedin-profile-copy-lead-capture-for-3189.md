## 3) 30/Day Outreach Cadence (No Spend)

### Daily Target
- 30 new outbound touches/day (mix of email + LinkedIn + phone)
- 5 follow-ups/day (Day-2 and Day-5)
- Goal: 2 discovery calls/day → 3 pilots/week → 1+ multi-location pilot

### Channel Mix (simple)
1) **Email (15/day):** Owners/Managers (use existing email sequence you already have)
2) **LinkedIn (10/day):** Connect + short note → Day-2 DM → Day-5 DM
3) **Phone (5/day):** Quick pitch + ask for best email to send pilot details

### Lead Sourcing (free methods)
- Google Maps search: “med spa + city”, “dentist + city”, “HVAC + city”, “personal injury lawyer + city”
- Yelp categories: same niches; capture businesses with 3.5–4.6 rating and recent reviews (signals they care)
- Prioritize: (a) multiple locations, (b) 10+ reviews/month, (c) visible unresponded reviews

### Qualification Quick Check (60 seconds per lead)
- Do they have recent reviews? (last 7–14 days)
- Are there unresponded reviews?
- Any <3-star in last 30 days?
- Multi-location? (more ops pain)

### Tracking Schema (use a simple sheet)
Columns:
- Date added
- Business name
- Niche
- City
- Locations (#)
- Website
- Contact name (if found)
- Email
- Phone
- LinkedIn URL
- Last review date
- Rating
- Unresponded reviews? (Y/N)
- Touch 1 date/channel
- Touch 2 date/channel
- Status: New / Contacted / Replied / Call booked / Pilot started / Not fit
- Notes

### Daily Workflow (45–90 minutes)
1) Build 15 leads (Google/Yelp)
2) Send 15 emails
3) Send 10 LinkedIn connection notes
4) Make 5 calls
5) Log everything
6) End-of-day: send 5 follow-ups

### Talk Track (for calls; 20 seconds)
“Hi, this is Bob. Quick one—do you handle your Google/Yelp review replies in-house? I run a free 7-day ‘review reply autopilot’ pilot: you forward your review notifications, we draft brand-safe replies fast, and you approve in one click. It helps prevent negative reviews from sitting unanswered. What’s the best email to send details?”
