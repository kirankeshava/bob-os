2 MINI CASE STUDIES (ANONYMIZED) — READY FOR LANDING PAGE / PROPOSALS

Case Study #1 — Dental Clinic (1 location)
Problem
The practice was getting steady 5★ reviews but responded inconsistently—sometimes weeks late. A couple of 1–2★ reviews sat unanswered, creating a “they don’t care” impression.

What we did
• Onboarded brand voice: friendly, professional, brief
• Set escalation threshold at ≤3★ with approval required
• Implemented 1-business-day SLA for all reviews
• Created a negative-review playbook: acknowledge → move offline → promise follow-up → no PHI

Results (first 30 days)
• Response time: reduced from “days–weeks” to < 24 hours (business days)
• 100% of new reviews received a reply within SLA
• Negative reviews: 3 escalations handled with calm, offline resolution language
• Outcome narrative: staff reported fewer tense front-desk calls because the public reply set expectations and offered a clear next step
Proof placeholders: screenshot of response timestamps; weekly KPI email PDF.

Case Study #2 — Home Services (HVAC/Plumbing style, 3 locations)
Problem
Multiple locations meant inconsistent messaging. Some replies were overly defensive, and dispatch managers didn’t have time to respond daily. The owner wanted a system that prevented public arguments.

What we did
• Built per-location signatures + unified brand voice
• Set Priority SLA (4 business hours Mon–Fri)
• Added red-flag rules (refund demands, safety complaints) → immediate escalation
• Weekly KPI report: review volume by location + recurring complaint themes

Results (first 6 weeks)
• Response consistency: standardized tone across 3 locations
• De-escalation: 5 red-flag reviews escalated with recommended offline scripts
• Rating lift narrative: improved “responsiveness signal” contributed to more confident booking (reported by call center: fewer ‘are they legit?’ objections)
