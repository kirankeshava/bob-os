# 7) One-Call Closing Script (12–15 minutes)

## Agenda (say this upfront)
“Want to use 12 minutes to confirm review volume, your preferred tone, escalation rules, and then I’ll recommend the right tier/pilot? Sound good?”

## Questions (keep tight)
1) “Which matters more right now: saving time or boosting conversions from your listing?”
2) “Roughly how many new reviews do you get per week on Google? Any Yelp volume?”
3) “Do you want draft-only or draft+post? Any compliance sensitivities?”
4) “For 1–2 stars: who approves and how fast should we escalate?”
5) “Any phrases you never want used (discounts, medical claims, guarantees)?”

## Close
“Based on that, I recommend starting with a 7-day pilot. If the replies match your voice and we improve coverage + speed, we roll into monthly. Want to start draft-only or draft+post?”

## Common objections
- “We already reply sometimes.”
Response: “Totally—consistency is the win. The businesses that see lift are the ones replying daily, especially to 3–4 star reviews. We make it automatic and brand-safe.”

- “I don’t want robotic AI replies.”
Response: “Agreed. We use AI to draft, but the output is constrained by your brand voice, banned phrases, and examples. Plus negatives are escalated before posting. You’ll see quality in the pilot.”

- “I don’t want to give access.”
Response: “No problem—start draft-only. You keep control; we still deliver the time savings on writing and the KPI reporting.”

- “What about Yelp rules?”
Response: “We avoid any incentivizing or gating language and keep responses policy-safe. If you prefer, we can do Yelp as draft-only initially.”
