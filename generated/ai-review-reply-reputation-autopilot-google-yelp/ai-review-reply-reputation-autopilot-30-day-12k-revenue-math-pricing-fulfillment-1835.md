5) FULFILLMENT SCOPE (Week-1 deliverable, no engineering)
Review sources
• Google Business Profile (GBP)
• Yelp

Posting method (no API dependency)
Default (recommended for Week 1): Human-in-the-loop
• If client grants access:
  - GBP: request Manager access to the business profile.
  - Yelp: request business owner access.
  - We post responses directly.
• If access is delayed/blocked (common on Yelp): client-post workflow
  - We draft responses and send “Ready to Post” daily digest.
  - Client pastes responses into GBP/Yelp.

Response SLA
• Growth: within 24 business hours of detection
• Pro: within 12 business hours of detection

Approval rules (brand safety)
Auto-approve posting (or “OK to post”) for:
• 4–5★ reviews that do not mention regulated topics, legal threats, personal data, or medical claims.
Require approval before posting for:
• 1–3★ reviews
• Any mention of: refund requests, injury, discrimination, legal action, HIPAA/medical specifics, doxxing/personal info
• Any request for compensation, threats, or extortion

Escalation rules
• Create an escalation ticket within SLA when flagged.
• Escalation ticket includes: review text, severity, suggested response, private resolution steps, and who should contact customer.
• Track: status (New / Contacted / Resolved), outcome notes.

Weekly KPI report (one page)
• New reviews (Google, Yelp)
• Average rating (current + 7-day delta)
• Response rate (%)
• Median response time
• Negative reviews count (1–2★)
• Escalations opened vs resolved
• Top 3 themes (e.g., wait time, pricing, staff)

Explicit constraints / compliance
• No incentivizing reviews (discounts, gifts for reviews)
• No review gating (“only ask happy customers”)
• No removal promises; we can suggest dispute steps but not impersonate or violate platform policies
• We do not collect customer sensitive data; we redact personal info in internal docs
