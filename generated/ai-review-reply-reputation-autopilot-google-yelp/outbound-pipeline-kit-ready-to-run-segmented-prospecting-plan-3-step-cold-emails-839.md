## 1) Segmented Prospecting Plan (what to target first)

### Vertical focus (first 30 days)
1) Dentists (high LTV, steady review flow)
2) Med spas / aesthetic clinics (very review-sensitive, high competition)
3) HVAC/Plumbing (high urgency, high review velocity, owner-operated)
Parallel lane: Local marketing agencies that manage GBP/Yelp for these verticals.

### Segments + Priority
We segment by visible signals on Google Business Profile (GBP). Use Yelp as optional secondary proof.

**Segment definitions (operational):**
- **Not responding:** In the last 10 reviews, owner/manager responses <= 2 (<=20%).
- **Low rating:** Google rating < 4.2.
- **High volume:** Review count >= 200 OR last review date within 14 days.

**Priority scoring (who gets emailed first):**
- **Priority A:** (Not responding AND High volume) OR (Low rating AND High volume). These feel pain now and have lots of “surface area.”
- **Priority B:** Not responding OR Low rating (but not high volume).
- **Priority C:** High volume only (rating is fine; pitch as time-saver + consistency).

### Message angle by segment (choose one per prospect)
- **Not responding:** “You’re getting reviews, but responses are inconsistent/missing. We reply within 12 hours, brand-safe, you approve.”
- **Low rating:** “Negative reviews need fast, careful replies + escalation. We draft empathetic responses and flag urgent issues.”
- **High volume:** “You’re getting a lot of reviews—replying is operationally hard. We keep responses consistent and on-brand.”

### Offer (low-friction)
- **Core offer:** “We draft (and can post) brand-safe responses to Google + Yelp reviews, within 12 hours. You can approve before anything goes live. Negative reviews are escalated. Weekly KPI email.”
- **CTA options:**
  1) “Want me to draft replies to your last 3 reviews so you can see the quality?”
  2) “Open to a 10-minute call to see if this fits?”
  3) “Reply ‘yes’ and I’ll send 2 sample replies for free.”

## 3) Outbound Ops Checklist (daily sending + QA)

### List QA (before sending)
- Verify category matches vertical (avoid irrelevant listings)
- Exclude obvious chains/franchises unless multi-location is intentional
- Confirm GBP has recent review activity (prefer last review <= 30 days)
- Capture: rating, review count, last review date
- Calculate response-rate proxy from last 10 reviews (count owner replies)
- Capture a safe personalization snippet (6–12 words max) OR paraphrase

### 14-day sending ramp (per inbox)
- Days 1–2: 20/day
- Days 3–4: 30/day
- Days 5–6: 40/day
- Days 7–10: 60/day
- Days 11–14: 80–100/day
Rules:
- Keep follow-ups on; they drive most replies.
- Stop/slow if bounces > 3% in a day or complaints > 0.1%.
- Reply SLA: same day for positive replies; within 2 hours for “interested.”

### Daily workflow (60–90 minutes)
1) Pull today’s segment batch (Priority A first)
2) Personalize first line (review snippet + response gap)
3) Send Email 1
4) Update CRM stage to “Sent”
5) Process replies twice/day: morning + afternoon
6) Book calls, send samples, or mark not interested

### Weekly workflow
- Monday: refill 100–200 new leads
- Wednesday: QA sample 20 records for accuracy
- Friday: review KPIs (sent, open, reply, positive reply, demos booked, bounces)


## 4) CRM Stages (simple pipeline)
1) **Prospect (Unsent):** lead is validated, has required fields.
2) **Sent (E1):** initial email sent.
3) **Follow-up Scheduled:** E2/E3 queued.
4) **Replied:** any reply received.
5) **Qualified:** they confirm ownership/decision-maker + interest.
6) **Demo Booked:** meeting scheduled.
7) **Trial / Pilot:** sample replies delivered or pilot started.
8) **Paid:** payment confirmed.
9) **Lost:** not now / no fit / wrong contact.

Notes to capture in CRM:
- Segment (Not responding / Low rating / High volume)
- Priority (A/B/C)
- Hook used (snippet + response gap)
- Next action date
