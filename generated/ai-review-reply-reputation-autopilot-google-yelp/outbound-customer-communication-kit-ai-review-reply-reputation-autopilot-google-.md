5) DISCOVERY CALL SCRIPT (10–12 minutes)
====================

Goal: qualify, confirm pain, define scope (locations + volume + SLA), and close into pilot or month 1.

Opening (30 sec)
“Thanks for taking the call. In ~10 minutes we’ll confirm whether this makes sense, and if it does I’ll outline the quickest way to start—either a 7-day pilot or straight to monthly. Sound good?”

Context questions (2–3 min)
1) “How important are reviews to new bookings/calls for you today?”
2) “Who is currently replying to reviews, and how often?”
3) “Which platforms matter most—Google, Yelp, or both?”
4) “How many locations? Any shared brand voice guidelines?”

Pain + urgency (2 min)
1) “What happens when a negative review sits unanswered?”
2) “Have you had any recent review incidents (refund disputes, staff complaints, HIPAA/privacy concerns, etc.)?”

Volume + workflow fit (2 min)
1) “Approx reviews/week?”
2) “Do you prefer we draft-only (you post) or draft+post with manager access?”
3) “For negative reviews: who approves, and what’s the expected turnaround time?”

Positioning (60 sec)
“Our system does three things: consistent on-brand replies, faster response times, and escalation so you don’t accidentally post the wrong thing. It’s AI-assisted, but always brand-safe with rules and approvals.”

Close (2–3 min)
Option A (Pilot):
“I recommend we start with a 7-day pilot. We’ll set up access, tone rules, and start daily replies. At the end you’ll get a KPI snapshot and we either continue monthly or stop—no hard feelings. Want to start with that?”

Option B (Monthly):
“If you’d rather skip the pilot, we can start month-to-month. We’ll cap volume by plan and set the SLA for replies and escalations. Which option do you prefer?”

Next steps (final 30 sec)
“Great—please email your GBP/Yelp links to [YOUR_EMAIL]. I’ll send the onboarding checklist (access + brand voice + escalation contacts). Once access is in, we can begin within 24–48 hours.”
