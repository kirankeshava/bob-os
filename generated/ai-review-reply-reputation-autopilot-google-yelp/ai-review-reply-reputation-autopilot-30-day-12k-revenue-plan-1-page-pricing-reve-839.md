3) FULFILLMENT SCOPE (WEEK-1 DELIVERABLE, NO API DEPENDENCY)

Sources covered:
• Google Business Profile reviews (GBP)
• Yelp reviews

Posting method (designed for week 1 reliability):
• Preferred: client grants access
  – Google: add us as Manager on GBP.
  – Yelp: add us as user on Yelp Business Owner account OR provide delegated access if available.
• Fallback (lowest friction): “Draft + Client Posts”
  – We send ready-to-paste responses daily by email (or shared doc). Client posts inside GBP/Yelp.

SLA & cadence:
• Monitor/check reviews: daily (business days) via manual check + email alerts/screenshots.
• Draft response: within 24h (Growth/Agency Starter) or 12h (Pro/Agency Pro) of new review being observed.

Brand safety & policy rules:
• No incentives for reviews, no review gating, no falsified reviews.
• We do not remove reviews. We can suggest dispute steps, but client executes.
• We avoid sensitive claims (medical/legal/guarantees). If client is regulated, we use a conservative template.

Escalation rules (negative review escalation ticket triggered if any):
• 1–2 star review
• 3-star with complaint keywords (refund, scam, lawsuit, rude, unsafe, fraud, discrimination)
• Any mention of personal data, injury, health outcomes, or legal threats
Escalation process:
1) Create escalation note (what happened + suggested response options)
2) Notify client escalation contact (email/text) within SLA
3) Client chooses: approve response / request edit / take offline resolution first
4) Track outcome: responded? resolved? updated review?

Approval rules (to ship fast while remaining safe):
• Auto-approve 4–5★ responses unless client flags a “never say” list.
• Require approval for 1–3★, legal/medical keywords, or anything requesting refunds.

Weekly KPI report (one-page, emailed every Monday):
• New reviews count (Google/Yelp)
• Average rating (current + change vs prior week)
• Response rate (% responded)
• Median response time
• Negative review count (1–3★)
• Escalations opened / resolved
• Top 3 themes from reviews (quality, speed, pricing, staff)
• Next-week action list (2–3 bullets)
