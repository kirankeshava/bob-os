E) 90-SECOND DISCOVERY CALL SCRIPT
====================================

Goal: qualify volume + pain + permissions + close next step (pilot or plan).

1) Opener (10s)
“Thanks for taking the time—this will be quick. I just want to understand your review flow and see if daily replies + escalation would help.”

2) Situation questions (30s)
• “Which matters most for you right now—Google, Yelp, or both?”
• “About how many reviews do you get per week per location?”
• “Who replies today? And how quickly, typically?”

3) Pain / stakes (20s)
• “When a 1–3 star review comes in, what happens—do you have a process to escalate and respond calmly?”
• “Have you ever lost a booking because someone referenced a review?”

4) Explain solution (25s)
“We do two things: (1) brand-safe drafted replies daily so your profile stays responsive, and (2) negative-review escalation so sensitive situations get human approval. You’ll also get a simple weekly KPI summary: response rate, response time, negative volume, and themes.”

5) Close (15s)
“Best next step is either a 7-day pilot or we start with [Draft-only / Draft+Post] for one location. Which would you prefer?”

Common objections (bullets)
• “We already reply sometimes.” → “Totally—this is about consistency + speed. Same-day responses reduce the ‘no one cares’ perception, especially on negatives.”
• “We’re worried about saying the wrong thing.” → “That’s exactly why we use approved brand voice + an escalation rule for anything sensitive.”
• “Can you post for us?” → “Yes if you grant manager access; otherwise we stay draft-only and you post.”
