4) FULFILLMENT SCOPE (week-1 deliverable, no APIs required)
Review sources supported week 1:
- Google Business Profile (GBP)
- Yelp Business
Posting method (choose per client based on access):
- Preferred: client grants us Manager access on GBP and access to Yelp Business Owner account (or adds our team email as user).
- Fallback (always available): “Client-Post Mode” — we draft replies in a shared doc/email; client posts using their credentials. SLA applies to drafting, not posting.

Response SLA definition:
- SLA clock starts when review is visible in the profile/alert inbox.
- Growth: draft/post within 24 business hours; Pro: within 12 business hours.

Escalation rules (auto-flag to client immediately):
- Any 1–3★ review
- Any review mentioning safety/legal/medical outcomes, discrimination, threats, chargebacks
- Any mention of employee names + misconduct
- Any request for refunds, lawsuits, insurance, HIPAA/PHI risk
Escalation handling:
- We create an escalation ticket with: review text, proposed response options (2), recommended offline resolution step, and deadline.
- Client approves final public reply for escalations unless they opt into “pre-approved playbook mode.”

What we do NOT do (to stay compliant and deliverable):
- No review gating, no incentives, no “filtering” unhappy customers
- No removal promises; we can draft dispute language but client submits
- No pretending to be the owner; always authorized access or client-post fallback

5) FULFILLMENT SOP OUTLINE (operator checklist)
Onboarding (Day 0–2)
1) Collect access + info:
- GBP Manager invite + Yelp user access (or confirm client-post mode)
- Business name, locations, hours, services, refund policy, compliance constraints
- Brand voice: friendly/professional, do/don’t phrases, emoji policy, sign-off name
- Promo policy: what offers can/can’t be mentioned
- Escalation contacts: name/email/phone, response deadline
2) Create Response Guardrails:
- 10 approved “5★ thank you” variations
- 10 approved “4★ feedback” variations
- Category-specific templates (e.g., appointment delays, pricing concerns)
3) Baseline metrics snapshot (start point): avg rating, total reviews, last 30-day review count, current response rate.

Daily Operations (Mon–Fri)
1) Review capture: check GBP/Yelp alerts or manual scan at set times (e.g., 9am/2pm local)
2) Draft response using guardrails
3) QA pass: policy, tone, no admissions of fault, no PHI, no incentives
4) Decide:
- 4–5★ and not flagged → post (or send to client in client-post mode)
- 1–3★ or flagged → create escalation ticket + request approval
5) Update tracker: status, posted time, SLA met (Y/N), escalation count

Weekly Reporting (every Monday)
Send 1-page KPI report with:
- New reviews (G/Y), average rating trend, response rate, median response time
- Negative reviews (count), escalations created, escalations resolved
- Top 3 themes (what customers mention) + 3 recommended fixes
