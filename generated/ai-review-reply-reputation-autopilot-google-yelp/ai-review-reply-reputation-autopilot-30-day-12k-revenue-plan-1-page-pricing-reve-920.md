1) Preferred: Client grants Manager access to Google Business Profile and access to Yelp Business Owner account (or adds us as user).
2) Fallback (Yelp or any system): “Client-post workflow” — we draft responses in a shared doc/portal; client posts; we track completion.

SLA definition:
- DFY Growth: respond within 24 business hours
- DFY Pro: respond within 12 business hours

Brand safety rules (human-in-the-loop QA):
- We draft all replies using client-approved Brand Voice Library.
- We do not admit liability, promise outcomes, or discuss private details.
- We avoid HIPAA/medical claims, legal admissions, or refund commitments unless client supplies approved language.

Escalation rules (automatic escalation triggers):
Escalate (and pause posting until client approves) when:
- 1–2 star review OR 3-star with severe complaint
- Mentions: “lawsuit,” “scam,” “fraud,” “report,” “BBB,” “attorney,” “chargeback,” “police,” “harassment,” “discrimination,” “HIPAA,” “injury”
- Requests refunds, threats, or personally identifying info
Escalation outputs:
- 1 internal incident ticket with summary + recommended reply options
- Request for client facts + preferred resolution path
- Once approved, we post (or send finalized copy for client-post)

Explicit exclusions (to keep deliverable + compliant):
- No paying for reviews, incentives for reviews, or review gating by rating
- No removal guarantees; disputes/flags only with client authorization
- No “fake review” accusations without client-provided proof
