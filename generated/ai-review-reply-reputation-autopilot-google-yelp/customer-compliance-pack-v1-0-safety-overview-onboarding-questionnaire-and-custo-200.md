1) Compliance & Brand-Safety Overview (Customer-Facing One-Pager)

What this product does
- Drafts brand-safe responses to reviews on Google Business Profile and Yelp.
- Flags and escalates high-risk reviews (e.g., safety incidents, legal threats, PHI/HIPAA mentions).
- Produces weekly reputation KPIs (response rate/time, rating trend, escalations).

What this product will NOT do (policy & safety constraints)
- No incentives or “review gating”: We do not offer discounts, gifts, refunds, or any benefit in exchange for reviews, and we do not ask only happy customers to post reviews.
- No fake or manipulated reviews: We do not generate, post, or encourage fake reviews.
- No promises about platform enforcement: We do not claim we can remove reviews or influence Yelp/Google moderation (“we’ll get this taken down”).
- No competitor attacks: We do not disparage competitors or compare in a hostile way.
- No liability admissions: Responses avoid language like “we caused/damaged/broke,” “our fault,” or “we admit negligence.”
- No medical guarantees: For healthcare/med spa, responses avoid outcomes-based promises (e.g., “guaranteed results,” “we fixed your condition”).
- No PHI/HIPAA confirmation: We do not confirm anyone is a patient/client or reference charts/records/visits/appointments. If a reviewer shares details, we still respond generically and move the conversation offline.

How negative reviews are handled
- Mild/standard negatives: We acknowledge feelings, apologize for the experience (without admitting fault), and invite the reviewer to contact the business offline.
- High-risk reviews trigger escalation and may block posting (“manual-only hold”), including:
  - Legal threats (e.g., “lawsuit,” “attorney,” “sue”)
  - Safety incidents or injury claims
  - PHI/HIPAA-related language (healthcare contexts)
  - Harassment, discrimination allegations, or doxxing attempts
- In “manual-only hold,” the system can draft an internal recommendation, but it will not post a public response until a designated approver explicitly approves (or you decide not to respond publicly).

Platform alignment (high-level)
- Yelp and Google both expect honest, respectful responses.
- Our response style avoids: incentives, threats, retaliation, personal data, and arguments.
- We use an “offline resolution CTA” (call/email/message) that does not include promotional language.

Audit trail & accountability
- Every response has traceability (draft version, flags, approver, timestamps, and posting status).
- You control who can approve and whether auto-post is enabled for low-risk cases.
