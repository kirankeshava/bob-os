Escalation rules (negative review handling):
Escalate when any of the following occurs:
- Rating 1–2 stars
- Allegations of harm, fraud, discrimination, illegal activity
- Medical/legal/financial advice implications
- Profanity, threats, or doxxing
- Refund/chargeback demands
Escalation deliverable:
- Internal “incident ticket” with summary, recommended response options (2–3), and proposed next step
- Client chooses: (a) post public response, (b) request offline resolution, (c) no response (documented)

Brand safety / compliance guardrails:
- No incentives for reviews, no review gating, no “we’ll give you X for a review.”
- No impersonation of the reviewer.
- No claiming to remove or delete reviews.
- No disputing reviews without explicit authorization.

Posting method (Week-1 deliverable without APIs):
Default (preferred):
- Client grants us Google Business Profile Manager access (role: Manager) for posting.
- For Yelp: client grants Yelp Business Owner / user access where possible.
Fallback (always available):
- We draft responses and send “copy/paste-ready” replies in email/Sheet.
- Client posts themselves (we track time-to-post and response rate).
This ensures deliverability even if Yelp access is delayed or API access is unavailable.
