Platform policy alignment (Google + Yelp)
We design responses to follow platform expectations:
1) No incentives or "review gating"
We do not ask customers to change, remove, or update reviews in exchange for discounts, refunds, gifts, or special treatment. We do not selectively solicit positive reviews.

2) No promises about platform enforcement
We do not promise review removal, claim we can influence Yelp/Google moderation, or instruct customers to “report to Yelp/Google” as a threat.

3) No competitor disparagement
We do not attack competitors or speculate about other businesses.

4) Privacy-first responses
We avoid personal data and sensitive details. For healthcare-adjacent businesses (e.g., dentists, med spas), we do not confirm whether someone is a patient/client and we do not reference appointment details, charts, records, procedures, or outcomes.

5) Brand-safe, non-inflammatory tone
Replies are neutral, empathetic, and non-argumentative. For negative reviews, we move the conversation offline with a clear contact path.

High-risk situations are held for manual review
Some reviews should not be auto-posted. We flag and hold these for a human decision, including:
• Legal threats or mentions of attorneys/lawsuits/suing
• Safety incidents (injury, property damage, hazardous work claims)
• Discrimination/harassment allegations
• Requests to discuss medical details or any PHI/records/visit confirmation
• Doxxing attempts (full names, phone numbers, addresses) or threats

What our replies typically include (safe structure)
• Thanks/acknowledgement without admitting fault
• A neutral statement that we take feedback seriously
• A request to contact us directly (offline CTA)
• A commitment to look into the issue (without referencing private records)

Examples of safe offline CTA language
• “We’d like to learn more and help. Please contact our team at [phone/email] so we can follow up directly.”
• “For privacy, we can’t discuss details here, but we’re happy to speak offline to address your concerns.”

What we avoid (examples)
• Liability admission: “We caused this” / “This is our fault”
• Medical outcomes: “Guaranteed results” / “Cured” / “Permanent fix”
• PHI confirmation: “We reviewed your chart/records/visit”
• Incentives: “We’ll give you a discount/refund if you update this review”

How you (the business) stay in control
• You can require approval before any response is posted.
• Escalations are routed with clear reasons and recommended next steps.
• An audit trail can record who approved, what was posted, and why a review was held.
