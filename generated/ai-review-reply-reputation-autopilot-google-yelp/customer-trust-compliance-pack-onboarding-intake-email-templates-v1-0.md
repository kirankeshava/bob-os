## 1) What the product does (plain language)
We help you respond to Google Business Profile and Yelp reviews consistently and professionally.
- Drafts brand-safe responses to new reviews.
- Flags risky/negative reviews for escalation.
- Produces weekly reputation KPIs (response rate/time, sentiment buckets, escalation counts).

You choose your posting mode:
- **Approval-first (recommended):** we draft; you approve; we post (or you post).
- **Autopost (optional, only for low-risk categories):** only low-risk positive/neutral reviews may be posted automatically.

## 2) Brand-safety commitments (what we will NOT do)
To protect your business, our system is designed to avoid:
- **Admitting liability or fault** (e.g., “we caused…”, “our technician damaged…”) in public.
- **Confirming private customer or patient details** (PHI/PII). We do not confirm whether someone is/was a client/patient.
- **Medical outcome guarantees** (e.g., “this will cure…”, “guaranteed results”).
- **Incentives for reviews** (e.g., discounts, gifts, refunds offered in exchange for review changes/removal).
- **Threats, retaliation, or arguments** with reviewers.
- **Doxxing** (names, addresses, phone numbers, appointment times, account numbers) unless you explicitly provide and approve a public-safe version.

## 3) Platform policy alignment (Google + Yelp)
We align with common policy expectations:
- No fake reviews, no “review gating,” no pressure campaigns.
- No asking for removal or implying platform enforcement (e.g., “Yelp will remove this”).
- No competitor disparagement.
- We use **offline resolution** language for negative reviews (“Please contact us directly so we can help.”).

## 4) “Manual-only hold” (when we block posting)
Certain review content triggers a **manual-only hold**. In these cases, the system will draft guidance internally but will not post publicly until a human decides.
Common hold triggers:
- **Legal threats** (e.g., “lawsuit,” “attorney,” “sue”).
- **Safety incidents / injuries / property damage**.
- **HIPAA/PHI risk** (mentions of “records,” “chart,” “diagnosis,” “visit details”).
- Harassment/hate speech, extortion, or credible threats.

## 5) Escalation levels (how we route)
- **Level 0 (OK to respond):** positive/neutral, no risk flags.
- **Level 1 (Service recovery):** mild dissatisfaction; respond politely and take offline.
- **Level 2 (Sensitive):** billing dispute, alleged misconduct, potential misinformation; requires approval-first.
- **Level 3 (Legal/Safety/PHI):** manual-only hold; route to owner/GM/legal immediately.

## 6) Audit trail (accountability)
For every draft and post attempt, we maintain an audit trail (as supported by your integration mode), including:
- Review source (Google/Yelp), review ID, timestamps.
- Risk flags + escalation level.
- Draft version, approver identity (if approval-first), posted/blocked status.
- Final response text and rule/detector version for traceability.

## 7) Customer responsibilities
To stay compliant and safe, you agree to:
- Not instruct us to offer incentives for reviews.
- Provide a real escalation contact (owner/GM/legal) for sensitive cases.
- Confirm your preferred posting mode (approval-first vs limited autopost).
