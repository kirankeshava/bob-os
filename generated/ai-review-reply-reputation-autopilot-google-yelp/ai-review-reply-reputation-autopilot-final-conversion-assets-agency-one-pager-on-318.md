# 3) 2 MINI CASE-STUDY MODULES (ANONYMIZED)

## Case Study #1 — Home Services (HVAC)
**Problem**
A multi-truck HVAC company was getting steady Google reviews but responding inconsistently. Negative reviews sometimes sat unanswered for a week, creating a “no one cares” impression during peak season.

**What we did**
- Implemented a **24-hour SLA** for drafting replies.
- Created a brand voice: concise, respectful, action-oriented (no over-apologizing; always offer a clear next step).
- Built an escalation rule: **1–2★ always requires approval** before posting.
- Added weekly KPI reporting with a “top 3 recurring issues” section for ops.

**Results (example narrative + KPI placeholders)**
- **Median response time** dropped from “several days” to **< 24 hours**.
- **Reply rate** increased to **90–100%** of new reviews.
- Negative reviews were de-escalated faster with a consistent “take it offline” process.
- Outcome: improved conversion confidence for new callers (“they respond to customers”).

## Case Study #2 — Med Spa / Aesthetics
**Problem**
A med spa had strong service quality but struggled with review replies that felt either too generic (“Thanks!”) or too risky (over-promising results). They needed a compliant, luxury tone and a safer process for 1–2★ reviews.

**What we did**
- Built a **brand-safe reply library** (no medical claims, no guarantees, no discussing personal details).
- Switched to a **Same-Day (Mon–Fri) SLA** for drafts to keep the profile active.
- Introduced an approval step for any review mentioning outcomes, staff names, or billing.
- Weekly KPI reporting focused on response speed + sentiment themes.

**Results (example narrative + KPI placeholders)**
- Replies became consistently on-brand: warm, premium, and compliant.
- Faster response time reduced “pile-up” anxiety and improved customer perception.
- Narrative rating lift: after consistent replies and quicker resolutions, the location saw a gradual positive shift in new review tone and fewer unresolved negatives.
