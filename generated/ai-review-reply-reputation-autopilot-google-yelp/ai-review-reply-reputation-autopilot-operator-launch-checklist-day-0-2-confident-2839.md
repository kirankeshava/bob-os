===========================
A) OPERATOR LAUNCH CHECKLIST (DAY 0–2 SLA)
===========================
Goal: Activate a new location in <48 business hours from “PAID” (or “START APPROVED”) to “LIVE”.

Prereqs (from Close)
1) Confirm scope: platforms (Google, Yelp, or both), # locations, response cadence (daily / 3x-week / weekly), escalation SLA.
2) Confirm start date: “We’ll be live within 48 business hours of getting access + your first reply approvals.”
3) Confirm primary contact + escalation contact (name, phone, email).

DAY 0 (same day as close)
Operator steps:
1) Create CRM entry:
   - Stage: Closed–Won → Paid → Onboarding
   - Record: business name, location(s), platform(s), contact info, review volume estimate, existing pain notes.
2) Send “Day 0 Kickoff Email” (from playbook) including:
   - Intake form link/questions
   - Access request instructions (GBP + Yelp)
   - Request for brand voice + do-not-say list
   - Request for any sensitive topics (pricing, refunds, compliance)
3) Send forwardable access-request email(s):
   - Google Business Profile access request (manager access)
   - Yelp access request (Business Owner / agency access)
4) Set internal SLA timers:
   - T+24h: follow-up if intake/access not received
   - T+48h: escalation to contact if blocked

Customer outputs expected by end of Day 0:
- Intake form completed (or 80% complete)
- Access granted (or invitation sent / agency looped in)

DAY 1 (within 24 business hours)
Operator steps:
1) Verify access:
   - GBP: confirm location(s) visible and review inbox accessible
   - Yelp: confirm business page access or message center access
2) Pull last 10–20 reviews per platform and categorize:
   - Positive / Neutral / Negative
   - Themes (wait time, pricing, staff, quality)
   - Any compliance-sensitive patterns
3) Draft “Sample Pack” for approval (send to customer):
   - 2 positive replies (different tones)
   - 2 negative replies (de-escalation + invite offline)
   - 1 edge case (refund demand / legal threat / profanity)
   Include: one escalation note template.
4) Configure escalation rules (internal):
   - What triggers escalation (≤3 stars, keywords, threats, refunds)
   - Who gets notified and how fast
   - Hold-for-approval vs auto-post boundaries

Customer outputs expected by end of Day 1:
- Written approval on sample tone OR requested edits
- Confirm “auto-post allowed?” yes/no per platform and star threshold

DAY 2 (within 48 business hours)
Operator steps:
1) Go-live checklist:
   - Apply approved voice guidelines to response templates
   - Confirm posting workflow: direct posting vs draft-only
   - Confirm escalation channel tested (email/phone)
2) Process backlog:
   - Respond to last 10–30 most recent unresponded reviews (as agreed)
   - Escalate any high-risk negatives immediately
3) Send “Go-Live Confirmation” email:
   - What’s live
   - Posting cadence
   - How escalations will work
   - First report date

First-week success criteria (send in kickoff + track internally)
- Response time: <24–48h on new reviews (or agreed cadence)
- Coverage: 90%+ of new reviews responded to
- Zero brand-safety incidents (no prohibited claims; no sharing customer PHI/PII)
- Weekly KPI report delivered on schedule
