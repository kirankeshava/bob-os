### Day 0 (Immediately after verbal yes / payment)
1) **Send Kickoff Email (Day 0)**
   - Include: legitimacy URL, support inbox, intake form link/text, and access-request instructions.
2) **Send/Provide Access-Request Emails**
   - One email for Google Business Profile (GBP), one for Yelp.
   - Ask them to forward to: owner / manager / marketing agency.
3) **Collect Intake (must-have fields)**
   - Brand voice (formal/friendly), do-not-say list, escalation contacts, hours, locations, services, refund/comp policy, competitor mentions policy.
4) **Confirm Review Channels + Volume**
   - GBP only? Yelp too? Any others (Facebook/Tripadvisor) noted for later.
5) **Set first-week success criteria (confirm by email)**
   - Response time target (e.g., <24 business hours).
   - Coverage target (e.g., 90–100% of new reviews responded).
   - Escalation SLA (e.g., negatives escalated same day).

### Day 1 (Access + first drafts)
6) **Verify Access**
   - GBP: manager access received and verified.
   - Yelp: business account access (or equivalent) verified.
7) **Create “Reply Rules” doc (internal)**
   - 5 bullets: tone, apology rules, compensation language, privacy language, invite to offline resolution.
8) **Prepare Sample Approvals (10-minute approval loop)**
   - Generate 3 replies from their real reviews (or use anonymized if no access yet):
     - 1 positive, 1 neutral, 1 negative.
   - Send for approval with binary choices:
     - “Approve tone as-is” or “Adjust: warmer/more formal/more concise/more empathetic.”
9) **Escalation Contacts Test**
   - Send a test escalation note to the provided contact(s) so they recognize the format.

### Day 2 (Go live)
10) **Turn on Live Replying**
   - Start responding to any backlog agreed upon (optional) and all new reviews.
11) **Negative Review Escalation Live**
   - Any 1–3 star review triggers escalation note to customer contact within same business day.
12) **Confirm Weekly Reporting Schedule**
   - Choose send day/time (e.g., Monday 9am) and recipients.
13) **Document “Week 1 baseline”**
   - Current rating, # reviews last 30 days, avg response time (if available), unresolved negatives.

### First-week success criteria (send in kickoff + revisit Day 7)
- Response coverage: 90–100% of new reviews responded.
- Speed: replies within 24 business hours (or within 4 hours during business hours if premium).
- Safety: zero policy-violating replies; no customer PII included.
- Escalation: 100% of negative reviews escalated same day with clear next steps.
- Weekly report delivered with: rating trend, review volume, response rate, response time, negative themes, actions taken.
