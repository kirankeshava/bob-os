## B) Upwork: Daily Routine + 15 Ready-to-Send Proposals
### Daily routine (45–60 min)
1) Check saved searches (GBP, Yelp, reputation management, respond to reviews, local SEO support, customer feedback). Filter: payment verified preferred; budget >$100; posted <24h.
2) Shortlist 5 jobs/day. Personalize opener with 1 line referencing their niche + 1 audit bullet.
3) Send 5 proposals/day. Spend cap connects: avg 12–16 per proposal; stop when weekly cap hit.
4) Follow-up cadence (if no reply): T+24h, T+72h, T+7d with 1 extra audit bullet.
5) Track: proposals sent, replies, calls booked, closes, time-to-first-reply.

### 15 niche-specific proposal openers + audit bullets
Use: replace {Niche-specific audit bullet} with one of these.

#### Home Services (HVAC/Plumbing/Roofing) — 5
1) “For home services, the fastest ROI is replying to ‘on-time / pricing / cleanup’ reviews—those keywords affect whether the next homeowner calls.”
2) “If a review mentions ‘no-show’ or ‘late,’ a calm reply + escalation note often prevents a public back-and-forth.”
3) “Many contractors miss chances to ask for photos/next step in 5-star replies (e.g., ‘text us before your next seasonal tune-up’).”
4) “I can standardize responses so every tech sounds like the same brand—no defensive tone, no overpromising.”
5) “We can tag review themes (price, scheduling, workmanship) so you see what’s hurting conversion.”

#### Dental — 5
1) “Dental reviews need a HIPAA-safe voice—no confirming patient status, no specifics—while still sounding warm and human.”
2) “A lot of 3-star dental reviews are ‘great staff, long wait’—the reply should acknowledge and show improvement without excuses.”
3) “I can build a compliant response library for common topics: pain, billing, scheduling, insurance, wait time.”
4) “Fast responses to negative reviews reduce call drop-off from prospects who read the last 3 reviews.”
5) “Your 5-star replies can subtly reinforce high-value services (implants, Invisalign, emergencies) without sounding salesy.”

#### Medspa — 5
1) “Medspa reviews often mention specific treatments—responses must stay privacy-safe while still inviting them back.”
2) “When someone mentions results dissatisfaction, the best practice is: empathize → invite offline → never debate outcomes publicly.”
3) “I can align replies to your brand voice (luxury/clinical/friendly) so you look premium and consistent.”
4) “We can escalate any mention of burns, reactions, or refund requests within 1 hour.”
5) “A weekly KPI note on top complaint themes helps your front desk reduce booking friction.”

#### Restaurants — 5
1) “Restaurant reviews are time-sensitive—responding within 24 hours to service issues reduces ‘pile-on’ negativity.”
2) “For 5-star reviews, adding a simple ‘what to try next time’ increases repeat visits.”
3) “For 1–2 star reviews about wait times, a short apology + offline invite beats a long explanation.”
4) “I can create response templates for delivery issues vs dine-in vs reservation problems.”
5) “We’ll track complaint themes (service, food temp, cleanliness) so you can fix what’s hurting star rating.”

#### Auto (repair/detailing/dealer) — 5
1) “Auto reviews often include pricing skepticism—responses should clarify value without getting into invoice detail.”
2) “If someone mentions ‘upsold me,’ we reply with empathy + invite to discuss—never argue line items publicly.”
3) “For detailing, asking for before/after photos in replies can generate UGC and referrals.”
4) “We can standardize how you respond to warranty/return complaints so it doesn’t look evasive.”
5) “Weekly trend report: recurring makes/models/issue types mentioned—useful for ops and marketing.”
