D) 2-CALL SALES SCRIPT (DISCOVERY + DEMO/CLOSE)
=================================

CALL 1 — DISCOVERY (15 minutes)
Goal: qualify, quantify impact, confirm workflow + risk concerns, book call 2.

1) Set agenda (30s)
“Thanks for the time. I’d like to understand your current review flow for Google/Yelp, what ‘good’ looks like, and whether an autopilot approach makes sense. If it does, we’ll book a quick demo and show sample replies in your brand voice. Sound good?”

2) Current state (3–4 mins)
- “Which platforms matter most for you right now — Google, Yelp, Facebook?”
- “Roughly how many reviews do you get per week?”
- “Who replies today, and how quickly?”
- “Any recent negative reviews that you wish had been handled differently?”

3) Stakes / value (3–4 mins)
- “How much do calls/bookings depend on your rating and recency?”
- “If you improved response speed to same-day, what would that be worth monthly?”
- “What’s your biggest concern: brand voice, compliance, or time?”

4) Workflow + compliance (3 mins)
“We keep everything policy-safe: no gating, no incentives, no weird templates. For negatives, we escalate for approval based on your rules.”
- “Do you prefer draft-only (you approve/post) or draft+post (we post with manager access under your escalation settings)?”
- “Who should be notified when a 1–2 star review lands?”

5) Close to next step (1–2 mins)
“Next step: we’ll set your brand voice and escalation rules, then show you sample replies and the weekly KPI view. Can we schedule 15 minutes [date/time]?”


CALL 2 — DEMO / CLOSE (20–25 minutes)
Goal: show output quality, remove risk, confirm scope, close.

1) Reconfirm goals (1 min)
“Quick recap: you want [same-day replies / brand-safe tone / escalations] and you’re seeing [#] reviews/week. Correct?”

2) Show the ‘before/after’ in words (3 mins)
“Today, reviews get answered [timing]. With autopilot, positives get handled fast, and negatives get escalated with a calm, de-escalating response + offline resolution path.”

3) Walk through workflow (5 mins)
- Intake: new reviews detected daily
- Drafting: brand voice + service specifics
- Escalation: rules for 1–2 star, keywords (refund, rude, lawsuit, etc.)
- Approval/posting: draft-only or post-with-access
- Reporting: weekly KPIs + themes

4) Offer + scope clarity (5 mins)
“Our plans are per location with review-volume bands. All include:
- brand-safe reply drafting for Google and/or Yelp (per plan)
- escalation + approval rules for negatives
- weekly KPI summary
Not included: incentivizing reviews, review gating, or deleting reviews (we stay on-policy).”

5) Objections (5 mins)
- Brand voice: “We start with 10 ‘seed replies’ and adjust until it matches your tone. You can approve early replies if you want.”
- Fear of wrong reply: “Negatives and sensitive keywords always escalate for approval.”
- ‘We can do it ourselves’: “Totally—this is for speed + consistency so it doesn’t steal operator time.”

6) Close (2–3 mins)
“Do you want to start with a pilot week so you can evaluate quality and response time? If yes, I’ll send the onboarding checklist (GBP/Yelp access + brand voice) and we can go live within 24–48 hours.”
