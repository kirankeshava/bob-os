# Two Mini Case Studies (Anonymized, Paste-Ready)
## Case Study 1 — Home Services (HVAC / Plumbing)
**Problem:** Owner was responding once every 7–14 days, and negative reviews were going unanswered. Customers mentioned “no response” and “unprofessional.”

**What we did:**
- Implemented **Priority SLA (4 business hours)**
- Set escalation rules for 1–2 star reviews + refund/chargeback language
- Drafted brand-safe replies focused on de-escalation and moving the conversation offline

**Results (first 30 days):**
- Avg response time improved from **~9 days → < 6 hours**
- Reply rate increased to **95%+**
- Negative reviews received same-day acknowledgements, reducing public back-and-forth
- Owner reported more inbound calls mentioning “you actually respond”

## Case Study 2 — Med Spa / Aesthetics
**Problem:** Team was nervous about replying due to compliance and tone; responses were inconsistent across providers. Several 3-star reviews cited “felt rushed” and “front desk vibes.”

**What we did:**
- Built a **brand voice playbook** (luxury, warm, concise)
- Created “safe” response patterns for appointment wait time, bedside manner, pricing clarity
- Escalation rules for clinical outcome claims and refund requests

**Results (6 weeks):**
- Consistent tone across all reviews (no defensive replies)
- Avg response time improved to **same-day** for 5-star and **approval-routed** for sensitive cases
- Narrative shift: more reviewers referenced “professional team” and “they reached out to fix it”
