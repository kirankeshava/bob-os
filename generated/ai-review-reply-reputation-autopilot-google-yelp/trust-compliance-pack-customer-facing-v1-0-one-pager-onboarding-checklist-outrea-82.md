1) TRUST & COMPLIANCE ONE-PAGER (Customer-Facing)
============================================================

AI Review Reply & Reputation Autopilot (Google/Yelp)
Brand-Safe Review Responses + Escalation + Weekly Reporting

What we do
- Draft brand-safe, professional responses to Google Business Profile and Yelp reviews.
- Escalate negative/high-risk reviews to your team for human handling.
- Provide weekly reputation KPIs (response rate, response time, rating trend, escalations).

What we will NOT do (policy alignment)
- We do not offer, request, or suggest incentives/discounts in exchange for reviews.
- We do not write or solicit fake reviews.
- We do not “review-gate” (e.g., asking happy customers to review publicly while routing unhappy customers privately).
- We do not promise review removal or claim we can influence Yelp/Google enforcement.
- We do not disparage competitors or engage in public arguments.

Brand-safety guardrails (hallucination + liability control)
Our response system is designed to avoid high-risk language and unverified claims:
- No admission of liability (“we caused/ruined/broke…”) or definitive fault statements.
- No medical outcome guarantees or “you’re cured” type promises.
- No confirmation of private customer status (e.g., “we reviewed your chart/records/visit”).
- No personal data, doxxing, or naming individuals (employees or patients/clients) in responses.
- De-escalation tone rules: calm, appreciative, non-inflammatory, and solution-oriented.

Negative review escalation (human-in-the-loop)
Certain scenarios automatically trigger “manual-only hold” and escalation—meaning the system will NOT post a response:
- Legal threats (e.g., “lawsuit,” “attorney,” “sue”)
- Potential PHI/HIPAA risk or private records references
- Safety incidents, injury claims, discrimination/harassment allegations
- High-severity service damage claims (e.g., property damage)

In these cases, we draft a conservative, policy-safe response (optional) and route it to your designated approver.

Audit trail & accountability
Every draft and action is logged for traceability:
- Review source (Google/Yelp), review ID, timestamps
- Risk flags and escalation level
- Draft version and final approved response
- Who approved it, when it was posted (or blocked/held), and any posting errors

Weekly KPI reporting (what you’ll see)
- Response rate (% of reviews responded to)
- Time to first response (avg/median)
- Rating trend (7/30-day)
- Escalations by reason and unresolved aging
- Posted vs. approved vs. blocked/held reconciliation
