## 3) Two Case-Study Style Examples (Anonymized)
### Case Study #1 — Home Services (HVAC)
**Problem:** A 2-location HVAC company was getting bursts of reviews during peak season but leaving many unanswered for 7–21 days. A handful of 1–2 star reviews were going unaddressed, creating a “pile-on” effect.

**What we did:**
- Implemented daily monitoring + response drafting
- Set escalation rules for pricing disputes and missed appointments
- Used “acknowledge → apologize → take offline → resolution path” playbook for negatives
- Delivered weekly KPI recap with the top complaint themes and recommended operational fixes

**Results (6 weeks):**
- Response time improved from **~10 days to <24 hours**
- Response rate increased from **~55% to 95%+**
- Negative-review threads de-escalated with more customers updating or softening language (proof: screenshots placeholder)
- Narrative lift: prospects saw “owner replies quickly” as a recurring theme

**Proof placeholders:** before/after screenshots of GBP reviews tab; weekly KPI email excerpt.

### Case Study #2 — Med Spa / Aesthetics
**Problem:** A med spa had strong service quality but inconsistent review responses. They were hesitant to reply to negatives due to sensitivity and compliance risk.

**What we did:**
- Built a compliant response style guide (no PHI acknowledgment)
- Draft-only workflow with rapid approvals for 1–2 star reviews
- Templates for common issues (wait time, pricing confusion, membership misunderstandings)

**Results (8 weeks):**
- Median response time reduced from **~5 days to same-day**
- Reduced owner time spent on reviews by **~3–5 hours/week**
- Ratings trend stabilized and began trending upward as responsiveness improved (rating chart placeholder)
- Staff alignment improved via weekly “top 3 complaint drivers” reporting

**Proof placeholders:** anonymized KPI report; screenshot of response cadence.
