3) DISCOVERY CALL SCRIPT (10–12 MINUTES)
====================================================

Goal: confirm fit, quantify review volume + locations, set SLA expectation, close Pilot or Monthly.

0:00–1:00 — Set agenda
“Thanks for the time. I’ll ask a few questions about your locations, review volume, and how you want to sound in replies. Then I’ll recommend the simplest plan and next steps. Sound good?”

1:00–3:00 — Current state
1) “How many locations do you have?”
2) “Which platforms matter most—Google only or Google + Yelp?”
3) “Roughly how many reviews per week?”
4) “Who responds today and how fast do you usually respond?”
5) “Any recent negative review that felt hard to answer?”

3:00–5:00 — Pain + stakes
“What happens when a negative review sits for a few days?”
“Do you ever worry the replies feel inconsistent or too defensive?”
“Do you track response time or rating trends?”

5:00–7:00 — Explain solution (simple)
“Here’s how we run it:
- We draft brand-safe replies daily (your tone, your policies)
- Negative reviews trigger an escalation rule (notify + approval)
- Weekly KPI email shows volume, response time, sentiment, and any risk items
We can do draft-only, or draft+post if you grant manager access.”

7:00–9:00 — Confirm governance (brand safety)
“Do you want us to auto-approve most replies and only ask approval for 1–2 star reviews, or do you prefer approving everything at first?”
“Any compliance sensitivities (HIPAA, refunds, legal threats, staff call-outs)?”

9:00–11:00 — Close
“Based on {{locations}} locations and about {{reviews/week}} reviews/week, I’d recommend {{Plan}} with {{SLA}}.
Next step: we send a short onboarding form + access request checklist today. Once access is in place, we can start within 24–48 hours.”

Offer close options:
A) Pilot close: “Want to start with a 7-day pilot so you can see response speed + tone before committing?”
B) Monthly close: “If you’re ready, we can start monthly and you can cancel anytime after the first cycle.”

Objection handling (quick)
- “We can do it ourselves.” → “Totally. This is for consistency + speed + escalation + reporting. If you want, start with pilot to compare.”
- “Worried about AI saying the wrong thing.” → “We use strict brand rules, prohibited phrases, and escalation/approval for negatives. You can require approval for the first week.”
- “Yelp is sensitive.” → “We follow policy-safe language (no incentives/gating). Draft-only is also an option if you prefer.”

11:00–12:00 — Confirm next steps
“I’ll email the onboarding link/checklist and a one-page summary of scope and SLA. What’s the best email for that?”
