What this tool does (and does not do)
Our autopilot drafts brand-safe responses to public reviews and helps you respond faster—without escalating conflict or creating compliance risk. It is designed to be conservative: if a review contains sensitive content or legal risk, the system will not post and will instead escalate for manual handling.

Hard safety rules (applies to all verticals)
1) No admission of liability
- We avoid language like “we were at fault,” “we caused,” “our mistake,” or any statement that accepts legal responsibility.
- We use neutral phrasing focused on listening, investigating, and resolving privately.

2) No personal data, doxxing, or sensitive identifiers
- Responses never include customer phone numbers, addresses, appointment dates/times, record numbers, invoices, or staff personal details.
- If a reviewer names an employee, we do not confirm employment details or discuss internal actions publicly.

3) Medical/healthcare sensitivity (HIPAA-safe behavior)
- For dentists and med spas, we do not confirm that someone is a patient/client.
- We do not reference “your chart,” “your records,” “your visit,” “your treatment plan,” or any protected health information.
- We do not make medical outcome guarantees (e.g., “permanent results,” “pain-free,” “100% effective”).

4) No incentives, no review gating, no manipulation
- We do not offer discounts, gifts, refunds, or incentives in exchange for reviews.
- We do not ask only happy customers to review or attempt to “gate” reviews.
- We do not request removal or imply that the platform will remove reviews.

5) No harassment, threats, or inflammatory language
- Responses remain polite, brief, and non-argumentative.
- We do not accuse the reviewer of lying, fraud, or wrongdoing; we keep language factual and invite offline resolution.

6) Legal threat handling (manual-only hold)
- If a review includes legal threats (e.g., “lawyer,” “lawsuit,” “sue,” “attorney general”), the system switches to manual-only hold.
- No public reply is posted automatically. The review is escalated internally with a recommended response strategy.

Required response structure (golden template)
Every posted response must include:
- Thank you + acknowledgement (no blame, no admissions)
- A neutral invitation to resolve offline
- A single contact method (phone/email/website contact form) and a request for minimal details
- A closing that reinforces professionalism

Example structure:
“Thanks for your feedback. We take concerns seriously and would like to learn more so we can make it right. Please contact us at [CONTACT] so we can review the situation privately. — [Business/Team Name]”

When we escalate instead of posting
The system should escalate (and potentially block posting) when reviews include:
- PHI/medical details or any hint of patient record confirmation
- Allegations of injury, safety hazards, or negligence
- Discrimination/harassment claims
- Demands for refunds tied to threats
- Legal threats or mentions of attorneys/lawsuits
- Requests to discuss billing specifics publicly

Google Business Profile + Yelp policy alignment (plain English)
- We do not post fake reviews, and we do not encourage fake reviews.
- We do not offer incentives for reviews.
- We do not promise review removal or claim we can influence platform enforcement.
- We keep responses focused on customer service and private resolution.

Audit trail & accountability
For each review, the system maintains an audit trail (where implemented) including:
- Review source (Google/Yelp) and review ID
- Detected risk flags (PHI, legal, harassment, etc.)
- Whether the draft was posted, approved, held, or blocked
- Final response text and timestamps
This supports transparency, troubleshooting, and weekly KPI reporting.
