# 1) Close Process (from discovery to paid + onboarded in 48 hours)

## Goal
Collect **Setup Fee + First Month upfront** within 24 hours of the discovery call, then complete onboarding and go-live within **48 hours**.

## Definitions
- **Auto-post mode**: We post without approval unless escalation triggers.
- **Post-approval mode**: We draft; client approves inside email/Doc/portal.
- **Manual mode**: We draft; client copies/pastes (least preferred).

## A. End-of-Call Close (last 3 minutes)
**1) Recap (20 seconds)**
“Here’s what I heard: you’re getting ~__ reviews/month across __ locations, you respond to ~__%, and the main business impact is __ (missed calls/appointments, lower conversion, staff time, negative review risk).”

**2) Present 2 options (60 seconds)**
Option 1 (recommended): **DFY Autopilot** — we respond daily, escalate negatives instantly, and send weekly KPI reporting.
Option 2: **Lite / Starter** — lower volume, slower cadence, fewer locations.

**3) Risk reversal + safety (30 seconds)**
“We use brand voice rules + prohibited topics + escalation triggers. You choose approval mode. Negative reviews never get a ‘clever AI reply’; they get escalated.”

**4) The close (single sentence)**
“If I send the order form with the setup + first month due today, can we get you live within 48 hours if you grant access and answer a 10-minute intake?”

**5) If yes: define next steps (60 seconds)**
- “I’ll email the proposal + order form within 2 hours.”
- “Once paid, you’ll receive the intake + access request.”
- “We go live within 48 hours.”

## B. Post-Call Timeline (non-negotiable execution)
**T+0 to T+2 hours:** Send Proposal + Order Form + Pay Link email (template below).

**T+24 hours if unpaid:** Follow-up #1 (short, assumes busy).

**T+48 hours if unpaid:** Follow-up #2 (deadline + hold slot).

**Upon payment (immediate):** Send Welcome + Intake + Access instructions (template below).

**Within 48 hours of access + intake received:** Go-live + first weekly baseline KPI snapshot sent.

## C. Objection Handling (use verbatim)

### Objection 1: “We can do this ourselves.”
“Totally—you *can*. The question is whether it consistently happens daily, with the right tone, and with negative review escalation every time. Most owners either (1) respond inconsistently, or (2) staff reply off-brand when stressed. We’re buying back your time *and* protecting revenue by keeping response rate high and preventing 1-star situations from compounding.”
**Close:** “If we can keep you at near-100% response and reduce negative-review damage with escalation, is it worth $__/month to not think about it?”

### Objection 2: “AI risk / I don’t want weird replies.”
“Agreed. The product is not ‘AI posts whatever.’ It’s a controlled workflow: brand voice rules + prohibited topics + escalation triggers + audit log. You can start in approval mode for week 1, then shift to auto-post once you’re comfortable.”
**Close:** “Do you want to start with approval mode for the first 7 days and then switch to autopilot?”

### Objection 3: “We don’t get many reviews.”
“Even at low volume, response rate and speed matter because each review sits there for months. Also, the real ROI is not just replying—it’s catching service failures early so you don’t accumulate 1-stars. If you average even 5 reviews/month, one bad review handled wrong can cost far more than the subscription.”

### Objection 4: “Price is high.”
“Let’s anchor to outcomes: if improving rating and responsiveness produces even 1–3 extra appointments per month, this pays for itself. If budget is the constraint, we can start with the Lite tier and upgrade once the process proves value.”

### Objection 5: “We have multiple locations.”
“Great—multi-location is where this wins because consistency is hardest. We can price per location with a clear review-volume cap and overage rate.”

# 5) 48-Hour Onboarding SOP (client-facing + internal checklist)

## Onboarding promise
**Within 48 hours of receiving (a) required access + (b) intake**, we will go live.

## A. Welcome Email (send immediately after payment)
**Subject:** Welcome — 10-minute intake + access to go live in 48 hours

Hi {{Name}},

Welcome aboard. To go live within 48 hours, please complete:

1) **Intake (10 min):** {{Intake Link or questions below}}
2) **Access:**
   - Google Business Profile: add {{email}} as a **Manager** for each location
   - Yelp: invite {{email}} as {{role}} (or provide agent access)

Once both are done, we’ll:
- confirm brand voice + prohibited topics
- set escalation contacts
- begin drafting/posted replies per your approval mode

Thanks,
{{Sender}}

## B. Intake Form Questions (copy/paste into Typeform/Google Form/email)
1) Business name + locations (addresses)
2) Primary services + top 3 revenue services
3) Target customer description (1–2 sentences)
4) Brand voice (choose): Professional / Friendly / Warm / Direct / Luxury / Playful
5) Words/phrases to use (3–10)
6) Words/phrases to avoid (3–10)
7) Offers you want mentioned (if any) + rules (e.g., never discount)
8) Customer support signature format (name/title)
9) Escalation contacts (name, email, phone) for:
   - negative reviews (≤3 stars)
   - legal/medical claims
   - refund demands
   - harassment/hate speech
10) Approval mode preference:
   - Auto-post (recommended once stable)
   - Post-approval
   - Manual
11) Competitors to never mention
12) Any compliance constraints (HIPAA, financial, etc.)

## C. Access Collection Steps (internal checklist)
### Google Business Profile (GBP)
- Confirm number of locations
- Request Manager access to each location
- Verify you can view reviews + post responses
- Confirm timezone + business hours (for response timing)

### Yelp
- Confirm Yelp listing ownership status
- Request access/invite
- Verify you can view reviews + draft/post replies (or confirm workflow if Yelp posting is restricted)

## D. Go-live QA checklist (must complete before posting)
- Brand voice rules documented
- Prohibited topics documented
- Escalation triggers set (≤3 stars, safety, legal, refund, harassment, discrimination)
- Approval mode confirmed
- Test draft created (1 positive, 1 neutral, 1 negative) and approved if in approval mode
- Weekly KPI report baseline captured (current rating, total reviews, response rate, avg response time if available)
