10) Discovery-to-Close Call Script (10–15 minutes)
========================
Goal: Confirm pain, volume, platforms, and decision maker; offer the simplest next step (pilot or month 1).

A) Opener (30 sec)
“Thanks for the time. In 10 minutes we’ll (1) see how reviews impact your leads, (2) confirm what’s broken today—speed, tone, escalation, or bandwidth—and (3) decide if a short pilot makes sense.”

B) Diagnostic Questions (4 minutes)
1) “Which platforms matter most for you—Google, Yelp, Facebook?”
2) “Roughly how many reviews do you get per week per location?”
3) “Who replies today, and what’s the typical response time?”
4) “What happens when you get a 1–3 star review—who sees it, and how fast?”
5) “Any brand voice preferences? (friendly, premium, direct, clinical, playful)”
6) “Any compliance/sensitivity rules? (no patient info, no compensation, no admitting fault, etc.)”

C) Align to their pain (1 minute)
Reflect back:
“Got it—so the main issue is [speed/tone/consistency/negative escalation]. That’s exactly what the Autopilot fixes: daily replies + a clean escalation path + weekly KPIs so you can track improvement.”

D) Offer options (2 minutes)
Option 1: Draft-Only
“We draft responses for every new review within the SLA, you approve/post. Best if you want full control.”

Option 2: Draft + Post
“We draft and post with manager access, and anything negative/sensitive routes to you first per your rules.”

E) Close (2 minutes)
“Best next step is a 7-day pilot for one location. We set your voice, connect platforms, and you’ll see daily replies and the weekly KPI snapshot. Want to start with draft-only or draft+post?”

F) If price objection
“Totally fair. The ROI is usually in (1) protecting conversion when prospects read negatives and (2) increasing trust through fast, consistent responses. If you want, we start draft-only to prove the workflow, then upgrade to posting once you’re comfortable.”

G) Next steps (final 1 minute)
“I’ll send the onboarding checklist and access request. Once connected, first replies go out within [SLA]. What’s the best email?”
