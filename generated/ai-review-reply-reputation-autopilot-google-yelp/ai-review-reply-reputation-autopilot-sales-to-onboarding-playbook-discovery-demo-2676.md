## 0) Offer Summary (what we sell)
**We draft and (optionally) post brand-safe replies** to Google Business Profile and Yelp reviews, **escalate negative reviews fast**, and send a **weekly KPI report**. Target: local businesses where reviews drive calls/bookings and the owner/manager is too busy to keep up.

**Activation promise:** new location live in **<48 hours** once access + brand voice rules are provided.

**Core outcomes to anchor:**
- Faster response time (hours/days → same-day)
- Consistent brand-safe tone
- Less risk in public replies
- Better conversion from review readers (more calls/visits/bookings)
- Owner peace of mind + clear escalation

## 1) Discovery Call Script (15–20 min)
**Goal:** qualify fast, quantify pain, and book the demo or move to close. Keep it tight.

### 1.1 Opening (60 seconds)
“Thanks for taking the time. The goal today is to see if we can take review replies off your plate without brand risk, and whether it’s worth doing for your location(s). If it’s a fit, I’ll show you a 5-minute demo and we can talk next steps to launch within 48 hours. Sound good?”

### 1.2 Qualification gates (must-have)
1) “Are Google reviews and/or Yelp reviews a meaningful driver of calls or bookings for you?”
2) “How many locations are we talking about today?”
3) “Who currently responds to reviews, and how consistently?”
4) “If this works, are you the person who can approve access and start, or do we need someone else involved?”

**Disqualify / deprioritize if:** no reviews matter, <1 review/month and no intent to grow, no decision-maker access path.

### 1.3 Pain + volume + responsiveness (quantify)
Ask and write numbers down.
- “Roughly how many new reviews do you get per week on Google? On Yelp?”
- “What’s your current response time on average? Same day, days, weeks, or only when you remember?”
- “What % of reviews get a response today?”
- “When you do respond, how long does it take you or your staff per review?”

**Missed revenue angle (simple math):**
“Many customers read reviews before calling. If someone reads a negative review with no owner response, they assume you don’t care. Even recovering 1–3 lost customers a month often covers this.”

### 1.4 Process + risk + brand voice
- “Do you have brand/tone guidelines? (Friendly? Formal? Short? With offers?)”
- “Any phrases you never want used publicly? Any compliance constraints?”
- “What types of reviews should be escalated immediately? (refund demands, safety issues, discrimination claims, legal threats, medical outcomes, etc.)”

### 1.5 Current tools / blockers
- “Do you already have an agency doing this, or is it internal?”
- “What’s not working with the current setup?”

### 1.6 Close temperature check
“On a scale of 1–10, how important is it to get reviews responded to quickly and consistently?”
Follow-up: “What happens if nothing changes over the next 90 days?”

### 1.7 Transition to demo
“Got it. I’ll show you exactly what you’d get: sample replies, how escalation works, and the weekly KPI report. It’ll take 5 minutes.”

## 2) Objection Handling (quick responses)
### “We already reply to reviews.”
“Totally — the difference is consistency and speed. If you miss even a handful of reviews or take days to respond, that’s when revenue leaks. We’ll either take it over or draft replies for approval so nothing slips.”

### “I don’t want AI saying the wrong thing publicly.”
“Agreed. We run on **brand-safe rules**: you approve tone, banned phrases, and escalation triggers. For anything sensitive, we escalate for human handling. We can also start in **draft-only mode** until you’re comfortable.”

### “Yelp is difficult / we got burned by Yelp before.”
“Understood. We’re not selling Yelp ads. We’re simply managing replies and monitoring. If Yelp access is limited, we work within what’s possible and still report on response rate and sentiment.”

### “Too expensive.”
“Compared to the time cost, it’s usually cheaper than 1–2 staff hours a week. And if better responsiveness wins even a couple incremental customers/month, it pays for itself. If needed, we can start with Google-only or 1 location to prove ROI.”

### “Can you guarantee rating improvement?”
“We can’t control what customers post, but we can guarantee: consistent responses, faster response time, escalation, and clear reporting. Those inputs typically correlate with better trust and conversions.”

## 3) 5-Minute Demo Flow (live or async)
**Goal:** show outcome in minutes: (1) reply quality, (2) negative escalation, (3) weekly KPI report.

### Demo checklist
1) **Show 2–3 sample drafted replies**
   - 1 positive review
   - 1 neutral review
   - 1 negative review
   Emphasize tone matching + personalization (service mentioned, staff names, location info).

2) **Show escalation logic**
   - “If review contains X (refund demand/legal threat/safety), we don’t freestyle. We flag it and notify your escalation contact immediately.”

3) **Show weekly KPI report (one screen)**
   - New reviews (Google/Yelp)
   - Response rate
   - Median response time
   - Sentiment mix (positive/neutral/negative)
   - Escalations & resolution notes
   - Suggested operational themes (e.g., ‘wait time’ mentions)

4) **Close with the activation promise**
   - “If we get access + brand voice, we can be live in <48 hours.”

## 5) Onboarding SOP (internal) — Activate in <48 hours
### Day 0 (same day as close)
1) Send **Kickoff Email** + Intake Form.
2) Send **Access Request Emails** (GBP + Yelp).
3) Create location record(s): business name, address, phone, GBP URL, Yelp URL.

### Day 1
1) Confirm access granted.
2) Configure brand voice: tone, signature, offers, banned phrases, compliance.
3) Configure escalation triggers + contacts.
4) Pull last 10–20 reviews for context.
5) Produce **Sample Batch**: 10 drafted replies (mix of positive/neutral/negative).

### Day 2
1) Customer approves sample batch (or edits tone rules).
2) Switch to draft-only or autopost mode.
3) Start monitoring cadence (daily).
4) Confirm weekly report schedule (e.g., Monday 9am).

### First-week success criteria
- ≥90% of new reviews responded to within agreed SLA (e.g., 24–48h)
- Escalations acknowledged within 2 business hours
- Tone approved and stable by end of week
- First weekly KPI report delivered
