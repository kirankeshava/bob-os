# Operator Launch Checklist (Day 0–2) — Activate a New Location in <48 Hours

## Day 0 (Immediately after close / payment)
**Goal:** get intake + access requests out within 30 minutes.

1) **Send Kickoff Email (Day 0)**
- Use the Kickoff template you already have.
- Include legitimacy URL: https://6ff73b98-dcff-4f53-a10d-9ad392f63342-00-1n4h4nuiq6w8j.picard.replit.dev/sites/1
- Support inbox: agent_bob_replit+review-bot@agentmail.to

2) **Send Intake Form** (copy/paste questions into email or Google Form)
Collect:
- Location name(s) + address + phone + website
- Primary services + top 3 customer promises (e.g., “same-day service”, “family-owned”, “insurance accepted”)
- Brand voice (friendly/professional/concise), do/don’t language, emoji policy
- Escalation contacts (name/email/phone) + when to escalate (1–2 star, legal threats, refunds, HIPAA/PHI, safety)
- Allowed offers (discounts, make-good policy) + refund authority
- Competitors to avoid mentioning

3) **Send Access-Request Emails (GBP + Yelp)**
- Forwardable to owner/manager/agency.
- Ask for least-privilege access.
- Confirm we can start with “draft-only approvals” if access is delayed.

4) **Set Operating Mode (choose one):**
A) **Autopilot posting** (preferred): we post approved replies directly in GBP/Yelp.
B) **Draft-only** (low trust / low access): we email drafts for approval + customer posts.

5) **Create Customer Folder + Threads**
- Create a single email thread per location titled: “Reputation Autopilot — [Location Name]”.
- Save escalation contacts and any “do-not-say” rules at top of thread.


## Day 1 (Once access or first review samples arrive)
**Goal:** finalize voice + publish first approved replies.

6) **Brand Voice Calibration (10–15 min)**
- Ask for 2–3 example replies they love (or competitor examples).
- Draft 3 sample replies (positive, neutral, negative) and send for approval.

7) **Approval Loop (fast)**
- Customer approves in email: “Approved” / “Revise with X tone”
- Lock voice rules in a short “Voice Notes” section.

8) **Start Handling New Reviews**
- SLA target: draft within 12 business hours of new review.
- Escalate per rules; never argue; invite offline resolution.


## Day 2 (Stabilize + confirm reporting)
**Goal:** make sure nothing blocks ongoing operations.

9) **Confirm Platforms + Permissions**
- GBP: correct locations, ability to respond.
- Yelp: confirm whether we can respond; if constraints exist, run “draft-only.”

10) **Confirm Weekly KPI Report Recipient(s)**
- Primary + backup email(s)
- Preferred send day/time

11) **First-Week Success Criteria (set expectations)**
- Response coverage: ≥80–100% of new reviews responded to (depending on volume)
- Response time: same day or <24 hours for most reviews
- Zero brand-safety incidents (no PHI, no arguments, no policy violations)
- Clear escalation handling on all 1–2 star reviews

Confidential Information does not include information that is publicly available without breach, independently developed without reference to the other party’s information, or rightfully received from a third party without restriction.
