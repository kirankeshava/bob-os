MINI CASE STUDY #1 (ANONYMIZED) — “FROM DAYS TO HOURS”
Client type: Local dental clinic (single location)

Problem
• Reviews were going unanswered for 3–10 days.
• A handful of 1–2 star reviews stayed visible with no public reply.
• Front desk had no time; owner didn’t want “robotic” responses.

What we did
• Set a calm, friendly brand voice + signature sign-off.
• Implemented daily monitoring and a 12-hour Priority SLA.
• Built an escalation rule: any 1–2 star review is flagged and routed to the owner for a 15-minute approval window.
• Drafted policy-safe responses that acknowledged concerns without admitting fault and moved the conversation offline.

Results (narrative)
Within the first two weeks, the client had responses on nearly every new review, and negative reviews were handled the same day instead of lingering. The public thread looked professional and consistent, and staff had a repeatable process for “what happens when a bad review hits.”

KPI callouts (example placeholders)
• Avg response time: ~72 hours → ~6–12 hours
• Response rate: ~40% → ~95%+
• Negative review handling: same-day escalation + approved reply posted

MINI CASE STUDY #2 (ANONYMIZED) — “DE-ESCALATION + RATING LIFT STORY”
Client type: Med spa / aesthetics (high-volume reviews)

Problem
• High review volume meant replies were inconsistent.
• Occasional 1-star reviews cited wait times and “rushed consults,” risking bookings.
• Team avoided replying publicly for fear of sounding defensive.

What we did
• Created reply templates with controlled empathy + clear next step.
• Added an escalation matrix: service complaints go to the manager; billing issues go to the owner.
• Delivered weekly KPI reporting with “top complaint themes” and suggested operational tweaks.

Results (narrative)
The client’s public responses became consistent, fast, and brand-aligned. Negative reviews were handled without public arguments, and repeated themes were surfaced early enough to fix the root causes. Over the following weeks, the overall story on the profile shifted from “unanswered complaints” to “responsive, professional service recovery,” supporting stronger conversion from profile views to bookings.

KPI callouts (example placeholders)
• Response consistency: uneven → daily coverage
• Negative-review time-to-first-action: days → same-day
• Rating trend: stabilized, then improved as new positive reviews accumulated and were acknowledged
