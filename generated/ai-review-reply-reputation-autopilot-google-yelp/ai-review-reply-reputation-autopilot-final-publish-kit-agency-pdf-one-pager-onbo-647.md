# 4) 2 MINI CASE STUDIES (ANONYMIZED, READY FOR LANDING/PROPOSALS)

## Case Study #1 — Home Services (HVAC / Plumbing)
**Situation**: A multi-tech home services team was receiving reviews daily, but responses were sporadic. Negative reviews stayed visible for weeks, and positive reviewers rarely got replies.

**What we did**:
- Implemented brand voice rules (friendly, concise, “call us and we’ll fix it” tone)
- Responded to every new Google review within SLA
- Escalated 1–2 star reviews to the owner for approval with a suggested “make-it-right” script
- Delivered weekly KPI summary: response time, response rate, recurring themes

**Result (first 30–45 days)**:
- Response time improved from “often 5–10 days” to **same-day / next-day**
- Achieved **near-100% response rate** on new reviews
- Reduced public back-and-forth by moving unhappy customers offline (call-back CTA)

## Case Study #2 — Med Spa / Aesthetics
**Situation**: A med spa had strong service but inconsistent tone across replies (some too casual, some defensive). A handful of 1–2 star reviews were unanswered, and staff were unsure what was “safe” to say publicly.

**What we did**:
- Built a compliant reply playbook: no medical guarantees, no admission of fault, invite offline follow-up
- Set escalation rules for procedure-related complaints
- Deployed Draft+Post workflow for 4–5 star reviews and Draft-only approval for 1–2 stars
- Weekly reporting: rating trend, review themes, and negative review resolution notes

**Result (first 60 days)**:
- Consistent brand voice across all replies (premium, calm, non-defensive)
- Faster de-escalation: negative reviews moved to private resolution within **24 hours**
- Improved customer trust signals through visible responsiveness (more reviewers receiving replies)
