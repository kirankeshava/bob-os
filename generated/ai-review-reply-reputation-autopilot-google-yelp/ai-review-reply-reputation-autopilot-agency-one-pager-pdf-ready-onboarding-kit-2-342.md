TWO MINI CASE STUDIES (ANONYMIZED, READY TO PASTE)

Case Study 1 — Home Services (HVAC / Plumbing), Single Location
Problem
The owner was averaging 5–8 new reviews/week but replying inconsistently. Negative reviews sometimes sat unanswered for 10+ days, creating a “they don’t care” impression.
What we did
• Set up daily monitoring for Google Business Profile.
• Implemented a 24-hour draft SLA with an escalation rule for 1–2 star reviews.
• Standardized a calm, accountability-first tone with clear next steps (“Please contact our manager at… so we can resolve this”).
Results (first 30 days)
• Response time: ~10 days → under 24 hours (median)
• Response rate: inconsistent → 100% of new reviews drafted
• Negative reviews: faster de-escalation; several reviewers updated comments after follow-up
• Owner time saved: ~2–3 hours/week

Case Study 2 — Med Spa, Two Locations
Problem
Front desk staff were replying emotionally to some reviews and ignoring others. Tone inconsistency risked brand perception and repeat bookings.
What we did
• Built a “premium & concierge” voice guide with approved phrases and banned language.
• Added approval-required workflow for 1–2 star reviews and sensitive topics.
• Delivered a weekly KPI email highlighting themes (wait time, pricing clarity, aftercare questions).
Results (first 6 weeks)
• Brand consistency: replies became uniform across both locations
• Response speed: same-week replies → next-day replies (median)
• Reputation narrative: public replies addressed recurring concerns with empathy + concrete action
• Team efficiency: reduced internal back-and-forth; fewer ad-hoc reply decisions
