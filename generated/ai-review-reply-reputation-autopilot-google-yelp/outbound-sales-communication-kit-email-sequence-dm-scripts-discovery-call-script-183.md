3) 2-MINUTE DISCOVERY CALL SCRIPT (Owner-led)
========================================

GOAL
- Confirm pain, platforms (GBP/Yelp), volume, who approves negatives, and close to pilot.

OPEN (15 seconds)
“Thanks for the time, [NAME]. I’ll ask a few quick questions about reviews on Google/Yelp, then I’ll explain how our autopilot works and what a 7-day pilot would look like. Sound good?”

DIAGNOSE (60 seconds)
1) “Which matters more for you right now—getting more reviews, or handling/responding to the reviews you already get?”
2) “About how many new reviews do you get in a typical week on Google? Any Yelp volume?”
3) “Who responds today, and what’s the usual response time?”
4) “When you get a 1–3 star, who should be notified and what’s the best contact method?”
5) “Do you want us to draft only, or draft + post with manager access/approval?”

PITCH (30 seconds)
“Here’s the system: we monitor your Google Business Profile (and Yelp if needed), generate brand-safe replies in your voice, and either queue them for approval or post them under agreed rules. Any negative review triggers an escalation with a suggested response and an internal note for your team. Then every week you get a KPI email: new reviews, average rating trend, response time, and escalations.”

CLOSE (15 seconds)
“Next step is a 7-day pilot. We set your tone, escalation contacts, and SLA today. Want draft-only or draft+post?”

COMMON OBJECTIONS (quick responses)
- “We already respond sometimes.”
“Totally—this is about consistency and speed. Even good teams miss weekends/holidays. We make it automatic, policy-safe, and measurable with weekly KPIs.”

- “I don’t want robotic replies.”
“Agreed. We build a voice guide and use templates that sound human. You’ll see samples first; we iterate until it matches your brand.”

- “I’m worried about saying the wrong thing publicly.”
“That’s exactly why we use compliant language and escalation rules. Negatives get de-escalated and moved offline; we never argue or mention protected details.”
