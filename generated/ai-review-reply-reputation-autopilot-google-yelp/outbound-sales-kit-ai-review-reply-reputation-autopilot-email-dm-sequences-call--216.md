DISCOVERY CALL SCRIPT (10–12 minutes)
--------------------------------------------
Goal: qualify, identify pain, set scope, close to pilot or first month.

1) Opening (30 sec)
“Thanks for the time. In 10 minutes I’ll understand your review volume + process, show how our autopilot works, and if it’s a fit we’ll pick either a short pilot or monthly coverage. Sound good?”

2) Quick context questions (2–3 min)
• “Which matters more to you right now: Google, Yelp, or both?”
• “How many locations?”
• “Roughly how many new reviews per week?”
• “Who replies today—and how consistently?”
• “Any recent negative review that felt risky or time-consuming?”

3) Problem framing (1 min)
Summarize: “So the main issue is speed + consistency, and negatives require careful handling. That’s exactly what we solve.”

4) Explain the workflow (2 min)
“Our system monitors new reviews, drafts brand-safe replies aligned to your tone, and routes 1–3★ for escalation. You can run draft-only, or we can post with manager access and an approval rule for negatives. Every week you get a KPI snapshot: new reviews, response rate/time, sentiment, and flagged issues.”

5) Offer selection (2 min)
Option A: Draft-Only (safest)
• “We draft replies and you approve/post.”
Option B: Draft + Post
• “We post on your behalf for speed, with strict escalation/approval for negatives.”

Clarify SLA:
• “Standard SLA is within 24 hours (business days). Faster options exist for higher volume.”

6) Objections (2 min)
Objection: “Can’t I just use templates?”
Reply: “Templates work for 5★, but they break on nuanced or negative reviews. We keep tone consistent, personalize safely, and escalate issues so you don’t accidentally admit fault or trigger policy problems. Speed + judgment is the value.”

Objection: “Will this violate Google/Yelp policies?”
Reply: “We do not gate, incentivize, or manipulate reviews. We only respond to reviews and report KPIs. For Yelp, we can run draft-only if you want maximum control.”

7) Close (30 sec)
“Based on your volume, I recommend {{Plan}}. Next step: I’ll send a short onboarding form today (tone, escalation contacts, access). We can start within 1 business day. Want to begin with a 7-day pilot or go straight to monthly?”
