8) Discovery Call Script (30 minutes)
========================
Goal: qualify fast, confirm decision-maker, confirm review volume + risk, land on a pilot or monthly.

0–3 min: Frame
• “Thanks for the time. I’ll ask a few quick questions about your review volume, who handles replies today, and what tone you want. Then I’ll show the workflow and we’ll decide if a small pilot makes sense.”

3–10 min: Current state
1) “Which platforms matter most—Google, Yelp, Facebook?”
2) “Roughly how many new reviews do you get per week/month?”
3) “Who replies today, and what usually causes delays?”
4) “Any recent negative review that was tricky to handle?”
5) “What’s the brand voice you want? (friendly, premium, clinical, no-nonsense, etc.)”

10–18 min: Stakes + outcomes
6) “When someone reads reviews, what do you want them to feel?”
7) “What’s the biggest risk—saying the wrong thing, missing reviews, or not having time?”
8) “If replies were handled same-day, what would that change for you?”

18–25 min: Pitch the workflow (simple)
• “We monitor new reviews daily. Positive reviews get a fast on-brand reply. Negative/sensitive reviews get escalated for approval with 2–3 draft options. You choose: draft-only (you post) or draft+post (we post with your permission). Weekly you get KPIs: new reviews, response time, rating trend, sentiment.”

25–30 min: Close
Option A (pilot):
• “Want to start with a 7-day pilot so you can see the tone and turnaround before committing monthly?”
Option B (monthly):
• “Based on your volume, the best fit is [Tier]. We can start [date] once we have access + brand voice answers.”

9) Objection Handlers (short)
========================
“Can’t you just use ChatGPT?”
• “You can draft text anywhere—the value here is the operational system: daily monitoring, consistent brand voice, escalation/approval for negatives, and reporting so nothing falls through.”

“We’re worried about posting something wrong.”
• “Totally fair. We use an escalation matrix: anything negative/sensitive is ‘draft-only’ until you approve. You can also run draft-only for all reviews if you prefer.”

“We don’t get many reviews.”
• “That’s usually ideal—cheap to keep consistent. Even 2–5 reviews/week is enough to protect your rating and conversion.”

“We already have someone respond.”
• “Great—this can remove the busywork. We standardize voice, speed up replies, and make sure tricky cases are handled carefully with approvals.”
