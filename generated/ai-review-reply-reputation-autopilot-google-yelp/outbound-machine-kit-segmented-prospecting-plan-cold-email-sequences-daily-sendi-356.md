Core trigger segments (apply to Google Business Profile and/or Yelp):
Segment 1 — Not Responding:
- Response-rate proxy <= 20% in last ~10 reviews OR no owner responses visible recently.
Why it converts: the “easy win” is obvious; owners know responsiveness affects trust + rankings.

Segment 2 — Low Rating:
- Rating < 4.2 (or visible recent 1–2 star reviews).
Why it converts: immediate revenue impact; need brand-safe de-escalation and escalation workflow.

Segment 3 — High Volume:
- Review count >= 200 OR last review within 14 days (high review velocity).
Why it converts: workload pain; needs an ops system and speed SLA.

Priority scoring (simple and operational):
Priority A: (Not Responding AND High Volume) OR (Low Rating AND High Volume)
Priority B: Not Responding only OR Low Rating only
Priority C: High Volume only

Routing to email variant:
- Not Responding → “response gap” email angle + speed/SLA offer.
- Low Rating → “protect brand + escalate negatives” angle + approval workflow.
- High Volume → “ops throughput + weekly KPI reporting” angle.

Offer positioning (use in email):
- "We draft brand-safe replies for Google/Yelp, post fast (or send for approval), escalate negatives, and send a weekly KPI summary."
- Concrete promise: respond within 12 hours (business hours) OR within 1 business day.
- Low-friction CTA: "Want me to show you 2–3 draft replies for your latest reviews?"

5) Daily Sending Ops Checklist (14-day ramp + rules)

List QA (before sending):
- Verify business is truly in vertical (not a supplier, school, franchise HQ, directory).
- Confirm Google rating + review count + last review date.
- Capture response-rate proxy from last ~10 reviews: (# reviews with owner reply) / 10.
- Add segment tag + Priority A/B/C.
- Add personalization snippet (quote short or paraphrase).

Ramp schedule (per inbox; keep conservative to protect deliverability):
Days 1–2: 10–15 new emails/day + 5–10 replies/follow-ups
Days 3–4: 20/day
Days 5–7: 30/day
Week 2: 40–60/day (only if bounces < 3% and spam complaints ~0)

Reply-handling SLA:
- Respond to positive replies within 2 business hours.
- For “not now” replies: ask permission to follow up in 60–90 days.
- For objections: offer to send 2 sample replies and a 1-page workflow.

Bounce/complaint thresholds (stop and fix if triggered):
- Hard bounce rate > 3% in a day → pause new sends, re-check list quality.
- Any spam complaints → reduce volume and tighten targeting.

Follow-up rules:
- Always follow up even if no open tracking.
- Send Email 2 two business days after Email 1; Email 3 four to six business days after Email 2.
- If they reply at any point, stop sequence and move to “Replied”.
