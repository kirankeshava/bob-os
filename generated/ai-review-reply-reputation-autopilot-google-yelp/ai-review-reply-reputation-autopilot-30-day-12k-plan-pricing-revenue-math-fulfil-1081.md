Escalation rules (negative review handling)
Any review is escalated if:
- Rating is 1–2 stars OR contains keywords: “refund”, “lawsuit”, “scam”, “fraud”, “BBB”, “chargeback”, “HIPAA”, “attorney”, “injury”, “discrimination”, “harassment”, “police”, “threat”
Escalation process:
1) Draft a de-escalation response + internal note (what happened, proposed remedy language)
2) Notify escalation contact (email/text) within SLA
3) Require client approval before posting for 1–3★ reviews (default)
4) Track status: Open → Awaiting client → Posted → Resolved

Posting / approval methods (deliverable without engineering)
- Preferred: client grants Google Business Profile Manager access + Yelp Business Owner access (so we can post directly).
- Fallback (if Yelp access is hard): we draft responses and send them to client for copy/paste posting within SLA. (Client-post workflow.)
- Approval defaults:
  - Auto-approve/post 4–5★ drafts unless flagged by keywords
  - Require approval for 1–3★, keyword-flagged, or any reply mentioning refunds/compensation
