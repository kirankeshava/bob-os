## 1) 20-minute Discovery Call Script (readable talk track)
**Goal:** qualify, quantify revenue impact, de-risk “AI” objection, close for **Setup + Month 1 upfront**.

### Minute 0–2: Opening + Agenda
“Thanks for taking the time. I’m Bob. We help local businesses respond to Google and Yelp reviews quickly and safely—especially when negative reviews hit—so ratings and conversions don’t leak. Quick agenda: (1) your current review volume + who responds, (2) impact on calls/appointments, (3) how our workflow reduces risk, and (4) if it’s a fit, we’ll pick a plan and I’ll send a proposal + invoice today. Sound good?”

### Minute 2–6: Current state (diagnosis)
1) “Which platforms matter most for you—Google Business Profile, Yelp, or both?”
2) “Approx how many reviews do you get per month?” (Google ___ / Yelp ___)
3) “Do you respond to every review today? If not, what % get a response?”
4) “How fast do you typically respond to a new review—same day, days, weeks?”
5) “Who’s responsible right now? Owner, front desk, marketing, agency?”
6) “Any past issues: a bad reply, a policy violation, an argument thread, or a review that cost you business?”

### Minute 6–10: Revenue impact + ROI prompts
“Let’s translate reviews into money for a second.”
- “Roughly how many inbound calls/appointments come from Google per month?”
- “What’s an average new customer value (or first visit value)?”
- “If your rating moved from X to X+0.2 or your response rate went from Y% to near 100%, what would 1–2 extra customers/month mean?”

**Anchors you can say (choose 1):**
- “Most buyers read recent reviews and judge responsiveness as a proxy for service. If you’re not responding consistently—especially to negatives—you’re leaving conversions on the table.”
- “A single unresolved negative review can sit at the top for months. The fix is speed + tone + escalation.”

### Minute 10–14: Platform access + operational fit
“Operationally, we need two things.”
1) “Can you add us as a manager on Google Business Profile for each location?”
2) “For Yelp, do you have a claimed owner account? We can work through approved access or via a managed workflow depending on what Yelp allows for your setup.”

“Do you need approvals on every reply, or can we auto-post within guardrails?” (Pick one)
- **Auto-post mode:** “We post immediately using your brand voice + prohibited topics list; negatives escalate.”
- **Approval mode:** “We draft; you approve via email; then we post.”

### Minute 14–17: AI-risk objection handling (brand safety)
If they say **“AI scares me / we can’t risk it”**:
“Totally fair. We don’t do ‘AI free-for-all.’ We use a safety workflow: brand voice calibration, prohibited phrases/topics, escalation triggers, and a human review option. Negative reviews automatically escalate to a named contact before anything sensitive is posted. We also keep an audit log of what was drafted/posted.”

If they say **“We can do this ourselves”**:
“You absolutely can. The question is consistency and speed. Most teams stop replying when busy, and that’s when negatives hit. We’re buying back your time and protecting conversion rates—while making sure nothing off-brand goes out.”

If they say **“Is this allowed on Google/Yelp?”**:
“We’re responding as your business with authorized access, following platform guidelines. We never fake reviews, never incentivize reviews, and we avoid prohibited claims. The goal is professional, compliant responses and fast escalation.”

### Minute 17–20: Close (two-option close)
“Based on what you shared—__ reviews/month and response gaps—this is a fit. The two best options are:
- **Plan A:** [Tier that matches their volume] for __/month
- **Plan B:** the next tier up if you want faster SLA / more volume.

We start with a one-time setup to calibrate your voice and escalation rules, then we go live within 48 hours once access is granted. If you’re good, I’ll email the proposal and invoice today—**setup + first month upfront**—and we’ll schedule onboarding.”

**If they hesitate on price:**
“Totally. If this prevents even 1 lost customer a month, it usually pays for itself. Do you want to start on the smaller tier and upgrade after two weeks once you see the workflow?”

**Objective:** go-live within 48 hours of payment + access.

- Brand voice: 3 adjectives (e.g., warm, professional, concise)
- Do-not-say list (phrases, sensitive topics)
- Escalation contacts (name, phone, email) + hours

### Step B — Access
**Google Business Profile:** client adds agent_bob_replit+review-bot@agentmail.to as Manager for each location.
**Yelp:** client provides owner access or preferred workflow (draft-to-approve if posting access is limited).

### Step C — Calibration
- Create 10 response examples (5 positive, 3 neutral, 2 negative) in client voice
- Client approves or edits; we lock tone rules
- Confirm escalation triggers

### Step D — Go-live QA
- Verify we can see reviews and post/respond (or send drafts)
- Test one real reply end-to-end
- Confirm reporting cadence + recipient

### Step E — Weekly KPI Report (simple format)
- New reviews this week
- Average rating (start vs end)
- Response rate %
- Median response time
- # negatives escalated + status
- 3 suggested ops improvements (service fixes recurring in reviews)
