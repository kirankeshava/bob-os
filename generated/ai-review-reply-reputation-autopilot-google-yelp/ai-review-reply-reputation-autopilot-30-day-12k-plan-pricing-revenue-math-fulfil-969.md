Escalation rules (brand safety + revenue protection):
Auto-approve eligible reviews:
• 4–5★ reviews that do not mention sensitive topics, refunds, legal threats, protected health info, discrimination, or named employees in a complaint.

• Any 1–3★ review
• Any review mentioning: lawsuit/legal, refunds/chargebacks, injury, medical claims, discrimination, harassment, threats, fraud, or employee misconduct
Escalation ticket created when:
• 1–2★ review OR explicit request for refund OR claim of no-show/late/poor service OR any legal/safety keyword.
Escalation SLA:
• We notify the business within 2 business hours (Pro) / 6 business hours (Growth) with recommended response options + next step script.

Explicit exclusions (set expectations):
• We do not delete reviews, offer incentives for positive reviews, or “gate” review requests.
• We do not dispute/appeal reviews without explicit written authorization.
• We do not provide legal advice; legal-sensitive responses require client approval.

6) FULFILLMENT SOP OUTLINE (operator checklist)
Onboarding (Day 0–2):
1) Collect business info (name, address, categories, hours, top services, offers).
2) Brand voice settings: tone (friendly/professional), sign-off name, taboo phrases, competitor mentions, refund policy summary.
3) Access:
   • Google Business Profile: add agent as Manager (preferred)
   • Yelp Business Owner: add user (preferred) OR enable client-post workflow
4) Define escalation contacts: owner/manager phone + email.
5) Define approval rules (default: 4–5★ auto; 1–3★ approval required).

Daily operations (Mon–Fri):
1) Review capture: check Google/Yelp or use platform alerts.
2) Draft replies: generate 1–3 options; choose the safest on-brand one.
3) QA: policy check + tone check + avoid admissions of fault + no incentives.
4) Post or send to client:
   • If access: post directly.
   • If no access: email the client a “copy/paste packet” with links.
5) Escalations: create ticket, notify contact, track resolution.

Weekly report (every Monday):
• Send KPI scorecard: new reviews, average rating, response rate, median response time, negatives (1–2★), resolved escalations, top themes, and 1–3 recommendations.
