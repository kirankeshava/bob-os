A) 20-MIN DISCOVERY CALL SCRIPT (READ-ALONG)
====================
Goal: diagnose revenue impact + confirm fit + close on Setup + Month 1 paid today.

0:00–2:00 Opener + agenda
“Hi [Name], Bob here. Thanks for making time. Quick agenda: (1) I’ll ask a few questions about your review volume and how you handle replies today, (2) we’ll estimate what better responsiveness/ratings could mean for calls/appointments, and (3) if it’s a fit I’ll recommend one of two plans and we can get you set up today. Sound good?”
Legitimacy line (if needed): “And if you want to see what we do, here’s our site: https://6ff73b98-dcff-4f53-a10d-9ad392f63342-00-1n4h4nuiq6w8j-nnj9kx2i.picard.replit.dev/sites/1.”

2:00–8:00 Current state diagnosis (quant + workflow)
1) “Which platforms matter most for you—Google, Yelp, both?”
2) “Roughly how many reviews do you get per week/month?”
3) “Who replies today, and how quickly do replies usually go out?”
4) “Do you have any guidelines for tone/brand voice, or is it ad-hoc?”
5) “What happens when a 1–2 star review comes in—who gets alerted and what’s the process?”
6) “Any compliance constraints? (HIPAA/medical, legal, regulated industries) Any topics you never want mentioned?”

Qualification gates (say it plainly):
- “If you’re getting fewer than ~10 reviews/month and there’s no revenue sensitivity to ratings, this may be overkill. Does your rating and responsiveness affect bookings or calls?”
- “Do you have someone who can grant us manager access to Google Business Profile (and/or Yelp), or do you prefer we draft responses for approval without posting?”

8:00–13:00 Revenue impact framing (ROI prompts)
“Let’s translate this into revenue impact.
- About how many calls/appointments/leads do you get from Google each month?
- What’s an average new customer worth (or average ticket + repeat rate)?”

ROI math prompt (keep it conservative):
“Even a small lift—like more people choosing you because they see fast, professional replies and fewer unresolved negatives—can mean just a few extra bookings per month. If 3 extra customers/month are worth $X each, that’s $3X/month. Our goal is to systematically protect and improve that.”

13:00–16:00 Present solution + safety workflow (neutralize AI risk)
“What we do: we draft brand-safe responses to every review, post them (or route for approval), and we escalate negatives fast. You also get a weekly KPI email (response rate, median response time, rating trend, negative themes).

Safety/AI-risk controls:
- You choose approval mode: (1) Auto-post for 4–5 star only, (2) Manual approval for all, or (3) Hybrid.
- Hard rules: we never argue, never admit liability, never discuss private customer data, and we escalate certain triggers (refund demands, threats, discrimination claims, safety allegations).
- Audit trail: every drafted response is logged and can be reviewed.”

16:00–18:00 Objections (quick handling)
Objection: “We can do it ourselves.”
“Totally—most owners can. The question is consistency and speed. The benefit here is you get a system: every review gets handled in a brand-safe way, every time, without stealing owner/manager time, and negatives get escalated instantly.”

Objection: “AI risk / brand voice.”
“That’s why we start with brand voice intake + a ‘do/don’t’ library and you can require approval at first. We can run the first week in ‘approval mode’ and only switch to auto-post once you’re comfortable.”

Objection: “Will you need access?”
“Optional. If you want us to post, you grant manager access. If you prefer no access, we send drafted replies for copy/paste and still track KPIs.”

18:00–20:00 Close (two-option close + pay today)
“Based on what you shared, I’d recommend either:
- Plan A: [Starter/Growth] — covers your current review volume and gets replies out fast.
- Plan B: [Growth/Pro] — adds more volume and tighter SLAs.

Next step is simple: I’ll email a 1-page scope + invoice. We do Setup + Month 1 upfront, then monthly thereafter. Once paid, we start onboarding immediately and go live within 48 hours. Which plan do you want to start with?”
