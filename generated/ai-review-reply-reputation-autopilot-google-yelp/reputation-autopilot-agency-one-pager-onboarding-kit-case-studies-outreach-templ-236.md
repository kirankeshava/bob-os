CASE STUDY #1 — Multi‑Location Home Services (Google)
Problem
A 3‑location home services brand had inconsistent review replies. Owners responded sporadically, negative reviews sat for days, and sentiment in the review feed skewed negative even when issues were resolved offline.

What we did
• Implemented Reputation Autopilot with a clear brand voice (professional + solution-focused)
• Set escalation rules for 1–2 star reviews and “staff accusation” keywords
• Replied daily to new reviews and routed high‑risk reviews for approval
• Added weekly KPI reporting + theme tracking (pricing confusion, schedule delays)

Results (first 30 days)
• Response time: reduced from ~5–7 days to < 24 hours (Standard SLA)
• Response rate: increased from ~35% to > 90%
• Negative review handling: 100% of 1–2 star reviews escalated and addressed with consistent service-recovery language
• Outcome narrative: fewer “pile-on” comments; reviewers more frequently updated tone after resolution

Proof you can show (placeholders)

• KPI table: before/after response rate


CASE STUDY #2 — Med Spa (Yelp + Google)
Problem
A med spa received steady reviews but avoided replying due to HIPAA concerns and uncertainty about what to say publicly—especially on Yelp. Several 3‑star reviews mentioned wait times and “felt rushed,” creating a recurring theme.

What we did
• Built a “privacy-first” reply style (no treatment confirmation, invite to private resolution)
• Implemented Draft‑only mode for the first week, then switched to Draft+Post once approved
• Flagged any mentions of procedures, pricing disputes, or staff names for approval
• Delivered weekly theme insights to adjust front-desk scripting and appointment buffer time

Results (first 6 weeks)
• Response time: improved from “rarely replies” to replies within 4 business hours (Priority SLA)
• Review feed sentiment: more neutral/negative reviews received calm, brand-consistent replies (no defensive language)
• Operational impact narrative: “wait time” theme reduced as scheduling buffers were introduced (reported by staff, supported by fewer mentions in new reviews)
