## Day 0 (Same day as close)

### 0.1 Create the deal record (internal)
- Add prospect to CRM tracker with stage = **Closed Won** (or **Paid** if payment already collected).
- Confirm: locations count, primary decision maker, escalation contact, approval contact.

### 0.2 Send “Kickoff + Access” email (customer)
Include:
- Start date (today/tomorrow)
- Access requirements (GBP + Yelp)
- Intake form questions (below)
- What happens in the next 48 hours
- Ask for 2–3 example replies they like (if any)

### 0.3 Collect intake (customer → you)
Minimum required to start:
- Business name + locations
- Brand voice (friendly/professional), banned phrases, signature name
- Escalation rules (what must be escalated immediately)

### 0.4 Request access (customer forwards)
- GBP access: add us as Manager (or Primary Owner’s preferred role)
- Yelp access: invite to Business Account / add as user

## Day 1 (Setup + first drafts)

### 1.1 Confirm access + verify locations
- GBP: confirm correct listing(s), hours, phone, categories.
- Yelp: confirm claimed listing and permissions.

### 1.2 Configure Reply Rules (internal)
Set:
- Tone/voice guardrails
- “Never say” list
- Compensation policy language (avoid promises; route to offline)
- Escalation triggers (1-star, accusations, safety, discrimination, refund demand, legal threats, staff names)

### 1.3 Draft the first batch
- Pull last 10–20 reviews (or last 30 days) from GBP/Yelp.
- Produce:
  - 5 positive replies
  - 3 neutral replies
  - 2 negative replies + an escalation note for each

### 1.4 Approval loop (if required)
Send:
- 10 draft replies in one email (or doc)
- Ask for edits to tone + 1 approval: “Approve this style going forward?”

---

## Day 2 (Go live)

### 2.1 Post replies (or schedule)
- Post approved replies to GBP/Yelp.
- Maintain response consistency across platforms.

### 2.2 Enable ongoing workflow
- Agree on cadence:
  - Standard: respond within 24–48 hours
  - High volume: daily sweep
- Set escalation SLA (e.g., same day for 1-star)

### 2.3 Confirm First-Week Success Criteria (customer-facing)
By Day 7 we aim to show:
- **Response time reduced** (baseline vs. now)
- **% of reviews responded** (target 90–100% new reviews)
- **# escalations handled** (with outcomes tracked)
- **Rating trend** (if enough volume)
- **Operational insights**: recurring complaints, staff shout-outs, service quality themes

---

## First-week “green lights” (internal)
A location is considered **Activated** when:
- Access verified for GBP (and Yelp if applicable)
- Brand voice rules locked
- First 10 replies approved OR customer opts out of approvals
- Replies are being posted on cadence
- Weekly KPI report scheduled
