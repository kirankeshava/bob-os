# 5) Call Opener + Discovery Script (10–15 minutes)
## Opener (30 seconds)
“Thanks for the time. In 10 minutes I want to understand your current review volume and how you handle replies today. Then I’ll tell you exactly what we can take off your plate, what we need for access, and what it costs. Sound good?”

## Discovery (ask + why it matters)
1) “Which platforms matter most for you—Google, Yelp, Facebook?”
2) “Roughly how many reviews/week do you get?”
3) “Who replies today? How quickly?”
4) “Any past issues—angry customers, sensitive services, compliance concerns?”
5) “Do you have multiple locations now (or planned)?”
6) “What’s a ‘bad’ outcome for a reply? Any words/phrases we should never use?”
7) “If we could get you to near-100% response rate with fast replies, what would that be worth monthly?”

## Qualification gates (must-have)
- Google: “Can we get **Google Business Profile Manager access**?” (preferred)
- Yelp: “Do you have **Yelp Business Owner access** OR would you prefer we draft and you post?”
- Primary contact for escalations + best phone/email.

## Close (clear next step)
“Based on what you said, I recommend **{PLAN}**. Setup is {SETUP_FEE} and then {MONTHLY}. We can start this week if we get access + brand voice rules. Want me to send the onboarding checklist today?”

# 6) Objection Handling (quick, non-pushy)
### “We can just reply ourselves.”
“Totally. Most teams can—until it becomes inconsistent. We’re buying you consistency + speed + a system for negative reviews. If you want, start with one month and judge by response time and rating trend.”

### “I don’t want AI saying the wrong thing.”
“Agreed. That’s why we do brand rules + banned phrases + a human QA step. Also, 1–3★ reviews can be approval-only so nothing goes out without your OK.”

### “Yelp is a pain / we don’t want to give passwords.”
“No problem. Week 1 we can operate in ‘draft + you post’ mode. If later you want full posting automation, we can switch once access is in place.”

### “What’s the ROI?”
“For local businesses, ratings + responsiveness impact calls and bookings. The KPI report shows response rate/time, rating trend, and themes—so you can tie improvements to conversions over time.”
