What we do
We help local businesses respond to Google and Yelp reviews quickly, consistently, and brand-safely. The system drafts responses, flags high-risk reviews for escalation, and generates weekly reputation KPIs.

Brand safety and “no surprises” controls
1) No liability admission: Drafts avoid wording that admits fault, confirms damage, or accepts legal responsibility. We use neutral, service-recovery language and move sensitive issues offline.
2) Medical/HIPAA-safe behavior (for health-related businesses): Drafts do not confirm patient status, appointments, records, treatments, outcomes, or any PHI. If a reviewer mentions PHI, responses remain generic and do not acknowledge the underlying details.
3) No prohibited incentives: We do not offer discounts, gifts, or compensation in exchange for reviews, and we do not encourage review gating. Language that can be interpreted as “leave a review for a reward” is blocked.
4) No doxxing or personal data: Drafts will not include phone numbers for staff, addresses beyond public business info, patient/client details, or any sensitive identifiers.
5) De-escalation by design: For negative reviews, we avoid arguments, accusations, or “public back-and-forth.” Responses include a calm acknowledgment and a clear invitation to continue offline.

Escalation & manual-only holds (high-risk scenarios)
Some reviews should not be posted to automatically. The system escalates and/or blocks posting for manual review when it detects:
- Legal threats (e.g., “lawsuit,” “attorney,” “sue”)
- Safety incidents or allegations of injury
- PHI/medical record references (e.g., “my chart,” “my visit,” “records”) in a way that could cause confirmation risk
- Harassment/hate/discrimination claims
- Allegations of fraud, theft, or serious misconduct

In these cases the system sets response mode to HOLD (manual-only). A manager/owner must approve a final response before anything is posted.

Audit trail & accountability
Every draft and action is logged so you can prove what happened:
- Review source (Google/Yelp), review ID, and text hash
- Detected risk flags and escalation level
- Draft version, prompt/model version
- Human approver ID + approval timestamp
- Post status (posted vs blocked/manual review) + error codes

Weekly reporting (what you get)
- Response rate and median/average first-response time
- Rating trend (7/30 day) and volume
- Sentiment buckets and escalation counts by reason
- Reconciliation: drafted vs approved vs posted vs blocked

Platform guideline alignment (plain-English)
- We do not promise review removal or claim special influence with Yelp/Google.
- We do not solicit incentivized reviews.
- We avoid competitor attacks and do not “call out” reviewers.
- We encourage resolution offline for disputes.

Sales Call Script (15 minutes)

Goal: Sell safety + time savings + measurable KPI improvement without overpromising.

“Thanks—quick agenda: I’ll ask how you handle reviews now, show how our drafts + escalation works, and if it’s a fit we’ll outline a simple pilot.”

2) Current state (3 minutes)
- “Who replies to reviews today?”
- “How fast do you typically respond?”
- “Any past situations where replying publicly made things worse?”
- “Do you manage both Google and Yelp?”

3) Value proposition (2 minutes)
“We draft on-brand replies, detect risky content, and require offline resolution language for negatives. For high-risk items (legal threats/PHI/safety) we block auto-posting and route to manual approval.”

4) Demo narrative (5 minutes)
- Show: positive review reply (warm, brief)
- Show: negative review reply (acknowledge, no admission, offline CTA)
- Show: escalation example (legal threat → HOLD/manual-only + escalation_level=Legal)
- Show: audit log concept (who approved, what posted, what was blocked)

5) Compliance positioning (2 minutes)
“We don’t offer incentives for reviews, we don’t promise removal, and we don’t confirm private details. The system is designed to keep you aligned with platform expectations and reduce liability risk.”

6) Close (2 minutes)
Option A (Pilot): “We can run a 14-day pilot: drafting only for the first week (approval workflow), then post-approved replies week two. You’ll get a weekly KPI report.”
Option B (Done-for-you): “If you want zero effort, you approve from a queue and we handle the rest.”

Objection handling (quick responses)
- “We can do this ourselves.” → “Totally—this mainly buys speed, consistency, and risk control. Most owners can reply; the problem is doing it daily and safely under stress.”
- “I’m worried about saying the wrong thing.” → “That’s exactly why we use blocked phrases + manual-only holds for legal/PHI/safety scenarios. High-risk never auto-posts.”
- “Will Yelp/Google penalize this?” → “We avoid incentives, review gating, and removal promises. Replies are written to de-escalate and move resolution offline.”
- “Can you remove bad reviews?” → “No—we won’t claim we can. We focus on professional responses and internal escalation to resolve underlying issues.”
