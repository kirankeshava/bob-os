1) 20-MINUTE DISCOVERY CALL SCRIPT (REVENUE IMPACT)
====================================================
Goal: qualify fast, quantify revenue impact, address AI risk, and close for setup + Month 1 upfront.

0:00–1:00 Open + permission
“Thanks for the time — I’m Bob. Before we jump in: this is a 20-minute working session. I’ll ask a few questions about reviews, how you handle them today, and what an improvement would be worth in calls/appointments. If it’s a fit, I’ll recommend a plan and the next step is setup + first month to start within 48 hours. Sound fair?”

1:00–3:00 Context + current process
1) “Which platforms matter most for you: Google Business Profile, Yelp, or both?”
2) “Roughly how many reviews do you get per month across those?”
3) “Who responds today, and how quickly do you typically respond?”
4) “Do you have any brand guidelines or words you avoid?”

3:00–8:00 Revenue impact (ROI prompts)
Use whichever applies:
- “When someone checks your Google/Yelp, what do you want them to do next — call, book online, request a quote?”
- “Do you track how many calls/appointments come from Google Business Profile?”
- “If your rating moved from X to X+0.2, what would that be worth monthly? Even one extra job/client covers most reputation tools.”
- “What’s your average ticket / customer value?”
- “If improving response speed and consistency produced just +2 extra customers/month, what’s that worth?”
(Do quick math live: 2 customers × avg value = $____/mo.)

8:00–11:00 Pain + urgency
- “What’s hardest today: speed, knowing what to say, staying consistent, or dealing with negative reviews?”
- “Any recent 1–3 star reviews that created stress or lost business?”
- “On a scale of 1–10, how important is improving review response this month?”

11:00–14:00 Product fit + safety/AI risk handling
“Here’s how our autopilot works:
1) We draft brand-safe responses for every new review on Google/Yelp.
2) Negative reviews trigger escalation so you can decide before anything posts.
3) We report weekly KPIs: response rate, response time, rating trend, negative review resolution.

Key question: do you want full autopilot, or approval before posting?”

If objection: “AI risk / I don’t want a robot embarrassing us.”
“Totally fair. We run guardrails:
- We calibrate tone from your best existing responses.
- We maintain a ‘do-not-say’ list (pricing, legal, medical claims, competitor mentions, refunds, etc.).
- Negative reviews are escalated; you approve before anything sensitive.
- We keep an audit log of what was posted and when.
Most clients start with approval mode for 1–2 weeks, then switch to autopilot once it feels safe.”

14:00–16:00 Qualification gates (must-haves)
- “Can you add us as a Manager on Google Business Profile?”
- “For Yelp: can you grant access or we can provide drafts for you to paste?”
- “Who should be the escalation contact for 1–2 star reviews?”
- “Any compliance constraints (medical, legal, financial)?”
If they can’t grant access: offer ‘Draft-Only’ mode (you email/Slack drafts).

16:00–18:00 Recommend plan (two-option close)
“Based on your volume and the fact that (pain point), I’d recommend either:
Option A: Starter — best if you get ~50 reviews/month and want consistent replies.
Option B: Growth — best if you want faster SLA and more volume coverage.
Either way, we start with a one-time setup and collect Month 1 upfront. We can be live within 48 hours after access + brand voice intake.”

18:00–20:00 Close + next steps
“If we do this, are you comfortable moving forward today so we can start within 48 hours?”
If yes: “Great — I’ll email the order form + invoice to pay setup + Month 1. Once paid, you’ll receive our intake + access steps. What’s the best email?”
