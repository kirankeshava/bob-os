1) Compliance & Brand-Safety Overview (Customer-Facing One-Pager)

What this is
AI Review Reply & Reputation Autopilot drafts (and, when enabled, posts) brand-safe responses to Google Business Profile and Yelp reviews. It escalates negative or high-risk reviews for human handling and generates weekly reputation KPIs.

Core safety principles (built-in)
A) No private-data confirmation (PHI/PII safe)
- The system is designed to avoid confirming whether someone is a patient/client/customer.
- It avoids referencing appointment/visit details, charts/records, treatment specifics, or any information not explicitly stated in the public review.
- When a review mentions sensitive info, the response is forced into generic wording and encourages offline contact.

B) No liability admission
- Responses avoid admitting fault (“we messed up,” “our technician damaged…,” “we caused…”) or making definitive factual findings.
- Language is framed as: “We’re sorry to hear this,” “We’d like to learn more,” “Please contact us so we can look into it.”

C) No inflammatory or argumentative tone
- Responses are non-retaliatory, do not shame the reviewer, and do not escalate conflicts publicly.
- The default is empathetic + brief + take it offline.

D) No prohibited incentives or review gating
- Responses never offer discounts, freebies, refunds, or gifts in exchange for reviews.
- No “leave us a 5-star review” prompts; no filtering (“contact us first before posting”).

E) Platform policy alignment (Google Business Profile + Yelp)
- No promises to remove reviews or references to “we’ll get Yelp/Google to take this down.”
- No competitor attacks or comparisons.
- No doxxing: no staff personal data, no customer personal data, no addresses/phone numbers unless the business chooses to include a generic public contact.

Escalation & human control
- Negative reviews and high-risk topics can be auto-flagged for escalation (billing disputes, safety issues, discrimination claims, suspected fake reviews, threats/harassment).
- Legal-threat language (e.g., “lawsuit,” “attorney,” “sue”) triggers a “manual-only hold,” meaning: the system will not post automatically. It requires an authorized human approver.

Audit trail & accountability
For every review, the system can retain an audit record including:
- Source (Google/Yelp), review ID, timestamps
- Detected risk flags and escalation level
- Draft version history
- Human approval identity + timestamp (if approvals are enabled)
- Posted status (posted / blocked_manual_review / error), and final response text
This supports operational oversight and helps ensure consistent, policy-safe responses.

Important note
This product provides drafting and workflow support. It is not legal advice or medical advice. Customers control whether posting is automatic or approval-based.

3) Customer Onboarding + Approval Workflow (Copy for onboarding doc)

Step 1 — Define your “posting mode”
Choose one:
A) Approval-first (recommended for regulated or risk-sensitive businesses)
- The system drafts responses.
- A human approver must click Approve before anything posts.

B) Auto-post for low-risk, manual for high-risk
- Positive/neutral reviews can auto-post.
- Negative/high-risk reviews route to approval.
- Legal-threat language always triggers manual-only hold (no posting).

Step 2 — Set your escalation contacts + SLAs
Provide who should receive escalations:
- Operations / Customer Care (service issues)
- Billing (pricing/refund disputes)
- Owner/GM (reputation-critical)
- Legal (threats, harassment, litigation language)

Step 3 — Confirm safety constraints (non-negotiables)
We will not publish replies that:
- Confirm patient/client status or reference records/visits
- Promise medical outcomes or guaranteed results
- Offer incentives for reviews or request only positive reviews
- Threaten, retaliate, or argue with reviewers
- Disclose personal data (yours or the reviewer’s)

Step 4 — What happens on a “manual-only hold”
If a review contains legal-threat indicators (e.g., “attorney,” “lawsuit,” “sue”) or other high-risk triggers, the system:
- Marks the item as blocked_manual_review
- Requires a designated human approver to decide next steps
- Recommends taking the conversation offline and gathering facts internally

Step 5 — Audit trail & reporting
Each drafted/posted response can be logged with:
- Review source and ID, timestamps
- Risk flags + escalation reason
- Draft version and final response text
- Approver identity (if approvals enabled)
- Posted/blocked/error status
Weekly KPIs include response rate, response time, rating trend, sentiment buckets, and escalations by reason.
