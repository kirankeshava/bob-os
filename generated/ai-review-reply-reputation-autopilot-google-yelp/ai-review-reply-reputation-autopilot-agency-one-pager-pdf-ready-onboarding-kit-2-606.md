# 3) TWO MINI CASE STUDIES (ANONYMIZED; READY TO PASTE)

## Case Study 1 — Home Services (HVAC)
**Problem:** Reviews were coming in weekly but responses were sporadic. Several 1★–2★ reviews sat unanswered, and positive reviews weren’t reinforced. Owner had no time and no consistent tone across staff.

**What we did:**
- Set up daily monitoring and a consistent “owner-team” voice
- Implemented an escalation rule: 1★–2★ → same-day draft + manager call-back template
- Standardized replies to 4★–5★ reviews to reinforce service quality and encourage re-booking

**Results (first 30 days):**
- Response time improved from “often not responded” to **<24–48 hours**
- Negative reviews received calm, resolution-forward replies with offline follow-up
- Weekly report surfaced repeat complaint themes (arrival windows + communication), giving the owner clear ops fixes

## Case Study 2 — Med Spa
**Problem:** High review volume with sensitive topics. Staff replies risked sounding defensive or accidentally revealing appointment details. Several “mixed” 3★ reviews were ignored, missing an easy recovery opportunity.

**What we did:**
- Built a compliance-safe voice guide (no treatment confirmation, no client detail)
- Replied to 3★ reviews with empathy + invite to private resolution
- Escalated any medical/privacy comments for approval before posting

**Results (first 6 weeks):**
- Reply consistency improved to near-complete coverage within SLA
- Reduced risk in public responses via a repeatable, policy-safe template system
- Improved customer sentiment in follow-ups (more “thank you” replies and edited reviews after resolution, where applicable)
