3) 20-MIN DISCOVERY SCRIPT (FAST CASH COLLECTION)
Goal: qualify, quantify impact, confirm access + risk tolerance, close on “setup + month 1 upfront.”

Minute 0–2: Set agenda
“Thanks for taking the time. In 20 minutes I want to (1) understand how reviews affect bookings here, (2) see your current review response process, (3) show what ‘brand-safe autopilot’ looks like, and if it’s a fit, (4) pick a plan and get you live in 48 hours. Sound good?”

Minute 2–8: Diagnose current state
1) “Roughly how many Google reviews do you get per month? Yelp?”
2) “What % do you reply to today? How fast?”
3) “Who writes responses—and how often does it slip?”
4) “How do you handle a 1–2 star review today? Any SLA?”
5) “Do you ever ask happy customers to leave reviews? (Even light prompts?)”

Minute 8–12: Revenue impact (get numbers)
1) “Do calls/appointments come directly from Google Maps/GBP?”
2) “If your rating moved from {{x}} to {{x+0.2}} over time, would that be meaningful?”
3) “What’s one booked job/appointment worth on average?”
4) “If consistent replies prevented even 1 lost customer/month—or converted 1 extra—would this pay for itself?”

Minute 12–16: Show process + safety (handle AI risk)
“Our system is not ‘let AI freestyle.’ It’s: brand voice rules + prohibited topics + escalation triggers + approval mode.
Approval modes:
A) Manual: you approve every reply.
B) Post-approval: we post, you can edit, negatives always escalated.
C) Autopilot: we post under guardrails; negatives escalated.
Escalation triggers: profanity, threats, refund demands, discrimination allegations, injury claims, HIPAA/medical specifics, legal threats.

Minute 16–18: Access + feasibility
“To do this, we’ll need manager access to Google Business Profile and the appropriate access for Yelp. If you prefer, we can start in ‘draft-only’ mode via email until access is set.”

Minute 18–20: Close (two options)
“Based on what you said, I recommend either {{Plan A}} or {{Plan B}}. We collect the setup fee + first month upfront, and we can be live within 48 hours once access + brand voice are in. Want to start with {{Plan}}?”

Core objections (short answers)
- “We can do it ourselves.” → “Totally. Most teams can—but it slips when busy. We’re buying consistency + speed + escalation + reporting. If it doesn’t save time or protect rating, you should cancel.”
- “AI might say something wrong.” → “That’s why we use guardrails + prohibited topics + escalation + your chosen approval mode. We can start manual for 2 weeks and switch to autopilot after we calibrate.”
- “Too expensive.” → “If one additional booked job/appointment covers the month, it’s justified. If not, we’re not a fit.”
