B) DISCOVERY CALL SCRIPT (10–12 MIN) + CLOSE
========================

Goal: qualify fast, diagnose pain, propose the right SLA + plan, close to pilot or monthly.

1) Open (60 sec)
“Thanks for the time, [NAME]. Quick agenda: I’ll ask a few questions about your reviews + current process, then I’ll show how the Autopilot works and recommend a plan. If it’s a fit, we’ll decide next steps (pilot or monthly). Sound good?”

2) Context questions (3 min)
• “How important are reviews to new bookings for you—very, somewhat, or critical?”
• “Who replies to reviews today, and how often does it actually happen?”
• “Roughly how many reviews do you get per week across Google and Yelp?”
• “What’s the biggest issue: time, tone/wording, negative reviews, or just consistency?”

3) Pain amplification (2 min)
• “What happens internally when a bad review comes in—who owns it?”
• “How long does it typically sit before anyone responds?”
• “Have you ever had a review spiral into back-and-forth publicly?”

4) Positioning (60 sec)
“Here’s what we do: we run an AI-assisted, brand-safe review response system that drafts (and optionally posts) replies daily, escalates 1–3 star reviews immediately, and sends a weekly KPI report. It’s DFY operations powered by software—so it’s consistent without adding work to your team.”

5) Offer fit (2 min)
Recommend based on volume:
• “Based on [X] reviews/week and [#] locations, you’re a fit for [Tier]. That includes up to [cap] replies/month, [SLA], and weekly reporting.”

6) Compliance / safety line (30 sec)
“We keep everything policy-safe: no incentives, no gating, no asking people to change ratings. And for sensitive industries we use strict language rules.”

7) Close (2 min)
Option A — Pilot close:
“Want to start with a 7-day pilot? We’ll set your brand voice, reply daily, and you’ll get a weekly report. If you like it, we roll into monthly. If not, you keep the templates and drafts.”

Option B — Monthly close:
“If you’re ready to solve this permanently, we can start monthly right away. We’ll still treat week one like onboarding and you’ll see the process immediately.”

8) Next steps checklist (30 sec)
“To start we need: Google Business Profile manager access (or draft-only workflow), Yelp access if applicable, brand voice answers, and your escalation contact.”

Objection handling mini-scripts:
• “We already reply sometimes.”
“Totally. The gap is consistency + speed—especially on 1–3 star reviews. The Autopilot ensures you never miss one, and the KPI report shows whether response time is improving. Most teams *intend* to reply; very few do it daily.”

• “I’m worried about saying the wrong thing publicly.”
“That’s exactly why we built an approval + escalation rule-set. For negatives, we can default to ‘acknowledge + take offline’ language and route sensitive issues to you before posting.”

• “Why not just use ChatGPT?”
“Generic AI doesn’t know your do/don’t rules, escalation paths, or compliance requirements. Our system includes brand voice controls, review-type templates, negative escalation, and weekly KPI reporting—plus optional posting so it actually gets done.”

• “What does this cost?”
“We price per location and review volume band so it’s predictable. If you tell me locations + average reviews/month, I’ll confirm the exact tier in writing after this call.”
