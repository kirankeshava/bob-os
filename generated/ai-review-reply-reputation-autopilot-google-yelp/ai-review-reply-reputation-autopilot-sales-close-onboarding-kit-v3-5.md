1) 20-MIN DISCOVERY CALL SCRIPT (READABLE TALK TRACK)
=============================

Goal of the call: Confirm revenue impact + urgency, verify access feasibility, qualify volume/locations, and close for Setup + Month 1 upfront.

0:00–2:00 | Opener + Agenda
“Thanks for taking this. In 20 minutes I’d like to (1) understand your review volume and how reviews affect calls/appointments, (2) show you how we safely respond and escalate negatives, and (3) if it’s a fit, pick a plan and get you live in 48 hours. Sound good?”

2:00–6:00 | Current State (metrics that tie to revenue)
1) “Which platforms matter most for you today—Google, Yelp, or both?”
2) “Roughly how many new reviews do you get per week or per month?”
3) “Who responds today, and how quickly do replies usually go out?”
4) “What’s your current star rating on Google? On Yelp?”
5) “In the last 90 days, can you recall any lost business because of a negative review or lack of response?”

ROI prompts (use at least one)
- “If your rating moved from 4.2 to 4.5+, what would that mean in calls or booked appointments per week?”
- “What’s an average new customer worth (first purchase + typical repeat)?”
- “If we prevent even 1–2 lost customers per month, does this pay for itself?”

6:00–10:00 | Pain + Stakes
1) “What’s the hardest part: time, consistency, knowing what to say, or dealing with negatives?”
2) “When a negative review hits, what’s the current escalation path? Who needs to know?”
3) “Any compliance constraints (medical, legal, finance), brand voice rules, or words you never want used?”

Qualification gates (disqualify/redirect if needed)
- “Do you control the Google Business Profile? If not, who can add a manager?”
- “For Yelp: do you have owner access, or do you use an agency login?”
- “Do you want replies posted automatically after guardrails, or do you require approval for negatives (or all)?”

10:00–14:00 | Explain the Solution (simple + safety-first)
“Here’s what we do: We draft brand-safe responses for every review on Google and Yelp, post them under an approvals mode you choose, and we escalate any negative or sensitive reviews immediately to the right person. Every week you get a KPI email: new reviews, response rate, average response time, rating movement, and negative-review resolution notes.”

Safety workflow (to address AI risk before the objection)
- “We don’t ‘let AI freestyle.’ We use your brand voice rules + prohibited topics list.”
- “Negatives trigger escalation and optional human approval.”
- “We keep an audit log of what was drafted/posted.”

14:00–17:00 | Objection Handling (tight answers)

Objection: “We can do it ourselves.”
“Totally. The question is whether it happens consistently and fast enough to protect revenue. Most owners start strong, then reviews pile up. Our value is: 95–100% response rate, faster responses, fewer public back-and-forths on negatives, and a weekly KPI view—without you spending hours.”

Objection: “AI risk / I don’t want something posted that sounds weird.”
“Agreed—that’s why we use guardrails + approval modes. You can start in ‘Approve negatives only’ so nothing sensitive goes live without your OK. After 1–2 weeks, most clients move to autopost for positives + approve negatives.”

Objection: “What if someone mentions medical/legal/private info?”
“We treat that as a sensitive trigger: we won’t reference private details, and we escalate immediately. We can also default to generic responses that avoid specifics.”

Objection: “Price.”
“Two ways to look at it: One avoided lost customer can cover the month. Also, reputation compounds—response rate and tone affect conversion. If budget is tight, we start with fewer locations or a lower review cap and scale once you see results.”

17:00–19:00 | Close (2-option close)
“Based on your volume and the fact that you want [approval mode], I recommend either Plan A or Plan B. Plan A is $X/mo for up to __ reviews with __ SLA. Plan B is $Y/mo for up to __ reviews and faster response. Either way, it’s a one-time setup of $Z, and we collect setup + first month to start. Which one do you want to start with?”

19:00–20:00 | Next Steps + Commit
“Great. I’ll send a 1-page order form + the pay link. Once paid and signed, we send the intake + access requests the same day. We go live within 48 hours.”

6) 48-HOUR ONBOARDING SOP (GBP + YELP)
=============================

Onboarding Goal: Connected + voice calibrated + escalation routing set + go-live QA within 48 hours.

A) Intake Form (copy/paste questions)
1) Business name(s), location(s), website, primary services
2) Brand voice: (choose) Friendly / Professional / Warm / Luxury / No-nonsense
3) Do-not-use phrases or topics
4) Offers/promos to mention (if any) + any forbidden claims
5) Escalation contacts: name/role/email/phone (negatives + sensitive)
6) Approval mode choice (autopost / approve negatives / approve all)
7) Competitors or brands you like the tone of (optional)

B) Access Collection
Google Business Profile (GBP)
- Request: add us as “Manager” (or “Owner” if necessary) to the correct GBP location(s).
- Verify: we can view and reply to reviews.

Yelp
- Request: grant Business Owner/agent access or connect via approved method.
- Verify: we can view and reply to reviews.

C) Voice Calibration (same day)
- Create 5 sample responses: 3 positive, 1 neutral, 1 negative.
- Client approves/edits once; lock rules.

D) Escalation Rules (defaults)
Escalate if:
- ≤3 stars
- Mentions: refund, lawsuit, scam, fraud, discrimination, HIPAA/medical, injury, police, chargeback
- Profanity/hate speech

E) Go-Live QA (within 48 hours)
- Confirm autopost or approval workflow is working.
- Confirm escalation contact receives alerts.
- Send “Go Live” email including: plan limits, SLA, and what to expect weekly.
