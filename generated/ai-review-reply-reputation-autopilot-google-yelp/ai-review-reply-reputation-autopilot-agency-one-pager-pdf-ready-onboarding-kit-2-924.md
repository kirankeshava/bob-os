Case Study #1 — Home Services (HVAC/Plumbing) | “From days to same-day replies”
Problem
A local home services company had strong work quality but inconsistent review responses. Positive reviews sat unanswered for weeks, and the occasional 1-star complaint went unaddressed—hurting trust during high-season.

What we did
• Set a professional, calm brand voice with short, service-forward replies.
• Implemented daily monitoring + a 1–2 star escalation rule (approval required before posting).
• Responded to all new Google reviews within the selected SLA.

Results (first 30 days)
• Average response time improved from “multiple days” to same-day on business days.
• Response rate increased to near-complete coverage of new reviews.
• Negative reviews were acknowledged quickly, de-escalated, and routed to the ops manager—reducing public back-and-forth.

Proof you can swap in
• Screenshot of “Owner responded” badges
• Weekly KPI snapshot: response time + response rate


Case Study #2 — Med Spa/Aesthetics | “Brand-safe replies + fewer reputation fires”
Problem
A med spa received frequent reviews mentioning specific treatments and outcomes. The owner wanted to be responsive without risking compliance issues or sounding defensive in public.

What we did
• Built a “safe reply framework” avoiding medical claims and patient-specific details.
• Created an escalation list for sensitive topics (refunds, adverse reactions, privacy).
• Drafted replies that thanked, redirected to private resolution, and maintained a premium tone.

Results (first 6 weeks)
• Consistent brand voice across every review (Google + Yelp).
• Faster responses reduced the chance of negative threads escalating publicly.
• Weekly reporting surfaced recurring friction points (wait time, scheduling) so the team could fix root causes.

• Weekly KPI snapshot: rating trend + negative-review handling notes
