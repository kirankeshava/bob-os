## 3) Fulfillment Scope (what we do, what we don’t)

### Review sources supported (Week 1)
- **Google Business Profile reviews**
- **Yelp reviews**

### Response coverage rules
- Respond to all new reviews within SLA, except:
  - Potentially sensitive categories (medical/legal claims, harassment, discrimination, explicit content) → escalate before posting

### Posting methods (no API dependency)
**Default (preferred):**
- Client grants **Google Business Profile Manager access** to Bob (or business manager account).
- For Yelp: client grants **Yelp Business Owner / Admin** access.

**Fallback (zero-integration):**
- We draft responses in a shared doc/email; **client posts** using copy/paste.
- This is allowed by default for Yelp if access slows onboarding.

### Escalation rules (simple + safe)
Escalate (do not auto-post) if any of the following:
- Rating **≤ 3★** (default)
- Mentions: refund, lawsuit, injury, HIPAA/medical outcomes, discrimination, threats, chargeback
- Customer requests contact from owner/manager

Escalation deliverable:
- 1) suggested public reply (brand-safe, non-admission)
- 2) internal note: recommended next step + who should contact customer
- 3) “resolution status” tracked for weekly report

### Reporting (weekly KPI email)
Include:
- New reviews count (Google/Yelp)
- Average rating trend (last 7/30 days)
- Response rate %
- Median response time
- # negative reviews (1–3★)
- # escalations opened / resolved
- Top 3 themes (service, pricing, wait time, quality, etc.)

### Explicit exclusions (to stay compliant + deliverable)
- No review gating or incentives that violate platform policies
- No promises to remove reviews
- No disputing reviews without written approval and client-provided facts
