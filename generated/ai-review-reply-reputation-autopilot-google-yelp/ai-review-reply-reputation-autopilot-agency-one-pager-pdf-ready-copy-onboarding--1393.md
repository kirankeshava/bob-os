CASE STUDY MODULE #1 (ANONYMIZED) — HOME SERVICES (HVAC/PLUMBING)
SHORT VERSION (for landing page cards)
Problem: Reviews were going unanswered for weeks, and 1–2 star complaints weren’t getting triaged.
What we did: Set up daily review ingest, wrote on-brand replies, and escalated negatives to an ops manager with a 4-hour approval window.
Result (8 weeks): Median response time improved from ~6 days to <12 hours; multiple negative threads de-escalated into “thanks for making it right” updates; rating trend stabilized and new inbound calls referenced “quick owner responses.”

LONG VERSION (for proposals)
Client profile: Multi-tech home services company (1 location), busy dispatcher team.
Baseline: Replies were inconsistent; negative reviews were emotional and unstructured; owners rarely logged into GBP.
Implementation: We collected brand voice rules (calm, accountable, no excuses), created escalation triggers (1–2 stars, refund mentions, safety concerns), and ran Priority SLA.
Operational flow: Draft within hours → approver confirms facts → post → weekly report highlights recurring issues (missed appointment windows; billing confusion).
Outcomes: Faster response time, fewer public back-and-forths, and a weekly “issues surfaced” list that improved operations. The company used our report to update scripts and appointment reminders.

CASE STUDY MODULE #2 (ANONYMIZED) — MED SPA / AESTHETICS
SHORT VERSION
Problem: The owner feared responding incorrectly (privacy + tone risk) and avoided replies, especially on negatives.
What we did: Implemented privacy-safe templates, escalated any treatment-specific complaints, and maintained a luxury/premium tone.
Result (10 weeks): Response coverage went from sporadic to near-100%; negative reviews received fast, empathetic replies with private-resolution invites; rating narrative improved as prospects saw consistent professionalism.

LONG VERSION
Client profile: Single-location med spa with high-ticket services.
Baseline: Responses were rare; staff didn’t know what could be said publicly; the owner was overwhelmed.
Implementation: Built do/don’t rules (no confirming patient status, no treatment details, no defensiveness), set Premium SLA for same-day coverage, and created an escalation path for anything medical or refund-related.
Operational flow: Draft → escalate negatives → owner approves with one-click edits → post.
Outcomes: Better trust signals on the listing (consistent, professional voice), fewer unanswered negatives, and improved lead confidence as indicated by consult calls referencing “how you handle feedback.”
