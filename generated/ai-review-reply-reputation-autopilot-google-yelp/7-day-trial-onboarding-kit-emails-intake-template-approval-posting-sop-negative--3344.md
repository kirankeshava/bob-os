C) APPROVAL + POSTING SOP (FIRST 7 DAYS)
========================
Goal: keep response time within 24 business hours and build trust fast.

Day 0 (Activation)
1) Confirm platforms + links, collect intake, confirm approval workflow.
2) Confirm reply volume expectation (estimate based on last 30 days reviews).
3) Confirm escalation contact and “auto-post rules” (if any).

Day 1 (Baseline + first batch)
1) Pull latest reviews from Google/Yelp.
2) Draft replies for any new reviews in the last 7–14 days (prioritize newest and any <4 stars).
3) Send batch for approval (EMAIL 2 format) within 24 business hours.

Days 2–6 (Daily cadence)
1) Check platforms once per business day (twice if high volume).
2) Draft replies for new reviews.
3) If 4–5 stars: draft + send for approval (or auto-post if allowed).
4) If 1–2 stars: trigger escalation playbook immediately, draft response, send for approval.
5) Maintain a simple log: date/time found, draft sent, approval received, posted time.

Day 6 (Conversion)
1) Send recap (EMAIL 3) with drafted/posted counts and response time.
2) Ask for “CONTINUE” and confirm plan tier.

Day 7 (Handoff)
1) Deliver a 1-page weekly KPI snapshot (counts, avg rating trend, response time).
2) Confirm ongoing cadence and any updated guardrails.

========================
D) NEGATIVE REVIEW ESCALATION PLAYBOOK (OPERATIONAL + SCRIPTS)
========================
Trigger conditions:
• Any 1–2 star review OR any review alleging safety/legal/compliance issues OR a named staff complaint.

Steps (do these in order):
1) Screenshot + log the review immediately (date/time, platform, reviewer name).
2) Notify escalation contact with:
• Review text
• Suggested response (draft)
• Proposed remediation action (if any)
3) Decide response approach:
A) If the complaint seems legitimate: acknowledge, apologize, move offline.
B) If unclear/possible mismatch: polite clarification, move offline.
C) If abusive/spam: minimal neutral response + report to platform if eligible.

Safe reply structure (brand-safe):
• Thank them for feedback
• Acknowledge experience (no admissions of fault unless approved)
• Invite offline resolution with a direct contact method
• Keep it short, calm, and non-defensive

Negative review reply templates (edit for niche):
Template 1 — Legitimate service issue
“Hi {{Name}}, thank you for the feedback. We’re sorry to hear your experience didn’t meet expectations. We’d like to learn more and make this right — please contact {{Phone/Email}} so our team can look into the details and follow up.”

Template 2 — Unclear / can’t locate customer
“Hi {{Name}}, thanks for bringing this to our attention. We take concerns seriously, but we’re not able to find your visit based on the information here. Please contact {{Phone/Email}} so we can understand what happened and help.”

Template 3 — Abusive/spam tone
“Hi {{Name}}, we’re sorry you feel this way. We’d like to understand more — please reach out to {{Phone/Email}} so we can review the situation.”

Do-not-do list:
• Don’t argue facts in public.
• Don’t mention private customer details.
• Don’t blame the customer.
• Don’t promise refunds/compensation publicly unless explicitly approved.
