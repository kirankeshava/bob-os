# 1) Segmented Prospecting Plan (what to target, in what order)

## Verticals (2–3 to start)
1) **Dentists / dental clinics** (high LTV, high review sensitivity)
2) **Med spas / aesthetics / dermatology cosmetics** (high CAC, ratings drive bookings)
3) **HVAC + plumbing** (high volume, urgent jobs, reviews directly influence calls)

## Core segments (based on Google reviews)
- **Not Responding:** owner responses in last 10 reviews ≤ 2 (≤20% response-rate proxy)
- **Low Rating:** rating < 4.2
- **High Volume:** review_count ≥ 200 OR last review within 14 days

## Priority tiers (who gets emailed first)
- **Priority A:** (Not Responding AND High Volume) OR (Low Rating AND High Volume)
- **Priority B:** Not Responding OR Low Rating
- **Priority C:** High Volume only

## Offer positioning by segment
- **Not Responding:** “We respond within 12 hours, brand-safe, you approve. Never miss a review again.”
- **Low Rating:** “Rapid response + escalation workflow + service recovery playbook. Protect rating drift.”
- **High Volume:** “Ops throughput: drafts queued daily, approval workflow, weekly KPI report.”

## Daily activity targets (start lean; scale as deliverability proves out)
- **Day 1–3:** 20–30 new emails/day + 20 follow-ups/day
- **Day 4–7:** 40–60 new emails/day + 40 follow-ups/day
- **Day 8–14:** 60–100 new emails/day + 60–120 follow-ups/day

Routing rule: 
- Priority A prospects get **Email Variant A (strong personalization + urgency)**.
- Priority B get **Variant B (problem/solution + quick proof)**.
- Priority C get **Variant C (efficiency + workflow + KPI report)**.

## Two outbound lanes
### Lane 1 — Direct-to-local (owners/managers)
- Goal: book demos/trials directly with the business.
- ICP roles: owner, practice manager, office manager, operations manager, GM.

### Lane 2 — Agencies/resellers (fast distribution)
Target agencies who already manage local marketing:
- “Dental marketing agency”, “med spa marketing”, “home services marketing”, “reputation management agency”, “local SEO agency”.
Offer: white-label or referral fee, agency dashboard/reporting, bulk pricing for 10+ locations.

# 3) Daily Sending Ops Checklist + CRM Stages

## Pre-send checklist (daily)
1) **List QA (10% sample):** confirm correct vertical/category, not a franchise directory listing, has recent reviews, website/phone present.
2) **Personalization minimum:** include {{recent_review_snippet}} AND one of: response gap OR review recency.
3) **Compliance:** don’t mention sensitive health details; keep review snippet short; consider paraphrasing if needed.
4) **Bounce control:** if hard bounce rate > 3% in a day, stop and clean list.

## Send cadence
- New sends + follow-ups daily (Mon–Fri). Keep weekends optional.
- Keep each prospect to max **3 touches** in 7–10 days.

## Reply-handling SLA
- Reply within **2 business hours** to any positive reply.
- For “not interested”: tag + stop sequence.
- For “who are you?”: reply with 2 lines + website link + offer sample drafts.

## CRM stages (simple pipeline)
1) **Prospect (not contacted)**
2) **Sent (Email 1 sent)**
3) **Follow-up queued (FU1/FU2 scheduled)**
4) **Replied** (sub-tags: interested / objection / not now / wrong person)
5) **Qualified** (has Google/Yelp activity + decision-maker engaged)
6) **Demo booked**
7) **Trial / Pilot** (sample drafts or limited rollout)
8) **Paid**
9) **Lost** (reason: timing, price, already handled, no need)

## KPI tracking (weekly)
- Delivered rate, open rate (if tracked), reply rate, positive reply rate
- Meetings booked
- Conversion: meetings → trials → paid
- Segment performance: Priority A vs B vs C
