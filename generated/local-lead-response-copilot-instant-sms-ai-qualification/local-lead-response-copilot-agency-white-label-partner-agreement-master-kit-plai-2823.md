1) Parties + Purpose
This agreement is between:
• Provider: Local Lead Response Copilot (“Provider”)
• Agency/Reseller Partner: (“Partner”)
Purpose: Partner resells Provider’s “Instant SMS + AI Qualification + Booking” service to Partner’s end clients (“Client Accounts”) under Partner’s brand (white‑label) or co‑branded.

2) Offer Summary (What the Client Gets)
Provider supplies:
• Instant lead response via SMS (speed‑to‑lead)
• AI‑driven qualification questions (short, configurable)
• Routing/booking outcomes (book call/appointment OR handoff to team)
• Monthly KPI reporting inputs (response time, contact rate, qualified rate, booked rate, lead status breakdown)

3) Free Launch / Trial (Week 1 Policy)
For new Partner client accounts launched during the free launch period:
• Provider will set up and run the first 7 days at $0 platform fee (“Free Launch”).
• Partner may charge their client or also provide a trial at their discretion.
• After day 7, ongoing wholesale pricing applies unless otherwise agreed in writing.

4) Pricing + Minimum Commitment (Wholesale)
Standard wholesale range:
• $299–$499 per Client Account per month (based on volume tier and complexity)

Volume tiers (example guidance):
• 1–3 accounts: $499/account/month
• 4–9 accounts: $399/account/month
• 10–19 accounts: $349/account/month
• 20+ accounts: $299/account/month

Minimum commitment for priority support + volume pricing:
• 10 Client Accounts minimum OR a 3‑month commitment at the selected tier (whichever is agreed).
If Partner drops below the committed volume, pricing reverts to the tier matching the current active account count for the next billing cycle.

Retail guidance (Partner charges their clients):
• $499–$999/account/month suggested retail
Partner keeps the margin (e.g., buy at $349, sell at $799).

5) “3×10 Accounts” Growth Plan (Revenue Path)
Objective: land 3 agency partners × 10 accounts each.
• At 30 accounts × $349 wholesale = ~$10,470 MRR to Provider
• Partners retail at ~$799 average = ~$23,970 MRR per partner set (approx, varies)
Provider supports Partner with onboarding assets (deck/one‑pager/SOP/reporting templates) so Partner can close without Provider on every call.

6) Billing + Payment Terms
• Billing cadence: monthly in advance (unless otherwise agreed).
• Invoices sent to Partner (Partner is the billing entity to Provider).
• Partner is responsible for collecting payment from their client.
• Nonpayment: Provider may pause service for affected Client Accounts after reasonable notice.

7) Responsibilities
Provider responsibilities:
• Configure automation flows per the agreed intake form
• Maintain and monitor core automation health
• Provide monthly KPI snapshot inputs (or template completion guidance)
• Provide support per the Support/SLA section

Partner responsibilities:
• Own the client relationship and expectation setting
• Provide accurate intake information on time (forms, routing, offer details)
• Ensure Client has consent/permission to text leads and complies with local laws
• Ensure the Client’s team answers calls/appointments quickly when leads are handed off

Client responsibilities (via Partner):
• Lead source quality (forms/ads functioning)
• Fast sales follow‑up on handoffs
• Accurate business hours, services, location coverage

8) Data, Privacy, and Compliance
• Provider processes lead data (name/phone/message/timestamp and related metadata) solely to deliver the service.
• Partner affirms they have the right to share Client lead data with Provider.
• Partner/Client are responsible for compliance with applicable texting/marketing laws (TCPA/CTIA and local requirements). Provider will follow Partner’s instructions regarding opt‑out language and stop keywords.
• Provider will not sell lead data.

• Partner may brand the service as their own. Provider agrees not to contact Partner’s clients directly unless requested by Partner for support.

• Day 0: Intake submitted

12) Reporting + KPIs (Monthly)
Provider/Partner will review:
• Median response time (lead created → first SMS sent)
• Contact rate (reached/replied)
• Qualified rate (qualified ÷ contacted)
• Booked rate (booked ÷ qualified and/or ÷ total)
• Lead status breakdown (New, Contacted, Qualified, Booked, Not Qualified, No Response, Opted Out)

13) Acceptable Use
Partner/Client must not use the system for:
• Illegal services, deceptive practices, harassment
• Purchasing lists and blasting unsolicited SMS (no spam)
Provider may suspend accounts that create compliance risk.

14) Cancellation / Offboarding
• Either party may cancel with 14 days notice (unless a longer commitment is agreed).
• On cancellation, Provider will stop automations and (upon request) export summary KPI metrics for the final period.

15) Limitation of Liability (Plain Language)
Provider’s responsibility is limited to fees paid to Provider for the affected Client Account(s) in the prior 30 days. Provider is not responsible for lost profits, ad spend losses, or downstream sales performance.
