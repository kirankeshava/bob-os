C) IMPLEMENTATION SOP (agency-facing)

Objective
Launch Copilot for each client quickly (24–72 hours after intake) and generate measurable lift in response time, contact rate, qualified rate, and booked rate.

Roles
• Agency (you): client relationship, collects intake, ensures booking/calendar access, reviews monthly report.
• Copilot team (us): configure flows, QA, launch monitoring, provide KPI report template + troubleshooting.

1) Pre‑sale checklist (agency)
Before promising a go-live date, confirm:
• Lead sources: website forms / FB leads / landing pages
• Primary conversion: phone call, calendar booking, or both
• Business hours + after-hours rules
• Service areas (cities/zip) and excluded areas
• Services offered + top 3 money services
• Appointment duration + availability rules (if booking)

2) Client intake (agency collects; send to partner email)
Email to: agent_bob_replit+lead-copilot@agentmail.to
Subject: “New Partner Client Intake — [Business Name] — [Niche]”
Include:
• Business name + main phone + website
• Time zone + business hours
• Lead source URLs (form link / FB lead form name)
• Desired first SMS message (or we provide)
• Qualification questions (or we propose)
• Disqualifiers: out-of-area, budget minimum, job type exclusions
• Booking link/calendar access (if applicable)
• Routing: who gets notified when qualified (name, phone/email)
• Compliance notes: opt-in language location (if on forms)

3) Build (us)
We create:
• SMS opener (friendly + confirms request)
• 2–5 qualification questions:
  - job type
  - zip code/city
  - timeline/urgency
  - budget range (optional)
  - photo upload link (optional)
• Decision rules:
  - Qualified → booking link or “call now” + notify team
  - Not qualified → polite close + optionally capture future interest
  - No response → follow-up sequence

4) QA testing (agency + us)
Test 10 scenarios:
• Normal lead (qualified)
• Out of area
• After-hours lead
• “Just shopping” lead
• Wrong job type
• No response sequence triggers
Confirm:
• Median first response time is near-instant
• Status tags apply correctly
• Booking/call routing works

5) Launch
Day 1–2:
• Monitor outcomes (contact/qualified/booked)
• Adjust phrasing if confusion occurs
• Tighten disqualifiers to reduce wasted calls

6) Optimization cadence
• Week 1: 1–2 iterations based on lead replies
• Month 1: lock “v1” and baseline KPIs
• Ongoing: monthly improvements (questions, routing, booking CTA)

7) Reporting + retention (agency)
Every month share a simple KPI summary:
• Median first response time
• Contact rate
• Qualified rate
• Booked rate
• Lead status breakdown
Tie the metrics to “same leads, more booked jobs.”
