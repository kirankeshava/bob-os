## 2) Partner Terms (Plain-language, non-legal)

**Document name:** Local Lead Response Copilot — Agency Partner Terms (Plain Language)

**Important:** This is not legal advice. It is a plain-language set of expectations to reduce confusion and speed up onboarding. If you want a legally reviewed version, consult an attorney.

### 2.1 Parties
- **Provider:** Local Lead Response Copilot ("we", "us")
- **Partner:** The agency/reseller ("you")

### 2.2 What you are reselling
You are reselling access to the Local Lead Response Copilot service, which:
1) instantly texts new leads after they submit a form / FB lead ad / inbound request,  
2) asks short qualification questions, and  
3) routes qualified leads to booking/calls/appointments.

### 2.3 Commercial model (wholesale → retail)
- You pay us **wholesale** per client account (your customer).  
- You charge your client **retail** at a price you set.

**Suggested economics:**
- Wholesale (you → us): **$299–$499 / account / month** (based on tier/volume).  
- Retail (you → client): **$499–$999 / account / month**.

**Usage-based pass-through:** If the underlying messaging/telephony provider charges usage (SMS minutes/messages), either:
- You pass-through usage to the client (recommended for transparency), or
- You include a fair-use cap and charge overage.

### 2.4 Volume tiers + minimum commitment
To qualify for volume pricing and priority support, Partner agrees to a minimum commitment:
- **Tier A (1–2 accounts):** Standard onboarding + standard support.
- **Tier B (3–9 accounts):** Volume discount + group onboarding.
- **Tier C (10+ accounts):** Best wholesale pricing + priority support.

**Minimum commitment for Tier C priority support:** 10 active accounts with an initial **90-day commitment**.

### 2.5 Billing and payment terms
- Billing is monthly in advance per active client account unless otherwise agreed.
- If a client account is paused/canceled mid-cycle, fees generally apply through the end of the billing period.
- If a payment fails, service may be paused until payment is resolved.

### 2.6 Onboarding responsibilities (what Partner must provide)
Partner will provide, per client account:
1) Business name + service area + hours  
2) Lead sources (forms, FB lead ads, website form, call tracking if applicable)  
3) Routing rules (who gets notified; where to book; call center vs owner)  
4) Qualification criteria (service type, zip codes, budget, urgency, etc.)  
5) Compliance instructions (opt-in language, required disclaimers, etc.)

### 2.7 Compliance & consent
- Partner is responsible for ensuring the client’s lead sources collect consent for SMS outreach and comply with applicable laws (e.g., TCPA, CTIA guidelines, local regulations).
- Provider can share recommended opt-in language, but Partner/client is ultimately responsible for compliance.
- Provider may suspend campaigns that show obvious compliance risk (e.g., missing opt-in).

### 2.8 Data handling & confidentiality
- We will use lead data only to operate and improve the service and to provide reporting to Partner.
- Partner should not send sensitive data unless required. Avoid storing sensitive personal data in free-text fields.
- Partner agrees to keep Provider’s internal materials and wholesale pricing confidential.

### 2.9 Support levels & response expectations
- **Standard support:** email support via agent_bob_replit+lead-copilot@agentmail.to with a target response time (business hours) of 1–2 business days.
- **Priority support (10+ accounts, committed):** faster response targets, structured onboarding calls, and escalation path.

### 2.10 Uptime & limitations
We aim for reliable uptime, but the service may rely on third-party providers (telephony/SMS, integrations). We cannot guarantee uninterrupted service. We will communicate known outages and remediation steps.

### 2.11 Reporting and performance
We provide a reporting view and monthly KPI exports/templates. Performance (booking rate, revenue) depends on:
- lead quality,
- offer/pricing,
- speed-to-lead settings,
- follow-up process,
- ability to answer calls / show up to appointments.

We do not guarantee a specific revenue outcome.

### 2.12 Cancellation and offboarding
- Partner may cancel accounts with notice (recommended: 7 days before renewal).
- If a client churns, Partner should notify us promptly so we can stop billing and disable messaging.
- Data export: We can provide a final KPI snapshot and lead status summary upon request.

### 2.13 Non-solicitation (optional expectation)
If Partner introduces a client, Provider will not knowingly bypass Partner to sell directly to that client during the active relationship, except if the client contacts Provider independently and requests direct contracting; in that case Provider will notify Partner.

### 2.14 Acceptance
By using the service and paying invoices, Partner indicates acceptance of these terms.

## 5) KPI Reporting (required metrics to include monthly)
Every monthly report should include:
- **Median response time** (seconds/minutes)
- **Contact rate** (% leads that replied or were reached)
- **Qualified rate** (% of contacted leads that meet criteria)
- **Booked rate** (% of qualified leads that booked or accepted a call)
- **Lead status breakdown** (New, Contacted, Unresponsive, Qualified, Disqualified, Booked, No-show, Won/Lost if tracked)

**Reminder:** Keep reporting simple. The KPI report’s job is retention: prove faster response → more conversations → more bookings.
