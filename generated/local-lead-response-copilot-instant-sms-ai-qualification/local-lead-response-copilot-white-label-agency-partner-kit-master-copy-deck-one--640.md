## D) Implementation SOP (Agency → Client → Launch)

### Roles
- **Agency:** owns client relationship, pricing, and first-line support
- **Copilot team (us):** builds flows, sets routing/booking logic, QA, and provides KPI reporting format

### Step 1 — Client Intake (Day 0)
Collect:
1) Business name + location(s)
2) Services offered + service area/zip codes
3) Hours of operation + after-hours rules
4) Lead sources (form URLs, FB lead forms, CRM)
5) Booking preference:
   - book to calendar link OR
   - call transfer / notify sales rep OR
   - “qualified lead only” to CRM
6) Qualification questions (choose 2–4):
   - “What service do you need?”
   - “What’s your address/zip code?”
   - “When do you need this?”
   - “Is this residential or commercial?”
7) Team notifications (who gets notified; SMS/email)
8) Compliance: opt-in language on forms (agency to confirm)

### Step 2 — Build (Days 1–3)
- Create lead intake triggers
- Write qualification flow + fallback logic
- Configure routing rules (service area, after-hours)
- Configure booking/handoff

### Step 3 — QA (Day 3–4)
- Test lead submission end-to-end
- Confirm:
  - response time < 60 seconds
  - qualification tagging works
  - booking link works
  - notifications received

### Step 4 — Launch (Day 4–5)
- Turn on for all live lead sources
- Monitor first 25 leads closely
- Adjust questions if needed (reduce friction)

### Step 5 — Optimize (Week 2+)
- Review KPIs weekly for first month
- Tune:
  - question count (2–4 max)
  - after-hours rules
  - routing by job type

Client-facing promise agencies should use:
- “We will get you responding to leads in under 60 seconds and track contact/qualified/booked rates monthly.”
