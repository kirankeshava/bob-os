Important: This is a plain-language partner agreement for speed and clarity. It is not legal advice. Agencies may adapt with counsel.

1) Parties
This Agency Partner Agreement (“Agreement”) is between:
(A) “Provider”: Local Lead Response Copilot (“LLRC”) and
(B) “Agency Partner”: the agency/entity accepting these terms (“Partner”).

2) What We Provide
LLRC provides a lead response automation service that:
- instantly sends SMS to new leads (e.g., web forms/FB lead ads)
- runs short qualification questions (AI-assisted scripting/logic)
- routes qualified leads to booked calls/appointments or to the Partner’s team
- provides reporting on response speed and conversion funnel metrics

3) White-Label / Resale Rights
3.1 Partner may resell LLRC as a managed service to its end-clients (“Clients”) under Partner branding.
3.2 Partner may describe the service as “powered by Local Lead Response Copilot” or fully white-label it.
3.3 Partner is responsible for all Client-facing sales, promises, and relationship management unless explicitly agreed otherwise in writing.

4) Pricing, Minimum Commitments, and Billing
4.1 Wholesale (Partner → Provider) subscription pricing:
- Tier A: 1–2 active client accounts: $499/account/month
- Tier B: 3–9 active client accounts: $399/account/month
- Tier C: 10–29 active client accounts: $349/account/month
- Tier D: 30+ active client accounts: $299/account/month
4.2 Suggested retail (Partner → Client): $499–$999/account/month depending on niche, lead volume, and appointment value.
4.3 Minimum commitment for priority support: 3 active client accounts on Tier B (or above) with a 90-day term.
4.4 Billing cadence: monthly in advance per active account.
4.5 Overages / third-party costs: Any pass-through usage fees (e.g., phone/SMS usage) must be clearly disclosed to Partner and/or Client in advance. If Partner wants “all-in” pricing, Partner can bake expected usage into retail pricing.

5) Setup & Onboarding
5.1 Standard onboarding includes: intake, workflow build, qualification script alignment, routing rules, QA, and launch.
5.2 Partner must provide required assets on time (see “Implementation Requirements” in SOP). Delays caused by missing inputs shift launch timelines.
5.3 Partner owns Client communication. Provider can join calls on request for larger rollouts or priority support tiers (subject to availability).

6) Support & Service Levels
6.1 Support channel: agent_bob_replit+lead-copilot@agentmail.to
6.2 Standard support: business days response target within 1–2 business days.
6.3 Priority support (requires minimum commitment in 4.3): response target within 4–8 business hours on business days.
6.4 Incident handling: Provider will use commercially reasonable efforts to restore service promptly if downtime occurs.

7) Acceptable Use & Compliance
7.1 Partner and Clients must comply with applicable laws and regulations including (where applicable) TCPA, CTIA guidelines, local texting/consent rules, and platform policies.
7.2 Consent responsibility: Partner/Client is responsible for collecting valid lead consent for SMS/communications.
7.3 Prohibited use: spam, harassment, deceptive content, illegal services, and any activity that risks carrier/number reputation.
7.4 Provider may suspend or terminate accounts for violations that threaten deliverability, compliance, or platform access.

8) Data Handling, Confidentiality, and Security
8.1 Data types: lead contact details, timestamps, qualification answers, routing outcomes, and appointment/booking events.
8.2 Confidentiality: both parties agree to keep non-public information confidential.
8.3 Data access: Partner may access reporting and exports for its Clients.
8.4 Data retention: Provider retains data for reasonable operational needs unless otherwise agreed.
8.5 Security: Provider will take reasonable measures to protect data; Partner is responsible for secure handling on its side (CRMs, spreadsheets, etc.).

9) Performance, ROI, and No Guarantees
9.1 LLRC is designed to improve speed-to-lead and conversion rates; however results depend on offer, market, lead quality, hours of operation, pricing, and follow-up.
9.2 Provider does not guarantee revenue outcomes, number of bookings, or conversion rate improvements.

10) Intellectual Property
10.1 Provider owns the platform, workflows, templates, and underlying system.
10.2 Partner owns its branding, client lists, and sales materials (except Provider-provided materials, which are licensed for resale use).

11) Term, Renewal, and Termination
11.1 Month-to-month by default unless a 90-day priority support term is selected.
11.2 Either party may terminate with 14 days written notice (email acceptable).
11.3 Non-payment: Provider may suspend service after reasonable notice.
11.4 Upon termination, Partner/Client access ends at the end of the paid period; data export can be provided upon request (subject to reasonable admin time).

12) Liability Limits
12.1 To the maximum extent permitted by law, Provider is not liable for indirect or consequential damages (lost profits, lost opportunities).
12.2 Provider’s total liability is limited to the amount paid by Partner to Provider in the prior 30 days for the affected account(s).

13) Publicity
Partner may reference “powered by Local Lead Response Copilot” with Provider approval. Provider may list Partner as a reseller unless Partner opts out in writing.

14) Operational Notes (So Agencies Can Sell Confidently)
- Typical launch time: 2–5 business days once intake is complete.
- KPIs we track monthly: median response time, contact rate, qualified rate, booked rate, and lead status breakdown.
- The #1 ROI driver: faster response time → higher contact + booking rates.

15) Acceptance
Partner accepts these terms by replying “ACCEPT” via email to agent_bob_replit+lead-copilot@agentmail.to or by signing and returning this agreement.
