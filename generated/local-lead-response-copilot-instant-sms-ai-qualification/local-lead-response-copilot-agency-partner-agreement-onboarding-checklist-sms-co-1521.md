Goal: Launch each client in 48–72 hours with consistent QA and measurable KPIs.

A) Client Snapshot (Required)
☐ Business name + location(s)
☐ Primary service(s) sold + “must-have” jobs vs jobs to avoid
☐ Service area (zip codes/cities) + travel radius
☐ Business hours + after-hours handling rules
☐ Primary offer (e.g., “$79 drain clearing,” “same-day estimate,” “free inspection”)
☐ Ideal customer + disqualifiers (renters vs homeowners, minimum job size, etc.)

B) Lead Sources (Required)
For each lead source, capture:
☐ Source type: Website form / FB lead ad / Google LSA / other
☐ Where lead lands (CRM? email? webhook?)
☐ Fields captured (name, phone, email, service, zip, message)
☐ Confirmation page URL (if any)
☐ Typical lead volume/day and peak times

C) Booking Destination (Choose One)
☐ Option 1: Schedule link (Calendly/CRM booking) + rules (appointment length, buffers)
☐ Option 2: “Call now” routing to phone number (tracking number or main line)
☐ Option 3: Two-step: qualify → notify human → human books

D) Qualification Script Inputs (Required)
☐ 3–5 qualifying questions (short, multiple choice when possible)
  Examples:
  • “What service do you need? (1) Plumbing (2) Drain (3) Water heater (4) Other”
  • “Is this residential or commercial?”
  • “What’s your zip code?”
  • “How soon do you need help? (Now/Today/This week)”
  • “Do you own the property? (Yes/No)”
☐ Disqualification rules (e.g., outside service area, not homeowner, wrong service)
☐ Tone/brand notes (friendly, direct, premium)

E) Notifications & Escalations (Required)
☐ Who receives alerts for qualified leads (name + email/phone)
☐ Escalation window (e.g., alert if no reply within 2 minutes)
☐ After-hours message + next-step promise

F) Compliance Requirements (Required)
☐ Confirm lead capture includes consent language (see templates below)
☐ Confirm opt-out language included in first or second SMS (“Reply STOP to opt out”)
☐ Confirm business identity in message (“This is {Business Name}…”) 

G) QA Checklist (Do Not Skip)
☐ Send 3 test leads through each source
☐ Validate: first SMS sent within 60 seconds
☐ Validate: answers captured and lead status updates correctly
☐ Validate: booking link works / call routing correct
☐ Validate: STOP opt-out works and suppresses future messages
☐ Validate: reporting metrics populate (response time, contact, qualified, booked)

H) Go-Live Criteria
☐ Client approved script and offer
☐ All lead sources tested
☐ Notifications tested
☐ KPI baseline date recorded
☐ Agency scheduled first 7-day optimization review

I) Weekly Optimization Prompts (Agency Ops)
☐ Are we asking too many questions? Reduce friction.
☐ Are we disqualifying too aggressively? Review lead losses.
☐ Are booked leads actually showing? Improve reminders/hand-off.
☐ Are missed leads happening after-hours? Add next-morning priority workflow.

3) SMS CONSENT + OPT-OUT COMPLIANCE COPY (CLIENT-FACING)
============================================================
Use these snippets in forms/lead ads and in the first SMS. Agency should review for fit and compliance.

A) Website Form Consent (Short)
“By submitting this form, you agree to receive text messages from {Business Name} about your request. Reply STOP to opt out.”

B) Website Form Consent (Expanded)
“By clicking Submit, you authorize {Business Name} to contact you via call or text at the number provided regarding your request (including automated texts). Consent is not a condition of purchase. Reply STOP to opt out.”

C) Facebook Lead Ad (Description/Disclaimer Field)
“By submitting, you agree to receive texts/calls from {Business Name} about your request. Reply STOP to opt out.”

D) First SMS (Immediate Response)
“Hi {First Name} — this is {Business Name}. Got your request. Quick question so we can help fast: what service do you need? (1) {Service A} (2) {Service B} (3) {Service C}. Reply STOP to opt out.”

E) After-Hours SMS
“Hi {First Name} — this is {Business Name}. We’re currently closed, but we received your request. Reply with the best time tomorrow and we’ll confirm ASAP. Reply STOP to opt out.”

F) Disqualified Lead Message (Polite)
“Thanks {First Name} — it looks like we may not be the best fit for this request. If you believe this is an error, reply with more details and our team will review. Reply STOP to opt out.”

G) Opt-Out Confirmation (If needed)
“You’re opted out and won’t receive further texts from {Business Name}. Reply START to re-subscribe.”
