# 7-Day “3×10 Accounts” Execution Plan (Agency-First, Distribution-First)

**Goal:** 30 active End Client accounts in 30 days (three cohorts of 10).  
**Core math:** Partners pay $349/account at 10–29 tier. Retail at $799.  
Margin ≈ $450/account/month. At 30 accounts: ≈ $13.5k/mo margin for the agency; Provider revenue ≈ $10.5k/mo.

## Setup (Day 0 / Prep)
- Pick a niche for the first sprint (HVAC, roofing, plumbing, pest, med spa).
- Create a single “Speed-to-Lead” offer page + attach the reseller deck + one-pager.
- Decide retail price: **$799/mo** (recommended default) with a “founder cohort” discount to $699 for first 10 logos if needed.

## Day 1 — Build the Lead List (100 targets)
- Pull 100 local lead-gen agencies or niche agencies from LinkedIn/Google (“[niche] marketing agency”, “lead generation [city]”).
- Tag each: niche, size, decision maker name, email, LinkedIn.
- Outcome: 100 targets, 30 high-priority.

## Day 2 — Outreach Sprint #1 (50 touches)
- Send partner outreach email + LinkedIn message offering a 15-min demo.
- Hook: “Instant SMS + AI qualification + booking. Partners charge $499–$999. You keep the margin.”
- KPI for the day: 10 replies, 5 meetings booked.

## Day 3 — Run Demos + Close Cohort 1 (10 agencies or 10 end-clients via 1 agency)
- Demo agenda: problem → speed-to-lead math → workflow → reporting → pricing tiers → next steps.
- Close motion: “Start with 10 accounts for Priority Support; ramp within 30 days.”
- KPI: 3 partner closes or 10 end-client intros.

## Day 4 — Onboard Cohort 1 (first 3–5 accounts live)
- Use the SOP intake checklist.
- Launch within 24–48 hours for each account.
- KPI: median response time under 60 seconds after go-live.

## Day 5 — Outreach Sprint #2 (50 touches)
- Repeat outreach; include “we can show you a live dashboard + script.”
- KPI: 5 more meetings booked.

## Day 6 — Close Cohort 2 (next 10 accounts)
- Offer volume discount narrative: “Hit 10 accounts → $349 wholesale. Hit 30 → $299.”
- KPI: 10 accounts in pipeline with launch dates.

## Day 7 — Retention + Proof
- Send first KPI snapshot to partners (even if early): response time + contact rate.
- Collect 1 testimonial quote from a warm client: “We reached leads in under a minute.”
- KPI: 1 proof asset created + 20 more targets added for week 2.

**Pipeline targets per week:** 150 touches → 15 conversations → 6 demos → 2 closes → 10 accounts launched.

# Monthly KPI Report + QBR Narrative Template (What to Say)

Use these KPIs each month:
- **Median Response Time** (goal: < 60 seconds)
- **Contact Rate** = contacted / total leads
- **Qualified Rate** = qualified / contacted
- **Booked Rate** = booked / qualified (or booked / total)
- **Lead Status Breakdown** = booked, qualified-not-booked, unqualified, no response, bad info

## Slide/Email Section: Executive Summary (Copy/Paste)
**This month’s performance:**
- Median first-response time: **[X sec/min]** (last month: [Y])
- Contact rate: **[X%]**
- Qualified rate: **[X%]**
- Booked rate: **[X%]**
- Total booked appointments: **[X]**

**Bottom line:** Faster response time is translating into **[more contacts / more qualified leads / more booked calls]**. Next month we will focus on **[improving contact rate / tightening qualification / increasing booking conversion]**.

## Interpretation Rules (So partners know what to fix)
### If response time is good but contact rate is low
Likely causes: poor phone numbers, weak form intent, people not seeing texts.
Actions:
1) Add “We’ll text you in 60 seconds” to the form.
2) Add a second contact attempt at +3 minutes.
3) Ask for preferred contact time in Q1.

### If contact rate is good but qualified rate is low
Likely causes: wrong traffic, broad targeting, or qualification too strict.
Actions:
1) Adjust Q1 to filter for service area / job type.
2) Add a disqualifier message that still captures referral (“Not a fit—want a quote for X instead?”).
3) Review ad keywords/targeting for intent.

### If qualified rate is good but booked rate is low
Likely causes: booking friction or calendar availability.
Actions:
1) Offer 2 time options inside SMS (“Today 3pm or tomorrow 10am?”).
2) Route hot leads to a human call within 5 minutes.
3) Reduce steps: fewer questions before booking.

## Lead Status Breakdown Narrative
“Out of **[Total Leads]** leads: **[Booked]** booked, **[Qualified Not Booked]** were qualified but didn’t schedule, **[Unqualified]** were out of scope, and **[No Response]** didn’t reply. Next month our focus is converting the ‘Qualified Not Booked’ segment by **[follow-up sequence / faster human handoff / booking link changes]**.”

## Renewal / Expansion Ask (Copy)
“Based on performance, the next lever is **[after-hours coverage / additional lead source / second location]**. We recommend adding **[X]** to expand bookings while keeping response time under **60 seconds**.”
