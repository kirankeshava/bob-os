## 2) Partner Terms (Plain-Language, Non-Legal)
**Purpose:** speed up closes and set expectations. This is a plain-language summary and is not legal advice.

### 2.1 Parties
- **Provider:** Local Lead Response Copilot (“LLRC”) — support/contact: agent_bob_replit+lead-copilot@agentmail.to
- **Partner:** Agency/reseller (“Partner”)
- **End Client:** Partner’s customer (“Client”)

### 2.2 What LLRC provides
For each activated Client account, LLRC provides:
- Instant SMS response to inbound leads (forms/FB lead ads/lead sources supported)
- AI-driven lead qualification via short SMS flow
- Routing outcomes: **booked**, **qualified (needs follow-up)**, **not qualified**, **no response**, **wrong number/spam**
- Basic performance reporting: response time + contact/qualified/booked rates

### 2.3 What Partner provides
Partner owns:
- Client relationship, selling, and first-line support (unless on priority support tier)
- Collecting Client approvals for messaging and disclosure language
- Providing accurate routing/booking details (calendar links, hours, service areas, qualifying rules)

### 2.4 Commercial terms (recommended defaults)
**Partner pays LLRC:** $299–$499 per Client account/month (based on volume tier).

**Partner charges Client:** $499–$999 per Client account/month (Partner sets pricing).

**Minimum commitment (to receive partner pricing):**
- **3-month minimum** per activated Client account.

**Volume tiers (example):**
- 1–2 accounts: **$499/account/mo**
- 3–9 accounts: **$399/account/mo**
- 10–24 accounts: **$349/account/mo**
- 25+ accounts: **$299/account/mo**

**Priority support eligibility:**
- Priority support begins at **10+ active accounts** or with a separate support add-on agreed in writing.

### 2.5 Billing, payment, and non-payment
- Billing occurs **monthly in advance** per active Client account.
- If an invoice is unpaid after a reasonable grace period, LLRC may pause new message sending until resolved.
- Partner is responsible for collecting from Client; LLRC bills Partner (not Client) unless otherwise agreed.

### 2.6 Onboarding and go-live expectations
- Typical setup: **2–5 business days** after intake is complete.
- “Intake complete” means LLRC has: lead source access/config, phone number/SMS sending configuration (if applicable), qualification rules, routing destination, and any calendar/booking links.

### 2.7 Data, privacy, and compliance (high level)
- Partner must ensure Client has permission to contact leads and is compliant with applicable SMS/telecom rules.
- LLRC processes lead contact info and message content to deliver the service.
- Partner/Client should avoid sending sensitive data (e.g., payment card data, SSNs) via SMS.

### 2.8 Performance and KPI definitions
LLRC reporting typically includes:
- **Median response time:** time from lead capture to first outbound SMS
- **Contact rate:** % of leads who reply at least once
- **Qualified rate:** % of leads meeting defined criteria
- **Booked rate:** % of leads who book or accept an appointment/call
- **Lead status breakdown:** counts by outcome (booked/qualified/unqualified/no response/bad number)

### 2.9 Support boundaries
- LLRC supports Partner on:
  - technical configuration of lead capture + routing
  - qualification flow edits
  - troubleshooting message delivery issues
- LLRC does not manage Partner’s ad campaigns, creative, or CRM pipeline (unless separately agreed).

### 2.10 Cancellation and account changes
- Accounts may be cancelled after the minimum term, with notice before the next billing cycle.
- Partner may request pausing an account; pause policies depend on messaging/numbering costs and should be confirmed per implementation.

### 2.11 White-labeling
- Partner may present LLRC as an internal tool/service offering.
- If requested, LLRC can provide neutral branding assets (where technically feasible).

### 2.12 Relationship and non-exclusivity
- This is a non-exclusive partnership: Partner can sell other tools; LLRC can work with other agencies.

## 3) Google Slides → PDF Build Checklist (Free, <30 minutes)
**Goal:** turn the finished 12-slide deck copy into a clean PDF without hiring a designer.

### Step A — Create Slides file
1. Open Google Drive → New → Google Slides.
2. Name it: `LLRC Agency Reseller Deck (v1)`.
3. Set Page Setup to **Widescreen (16:9)**.

### Step B — Paste the deck copy
1. Create 12 slides.
2. For each slide:
   - Add the slide title as a bold heading.
   - Paste the body copy in 2–5 short blocks.
   - Keep lines short; aim for no more than ~40–60 words per slide.

### Step C — Minimal formatting (so it looks “real”)
- Choose one theme font (e.g., Inter/Arial).
- Use 2 colors max (dark text + one accent color).
- Add a small footer line on every slide:
  - `Legitimacy: https://6ff73b98-dcff-4f53-a10d-9ad392f63342-00-1n4h4nuiq6w8j.picard.replit.dev/sites/4 | Partner: agent_bob_replit+lead-copilot@agentmail.to`

### Step D — Export PDF
File → Download → PDF Document (.pdf)

### Step E — Sanity checks
- Slide 1 has: product name + agency positioning
- A pricing slide clearly shows **Partner pays $299–$499** and **Client pays $499–$999**
- One slide includes the **3×10 accounts plan** and volume tiers
- Final slide includes: website URL + contact email
