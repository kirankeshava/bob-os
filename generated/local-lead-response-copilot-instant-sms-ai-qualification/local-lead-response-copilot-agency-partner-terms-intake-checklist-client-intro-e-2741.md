## 1) Agency Partner Terms (Plain-Language, Non‑Legal)
**Purpose:** This document sets clear expectations so agencies can resell Lead Response Copilot (“LRC”) to their clients without founder involvement.

### Parties
- **Provider:** Local Lead Response Copilot (“LRC”). Contact: agent_bob_replit+lead-copilot@agentmail.to
- **Agency Partner:** The agency reselling LRC.
- **Client:** The end business receiving lead response + qualification.

### What LRC Does
LRC instantly responds to new leads (from web forms, Facebook Lead Ads, etc.) via SMS and asks a short qualification flow. Based on answers, LRC routes outcomes (booked, qualified for callback, not qualified, no response).

### White-Label / Client-Facing Positioning
- Agency may present LRC as “powered by” the agency or as an internal automation system.
- If client asks for vendor info, Agency may share the legitimacy link above.

### Pricing, Billing, and Minimum Commitment (Agency → LRC)
- Agency is billed per **active client account** at the agreed wholesale price (typical: **$299–$499/account/month**).
- **Minimum commitment for priority support:** minimum **10 active accounts** (can be ramped over 30 days by written plan). Priority support is defined below.
- Agency may set its own retail pricing to clients (typical: **$499–$999/account/month**).

### Setup / Implementation
- Standard setup target: **24–48 business hours** after Agency delivers complete intake requirements (see Intake Checklist below).
- Changes requested after launch (new questions, routing logic, booking rules, or multiple pipelines) may require an updated timeline.

### Support & Escalation
- **Standard support:** email support via agent_bob_replit+lead-copilot@agentmail.to. Target response: 1–2 business days.
- **Priority support (10+ active accounts):** target response: same business day for critical issues.
- **Critical issue definition:** Lead delivery failure, SMS delivery failure across many leads, booking link down, or system-wide outages.

### Performance Disclaimer (Important)
LRC improves speed-to-lead and consistency. Final outcomes depend on lead quality, client pricing, client fulfillment capacity, and the client’s willingness to answer qualification questions. LRC does not guarantee revenue, booked jobs, or specific conversion rates.

### Compliance, Consent, and Acceptable Use
Agency and Client agree to:
- Only message leads who provided consent or otherwise have a lawful basis to be contacted.
- Follow local regulations (TCPA/CTIA guidelines in the U.S. where applicable).
- Not use LRC for prohibited categories (spam, harassment, illegal products/services, deceptive claims).

### Data Handling (DPA‑Lite)
- LRC processes lead data solely to provide the service (respond, qualify, route, report).
- Data typically includes: name, phone, email (if captured), lead source, answers to qualification questions, timestamps.
- Agency is responsible for ensuring the Client has permission to collect and share lead data.
- LRC will not sell lead data.

### Access & Security
- Agency designates at least one admin contact.
- Client-facing access (if any) is provided at Agency’s discretion.

### Reporting
Monthly reporting includes, at minimum:
- Median response time
- Contact rate
- Qualified rate
- Booked rate
- Lead status breakdown
(Template included in the main partner kit.)

### Suspension / Termination
- Accounts may be suspended for non-payment (where applicable), abuse, or compliance violations.
- Either party may terminate an account with reasonable notice. Agency is responsible for informing Clients of any changes.

### Liability (Plain Language)
LRC is not responsible for indirect losses (missed revenue, missed jobs, etc.). LRC’s responsibility is limited to providing the described automation and fixing service issues in a reasonable timeframe.

**Acknowledgment:** Agency acknowledges these are operational terms (not legal advice) and may incorporate them into its own client agreement.

## 2) Agency Onboarding Checklist + Kickoff Form Fields (Copy/Paste)
**Goal:** Launch each new client in <48 hours.

### A) Client Info
- Business name
- Service area (cities/zip codes)
- Business hours (including weekends)
- Primary contact name + phone (for escalation)

### B) Lead Sources (choose all that apply)
- Website form (URL + form tool: WP/Elementor/Webflow/etc.)
- Facebook Lead Ads (page + ad account access plan)
- Google Local Services Ads / other
- Call tracking provider (if leads arrive via SMS/email)

### C) Routing Rules (what happens after qualification?)
Choose one:
1) **Book appointment automatically** (preferred)
2) **Warm transfer to sales** (text “Ready now?” then call)
3) **Collect details and create a “Qualified - Call Back” task**

### D) Booking Details (if booking)
- Booking platform (Calendly / Acuity / Google Calendar booking / CRM)
- Booking link(s)
- Available days/times
- Appointment duration
- Buffer time
- Time zone

### E) Qualification Flow (keep it short)
Provide:
- Top 3–5 questions (example: job type, urgency, zip code, budget range, photos)
- Disqualifiers (examples: outside service area, minimum job size, not urgent)
- “Qualified” definition (what must be true)

### F) Offer + Messaging Tone
- Brand voice: friendly/professional/direct
- Any required disclaimers
- Promo wording (if any)

### G) Team Notifications
- Who gets notified on **Qualified** leads (names + phones/emails)
- Who gets notified on **Booked**
- Who gets notified on **Hot lead / urgent**

### H) Lead Status Labels (for reporting)
Use these defaults (edit if needed):
- New Lead
- Contacted
- No Response
- Qualified
- Not Qualified
- Booked
- Canceled/No-show

### I) QA Checklist (Agency does this before “Go Live”)
- Submit 3 test leads (morning/afternoon/evening)
- Confirm response time < 60 seconds
- Confirm questions are correct + not too long
- Confirm booking link works on mobile
- Confirm notifications go to the right people
- Confirm reporting table is populating
