IMPORTANT: This is a plain-language business agreement template, not legal advice.

A) Parties & Purpose
- Vendor: Local Lead Response Copilot (“Copilot”) provides instant SMS response + automated lead qualification + booking workflows.
- Agency Partner (“Agency”): Resells Copilot to Agency’s clients and manages client relationships.
- Purpose: Agency purchases Copilot accounts from Vendor and resells to clients under Agency’s brand (white-label permitted).

B) White‑Label & Branding
- Agency may present the service as part of Agency’s offering.
- Vendor name does not need to appear in Agency’s client-facing materials.
- Agency may request basic branding adjustments (copy, tone, initial message) per client.

C) Commercial Terms (Baseline)
- Agency pays Vendor per active client account at the agreed wholesale rate ($299–$499/account/month depending on tier/volume).
- Agency sets its own retail price to client (recommended $499–$999/account/month).
- Wholesale fees are billed monthly in arrears or monthly in advance (choose one operationally and keep consistent).
- Usage-based pass-through costs (e.g., SMS/telephony) are handled as:
  Option 1 (Preferred simplicity): included up to a reasonable fair-use threshold; overages billed at cost.
  Option 2: billed directly to client by Agency.
  (Agency selects and documents chosen approach per client.)

D) Minimum Commitment & Volume Discounts
- Minimum commitment for priority support/fast-launch: 3 active accounts with a 60-day commitment.
- Volume discount tiers (example):
  • 1–2 accounts: $499/account
  • 3–9 accounts: $399/account
  • 10–24 accounts: $349/account
  • 25+ accounts: $299/account
- If active accounts drop below the tier minimum, pricing reverts to the applicable tier next billing cycle.

E) Implementation & Launch Responsibilities
Vendor provides:
- Intake + configuration support, qualification flow setup, booking link routing, and QA.
- Best-practice templates for home services/local lead gen.

Agency provides:
- Client’s lead sources (form URL/fields, FB Lead Ads access/export, webhook/Zapier details if used).
- Client’s business rules (service area, job types, hours, booking availability).
- Compliance confirmation (opt-in language on forms/ads; required disclaimers).

F) Support Levels & Response Time
- Standard support: business-day response (target 1–2 business days).
- Priority support (requires minimum commitment above): target same-day response on business days.
- Emergency issues (system down/lead routing failure): Agency flags “URGENT” via email to agent_bob_replit+lead-copilot@agentmail.to.

G) Data Handling & Privacy
- Agency remains the primary owner of client relationship.
- Vendor processes lead data solely to provide the service (respond/qualify/book/report).
- Vendor will not sell lead data.
- Agency is responsible for ensuring the client has appropriate consent language for SMS/contact.
- Upon termination, Vendor will provide export of conversation logs/lead statuses upon request (subject to reasonable limits).

H) Performance & Disclaimers
- Vendor will provide reporting on speed-to-lead and funnel conversion metrics.
- Vendor does not guarantee a specific revenue result; outcomes depend on lead quality, offer, pricing, availability, and follow-up.

I) Cancellation & Pausing
- Month-to-month after any stated commitment period.
- Cancellation requires notice before the next billing date to avoid renewal.
- Agencies can pause an account (e.g., seasonal) with 7 days notice; paused accounts may incur a reduced maintenance fee if resources are still reserved (optional; define later).

J) Non-Circumvention (Optional)
- Vendor will not directly solicit Agency’s end clients.
- Agency will not attempt to replicate Vendor’s proprietary workflows by extracting system prompts/logic beyond what is necessary to operate the service.
