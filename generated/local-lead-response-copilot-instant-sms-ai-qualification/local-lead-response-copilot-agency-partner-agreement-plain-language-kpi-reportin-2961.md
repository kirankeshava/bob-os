2) What you’re reselling
Provider delivers an Instant SMS + AI Qualification + Booking workflow that:
• Instantly responds to new leads (forms/FB leads/web chat) via SMS
• Qualifies via short, adaptive questions
• Routes/flags leads and triggers booking (call/appointment) per rules
Partner may resell this service to its end clients (“Client Accounts”) under Partner’s brand (“white-label”) or as a named product, as Partner prefers.

3) Relationship + positioning
• Partner is the primary seller and first-line relationship owner for the end client.
• Provider is the fulfillment and technical enablement layer.
• Partner agrees not to represent Provider’s product capabilities beyond what is documented in the deck/SOP.

4) Pricing to Partner (what you pay Provider)
Partner fees are per Client Account, per month (platform + workflow management). Usage-based carrier/telephony fees may apply if applicable to the chosen stack.

Standard tiers (Partner → Provider):
• Tier A (1–2 accounts): $499/account/month
• Tier B (3–9 accounts): $399/account/month
• Tier C (10–24 accounts): $349/account/month
• Tier D (25+ accounts): $299/account/month

Minimum commitment for discounted tiers:
• Tier B+: 3-account minimum commitment for 60 days.
• Tier C+: 10-account minimum commitment for 90 days.
• Tier D: 25-account minimum commitment for 120 days.

If Partner falls below the committed account minimum, pricing reverts to the applicable lower tier at the next billing cycle.

Recommended retail (Partner → Client):
• $499–$999/account/month depending on niche and lead volume.
This preserves margin while keeping pricing easy to sell.

5) Implementation fee (Week 1 policy)
During free-launch week (first 7 days), Provider implementation is free.
After free-launch week, Partner may choose to charge its clients an onboarding/setup fee; Provider may introduce a setup fee later with prior notice.

6) Payment terms
• Partner is billed monthly in advance for active Client Accounts.
• Partner is responsible for collecting payment from its clients.
• Non-payment may result in suspension of affected Client Accounts after notice.

7) Onboarding + launch timeline (what Partner can promise)
Standard timeline per Client Account:
• Day 0–1: Intake completed (assets + routing rules)
• Day 1–3: Build + QA + test leads
• Day 3–5: Launch + monitoring
Partner should avoid promising “same-day full automation” unless the client’s assets are already complete.

8) Partner responsibilities (required for success)
Partner will:
• Provide accurate client intake data (lead sources, form URLs, ad accounts, CRM access, booking link)
• Ensure the client has a compliant consent/opt-in notice for SMS where required
• Handle client communication, expectations, and approvals
• Review KPI report monthly with the client and request adjustments

9) Provider responsibilities
Provider will:
• Configure and maintain automation workflows per the agreed SOP
• Provide a monthly KPI report (template below) for each Client Account
• Provide support during business hours (see Support below)
• Make reasonable improvements to question flows and routing logic based on data

10) Support levels + response time
Base support (included):
• Email support via agent_bob_replit+lead-copilot@agentmail.to
• Typical response: within 1 business day
Priority support (for Partners meeting minimum commitments):
• Tier C and above: same-day response (business days) for P1 issues
• Tier D: same-day response + 1 monthly optimization review call (optional)

Issue severity definitions:
• P1: automation down or leads not receiving any response
• P2: partial routing errors or KPI tracking issues
• P3: copy/flow improvements, new variations, minor edits

11) Service boundaries (avoid scope creep)
Included:
• One primary SMS qualification flow per Client Account
• Standard routing outcomes (e.g., booked, hot lead, needs follow-up, not qualified)
• Monthly reporting + light optimization

Not included (unless agreed separately):
• Full CRM build-outs, ad management, website development
• Reputation management or pipeline sales coaching
• 24/7 phone answering or human SDR services

12) Data handling + confidentiality
• Partner and Provider will keep Client Account information confidential.
• Provider will only use client data to operate and improve the automation.
• Partner confirms it has the right to share necessary client data with Provider for fulfillment.

13) Compliance note (SMS/marketing)
Partner is responsible for ensuring the end client:
• Collects opt-in/consent for texting where required
• Honors STOP/unsubscribe requests
• Follows applicable laws and carrier policies
Provider will implement standard STOP handling where supported by the stack.

14) Term + cancellation
• This Agreement starts on the Effective Date and continues month-to-month unless canceled.
• Either party may cancel with 14 days’ notice.
• For discounted tiers with minimum commitments, early exit may forfeit discounts and revert pricing to Tier A for the committed period.

15) Client Account suspension/removal
Provider may suspend a Client Account if:
• It violates policy/law (spam, prohibited content)
• It creates excessive technical risk
• Partner fails to pay
Provider will notify Partner with the reason and remediation steps when possible.

16) Non-solicitation (optional)
Provider will not knowingly solicit Partner’s end clients for a competing engagement during the term of this Agreement.

Appendix A — “3×10 Accounts” Plan (30-Day Partner Growth Plan)
Goal: 30 active Client Accounts in 30 days across 3 agencies (or one agency scaling to 30).
Model:
• Partner pays Provider: avg $349/account (Tier C)
• Partner charges clients: avg $749/account
• Gross margin per account: ~$400/month

Weekly activity targets (per agency):
Week 1:
• Outreach: 50 targeted agencies or 50 targeted local businesses/day (if selling direct)
• Book: 10 demos
• Close: 3 accounts (fast-launch niches: plumbers, HVAC, roofing, pest, towing)
Week 2:
• Book: 10–15 demos
• Close: 4–6 accounts
Week 3:
• Close: 6–8 accounts
Week 4:
• Close: 7–10 accounts

Minimum pipeline math:
• If close rate is 20% from demo → client, you need ~50 demos to get 10 clients.
• If demo booking is 10% from outreach → demo, you need ~500 outreach touches to get 50 demos.
Operationally: 25 touches/day for 20 business days = 500 touches.
