## Sending SOP (deliverability + cadence)
1) **Warmup (Day 0–1):** Send 10–20 1:1 emails to the highest-fit prospects (not a blast). 
   - Personalize 1–2 lines.
   - Do not include more than 1 link (use {booking_link} OR website URL, not both, for warmup).
   - Log every send in the CRM (Status = “Warmup Sent”).

2) **Ramp schedule:**
   - Day 1: 25 emails
   - Day 2: 35 emails
   - Day 3: 50 emails
   - Day 4: 50 emails
   - Day 5: 40 emails (or remaining to reach 200)

3) **Reply handling rules:**
   - Positive interest → respond within 10 minutes, push to {booking_link}.
   - “Not now” → ask permission to check back in 30 days.
   - “Already have something” → ask what they use + offer a 7-day pilot as a side-by-side.
   - Unsubscribe/stop → mark “Do Not Contact” immediately.

4) **Logging rules (minimum fields):**
   - Company, segment (Agency/Operator), niche, location
   - Contact name + role
   - Email + LinkedIn URL
   - Personalization line used
   - Touch 1 date, Touch 2 date
   - Outcome: No reply / Replied / Demo booked / Not a fit / DNC

## Personalization prompts (fast 30–60 seconds)
Pick **one** and write a single sentence.
- Agency: “Saw you run FB lead gen for {vertical} and highlight {offer/result} on your site—curious how fast your clients are getting contacted after form fills.”
- Operator: “Noticed you offer {service} in {city}; if leads come in after-hours, do they get a text back immediately or wait until morning?”
- Booking/after-hours angle: “If you miss the first 60 seconds on a new lead, CPL stays the same but booked jobs drop—this is built to fix that.”
