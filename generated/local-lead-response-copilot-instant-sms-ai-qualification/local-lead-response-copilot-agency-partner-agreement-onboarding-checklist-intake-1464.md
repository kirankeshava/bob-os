# Local Lead Response Copilot — Agency Partner Agreement (Plain Language, Non‑Legal)

**Last updated:** {{DATE}}

## 1) Purpose
Provider supplies an instant SMS + AI lead qualification + booking workflow (“Service”). Agency Partner resells the Service to its own clients (“Client Accounts”) either branded as Provider or white-labeled under Agency Partner.

## 2) What’s included (per Client Account)
- Instant first text to new leads (forms/FB lead ads/webhooks)
- AI qualification via short question flow (customizable)
- Routing rules + lead status tagging (New / Contacted / Qualified / Unqualified / Booked / No Response)
- Booking handoff (calendar link / call request / appointment request)
- Monthly KPI reporting inputs (median response time, contact, qualified, booked)

**Not included unless separately agreed:** paid phone numbers, paid SMS/telecom usage, CRM licenses, ad spend, landing pages, or custom software development.

## 3) Pricing, margins, and minimum commitment
Agency Partner agrees to the pricing tier selected below. Agency Partner sets its own end-client pricing.

**Standard tiers (Agency pays Provider):**
- **Starter:** $499 / account / month (1–2 accounts)
- **Growth:** $399 / account / month (3–9 accounts)
- **Scale:** $299 / account / month (10+ accounts)

**Minimum commitment for priority support:**
- Priority Support requires **a minimum of 3 active accounts** OR **$1,197/month minimum** (equivalent). If below minimum, support is standard.

**Billing cadence:** monthly in advance. (If invoices are needed, Agency Partner requests invoicing by email.)

## 4) 3×10 Accounts plan (shared target)
Agency Partner’s goal is to close **10 accounts in each of 3 verticals** (example: plumbing, HVAC, roofing). Provider supports repeatable deployment by using standardized intake, a launch checklist, and monthly KPI reporting.

## 5) White-label terms
- Agency Partner may present the Service as its own offering.
- Provider may remain invisible to end clients unless Agency Partner requests co-selling support.
- Agency Partner agrees not to reverse engineer or represent Provider’s internal systems as its own proprietary software.

## 6) Implementation responsibilities
**Provider responsibilities:**
- Configure workflows based on Agency Partner’s intake form
- Provide QA checklist and launch confirmation
- Provide KPI definitions and reporting template

**Agency Partner responsibilities:**
- Provide accurate Client Account inputs (brand, services, hours, qualification criteria)
- Obtain Client consent for SMS contact and data sharing
- Ensure lead sources are connected correctly (form/webhook/FB leads)
- Manage client relationship, billing, and expectations

## 7) Support & response times
- **Standard Support:** email support during business days; response typically within 1–2 business days.
- **Priority Support (requires minimum commitment):** faster responses targeted within 1 business day; limited “launch window” assistance for new accounts.

## 8) Service levels & limitations
- Provider does not guarantee specific revenue outcomes.
- Uptime depends on third-party providers (SMS carriers, webhooks, ad platforms). Provider will make reasonable efforts to restore service quickly.

## 9) Data handling & privacy
- Provider processes lead data solely to deliver the Service.
- Agency Partner retains ownership of its Client relationships.
- Agency Partner is responsible for ensuring all client data collection and SMS outreach complies with applicable laws (e.g., TCPA, CAN-SPAM where relevant) and that leads have consent to receive texts.

## 10) Compliance (SMS consent)
Agency Partner must ensure Client Accounts have:
- Clear opt-in language on forms (e.g., “By submitting, you agree to receive texts…”) 
- A method to opt-out (e.g., “Reply STOP to opt out”)
- Truthful identity of sender in initial text when required

## 11) Term, cancellation, and offboarding
- Month-to-month unless otherwise agreed.
- Either party may cancel with **14 days notice** before the next billing cycle.
- Upon cancellation, Provider will stop workflows at end of paid period.

## 12) Non-solicitation (optional)
If Agency Partner introduces Provider to a Client Account, Provider will not solicit that client directly for 12 months after termination, unless the client contacts Provider first.

## 13) Acceptance
By replying “I agree” via email from an authorized Agency Partner representative, this Agreement is accepted.

# Agency Partner Onboarding Checklist (Internal)
Use this checklist for every new agency and every new Client Account.

## A) Partner setup (one-time)
1. Confirm Partner primary contact + billing email
2. Confirm pricing tier (Starter/Growth/Scale) + minimum commitment status
3. Share Partner Welcome Packet links:
   - Product legitimacy URL: https://6ff73b98-dcff-4f53-a10d-9ad392f63342-00-1n4h4nuiq6w8j.picard.replit.dev/sites/4
   - Support email: agent_bob_replit+lead-copilot@agentmail.to
   - SOP + KPI template + deck/one-pager (provided separately)
4. Confirm whether partner is **white-label** or **co-branded**

## B) Client Account launch (repeatable)
**Goal:** launch in <48 hours once intake is complete.

1. Intake form completed (see next section)
2. Lead sources confirmed:
   - Website form(s) / webhook
   - FB Lead Ads connection
   - Other sources (Zapier, Make, CRM)
3. Qualification logic confirmed:
   - Services offered + service area
   - Disqualifiers (e.g., outside area, wrong job type)
   - After-hours handling
4. Booking path confirmed:
   - Calendar link OR
   - “Call now” routing OR
   - Appointment request form
5. SMS compliance items confirmed:
   - Opt-in language on forms
   - STOP/opt-out handling
6. QA test:
   - Submit test lead
   - Verify response time
   - Verify qualification questions
   - Verify booked/handoff event
7. Launch confirmation sent to partner
8. Schedule first KPI review date (end of week 1)
