1) Parties
This White‑Label Partner Agreement (“Agreement”) is between:
• Provider: Local Lead Response Copilot (“Provider”, “we”, “us”)
• Partner Agency: ______________________________ (“Partner”, “you”)
Effective date: ______________________________

2) What you’re reselling
Provider offers a micro‑SaaS that:
• Instantly texts new leads from forms/FB ads
• Asks short AI‑driven qualification questions
• Routes outcomes (qualified/not qualified/no response), and can book calls/appointments

You may resell this service to your clients under your agency brand (“white-label”) while Provider operates the underlying system.

3) White‑label rules
3.1 Brand and client relationship
• Partner owns the client relationship and billing.
• Provider may remain invisible to the end client unless Partner requests otherwise.

3.2 No misrepresentation
Partner will not claim the underlying software is fully built/owned by Partner if that would be materially misleading. Acceptable: “powered by our automation platform,” “built with our systems,” etc.

4) Pricing to Partner + minimum commitments
4.1 Partner wholesale pricing (per client account / per month)
Partner pays Provider according to the tier below. “Account” means one end‑client location/brand receiving lead responses.

Tier A (1–3 accounts): $499/account/month
Tier B (4–9 accounts): $399/account/month
Tier C (10–24 accounts): $349/account/month
Tier D (25+ accounts): $299/account/month

4.2 Minimum commitment for priority support
To receive Priority Support (defined in Section 6), Partner agrees to maintain at least 10 active accounts (“Minimum Commitment”).
• If active accounts fall below 10, Provider may move Partner to Standard Support the next billing cycle.

4.3 Recommended client retail pricing (your margin)
Partner is encouraged (not required) to charge clients:
• $499–$999/account/month depending on niche and lead volume.

5) Term, billing, and cancellation
5.1 Term
This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues month‑to‑month.

5.2 Billing
• Provider invoices Partner monthly for active accounts.
• Accounts are considered active if the system is live and able to respond to leads, regardless of lead volume.

5.3 Cancellation
• Partner may cancel an individual client account with 7 days’ written notice to agent_bob_replit+lead-copilot@agentmail.to.
• Provider may cancel an account for nonpayment, abuse, or compliance risk.

5.4 No refunds (simple rule)
Because usage and set‑up work is front‑loaded, monthly fees are non‑refundable once the billing period begins.

6) Support, response times, and boundaries
6.1 Standard Support (default)
• Email support to agent_bob_replit+lead-copilot@agentmail.to
• Target response time: within 2 business days

6.2 Priority Support (10+ active accounts)
• Email support to agent_bob_replit+lead-copilot@agentmail.to
• Target response time: within 1 business day
• Includes up to 2 optimization requests per account per month (prompt tweaks, routing tweaks, qualification flow tweaks)

6.3 What support includes
• Troubleshooting lead capture → SMS → qualification flow
• Updating qualification questions and routing rules
• Reporting assistance (KPIs in Section 9)

6.4 What support does NOT include
• Writing/overhauling Partner’s ad copy, buying media, or running ads
• Building complex custom software beyond the standard implementation SOP (unless separately agreed)

7) Implementation requirements (Partner responsibilities)
Partner will provide or coordinate:
• Lead source access/credentials or webhook/Zap details (form, landing page, CRM, FB lead ads)
• The client’s business hours + service area + service list
• Booking link or calendar rules (or call routing instructions)
• A compliant contact method for leads (phone/SMS-capable number) where applicable

If Partner cannot provide required inputs, go‑live timelines may slip.

8) Compliance + acceptable use
8.1 Consent
Partner is responsible for ensuring lead texting is compliant with applicable laws and platform rules (e.g., consent language on forms, opt‑out handling).

8.2 Opt‑out
Provider will support basic opt‑out language and suppression where applicable; Partner agrees not to instruct Provider to send spam.

8.3 Prohibited uses
No illegal products/services, harassment, hate, scams, or deceptive practices.

9) Reporting + KPIs (what we track monthly)
Provider will make available (or Partner can compile) a monthly KPI snapshot including:
• Median response time (seconds)
• Contact rate (% of leads who replied)
• Qualified rate (% of leads marked qualified)
• Booked rate (% of leads who booked / were scheduled)
• Lead status breakdown counts (New, Contacted, No Response, Qualified, Not Qualified, Booked, Lost)

10) Confidentiality
Both parties agree to keep confidential any non‑public information about systems, pricing, prompts, routing logic, and client data.

11) Data handling (simple)
• Partner remains the primary “owner” of the client relationship.
• Provider processes lead data solely to deliver the service.
• Partner should avoid sending sensitive data unless needed.

12) Public references
Partner may reference “automated speed-to-lead qualification” in marketing. Provider will not publicly list Partner’s clients without permission.

13) Limitation of liability (plain language)
Provider is not responsible for Partner’s ad performance, lead quality, or revenue outcomes. Our obligation is to operate the response/qualification system as described and support it under Section 6.

14) Agreement changes
Provider may update these terms with 30 days’ notice via email, primarily to reflect product changes or compliance requirements.
