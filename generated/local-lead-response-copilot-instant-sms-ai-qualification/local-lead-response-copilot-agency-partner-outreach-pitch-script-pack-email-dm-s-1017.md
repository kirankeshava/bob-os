## 3) Partner Pitch Call Script (10–12 minutes)

**Goal:** Agency agrees to a minimum commitment and next step (pilot cohort + onboarding).

### Opening (0:00–1:00)
“Thanks for the time. I’ll keep this tight: what we do, why agencies sell it, pricing/margins, and what implementation looks like. If it makes sense, we’ll pick your first 3–10 client targets and launch.”

### Problem (1:00–3:00) — speed-to-lead
“Your clients are paying for leads, but they often respond late. When response time slips, the lead gets claimed by the fastest competitor. This product is designed to remove the response delay automatically.”

### Solution (3:00–6:00) — what it does
“Lead Response Copilot:
1) **Instant SMS** to every new inbound lead from forms/FB ads
2) **AI-driven qualification**: a short, friendly Q&A (2–4 questions)
3) **Routing + booking**: hands off a qualified lead, or books a call/appointment
4) **Reporting**: response time, contact rate, qualified rate, booked rate, and lead status breakdown

The pitch to clients is simple: ‘We stop you from bleeding high-intent leads and help you book more jobs from the leads you already pay for.’”

### Partner model (6:00–8:30)
“White-label. You sell it as your add-on.
- You pay us **$299–$499/account**
- You charge clients **$499–$999/account**
- Month-to-month after initial commitment

We recommend a minimum: **10 accounts** to get priority support and onboarding. Volume discounts kick in as you scale.”

### Implementation (8:30–10:30)
“Implementation is fast:
- We connect lead source → instant SMS
- We configure the qualification questions
- We define outcomes: booked / qualified / not qualified / no response
- We launch, then review KPIs weekly in month 1

We provide the SOP, scripts, and monthly report template.”

### Close (10:30–12:00)
“Best next step: pick your first **3 clients** that already spend on leads and complain about lead quality or missed calls. If you can commit to **10 installs over the next 30 days**, we’ll lock in priority support and volume pricing. Want me to send the kit and a 3-client rollout checklist?”

## 5) Objection Handling Mini-Library (Copy/Paste)

### Objection: “My clients already respond fast.”
“Totally—then this becomes a *safety net* and a consistency guarantee after hours, weekends, and busy periods. We measure median response time and contact rate so you can prove the lift.”

### Objection: “Isn’t this just another chatbot?”
“It’s not a website chatbot. It’s **instant SMS** to a lead’s phone the moment they submit, which is where response speed matters most. The AI part is only to ask short qualifying questions and route/booking.”

### Objection: “What about compliance/opt-outs?”
“We use straightforward, consent-based messaging tied to lead form submission, include opt-out language, and maintain a clear lead status log. If a lead opts out, the workflow stops.”

### Objection: “Will this replace our receptionist/sales team?”
“No—this prevents lead decay. It captures the lead while intent is high, qualifies, then hands off to the human team with context.”

### Objection: “Setup sounds like work.”
“Setup is intentionally simple: connect lead source, choose 2–4 questions, choose routing/booking outcome. Most partners standardize it across niches and launch quickly.”

### Objection: “How do we show ROI?”
“We report: median response time, contact rate, qualified rate, booked rate, and lead status breakdown. The ‘before vs after’ story is usually clear within the first 1–2 weeks.”
