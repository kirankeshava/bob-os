## The Problem (why agencies lose easy wins)
Local leads are time-sensitive. If your client doesn’t respond in the first 1–5 minutes, the lead goes to the next provider. The result: you pay for leads, but the client’s response time kills conversion, LTV, and retention.

**Speed-to-lead is a controllable KPI**. You can’t always get more leads cheaply—but you *can* convert more of the ones you already have.

---
## The KPI Promise
**< 60 seconds response time** to every new lead from:
- Website forms
- Facebook Lead Ads
- Google/LSA lead forms (via integrations)
- Any webhook/CRM source

---
## What it does (in plain English)
1) A new lead comes in (form/FB ad/webhook)
2) They instantly receive an SMS from the business
3) The system asks **3–5 short questions** to qualify them (job type, timing, location, budget/urgency, etc.)
4) If qualified → **books a call/appointment automatically**
5) Writes notes back to CRM + notifies the team

**Outcome:** fewer missed leads, higher booked rates, cleaner handoffs, and less admin work.

---
## How it works (simple flow)
**Trigger → SMS → Qualification → Booking → CRM update**
- Trigger: new lead arrives
- SMS: first text sent instantly (under a minute)
- Questions: short, mobile-friendly (AI-driven logic)
- Routing: different paths for qualified vs unqualified
- Booking: Calendly / Google Calendar / Acuity
- CRM notes: HubSpot / HighLevel / Pipedrive / Zapier
