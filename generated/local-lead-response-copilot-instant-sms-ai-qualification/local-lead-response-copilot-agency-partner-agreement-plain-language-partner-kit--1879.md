1) Purpose
This agreement describes how Agency Partner (“Partner”) may resell the Local Lead Response Copilot service (“Service”) to Partner’s end clients (“Client(s)”). It is written in plain language for speed and clarity and is not legal advice.

2) What the Service Does
The Service is designed to:
- Instantly respond to new inbound leads via SMS.
- Ask a short sequence of qualification questions.
- Route outcomes (qualified / not qualified / no response) and optionally book a call/appointment.
- Provide basic monthly reporting on speed-to-lead and funnel outcomes.

3) Relationship & White-Label
- Partner may present the Service as “powered by” Local Lead Response Copilot or as a fully white-labeled component inside Partner’s offer.
- Local Lead Response Copilot remains the underlying service provider.
- Partner owns the client relationship (unless Partner requests a direct handoff).

4) Pricing, Margins, and Minimum Commitment
Standard wholesale pricing (what Partner pays Local Lead Response Copilot):
- 1–2 active client accounts: $499/account/month
- 3–9 active client accounts: $399/account/month
- 10–29 active client accounts: $349/account/month
- 30+ active client accounts: $299/account/month

Suggested retail pricing (what Partner charges Client): $499–$999/account/month.

Minimum commitment for discounted tiers:
- To receive 10+ pricing, Partner commits to maintaining at least 10 active accounts for a minimum of 60 days.
- If Partner drops below the committed tier, wholesale pricing reverts to the tier matching the current active account count starting the next billing cycle.

Week 1 policy note (this launch period):
- For the first 7 days, Service can be offered as a free trial to Clients; Partner and Local Lead Response Copilot coordinate activation without collecting payment in Week 1.

5) What Counts as an “Active Account”
An “active account” is a Client location/brand where the Service is live and responding to new leads. (Paused/off accounts are not active.)

6) Setup & Launch Responsibilities
Partner is responsible for collecting and providing the required intake information from the Client (see Section 7).
Local Lead Response Copilot is responsible for:
- Building the Client’s qualification flow and routing rules.
- Connecting lead sources (forms/FB leads via available integrations/zaps/webhooks as applicable).
- Running QA tests with Partner/Client.
- Launching and monitoring initial performance.

7) Client Intake Requirements (Partner Must Provide)
For each Client, Partner provides:
- Business name, service area, business hours.
- Lead sources: website form(s), FB Lead Ads, Google LSAs, or other.
- Intake goals: what is a “qualified lead” (e.g., job types, minimum ticket, zip codes, homeowner status).
- Booking instructions: booking link or calendar rules (or “handoff to office”).
- Routing: who receives notifications, backup contact, call center instructions.
- Compliance notes: required opt-in language on forms and/or privacy policy link.

8) Reporting & KPIs
Each month Partner receives a KPI summary per Client (or can export/share), including:
- Median response time (lead submission → first SMS sent)
- Contact rate (reached by SMS / total leads)
- Qualified rate (qualified / reached)
- Booked rate (booked / qualified)
- Lead status breakdown: New, Contacted, Engaged, Qualified, Not Qualified, Booked, No Response

Partner may share these KPIs with Clients as part of Partner’s own reporting.

9) Support, Priority Support & Communications
Default support:
- Support via email: agent_bob_replit+lead-copilot@agentmail.to
- Standard response target: within 1 business day.

Priority support (for Partners with minimum commitment of 10+ active accounts):
- Same-day response target during business days for launch issues and critical routing failures.
- Priority queue for new account launches.

What is considered “critical”:
- Leads not receiving the first message.
- Messages not routing to the correct party.
- Booking link failures or obvious mis-qualification due to a configuration error.

10) Data Handling & Privacy (Plain-Language)
- Lead data is used only to operate and improve the Service for the Client.
- Partner and Client are responsible for ensuring they have the right to contact leads (proper opt-in and compliant form language).
- Partner should not upload sensitive data (e.g., SSNs, credit card numbers) into lead form fields.
- If a Client requests deletion of lead data, Partner may submit the request via the support email.

11) Acceptable Use
Partner/Client will not use the Service to:
- Spam, harass, or contact leads without appropriate consent.
- Send unlawful content.
- Impersonate another business.
- Target restricted content categories where SMS compliance is not met.

12) Uptime & Limitations
- The Service depends on third-party SMS and integration providers; occasional delays can occur.
- The Service is designed to improve speed-to-lead and qualification; it does not guarantee revenue outcomes.

13) Term, Pausing, and Cancellation
- Partner may pause or cancel Client accounts with notice before the next billing cycle.
- If a Client account is paused, it will stop messaging new leads; paused accounts are not billed as active (unless otherwise agreed for reserved capacity).

14) Non-Solicitation (Optional / Recommended)
- Local Lead Response Copilot will not knowingly solicit Partner’s Clients directly for a competing service during the term of the partnership.
- Partner will not attempt to reverse engineer or resell the Service under a competing standalone product brand.

15) Agreement Acknowledgement
By replying “AGREE” to the email containing this agreement (or by onboarding the first Client account), Partner acknowledges and agrees to these terms.
