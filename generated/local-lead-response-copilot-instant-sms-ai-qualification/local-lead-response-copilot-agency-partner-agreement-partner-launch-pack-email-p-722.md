1) Purpose
Partner will resell Provider’s “Instant SMS + AI Qualification + Booking” solution (the “Service”) to Partner’s clients (“End Clients”). The parties agree to a white-label friendly relationship where Partner owns the End Client relationship and Provider supplies the underlying Service.

2) Commercial Terms (Pricing, Margin, Billing)
2.1 Wholesale pricing to Partner (per End Client account / location):
- Standard: $499/account/month
- Growth: $399/account/month (10+ active accounts)
- Scale: $299/account/month (30+ active accounts)

2.2 Partner retail pricing (recommended, Partner-controlled): $499–$999/account/month.

2.3 Setup/implementation fees:
- Partner may charge End Clients a setup fee (recommended: $250–$750/account) for onboarding and integration work.
- Provider wholesale setup fee to Partner: $0 unless custom work is requested beyond the standard SOP.

2.4 Billing
- Provider bills Partner monthly in advance for active accounts.
- Invoices are due upon receipt (or Net 7 by written approval).
- Non-payment may result in pausing Service on affected accounts after notice.

3) Minimum Commitment + Priority Support
3.1 Minimum commitment for priority support: Partner commits to a minimum of 10 active accounts within 60 days of signing OR a minimum monthly bill of $3,990 (Growth tier equivalent).

3.2 If Partner does not meet the minimum commitment, Partner may still use the Service at Standard tier, and support is provided on a standard (best-effort) basis.

4) White-Label + Branding
4.1 Partner may present the Service under Partner’s brand to End Clients.
4.2 Provider will not contact End Clients directly unless Partner requests it in writing for a specific support case.
4.3 Partner agrees not to represent Provider’s platform as owned by Partner (i.e., no false claims of proprietary ownership). “Powered by” language is optional.

5) What the Service Includes (Baseline)
Provider provides:
- Instant SMS outreach to new leads (from forms/FB leads/webhooks/CRM where supported)
- AI-driven qualification flow (short Q&A) aligned to Partner’s chosen vertical
- Routing logic and lead status tagging (New / Contacted / Qualified / Not Qualified / Booked / No Response / Bad Data)
- Booking link handoff and/or appointment booking flow (depending on End Client’s tools)
- Monthly KPI reporting template + exports (as available)

6) Partner Responsibilities
Partner is responsible for:
- Collecting End Client requirements (hours, booking link, services, service area, exclusions)
- Ensuring End Client has consent language on forms/lead ads as required by law/policies
- Providing access/credentials needed for integrations (e.g., webhooks/CRM/booking)
- First-line End Client communication and expectation setting

7) Compliance + Consent (SMS)
7.1 Partner and End Clients must comply with applicable texting laws and policies (e.g., TCPA, CTIA guidelines, platform ad lead policies).
7.2 End Client forms/ads must include clear consent language for SMS follow-up.
7.3 Provider may request proof of consent language if deliverability issues or complaints arise.

8) Data Handling + Confidentiality
8.1 Each party agrees to keep the other party’s confidential information private.
8.2 Lead data belongs to the End Client (and/or Partner as their agent). Provider uses data only to provide the Service.
8.3 Provider will take reasonable measures to protect data; however, no system can guarantee absolute security.

9) Support + Service Levels (SLA-lite)
9.1 Support channel: agent_bob_replit+lead-copilot@agentmail.to
9.2 Response targets:
- Priority Partners (meeting minimum commitment): first response within 1 business day for standard issues.
- Standard Partners: best-effort response.
9.3 Emergencies (system down / message failure): Provider will prioritize remediation and communicate status.

10) Reporting + KPIs
Provider will provide monthly performance reporting per account (minimum fields):
- Median response time
- Contact rate
- Qualified rate
- Booked rate
- Lead status breakdown

11) Term + Termination
11.1 Term: month-to-month unless otherwise agreed.
11.2 Termination: either party may terminate with 30 days written notice.
11.3 Upon termination:
- Provider will stop Service for the terminated accounts at end of paid period.
- Partner remains responsible for any unpaid invoices.

12) Non-Circumvention (Mutual Respect)
Provider agrees not to solicit Partner’s End Clients directly. Partner agrees not to reverse engineer or replicate Provider’s proprietary workflows for the purpose of building a competing, substantially similar service.

13) Publicity
Partner may list Provider as a vendor privately. Public co-marketing requires written approval by both parties.

14) Limitation of Liability (Plain Language)
Provider is not liable for indirect damages (lost profits, etc.). Provider’s total liability is limited to amounts paid by Partner for the affected account(s) in the prior 30 days.
