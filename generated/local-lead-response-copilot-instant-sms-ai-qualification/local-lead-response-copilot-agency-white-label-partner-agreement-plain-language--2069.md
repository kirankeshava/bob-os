1) Parties
This Partner Agreement (“Agreement”) is between:
• “Provider”: Local Lead Response Copilot (“LLRC”), contact agent_bob_replit+lead-copilot@agentmail.to
• “Agency Partner” (“Partner”): the reseller/agency implementing LLRC for its end clients.

2) What LLRC Does (Service Description)
LLRC provides an instant lead-response and qualification workflow that:
• Texts new leads immediately after form/lead events (e.g., website forms, lead ads, CRM events)
• Asks short, dynamic qualification questions
• Routes outcomes (book call/appointment, request more info, follow up, or mark unqualified)
• Provides reporting metrics: median response time, contact rate, qualified rate, booked rate, and lead status breakdown.

3) White-Label / Resale Rights
Partner may resell LLRC to end clients under Partner’s brand (“white-label”).
• Partner owns the client relationship.
• Provider remains the underlying service operator.
• Partner will not represent LLRC as a separate legal entity or claim ownership of Provider’s IP.

4) Pricing (Partner Cost) + Volume Discounts
Partner agrees to pay Provider a monthly platform fee per end-client account (“Account”).
Standard Partner pricing guidance:
• 1–2 Accounts: $499 / account / month
• 3–9 Accounts: $399 / account / month
• 10–24 Accounts: $349 / account / month
• 25+ Accounts: $299 / account / month

End-client suggested retail (Partner charges clients): $499–$999 / account / month (Partner sets final pricing).

5) Minimum Commitment + Priority Support
To access priority support and onboarding SLAs, Partner agrees to a minimum commitment of:
• 3 Accounts minimum, 90-day commitment (rolling), or
• 10 Accounts minimum for “priority + volume” tier.

If Partner is below 3 active Accounts, support is best-effort (still supported, but not SLA-backed).

6) Trial / Week 1 Launch Policy
If Provider is in a free-launch period (e.g., Week 1), Accounts may be onboarded at $0 for the defined trial window. After trial, Accounts convert to the applicable Partner pricing tier unless cancelled before the conversion date.

7) Billing, Payment Terms, and Taxes
• Billing cadence: monthly in advance per active Account.
• Activation date: the date an Account goes live (messages enabled) or 7 days after build completion—whichever comes first.
• Partner is responsible for any taxes/VAT due on resale to its clients.
• Non-payment may result in pausing message sending and automation for affected Accounts.

8) What Partner Must Provide (Implementation Requirements)
Partner is responsible for collecting and supplying accurate client inputs:
• Business name, service area, hours, and offer details
• Qualification questions and disqualifiers (or approval to use LLRC default flows)
• Booking link or appointment routing instructions
• Compliance/opt-in language used on lead forms (must include consent for SMS contact)
• Access/integration details: webhook/CRM/Zapier details as applicable

Partner must ensure the end client has obtained appropriate consent from leads for SMS contact (see Section 12).

9) Support + Onboarding Expectations (SLA-Style)
If Partner meets the minimum commitment for priority support:
• Initial build target: 2–5 business days after receiving complete intake
• Changes/optimizations: 2–3 business days for standard requests
• Incident response: acknowledgement within 1 business day

Support channels:
• Email: agent_bob_replit+lead-copilot@agentmail.to

Best-effort support applies outside priority tiers or when intake is incomplete.

10) Reporting & KPIs
Provider will make available a monthly KPI snapshot per Account including:
• Median response time (seconds/minutes)
• Contact rate (reached / total leads)
• Qualified rate (qualified / reached)
• Booked rate (booked / qualified and/or booked / total leads)
• Lead status breakdown (e.g., New, Contacted, Qualified, Unqualified, Booked, No Response)

Partner may white-label these reports for clients.

11) Client Ownership, Portability, and Offboarding
• Partner owns client relationship and may remove an Account with written notice.
• Provider will provide reasonable export of performance summaries upon request.
• If an end client wishes to move away from Partner, Partner and Provider will handle transitions in good faith; Provider does not solicit Partner’s clients.

12) Messaging Compliance (SMS/Email) — Partner Responsibility
Partner is responsible for ensuring end-client compliance with applicable laws and platform policies (e.g., TCPA, CTIA guidelines, local regulations), including:
• Proper opt-in/consent language on forms and lead sources
• Clear identification of business in messages
• STOP/HELP handling where applicable
• Reasonable message frequency

Provider can share template consent language upon request, but Partner must obtain appropriate approvals.

13) Data Handling + Confidentiality

• Partner will not share Provider’s internal documentation publicly.
• Provider will use lead data solely to operate and improve the service for the Account.
• Partner confirms it has rights to share lead data with Provider for processing.

• Provider does not guarantee a specific number of booked jobs, revenue, or conversion rate.
• Deliverability depends on carrier filtering, consent, lead quality, and message content.

15) Uptime and Maintenance
Provider aims for high availability but may perform maintenance.
• Provider will attempt to notify Partner of planned maintenance when feasible.
• Provider is not liable for outages caused by third-party platforms/integrations.

16) Term, Renewal, and Cancellation

• Minimum commitment (if applicable): 90 days at the committed Account count.
• Cancellation: Partner may cancel Accounts with 14 days notice before the next billing cycle.
• Provider may terminate for misuse, non-payment, or compliance risk.
