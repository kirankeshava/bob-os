2) What Provider Delivers (White‑Label Service)
Provider supplies the software and operational support to:
• Instantly respond to new inbound leads via SMS
• Ask short qualification questions (AI-driven branching where applicable)
• Route outcomes (booked / qualified / unqualified / no response / wrong number)
• Notify Partner/client and optionally book calls/appointments via calendar integration

3) What Partner Delivers (Client Relationship)
Partner owns the client relationship and is responsible for:
• Selling, contracting, and billing the end client (unless otherwise agreed)
• Ensuring the end client has required permissions (lead capture, texting consent where applicable)
• Providing/maintaining client assets (form access, ad platform leads access, calendar, business hours, offer details)
• First-line support to the client (Provider is second-line)

4) Commercial Terms (Pricing, Margins, Minimum Commitment)
4.1 Standard wholesale pricing (Partner pays Provider):
• Starter: $499 / account / month (1–2 accounts)
• Growth: $399 / account / month (3–9 accounts)
• Scale: $349 / account / month (10–24 accounts)
• Agency+ (Priority Support): $299 / account / month (25+ accounts)

4.2 Recommended retail pricing (Partner charges end clients):
• $499–$999 / account / month depending on niche + lead volume + routing/booking complexity.

4.3 Volume minimum for discounted tiers:
• Growth (3–9): minimum 3 active billed accounts
• Scale (10–24): minimum 10 active billed accounts
• Agency+ (25+): minimum 25 active billed accounts + priority support rules (Section 8)

4.4 Minimum term / commitment:
• Month-to-month by default.
• To receive Priority Support (Agency+), Partner commits to maintaining 25 active accounts for a minimum of 60 days. If Partner drops below 25 accounts during the commitment window, Provider may revert pricing to the applicable tier for that billing period.

4.5 Onboarding fees:
• Default: $0 setup fee.
• If Partner requests "done-for-you" migration or custom workflows beyond standard SOP, Provider may quote a one-time professional services fee (requires written approval; no charges without approval).

5) Billing and Payment
• Billing frequency: monthly in advance.
• Payment due: on invoice date unless otherwise stated.
• Late payment: Provider may pause message sending and automations for overdue invoices after reasonable notice.
• Taxes/fees: Partner is responsible for any taxes due on resale.

6) Account Definition and Fair Use
An "account" equals one end-client location/brand with its own lead sources and routing rules. Partner will not combine multiple unrelated end clients into a single account to bypass pricing.

7) Data, Privacy, and Client Content
7.1 Client Data
Partner and end clients may provide lead information (name, phone, source, etc.). Provider processes this data solely to deliver the service.

7.2 Ownership
Partner/end client owns their lead data. Provider does not sell lead data.

7.3 Confidentiality
Each party agrees to keep non-public information confidential, including pricing, workflows, prompt logic, and client lists.

7.4 Compliance
Partner is responsible for ensuring end client compliance with applicable laws and consent requirements for SMS/outreach in their jurisdiction. Provider provides tooling; Partner ensures the client’s consent language and processes are appropriate.

8) Support, SLAs, and Priority Support
8.1 Standard Support
• Channel: email to agent_bob_replit+lead-copilot@agentmail.to
• Response target: within 1 business day for non-urgent issues.

8.2 Priority Support (Agency+ tier only)
• Faster response target: within 4 business hours (business days) for production-impacting issues.
• Partner must provide complete intake details (client name, account, screenshots/logs, timestamps, affected lead examples).

8.3 What Support Covers
• Workflow configuration per SOP
• Troubleshooting lead ingestion, routing, booking, and qualification steps
• Minor copy edits to qualification questions

8.4 What Support Does Not Cover
• Managing client ad accounts, landing pages, or CRM builds outside the integration scope
• Legal compliance consulting
• Unlimited custom development without a separate written agreement

9) Implementation & Go‑Live Process (Summary)
Partner agrees to follow Provider’s SOP to reduce time-to-launch. Typical steps:
1) Intake form completed (lead sources, hours, qualification goals)
2) Connect lead source(s)
3) Configure qualification script + routing
4) QA with test leads
5) Go-live and first-week tuning

10) Reporting and KPIs
Provider will make performance metrics available for each account. Standard monthly KPIs include:
• Median response time (seconds)
• Contact rate (% leads that reply)
• Qualified rate (% leads qualified among contacted)
• Booked rate (% leads booked among qualified or total; Partner chooses)
• Lead status breakdown (New, Contacted, No Response, Qualified, Unqualified, Booked, Bad Number)

• Partner may market the service under its own brand.
• Partner will not represent Provider’s product as proprietary software built solely by Partner.

12) Non‑Exclusivity
This Agreement is non-exclusive. Provider may work with other agencies, and Partner may use other tools.

13) Term, Suspension, and Termination
• Term begins on Effective Date and continues month-to-month.
• Either party may terminate with 14 days’ notice.
• Provider may suspend affected accounts for non-payment, abuse, or suspected illegal activity.
• Upon termination, Provider will provide reasonable export/access to reporting data upon request (if available) and will stop processing new leads.

14) Limits of Liability
Provider is not liable for lost profits, indirect damages, or client ad spend waste. Provider’s maximum liability in any month is limited to the fees paid by Partner to Provider for the affected account(s) during that month.

15) Relationship
Partner and Provider are independent contractors. Nothing creates an employment, joint venture, or partnership relationship.
