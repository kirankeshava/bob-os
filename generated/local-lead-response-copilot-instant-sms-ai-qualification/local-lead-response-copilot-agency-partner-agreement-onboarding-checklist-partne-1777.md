3) PARTNER OPS SOP (INTERNAL) — INTAKE → PROVISION → SUPPORT → REPORTING
============================================================
Title: Partner Operations SOP — Local Lead Response Copilot (White‑Label)
Owner: Partner Ops (Bob)
Support inbox: agent_bob_replit+lead-copilot@agentmail.to

Goal
Provision new partner accounts fast, keep response-time KPIs high, and deliver monthly reporting consistently so partners retain clients.

A) Partner Intake (Day 0)
1. Receive inbound (email/web form/DM).
2. Send Partner Kit links (deck, one-pager, pricing, SOP, KPI template, agreement).
3. Confirm tier + expected number of accounts in next 30 days.
4. If partner wants Priority Support, confirm minimum commitment (10 accounts or $3,490/mo).
5. Create a Partner record:
   - Partner name
   - Primary contact
   - Preferred comms channel
   - Tier
   - Accounts list (blank initially)

B) Account Provisioning Workflow (per Client account)
Target SLA: launch within 3–5 business days after complete intake.
1. Receive completed Client Setup Checklist.
2. Validate inputs:
   - Lead sources identified
   - Booking route selected
   - Qualification rules clear
   - Compliance confirmation present
3. Build:
   - Connect lead source triggers
   - Configure instant SMS first response (under 60 seconds)
   - Configure qualification flow (3–6 short questions max)
   - Configure routing outcomes (booked / qualified-not-booked / not-qualified / no-response)
4. QA:
   - Submit 3 test leads
   - Confirm median response time in tests (target <60 seconds)
   - Confirm booking works + notifications route correctly
5. Launch:
   - Switch from test mode to live
   - Notify partner with “Live” confirmation + what to monitor first 48 hours

C) Support & Escalation
Principle: Partner is first-line; Provider is second-line.
1. If a Client pings Provider directly, reply: “Looping in your agency contact” and CC partner.
2. Categorize ticket:
   - P0 Outage (no leads sending, SMS failing)
   - P1 High impact (booking broken, wrong routing)
   - P2 Normal (copy edits, new qualifiers)
3. Response targets:
   - Priority Support: 1 business day
   - Standard: 2 business days
4. Resolution steps:
   - Reproduce issue using a test lead
   - Check integration status
   - Confirm lead payload fields
   - Patch + re-test + report back with what changed

D) Monthly Reporting Delivery (Partner-ready)
Timing: send within first 3 business days of each month.
For each Client account compile:
- Median response time (seconds)
- Contact rate (%)
- Qualified rate (%)
- Booked rate (%)
- Lead status breakdown counts: New, Contacted, Qualified, Booked, Not Qualified, No Response
Deliver:
- A simple table (copy/paste into Sheets)
- 3 bullets: what improved, what to test next month, any risks (compliance/integration)

E) Quarterly Optimization (Retention lever)
Every 90 days per account:
- Review question drop-off (where leads stop responding)
- Tighten/loosen qualifiers based on booked rate + sales feedback
- A/B test first message (short vs. value-first)
- Confirm booking availability isn’t limiting booked rate

F) 3×10 Accounts Growth Plan (Internal operating cadence)
Objective: recruit 3 agencies × 10 accounts each.
- Week 1: sign 3 agency partners (each commits to 10-account ramp).
- Week 2: launch first 3 accounts per agency (9 total).
- Week 3: launch next 4 accounts per agency (12 total).
- Week 4: launch final 3 accounts per agency (9 total).
Total: 30 accounts active.
Support rule: priority support only for partners meeting minimum commitment to prevent overload.
