1) Parties
This Partner Agreement (“Agreement”) is between:
• Provider: Local Lead Response Copilot (“Provider”)
• Agency Partner: __________________________ (“Partner”)
Effective Date: _____________________________

2) Purpose / What You’re Reselling
Provider offers a lead-response automation service that:
• Instantly texts new inbound leads from forms/ads
• Asks short qualifying questions (scripted/AI-driven)
• Routes outcomes (qualified/unqualified/no response)
• Notifies Partner/client and optionally books calls/appointments
Partner may resell the service to its end clients under Partner’s branding (“white-label”) unless otherwise agreed.

3) Pricing, Margins, and Minimum Commitment
3.1 Wholesale Pricing (paid by Partner to Provider)
Partner agrees to wholesale pricing per active client location/account according to the current tier sheet (example guidance):
• 1–2 accounts: $499/account/mo
• 3–9 accounts: $399/account/mo
• 10–29 accounts: $349/account/mo
• 30+ accounts: $299/account/mo
(Provider may update pricing for new accounts with 30 days’ notice; existing accounts remain at their current tier for the remainder of the commitment term.)

3.2 Partner Client Pricing (charged by Partner)
Partner may charge end clients $499–$999/account/mo (or higher) at Partner’s discretion.

3.3 Minimum Commitment for Priority Support
To receive “Priority Partner Support” (defined in Section 7), Partner maintains a minimum of:
• 3 active accounts OR $1,000/mo wholesale spend, and
• A 90-day commitment per new end-client account (unless otherwise agreed).

4) Billing Terms
• Billing cadence: monthly in advance.
• Accounts are billed as “active” if the automation is enabled and connected to live lead sources, phone number, or calendar.
• Past-due: Provider may pause automations if invoices are unpaid for 7+ days.
• Taxes/fees: Partner is responsible for any taxes due on resale; messaging/carrier fees may be passed through if applicable and disclosed.

5) Onboarding & Implementation Responsibilities
5.1 Provider Responsibilities
Provider will:
• Configure the automation workflow based on Partner/client inputs
• Connect lead sources (forms/webhooks/Zapier-like triggers as applicable)
• Configure qualification script + routing rules
• Run QA testing and provide a launch checklist

5.2 Partner Responsibilities
Partner will:
• Ensure end client has lawful consent to text leads (see Section 9)
• Provide timely access/credentials/integration approvals
• Provide call booking rules (hours, calendar owner, offer, service area)
• Provide approved message copy (or approve Provider’s draft)

5.3 Timeline
Typical setup target: 2–5 business days after Provider receives all required intake items. Complex integrations may take longer.

6) White‑Label / Branding
• Partner may present the service as its own.
• Provider branding will be hidden where feasible; some platform/system notices may still occur.
• Partner is responsible for its own marketing claims and representations to end clients.

7) Support and Service Levels
7.1 Standard Support (included)
• Email support via agent_bob_replit+lead-copilot@agentmail.to
• Response target: within 2 business days

7.2 Priority Partner Support (for Partners meeting minimums)
• Response target: within 1 business day
• Implementation queue priority
• Monthly performance review call (optional) for Partner’s top accounts

7.3 What Support Covers
• Workflow configuration adjustments
• Message copy tweaks
• Basic troubleshooting

7.4 What Support Does Not Cover (unless separately agreed)
• Agency sales enablement calls with end clients
• Full website/form rebuilds, ad management, or CRM migrations

8) Performance Disclaimer
Provider does not guarantee revenue outcomes. The service improves speed-to-lead and conversion opportunity; results depend on lead quality, offer, pricing, follow-up, and market conditions.

9) Compliance: Consent to Text / TCPA / Opt-Out
Partner represents and warrants that:
• End clients only text individuals who have provided appropriate consent (e.g., submitting a form with a clear SMS disclosure).
• All automated messages include opt-out instructions (e.g., “Reply STOP to opt out”).
• Partner/end client will honor opt-out requests promptly.
Provider can supply suggested disclosure language; Partner/end client is responsible for legal compliance.

10) Data Handling & Confidentiality
• Provider will treat Partner and end-client lead data as confidential.
• Provider uses lead data only to deliver and improve the service.
• Partner is responsible for securing its own systems and access to client CRMs/calendars.

11) Ownership
• Partner owns its client relationships.
• Provider owns its software, workflows, templates, and automation IP.

12) Term, Renewal, and Cancellation
• Term: month-to-month after any stated minimum commitment per account.
• Cancellation: Partner may cancel an account with 30 days’ written notice after the minimum commitment.
• Upon cancellation, automations may be disabled and phone numbers/integrations disconnected.

13) Non-Solicitation (Optional)
Provider will not knowingly solicit Partner’s end clients for a competing offer during the term, except via Partner introduction or with Partner’s written permission.

14) Limitation of Liability
To the maximum extent permitted by law:
• Provider is not liable for indirect/consequential damages.
• Provider’s total liability is limited to the fees paid by Partner to Provider in the prior 30 days for the affected account.

15) Publicity
Partner may list Provider as a backend technology partner only with Provider’s written consent. Provider may list Partner as a reseller unless Partner opts out in writing.

16) Agreement Updates
Provider may update these terms with 30 days’ notice. Continued use after the notice period constitutes acceptance.

PASTE‑READY END‑CLIENT PROPOSAL / CONTRACT INSERTS
(Agency can paste these into its proposal/MSA/SOW)

A) Scope of Work (Speed‑to‑Lead Automation)
“Agency will implement an automated lead-response workflow that contacts new inbound leads via SMS within ~60 seconds when possible, asks a short set of qualifying questions, and routes outcomes to the Client’s team and/or booking calendar. Workflow includes: (i) message copy setup, (ii) qualification steps, (iii) lead status tagging (e.g., New, Contacted, Qualified, Booked, Unresponsive), and (iv) notification/booking routing. Client acknowledges response time and booking outcomes depend on lead quality, offer, availability, and timely operational follow-up.”

B) Client Responsibilities (Access + Operations)
“Client will provide: (i) access to lead sources (forms/CRM/ads), (ii) a dedicated phone number or approved texting line, (iii) business hours and service area rules, (iv) calendar/booking access if appointment booking is enabled, and (v) timely approvals on message copy. Delays in providing these items may delay launch.”

C) SMS Consent / Opt‑Out Clause
“Client is responsible for ensuring it has obtained appropriate consent to send SMS messages to leads (e.g., via website form disclosure). Client agrees all SMS outreach will include opt-out language (e.g., ‘Reply STOP to opt out’) and that opt-out requests will be honored promptly. Client will not upload or message purchased lists or contacts without consent.”

Suggested Website Form Disclosure (Client can paste under form)
“By submitting this form, you agree to receive text messages and/or calls from [Business Name] about your request. Message & data rates may apply. Reply STOP to opt out.”

D) Reporting / KPI Deliverables
“Agency will provide a monthly performance snapshot including: median first response time, contact rate, qualified rate, booked rate, and lead status breakdown (e.g., Qualified, Unqualified, No Response, Opted Out).”

E) Service Limitations / No Guarantee
“Client understands this service improves speed-to-lead and qualification, but Agency does not guarantee specific revenue outcomes.”
