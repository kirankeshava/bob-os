1. Parties
These terms apply between:
- “Provider”: Local Lead Response Copilot (the software + implementation team)
- “Agency Partner”: the reseller/agency managing end clients

2. What you’re reselling
Provider supplies an “Instant SMS + AI Qualification + Booking automation” workflow for inbound leads from forms/FB lead ads and similar sources.

3. White-labeling
- Agency Partner may position the service under their own brand.
- Agency Partner may not represent themselves as the software developer/owner if asked directly.
- Provider may remain invisible to end clients unless Agency Partner requests otherwise.

4. Pricing + billing
- Agency Partner is billed per active end-client account on the agreed tier.
- Billing cadence: monthly in advance (unless otherwise agreed).
- Accounts are considered “active” once a phone number and at least one lead source is connected (or the account is launched), whichever comes first.

5. Minimum commitment + priority support
To access volume pricing and priority support:
- Minimum: 10 active accounts commitment (or equivalent agreed commitment).
- If active accounts drop below the minimum for two consecutive billing cycles, pricing may revert to the standard tier.

6. Payment terms
- Invoices are due upon receipt unless otherwise stated.
- Non-payment may result in pausing outbound messaging/automation until the invoice is resolved.

7. Agency responsibilities (required for success)
Agency Partner agrees to:
- Provide accurate client business info (hours, service area, qualification rules).
- Obtain end-client consent for texting and comply with local laws and platform policies.
- Maintain working lead source integrations and notify Provider of changes.
- Ensure a real human is available to take over when a lead wants to book or talk (unless using booking automation).

8. Data handling + access
- Provider will only access lead data needed to operate the automation.
- Agency Partner is responsible for what data is collected and how it’s used with the end client.
- Provider will not sell lead data.

9. Compliance + acceptable use
Agency Partner will not use the system for:
- Spam, purchased lead lists without consent, or deceptive messaging
- Illegal products/services
- Harassment or discriminatory messaging
Provider may suspend accounts that violate acceptable use.

10. Support + SLAs (plain language)
- Standard support: email support via agent_bob_replit+lead-copilot@agentmail.to
- Priority support (for committed partners): faster response targets and launch scheduling priority.
- Typical response target: within 1 business day (priority) / within 2 business days (standard).
- Urgent outages: best effort same-day during business hours.

11. Uptime and limitations
Provider aims for reliable operation but cannot guarantee uninterrupted service due to third-party dependencies (SMS carriers, form platforms, ad platforms).

12. End-client relationship
- Agency Partner owns the end-client relationship, pricing to end clients, and client support.
- Provider supports Agency Partner; Provider does not promise to provide end-client-facing support unless explicitly arranged.

13. Termination
- Either party may end the partnership with written notice.
- Active accounts will be offboarded at the end of the paid period unless renewed.

14. Confidentiality
Agency Partner agrees not to share Provider’s internal implementation details, pricing (if marked confidential), or operational playbooks beyond what is necessary to sell and operate.

15. Feedback and improvements
Agency Partner may submit feature requests. Provider may implement improvements at its discretion.

2) 30-DAY “3×10 ACCOUNTS” PARTNER ACQUISITION PLAN
=========================
Goal (30 days): Sign 3 agencies with a minimum 10-account commitment each (30 end-client accounts). 
Target economics:
- Agency pays Provider: $299–$499/account/mo (volume tier)
- Agency charges client: $499–$999/account/mo
- Agency margin: typically $200–$500/account/mo before their labor

Core pitch (one sentence):
“Local Lead Response Copilot turns every new lead into a 60-second SMS conversation that qualifies and books—so your clients stop losing high-intent leads to slow follow-up.”

Non-negotiable outcome metrics to sell:
- Median first response time: under 60 seconds
- Contact rate lift (vs baseline): +15–35% typical (varies by niche)
- Qualified rate: tracked and improved via question tuning
- Booked rate: measured and optimized

Week 1 (Build pipeline + assets + first meetings)
1. Build a list of 150–300 agencies that serve home services/local businesses:
   - Keywords: “home service marketing”, “lead gen agency”, “Google Ads plumber”, “HVAC marketing”, “roofing leads”, “PPC agency [city]”.
   - Focus on agencies already running FB/Google lead campaigns.
2. Outreach volume targets:
   - 20 LinkedIn connects/day
   - 20 cold emails/day
   - 10 follow-ups/day
3. Booking target:
   - 8–12 intro calls booked
4. Offer structure for fast closes:
   - “Pilot with 3 accounts in 7 days” OR “10-account commitment for priority launch slots + best pricing”

Week 2 (Close first partner + launch first accounts)
1. Close target: 1 agency partner with 10-account commitment.
2. Launch target: 3–5 client accounts live.
3. Record baseline KPIs per client:
   - Current response time (minutes)
   - Current contact rate
   - Current booked rate
4. Produce the first KPI report in week 2 to arm the agency with proof.

Week 3 (Scale to partner #2 + tighten SOP)
1. Close target: second agency with 10 accounts.
2. Launch target: 10–15 total live accounts.
3. Systemize:
   - Standardize templates for industries (HVAC, plumbing, roofing, pest control, med spa).
   - Build “default qualification trees” (3–5 questions).

Week 4 (Close partner #3 + stabilize reporting)
1. Close target: third agency (10 accounts).
2. Launch target: 25–30 live accounts.
3. Prove ROI:
   - “Speed-to-lead: under 60 seconds”
   - Booked-rate lift vs previous month
   - Lead status breakdown improvements

Close accelerators (use on calls)
- Scarcity: “We can support X priority launches/week.”
- Minimum commitment: “10-account minimum for priority support and volume pricing.”
- ROI math soundbite: “If you save 1 extra booked job/week, this pays for itself.”
