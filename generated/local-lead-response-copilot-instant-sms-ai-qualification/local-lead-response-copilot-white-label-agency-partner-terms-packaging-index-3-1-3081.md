1) Purpose
These terms describe how an agency partner ("Partner") can resell Local Lead Response Copilot to Partner’s clients ("End Clients") under a white-label relationship.

2) What the Service Does (Scope)
Provider supplies a “speed-to-lead” response and qualification system that:
- Instantly contacts new leads by SMS (optionally email)
- Asks short qualification questions
- Routes qualified leads to booking/calls/appointments based on rules
- Reports on response speed and funnel outcomes

3) Relationship Structure
- Partner owns the client relationship, pricing, and billing to End Clients.
- Provider supplies the software setup, automation logic, and ongoing platform support.
- Partner is responsible for client communications, expectation setting, and collecting any End Client assets (see Intake Checklist).

4) Pricing (Wholesale to Partner) + Minimum Commitment
Wholesale pricing (what Partner pays Provider) is per End Client account, per month:
- 1–2 accounts: $499 / account / month
- 3–9 accounts: $399 / account / month
- 10+ accounts: $299 / account / month

Minimum commitment for priority support: 3 accounts for 60 days.
- If Partner has fewer than 3 active accounts, Provider will still support, but turnaround times may be slower (see Support section).

Recommended retail pricing (what Partner charges End Clients):
- $499–$999 / account / month depending on niche, lead volume, and booking complexity.

5) 30‑Day Growth Plan Alignment (3×10 Accounts)
Provider’s volume tiers are designed to help Partner scale quickly:
- Target: 10+ accounts to unlock $299 wholesale pricing.
- Partner margin target: $200–$700 per account per month depending on retail price.

6) Billing & Payment
Week 1 policy (free launch): during initial free launch period, the service may be delivered at $0 to End Clients and/or Partners as agreed.
After free launch:
- Provider bills Partner monthly in advance (per active account).
- An account is “active” once provisioned and connected to a lead source OR once Partner requests “go live.”
- Partner is responsible for all End Client collections.

7) Setup & Implementation Timeline (Standard)
Typical implementation per End Client:
- Day 0–1: Intake (business info, lead source, qualification questions, booking route)
- Day 1–2: Build (automation + routing)
- Day 2–3: QA (test leads, edge cases)
- Day 3–5: Launch + first optimization pass
Timeline may vary based on how quickly Partner provides required assets.

8) Partner Responsibilities (Required)
Partner agrees to:
- Provide accurate client intake information and access/permissions needed to connect lead sources.
- Ensure End Clients comply with SMS/email marketing rules and have permission to contact leads.
- Set realistic expectations with End Clients (speed-to-lead improves contact and booking; results depend on lead quality, offer, hours of operation, and sales follow-up).

9) Provider Responsibilities (Required)
Provider agrees to:
- Configure the system according to the approved intake sheet.
- Maintain the automation logic and fix defects reasonably quickly.
- Provide monthly KPI reporting data and definitions (Partner can white-label in client reports).

Priority support (3+ accounts or 60-day minimum commitment):

- Fix/adjustment target: within 2–3 business days depending on complexity

Emergency issues (system down / no leads processing):

11) Data Handling & Access
- Provider will have access to lead data strictly for operating and improving the service.
- Partner and End Client data remains confidential.
- Provider will not market directly to Partner’s End Clients.
- Partner should avoid sharing unnecessary sensitive data (e.g., full credit card numbers).

12) White‑Labeling
- Partner may present the service as its own (white-label) unless otherwise agreed.
- Provider can supply unbranded KPI templates and implementation checklists.
- Partner may not resell Provider’s internal docs as standalone products.

13) Acceptable Use
Partner and End Clients agree not to use the service for:
- Spam, harassment, or contacting leads without consent
- Illegal or deceptive offers
- Circumventing carrier rules or sending prohibited content
Provider may suspend accounts that violate acceptable use.

14) Changes, Pauses, and Cancellations
- Partner can request configuration changes any time; turnaround depends on support tier.
- Partner may pause an End Client account at month-end with written notice.
- If an End Client cancels, Partner should notify Provider before the next billing cycle.

15) KPI Definitions (What Gets Reported)
Provider reports, at minimum:
- Median response time (lead received → first outbound message)
- Contact rate (reached: lead replied OR answered call OR booked)
- Qualified rate (met qualification criteria)
- Booked rate (booked appointment/call)
- Lead status breakdown (New, Contacted, Unresponsive, Qualified, Unqualified, Booked, No‑Show, Closed‑Won, Closed‑Lost)
