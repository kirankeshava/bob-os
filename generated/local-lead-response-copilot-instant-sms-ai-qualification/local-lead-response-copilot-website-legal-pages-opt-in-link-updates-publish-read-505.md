1) Who We Are
Local Lead Response Copilot provides automated tools to help local businesses respond to inbound leads (for example, from website forms, Typeform, Webflow, Facebook/Meta Lead Ads, Google Ads lead forms, and call tracking providers), ask qualifying questions by text, and help schedule calls or appointments.

2) Information We Collect
We collect information in three primary ways:

A. Information submitted by businesses (our customers)
• Account information (name, email)
• Business information (business name, service area, business hours)
• Configuration data (qualification questions, routing rules, calendars)

B. Information collected about leads/end-customers on behalf of businesses
When a lead submits a form or otherwise contacts a business, we may process:
• Contact information (name, phone number, email)
• Lead details (service requested, address or zip code, preferred timing)
• Message content (SMS conversation history)
• Consent details (timestamp, source form/ad, consent language shown, IP/user agent when available)

C. Technical and usage information
• Device and browser data, logs, and approximate location inferred from IP address
• Event data such as message delivery status, timestamps, and interaction events

3) How We Use Information
We use information to:
• Send fast responses to inbound leads via SMS
• Ask short qualification questions and route leads appropriately
• Schedule calls/appointments or notify business staff
• Maintain suppression lists (e.g., STOP requests)
• Detect and prevent abuse, spam, or fraud
• Provide support and improve the Service

4) SMS/Text Messaging Privacy and Consent
A. Consent and opt-in
Businesses using the Service are responsible for obtaining legally valid consent from their leads before sending marketing/promotional texts, and for providing required disclosures. The Service supports consent capture by storing:
• Consent language presented
• Timestamp and source (form/ad)
• Phone number and lead metadata

B. STOP/HELP handling
If a recipient texts STOP (or similar keywords), we suppress further texts to that number for that business unless the recipient later re-consents. If a recipient texts HELP, we provide a help message with contact instructions.

C. Quiet hours
Businesses can enable “quiet hours” to avoid texting at inappropriate times. If enabled, the Service queues messages until the next allowable time window based on the lead’s timezone (best-effort). Message delivery may be delayed to comply with quiet hours.

5) How We Share Information
We may share information with:
• Vendors who provide infrastructure (e.g., SMS carriers/aggregators like Twilio), hosting, analytics, and calendar integrations
• The business customer that the lead contacted (the intended recipient of the lead)
• Law enforcement or regulators if required by law

We do not sell personal information to data brokers.

6) Data Retention
We retain data as needed to provide the Service, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce agreements. Businesses may request deletion of their account data. Leads may contact the business they engaged with to request changes or deletion; we will assist the business as reasonably necessary.

7) Security
We use reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational measures to protect information. No system is 100% secure; please use the Service with appropriate safeguards.

8) Your Choices
• Opt-out of SMS: Reply STOP to any message.
• Help: Reply HELP for assistance.
