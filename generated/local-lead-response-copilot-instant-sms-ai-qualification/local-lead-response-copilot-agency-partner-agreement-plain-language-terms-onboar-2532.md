Local Lead Response Copilot — Agency Partner Agreement (Plain-Language Terms)
Version: v1.0 (Plain language, non-legal)

This Agency Partner Agreement (“Agreement”) is between Local Lead Response Copilot (“Provider”) and the agency/reseller (“Partner”). It is intended to clearly document how we work together so Partner can resell the service without Provider being on every call.

1) What the Service Is
Local Lead Response Copilot is an “instant speed-to-lead” system that (a) immediately texts new leads from forms/FB lead ads/other sources, (b) asks short qualifying questions, and (c) routes qualified leads to booking/call scheduling and/or notifies the business.

2) White-Label / Reseller Positioning
2.1 Partner may present the service under Partner’s brand (“white-label”) or as a co-branded solution.
2.2 If white-labeled, Provider will remain invisible to Partner’s end clients unless Partner requests otherwise.
2.3 Partner will not represent that they own Provider’s underlying software IP.

3) Partner Pricing, Margins, and Minimum Commitment
3.1 Wholesale pricing (paid by Partner to Provider):
- Tier A: 1–3 active client accounts: $499/account/month
- Tier B: 4–9 active client accounts: $399/account/month
- Tier C: 10+ active client accounts: $299/account/month
3.2 Recommended retail pricing (charged by Partner to end clients): $499–$999/account/month.
3.3 Minimum commitment for priority support and Tier C pricing: 10 active client accounts with a 90-day commitment.
3.4 “Active client account” means an end client instance that is live/connected and receiving leads.

4) Week 1 Free Launch Policy (No Spend)
4.1 During the initial free-launch period (first 7 days of program rollout), Partner may onboard clients on a 7-day free trial (no payment collection from end clients required).
4.2 Partner agrees to set proper expectations: trial validates response-time lift and qualification performance; continued service requires moving to a paid monthly plan.

5) Billing and Payment Terms
5.1 Provider invoices Partner monthly for all active client accounts.
5.2 Payment terms: net 7 days from invoice date unless otherwise agreed.
5.3 If Partner fails to pay within 14 days of invoice date, Provider may pause new provisioning and/or suspend service for accounts until payment is received.

6) Implementation Responsibilities (Who Does What)
6.1 Partner is the “client owner” and is responsible for:
- Getting end client approval to text leads and communicate via SMS.
- Collecting required intake items (see Section 7).
- Providing accurate lead source details (forms, webhook, CRM, FB lead routing, etc.).
- Confirming routing rules: who gets notified, when, and what counts as qualified.
6.2 Provider is responsible for:
- Setting up the Response Copilot flow based on Partner’s intake.
- Configuring qualification questions, lead statuses, and routing rules.
- Performing QA and launch checks.

7) Required Intake (Minimum Data Needed)
Partner will provide (per end client account):
- Business name, service area, and service categories
- Phone number(s) for call routing or booking link
- Hours of operation and after-hours handling
- Qualification targets (what “qualified” means)
- Lead source(s): website form/FB lead ads/landing page/CRM
- Compliance confirmation (end client has consent language on forms or equivalent)

8) Support, SLAs, and Priority Support
8.1 Standard support: email support via agent_bob_replit+lead-copilot@agentmail.to.
8.2 Response targets (business hours):
- Standard: within 1 business day
- Priority (10+ accounts with 90-day commitment): within 4 business hours
8.3 Provider will make reasonable efforts to maintain uptime; Provider is not liable for third-party outages (carrier delivery issues, FB outages, CRM/webhook downtime).

9) Service Levels (What We Measure)
Provider and Partner agree to measure performance using the monthly KPI template:
- Median response time (seconds)
- Contact rate (% leads that responded)
- Qualified rate (% contacted that qualify)
- Booked rate (% qualified that booked)
- Lead status breakdown (New/Contacted/Qualified/Booked/Unqualified/No-response)

10) Acceptable Use and Compliance
10.1 Partner/end client must not use the service for spam, illegal offers, harassment, or prohibited content.
10.2 Partner/end client is responsible for obtaining any required consent to send SMS messages and for honoring opt-out requests.
10.3 Provider will include opt-out language best practices, but Partner/end client retains compliance responsibility.

11) Data Handling and Confidentiality
11.1 Provider will treat Partner and end client data as confidential.
11.2 Provider may access lead data solely to operate, troubleshoot, and improve service performance.
11.3 Provider will not sell Partner or end client lead data.
11.4 Partner agrees to keep Provider’s internal workflows, playbooks, and configuration details confidential.

12) Intellectual Property
Provider retains all rights to software, templates, and automation logic. Partner retains rights to its own brand assets and client relationships.

13) Term, Cancellation, and Account Offboarding
13.1 Month-to-month unless a 90-day commitment is agreed for Tier C.
13.2 Partner may cancel an end client account with 30 days notice.
13.3 Provider will provide basic offboarding support (export/reporting handoff as available).

14) No Guarantees
Provider does not guarantee a specific revenue amount. The service improves speed-to-lead and workflow efficiency; outcomes depend on lead quality, offer, pricing, and follow-up.

15) Publicity
Partner may reference that it uses an “instant lead response + qualification system.” Provider will not publicly name Partner or Partner’s end clients without permission.

16) Limitation of Liability
To the maximum extent permitted, Provider’s liability is limited to the fees paid by Partner in the prior 30 days for the affected account(s). Provider is not liable for indirect or consequential damages.

17) How to Accept
Partner accepts these terms by replying “ACCEPT” to an email from agent_bob_replit+lead-copilot@agentmail.to or by requesting provisioning of accounts after receiving these terms.

Onboarding Packet Insert (Client-Facing) — “What to Expect in the First 7 Days”
(Agency partners can paste this into their own end-client onboarding email)

Subject: What to expect in your first 7 days (instant lead response + qualification)

You’re live (or about to be). Here’s exactly what will happen in the first week so you know what we’re measuring and why it matters.

Day 0–1: Intake + routing
- We confirm your business details, service area, hours, and what counts as a “qualified lead.”
- We connect your lead source (website form / FB lead ads / landing page / CRM) so every new lead triggers an immediate SMS.

Day 1–2: Qualification questions
- We install a short question flow that screens for intent and fit (ex: service type, urgency, zip code, budget range).
- We set the outcome rules: notify you, book an appointment, or tag as unqualified.

Day 2–3: QA + test leads
- We run test leads to verify response time, message tone, and routing.
- We confirm everyone who should be notified is receiving updates.

Day 4–7: Optimization
- We measure: median response time, contact rate, qualified rate, and booked rate.
- We tune wording, question order, and routing rules based on real lead responses.

What you should watch for
- Faster first response (seconds, not hours)
- More conversations started (contact rate)
- Fewer wasted calls (qualified rate)
- More booked jobs/calls (booked rate)
