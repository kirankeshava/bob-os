A) Target Prioritization (Who goes in the first 50)
Split Day-1 batch: 30 agencies / 20 operators.

Agencies (pick first):
1) Explicit “Facebook Ads / Lead Gen” positioning on Clutch/UpCity/LinkedIn.
2) Case studies mentioning home services (HVAC, plumbing, roofing, pest, restoration).
3) Offers: “Leads in 7 days”, “Pay per lead”, “Booked appointments”, “GoHighLevel/CRM automation”.
4) Team size 2–30 (owner-led or small team = fast decisions).

Operators (pick first):
1) High-intent, call-driven niches: water damage/restoration, HVAC emergency, plumbing, roofing.
2) Evidence of ad spend or lead capture: “Request a Quote” form, “Financing”, “24/7”, “Schedule online”.
3) Poor speed-to-lead signals: no chat, no instant booking, generic contact form, after-hours lead loss.

B) Enrichment Checklist (minimum fields per prospect)
For each of the first 50 prospects, capture:
1) Company name
2) Segment: Agency / Operator
3) Niche (HVAC/plumbing/roofing/etc or “FB lead gen agency”)
4) Location (city/state)
5) Decision-maker name (Owner/Founder/Head of Growth/GM/Marketing Manager)
6) Direct email (preferred) OR best contact path (contact form + LinkedIn)
7) LinkedIn URL (company + person if possible)
8) Source URL (Clutch/UpCity listing, website page, Google Maps profile)
9) Personalization line (1 sentence): prove you looked. Examples:
   - “Saw you run FB lead gen for HVAC and mention ‘booked appointments’ in your case study—speed-to-lead is usually the hidden lever.”
   - “Noticed your site has a ‘Request Service’ form but no instant confirmation—most companies lose after-hours leads there.”
10) Verification confidence: High / Medium / Low (if email is a guess).

C) Sending Plan (deliverability-first)
Day 0/1 warmup:
- Send 10–20 1:1 emails manually (not mail-merge), spaced out.
- Use plain text, no images, no attachments.
- Keep links to ONE link max (Calendly or website). If worried, Day-1 can omit Calendly link and ask “Should I send a link?”

Day 1–4 ramp:
- 50/day total (start at 30–40 if the domain is brand new).
- Mix segments to avoid pattern detection: alternate Agency/Operator.
- Throttle: 1 email every 3–6 minutes.
- If bounce rate > 5% in a batch: stop, tighten email verification, switch to contact form + LinkedIn for the remaining.

Follow-up cadence:
- Day 2: send Step-2 follow-up email to Day-1 non-replies.
- Day 3: LinkedIn follow-up to accepted connections.
- SMS: only 1:1 to known-consent leads or where prospect has clearly published a business number for inquiries; keep it compliant and optional.

D) Tracking/CRM (single-row lead tracking fields)
Use statuses:
- Not started
- Enriched
- Sent (Email 1)
- Follow-up due
- Sent (Email 2)
- Replied
- Qualified
- Demo booked
- No fit
- Closed (Pilot started)

Log per touch:
- Date sent, channel (Email/LinkedIn/SMS), template used (Agency v1 / Operator v1), outcome, next step date.
