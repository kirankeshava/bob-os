1) Parties & Purpose
This Agreement is between (a) “Provider” (Local Lead Response Copilot) and (b) “Partner” (your agency). The purpose is to allow Partner to resell and/or white‑label the Local Lead Response Copilot service to Partner’s end clients (“Clients”), where the system instantly texts new leads, qualifies them via automated questions, and routes/bookings based on responses.

2) What the Service Does (Scope)
Provider supplies:
- Automated instant SMS outreach to new leads (from forms, FB Lead Ads via integration, or other lead sources).
- AI‑assisted qualification flows (short Q&A) and routing logic (book call/appointment, notify team, or mark lead status).
- Basic reporting metrics (response time, contact rate, qualified rate, booked rate, lead status breakdown).

Not included unless explicitly agreed in writing:
- Paid ad management, creative, landing pages, lead generation.
- Call answering, live chat staffing, or human SDR services.
- Custom app development beyond normal configuration.

3) White‑Label & Resale
Partner may:
- Resell the Service to Clients under Partner’s brand (“white‑label”) or as “powered by Local Lead Response Copilot.”
- Set its own retail pricing.

Partner may not:
- Represent the software as proprietary technology built by Partner.
- Reverse engineer, copy, or create a competing service based on Provider configurations, prompts, flows, or templates.
- Publicly disclose Provider’s internal playbooks/templates without permission.

4) Pricing, Billing, & Payment Terms (Partner → Provider)
Partner pays Provider per active Client account as follows (default guidance; final tier set at onboarding):
- Tier 1 (1–3 accounts): $499/account/month
- Tier 2 (4–9 accounts): $399/account/month
- Tier 3 (10–19 accounts): $349/account/month
- Tier 4 (20+ accounts): $299/account/month

Minimum commitment for discounted tiers:
- Tier 2+: minimum 3‑month commitment at the tier level.
- Tier 3+: minimum 3‑month commitment + partner agrees to provide required client inputs within 5 business days of kickoff to keep launch timeline.

Invoicing & due dates:
- Provider invoices monthly in advance.
- Payment due within 7 days of invoice date.
- If payment is late by 10+ days, Provider may pause sending/automation for impacted accounts until current.

Pass‑through costs (SMS/telephony):
- Unless stated otherwise, standard platform messaging/telephony usage may be billed as pass‑through (at cost) or included up to a fair‑use threshold. Provider will disclose the approach at onboarding.

5) Your Client Pricing (Suggested)
Partner is encouraged (not required) to retail the Service at:
- $499–$999/client/month depending on niche, lead volume, and booking value.
This supports Partner margins while keeping Client ROI positive.

6) Onboarding & Implementation Responsibilities
Provider responsibilities:
- Provide onboarding form and implementation checklist.
- Configure core workflow per Client: lead capture → instant SMS → qualification → booking/routing → reporting.
- Provide a basic QA test and go‑live checklist.

Partner responsibilities:
- Collect and provide accurate Client inputs (see Section 7).
- Ensure Client understands consent/compliance requirements.
- Be primary point of contact for Client unless otherwise agreed.
- Handle Client billing and first‑line support (white‑label model), escalating technical issues to Provider.

7) Required Client Inputs (Partner Must Collect)
For each Client, Partner must provide:
- Business name, main phone number(s), service area, business hours.
- Lead sources (form URLs, FB Lead Ads access method, CRM/webhook details).
- Booking destination (calendar link, scheduling system, or internal handoff rules).
- Qualification questions (or approve recommended templates).
- Contact routing list (who gets notified; how; when).
- Compliance confirmation: Client has appropriate consent language on forms/ads for SMS.

8) Compliance, Consent, & Acceptable Use
Partner and Clients must comply with all applicable laws and policies for messaging/telephony, including but not limited to TCPA/CTIA guidelines and any platform terms.

Key requirements:
- Leads must have provided consent to be contacted by SMS (e.g., form checkbox language or ad lead form disclosure).
- Opt‑out must be honored (e.g., “STOP” keyword).
- No prohibited content (spam, harassment, illegal services).

Provider may suspend or terminate accounts that create compliance risk.

9) Data Handling & Privacy
- Provider processes lead contact data only to deliver the Service.
- Partner is responsible for its own privacy policy with Clients.
- Provider will take reasonable measures to protect data but does not guarantee against all security risks.
- Upon termination, Provider can provide an export of reporting summaries upon request (where technically feasible).

11) Reporting & Performance Disclaimers
Provider supplies reporting on:

Provider does not guarantee:
- Number of leads, revenue outcomes, booked jobs, or ad performance.
Results depend on lead quality, offer, pricing, availability, and Client follow‑through.

- Cancellations require 14 days notice before next billing cycle.
